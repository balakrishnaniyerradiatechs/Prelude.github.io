UPS Testing Procedure
Activant strongly suggests you test your UPS semi-annually to ensure it works correctly. The testing procedure is only for use with Powerware UPSs purchased through Activant. To test the UPS, use the following steps.
1
Confirm you have a good backup. This is strictly a safety measure and most likely will not be needed. You can use the previous nights backup if completed successfully.
2
Have all users log off the system. Lock users out. While logged in as root, at the command prompt, ENTER touch /etc/nologin.
3
Confirm that all continuous printers and schedulers are stopped.
4
Stop and re-start UniData. While logged in as root, ENTER stopud and ENTER startud.
5
On the console, leave one session logged in as root.
6
Record the exact time. Unplug the UPS from the wall outlet. The UPS should start beeping. Your system should still be up and running.
7
Watch the screen to keep track of time of events
-
A message notifying you that the system is coming down in 5 minutes, please log out is displayed about 5 minutes after the UPS is unplugged. Do not log out from the console.
-
After the message is displayed. ENTER showud every few seconds. The processes will stop being displayed after a while.
-
Shortly after UniData is down, the AIX starts shutting down.
8
The UPS should provide power to the system completely through the AIX shutdown. Halt Completed is displayed on the screen.
9
If the system stayed powered through the AIX shutdown, the test was successful. Do the following:
-
Plug the UPS into the wall outlet.
-
Bring the system back up.
-
Start UniData.
-
Start any continuous printers needed.
-
Start any scheduler processes needed.
-
Notify users the system is now available.
10
If the system failed keeping power through the AIX shutdown, the test was unsuccessful. Do the following:
-
Plug the UPS into the wall outlet.
-
Notify Prelude the UPS test failed.
