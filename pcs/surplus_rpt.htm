Surplus Report
The Surplus Report lists products with an available inventory balance that is greater than the EOQ (Economic Order Quantity) plus the line point, for a warehouse. The report also suggests warehouses that may be candidates for receiving a transfer of the surplus inventory.
Menu Path: Purchasing > Reports > Surplus Reports > Surplus Inventory
Selection Criteria
Use the following criteria to limit the information to print on the report:
•
Division. The highest possible grouping in your company (e.g., Eastern Division, Western Division).
•
Region. A subset of Division - if Division is used (e.g., Southeast Region, Northwestern Region).
•
Warehouse. The warehouse for listing surplus inventory.
•
Department. A subset of warehouse. Typically used to separate expenses within a warehouse (e.g., accounting, sales, warehouse).
•
Cost Center. A subset of Department - if Department is used. Typically used to separate expenses within a warehouse or department (e.g., shipping, receiving, outside sales, inside sales).
•
Product Line. The product lines to include.
•
Sort by Product Line/Vendor. Determines how you want to sort the report data:
-
P - Sort by product.
-
V - Sort by vendor.
Column Descriptions
Column Heading
Description
Whse
The warehouse containing the surplus inventory.
Product Number
Products considered to have a surplus quantity.
Description
The description of the product.
On-Hand
The quantity currently on hand of the product.
Commtd
The quantity committed to open sales orders, work orders, or transfers.
Op/Lp
The order point / line point of the product (set in Purchasing Parameters Maintenance).
Type
Purchase method for the product set up in Purchasing Parameters:
•
EOQ - Economic Order Quantity.
•
CLS - Class (always used for Replenishment Method T).
•
AWU - Average Weekly Usage. Refer to Tracking Average Weekly Usage” in the Inventory documentation.
•
DRP - Distribution Requirements Planning. Used with the Forecasting module primarily for products with long lead times.
•
DS - Days Supply. Defines a number of days supply to keep on hand.
Max/Rbq
The maximum recommended buy quantity.
Surplus
Surplus quantity of the product on hand. The amount over the EOQ plus the line point.
Cost
Unit cost of the product.
Ext-Surplus
The total extended dollar value of the surplus quantity.
Cl
Product Class. Refer to “Classifying Products” in the Inventory Module.
Candidates to Transfer To:
Whse
The warehouses that you might want to consider transferring the surplus quantity to.
Class
The inventory classification of the product in the receiving warehouse.
On-Hand
The on-hand quantity of the product in the receiving warehouse.
Op/Lp
The order point/line point of the product in the receiving warehouse.
Commtd
The quantity committed to sales orders, transfers, orders in the receiving warehouse.
Type
The purchase method for the product set up in Purchasing Parameters for the product in the receiving warehouse:
•
EOQ - Economic Order Quantity.
•
CLS - Class (always used for Replenishment Method T).
•
AWU - Average Weekly Usage. Refer to “Tracking Average Weekly Usage” in the Inventory Documentation.
•
DRP - Distribution Requirements Planning. Used with the Forecasting module primarily for products with long lead times.
•
DS - Days Supply. Defines a number of days supply to keep on hand.
Max/Rbq
The maximum recommended buy quantity of the product in the receiving warehouse.
Usage
The monthly usage of the product in the receiving warehouse.
