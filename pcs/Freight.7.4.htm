Applying Estimated Freight to P/Os and Receivers
To estimate freight on a purchase order:
1
For Freight Flag on the Purchase Order Header screen, choose one of the following:
•
Y - The system calculates an estimated freight amount and notifies accounts payable that you are responsible for any freight charges on the purchase order.
•
N - Freight is not estimated and accounts payable is notified that you are not responsible for freight charges for the purchase order.
Freight is estimated during Purchase Order Entry and Stock Receipts Entry and is based on the freight allocation method in the product’s product line (pound, dollar, unit, or volume). See Estimating Average Cost for Freight Charges.
If the Freight Allocation-P/O flag is set to Y, the system checks for a rate from either Product Warehouse Maintenance or from a freight matrix. See Entering Freight Rates.
2
When you receive the material and save the receiver, if the system finds a freight rate in the product line, the estimated freight amount is calculated and displayed in the Freight Closeout screen.
3
Accept the estimated Freight Amount or enter another amount.
4
Press F2-Save.
When you save the freight data, freight is allocated to each line item received by dividing each line item’s extended dollars, units, pounds, or volume by the total received amount. See step 1 in the following example.
The freight amount is calculated by multiplying the total freight amount by each line item’s percentage of freight. See step 2 in the following example.
After the freight is allocated to each line item, the amount is added to the line item’s extended total and divided by the quantity received to determine the adjusted cost for each unit. See step 3 in the example below.
Example:
A purchase order contains 2 line items for a total cost of $150.00:
-
Product A: 10 (Qty) x 6.00 (cost) = $60.00
-
Product B: 10 (Qty) x 9.00 (cost) = $90.00
The freight charged by the vendor is $12.00 and the Freight Allocation method is $.
1
Calculate the freight percentage per line:
•
Product A % of freight = $60.00 / $150.00 = 40%
•
Product B % of freight = $90.00 / $150.00 = 60%
2
Calculate freight amount per line:
•
Product A freight $ = $12.00 x 40% = $4.80
•
Product B freight $ = $12.00 x 60% = $7.20
3
Adjusted cost per unit:
•
Product A adjusted cost $ = ($60.00 + 4.80) / 10 = $6.48
•
Product B adjusted cost $ = ($90.00 + 7.20) / 10 = $9.72
Freight Allocation to Sales Orders
Option Parameters > Purchasing Parameters > Allocate Linked Frt To S/O: determines if the Freight to Sales Order screen appears in Stock Receipts Entry and Direct Stock Receipts Entry:
•
Y - Freight is allocated proportionally to Inbound Freight for any linked sales orders.
•
I - Same as Y, but it also Includes the freight cost paid expenses as part of the G/L posting for the receiver.
•
N - Freight for received material is not automatically passed on to linked sales orders.
Serialized Items
Stock receipts captures the estimated freight amount per serialized item. The system divides the freight among the line items and adds it to the received cost. For example, stock is received on a purchase order containing one line item for 2 pieces of a serialized product. The product’s cost is 10.00 and the estimated freight is 6.00. The system proportions the freight between the two products giving each a landed cost of 13.00 and a freight amount of 3.00 per serial number.
Vendor Invoice Reconciliation Entry captures the actual freight amount per serialized item. The actual freight replaces the estimated freight captured during stock receipts.
Core Products
Core products are not allocated freight if the exchange product is also on the purchase order. The freight is allocated to the exchange product instead of the core product. If a core item is on a purchase order and the exchange product is not, freight is allocated to the cost of the core product.
