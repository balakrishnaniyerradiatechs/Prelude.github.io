Confirmed/Expedite Date Exception Report
The Confirmed Expedite Date Exception report lists purchase orders for which the confirmed delivery date is later than the expected date. You can use this information to help analyze vendor performance. Refer to Tracking Vendor Performance.
To enter a confirmed delivery date on a purchase order, enter the date in Delivery Date on either the Purchase Order Header screen or the P/O Detail Information screen and select the Confirmed option.
Delivery Date Days Late
Del Except Delay Days in Option Parameters > Purchasing Parameters allows you to enter the number of days a confirmed delivery date is after the original purchase order delivery date that will cause a purchase order to appear on the Confirmed/Expedite Date Exception report.
For example, if Del Except Delay Days is set to 10, and you change the delivery date on a purchase order (header level or line level) to a date that is 11 days past the original delivery date, the P/O will appear on the Confirmed/Expedite Date Exception report.
Menu Path: Purchasing > Reports > Confirmed/Expedite Date Exception
Selection Criteria
Use the following criteria to limit the information to print on the report:
•
Division. The highest possible grouping in your company (e.g., Eastern Division, Western Division).
•
Region. A subset of Division - if Division is used (e.g., Southeast Region, Northwestern Region).
•
Warehouse. The warehouse assigned to receive the material.
•
Department. A subset of warehouse. Typically used to separate expenses within a warehouse (e.g., accounting, sales, warehouse).
•
Cost Center. A subset of Department - if Department is used. Typically used to separate expenses within a warehouse or department (e.g., shipping, receiving, outside sales, inside sales).
•
Vendor. Vendor(s) for which you want to list late delivery dates.
Column Descriptions
Column Heading
Description
Vend/Name
Line 1: Vendor number.
Line 2: Vendor description.
Expedite Contact/Phone
The expedite contact person’s name and phone number as entered in Vendor Maintenance > F7-Contacts.
P/O#
The purchase order number.
Product Number/
Description
Line 1: The products to expedite.
Line 2: The description of the product.
Qty-Opn
The quantity not yet received. If an item was partially shipped, this is the quantity originally ordered minus the quantity received to date.
U/M
The unit of measure for the product as entered on the purchase order.
Org-Date
The original date that the order was expected to be delivered.
Del-Date
The date the line item is expected to be delivered. Defaults from the header but can be overridden at the line item level. The date may be followed by one of the following status codes:
•
C - Date originally confirmed by vendor.
•
E - Date confirmed by vendor after expediting.
•
O - Date overridden by operator.
•
Blank - Date assigned when the purchase order was entered.
