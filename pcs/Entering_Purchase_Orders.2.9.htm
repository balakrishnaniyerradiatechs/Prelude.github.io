Splitting Line Items for Delivery Dates
If you want to divide the total quantity of a product on a purchase order into separate shipments, you can split the quantity to accommodate two or more delivery dates. Nightly Processing uses the delivery dates to calculate replenishment quantities.
You cannot split delivery dates for lot pack and linked items.
When you enter line items on a purchase order, Del-Date in the P/O Detail Information screen is the delivery date for the first part of the quantity to ship. To split the remaining quantities into separate delivery dates:
1
Place the cursor on the item to split. From the Action Bar, select Prod Func > Line Item Split. The PO Split Lines screen appears.
2
Enter the Quantity that you want to ship on the second delivery date.
3
For Del Date, enter the date when this quantity will be delivered. The following prompt appears:
This date has been...
4
Choose from the following:
•
Confirmed: The delivery date is the date originally confirmed by the vendor. A C appears by Del-Date.
•
Expedited: The delivery date is the date confirmed by vendor after expediting. An E appears by Del-Date.
•
Overridden: The original date was overridden. An O appears by Del-Date.
•
Return To Date: You can change the date entered.
5
For Number of Freight Days, enter the estimated number of days it takes for the merchandise to arrive at your warehouse for the ship via type selected.
6
Continue to enter quantities and delivery dates for as many split quantities as necessary. The overall quantity in the PO Split Lines screen must not be equal to or greater than the original line item quantity on the purchase order. The quantity remaining for the line item uses the original delivery date from the P/O Detail Information screen.
7
Press F2-Save. The split line items display on the purchase order with the respective new quantities and delivery dates. All other line item information mirrors the line to be split. The original quantity is modified to equal the original quantity minus the new split quantities.
When all line items are entered, you can complete the purchase order. Refer to Completing a Purchase Order.
