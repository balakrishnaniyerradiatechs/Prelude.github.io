Printing Purchase Orders
If your warehouse is not set up to continuously print, email, or fax purchase orders, you must use the batch print mode to generate all purchase orders in the queue. You do not have to print purchase orders to receive stock.
To batch print, email, or fax purchase orders:
1
Choose Purchasing > Purchase Order Printing to open the Purchase Order Printing screen.
2
Enter the P/O Number or leave blank to print all purchase orders.
3
Enter the Warehouse or leave blank to print purchase orders for all warehouses.
4
Enter the Buyer to print purchase orders just for that buyer or leave blank to print purchase orders for all buyers.
5
Enter the Purchase Order Source types to include:
•
ALL - All purchase order types.
•
R - Only P/Os entered through Purchase Order Entry.
•
S - Only P/Os entered through Sales Order Entry.
6
Enter the Printer Form Queue to indicate where the purchase orders should print.
7
Press ENTER and the following question appears:
Do You Wish To........
8
Choose Execute to print the selected purchase orders and the following prompt appears:
## Records Selected For Purchase Order Printing! Load Purchase Order Forms In Printer And Align Visually
9
Choose one of the following:
•
Print Purchase Order: Prints selected purchase orders.
•
Mask Print Test: Prints a mask of the P/O to make sure the paper is aligned (not necessary if using a laser printer). After the forms are correctly aligned select Print Purchase Order.
•
Abort Printing: No purchase orders print.
10
After you select Print Purchase Order, the following prompt displays:
Purchase Order Printing Complete...
11
Choose one of the following:
•
Process: Tells the system that the purchase orders printed successfully.
•
Reprint All: Reprints all purchase orders. This option is useful if only a couple of purchase orders printed successfully from a large print job. The system reprints the purchase orders and this prompt appears again.
•
Single Reprint: Reprints orders individually. This option is useful if only a couple of purchase orders jammed in the printer. Enter the purchase order numbers to reprint.
•
Abort: Cancels the purchase order print process.
12
Once you select Process, the following prompt appears:
Are You Sure You Want To Process.
13
Select one of the following:
•
Reprompt: Keeps this dialog box open.
•
Yes: Completes the purchase order printing process and updates the Purchase Order Log. See Purchase Order Log Inquiry.
•
No: Returns to step 9.
