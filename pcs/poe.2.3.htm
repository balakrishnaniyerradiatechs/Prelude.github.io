Purchase Order Types
The following purchase order types are available:
•
01 - Regular. Standard order of merchandise for delivery to your warehouse.
•
02 - Direct Ship. The vendor ships the material directly to your customer. You must specify the sales order number to link to the P/O. The shipping address from the sales order is the shipping address for the purchase order. The sales order must be a type 04 (direct ship) or contain line items with a D supply code.
•
03 - Will Call. You pick up the order from the vendor on the way to the customer. The merchandise is received automatically when you complete the purchase order, making the P/O a one-step process.
•
04 - Multi Branch. Material purchased for multiple warehouses on one purchase order. A separate purchase order prints for each warehouse with the corresponding address.
•
05 - Special. Non-stock material required for a special purpose. The items do not have to be linked to a sales order, transfer, or work order.
•
07 - Capital Assets. Company assets such as office furniture and copiers that are not products in the system. You must enter the G/L account numbers for the assets.
•
08 - Non Inventory Expense. Similar to the 07 (Capital Assets) type, but for products such as office supplies that are not capital assets.
•
09 - Future. You can enter a future date if the material is not for shipment until a later date. Nightly Processing uses this date to calculate re-order quantities. When the future date arrives, the purchase order automatically changes to a type 01 (Regular).
•
10 - Consigned. Vendor consigned inventory that is tracked and kept separately from regular inventory. Linked, lot-controlled, and serialized items are not allowed on this purchase order type. Consigned purchase orders are reconciled in Manual Invoice Entry.
•
11 - Recurring Billing. Allows an item to be received multiple times. You can use this to receive rental items multiple times until the rental is complete. Once the rental item is updated as complete, the purchase order is closed. This purchase order type is for non-stock items whose Recurring Billing flag is set to Y in Product Maintenance > F5-Add Info > F5-Add Info.
•
12 - OTW-Stock. On-the-water stock purchase orders used to import from an overseas vendor. You can create a regular type 01 purchase order; then, when the vendor sends an acknowledgement regarding shipping purchase orders, use On The Water Maintenance to combine multiple purchase orders to create a new OTW purchase order. Use the new OTW purchase order number to receive stock. Refer to “Receiving On-The-Water Merchandise” in the Inventory documentation for information on activating and receiving on-the-water merchandise.
•
13 - OTW-Direct. The same as type 12, except it is for direct shipments to your customer.
 
 
The following purchase order types cannot be changed when editing a purchase order:
•
03 - Will Call.
•
05 - Special.
•
07 - Capital Assets.
•
08 - Non-Inventory Expense.
 
The following purchase order types can be changed with the following restrictions:
•
01 - Regular - cannot be changed to types 02, 03, 07, 08 and 11.
•
02 - Direct Ship - cannot be changed to types 03, 04, 05, 07, 08, 09, 10, 11, 12, and 13.
•
04 - Multi Branch - cannot be changed to types 02, 03, 07, 08 and 11.
•
09 - Future - cannot be changed to types 02, 03, 07, 08 and 11.
•
10 - Consigned - cannot be changed to types 02, 03, 07, 08 and 11.
•
11 - Recurring Billing - cannot be changed to types 02, 03, 07, and 08.
•
12 - OTW Stock - cannot be changed to types 02, 03, 07, 08, and 11.
•
13 - OTW Direct - cannot be changed to types 02, 03, 07, 08, and 11.
