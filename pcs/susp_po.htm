Suspended Purchase Orders
The Suspended Purchase Order work queue holds purchase orders that were suspended in Purchase Order Entry. You can edit or merge these orders with other open purchase orders for the same vendor. See Merging With a Suspended Purchase Order.
For suspended purchaser orders to appear in the buyer queue, set Maintain Suspended P/O’s to Y in Option Parameters > Purchasing Parameters. When you suspend an order, you will have the option to track the order so that it will appear in the Buyer Queue Inquiry.
Editing and Releasing Suspended Purchase Orders
To list and release suspended purchase orders:
1
Select one of the following to access the Suspended P/O Inquiry screen:
•
Purchasing > Inquiries > Buyer Queue Inquiry. Place the cursor in front of Suspended Purchase Orders and press F5-Review.
•
Purchasing > Inquiries > Buyer Queue Inquiries > Suspended Purchase Orders. Enter the Buyer Number.
Suspended purchase orders appear.
2
Highlight the purchase order to edit and press F6-Edit. The Purchase Order Entry screen opens to allow you to edit and release the purchase order. Refer to Entering Products and Completing a Purchase Order for more information on entering line items and completing a purchase order.
3
After reviewing and/or editing all suspended purchase orders, press F2-Finish to close the screen.

•
E - Emergency order.

•
N - Not an emergency or a backorder.

The total weight of the order if weights are used.
P/O Date
The date the purchase order was entered.
P/O Amount
The total dollar amount of this purchase order.
#Lines
 
The number of lines items on the purchase order.
Target
 
Indicates if the recommended purchase order meets the target amount set up for the vendor line:
•
Y - The order meets the vendor line target amount.
•
N - The order does not meet the vendor line target amount.
F2-Finish
Closes the screen.

Opens the Purchase Order Display screen. Information regarding the header, linked items, credit information, and freight is also accessible from this screen.

Opens the Purchase Order Entry screen to allow you to edit and complete the order.

Allows you to enter a reference number for quick access. Valid codes are order number, page number, line number, B (bottom), or T (top).
