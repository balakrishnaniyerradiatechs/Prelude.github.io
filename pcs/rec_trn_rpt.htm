Recommended Stock Transfer Report
The Recommended Stock Transfer report lists recommended transfers for products with a T (transfer) replenishment method that are at or below the minimum stocking level. The report also includes non-stock and backordered products that need to be transferred. This information is generated by Nightly Processing and also appears in the Buyer Queue Inquiry under Recommended Stock Transfers.
Rec. Transfers By Buyer in Inventory Parameters > F5-Add Info, determines if the recommended stock transfers generated by Nightly Processing are separated by buyer on the Recommended Stock Transfer Report:
•
Y - Recommended stock transfers are separated by buyer. The buyer for transfers are set in Vendor Maintenance, but can be overridden in Vendor Line Maintenance.
•
N - Recommended stock transfers are not separated by buyer.
Menu Path: Purchasing > Reports > Nightly Processing Reports > Recommended Stock Transfer Report
Selection Criteria
Use the following criteria to limit the information to print on the report:
•
From Warehouse. The warehouse where the products will ship from.
•
To Warehouse. The warehouse where the products will ship to.
Column Descriptions
Column
Description
From
The shipping warehouse.
To
The receiving warehouse.
Transfer
The number for the recommended transfer.
Product Number/
Description
Line 1: Product number.
Line 2: Product description.
Avail
The available quantity at the shipping warehouse.
Usage
The usage at the shipping warehouse.
On Hand
The on-hand quantity at the receiving warehouse.
Comm
The committed quantity at the receiving warehouse.
Onord
The on-order quantity at the shipping warehouse.
Avail
The available quantity at the shipping warehouse.
Cls
The product’s class. Refer to “Classifying Products” in the Inventory documentation.
Sea
Indicates if the item is seasonal:
•
N - Not seasonal.
•
Y - Seasonal.
Month 1&4
Line 1: Last month’s usage at the receiving warehouse.
Line 2: Usage at the receiving warehouse from 4 months ago.
Month 2&5
Line 1: Usage at the receiving warehouse from 2 months ago.
Line 2: Usage at the receiving warehouse from 5 months ago.
Month 3&6
Line1: Usage at the receiving warehouse from 3 months ago.
Line 2: Usage at the receiving warehouse from 6 months ago.
Usage
The product’s usage at the receiving warehouse.
Op/Min
The minimum or order point. When the available quantity at the receiving warehouse falls below this number, a stock transfer is recommended.
RBQ
The product’s recommended buy quantity.
PKG
The package quantity of the product.
Unit Cost
The cost of one unit of the item.
Extension
The extended dollar value of the recommended transfer quantity.
