Turns Report By Vendor/Warehouse
The Turns Report By Vendor/Warehouse displays the average annual inventory turnover calculated for the total products sold for a vendor for a warehouse and compares it to the number of turns targeted for the vendor for the year.
The turnover is calculated by dividing the cost of stock sold in the last 12 months by the inventory value of the current on-hand quantity. The higher the turns the faster the products are selling, which usually indicates which products are more profitable to sell.
Menu Path: Purchasing > Reports > Vendor Turn Reports > Vendor Turns Report
Selection Criteria
Use the following criteria to limit the information to print on the report:
•
Division. The highest possible grouping in your company (e.g., Eastern Division, Western Division).
•
Region. A subset of Division - if Division is used (e.g., Southeast Region, Northwestern Region).
•
Selling Warehouse. The warehouse responsible for selling the material.
•
Department. A subset of warehouse. Typically used to separate expenses within a warehouse (e.g., accounting, sales, warehouse).
•
Cost Center. A subset of Department - if Department is used. Typically used to separate expenses within a warehouse or department (e.g., shipping, receiving, outside sales, inside sales).
•
Vendor. Vendor(s) for which you want to display inventory turns.
•
Stocking Status. The stocking status of the products to include on the report:
-
S - Stock.
-
N - Non-stock.
-
B - Both stock and non-stock.
-
C - Consignment.
-
A - All (stock, non-stock, consignment).
•
Include Transfer Usage?. Determines if product usage from transfers is included to calculate usage value (cost of goods sold):
-
Y - Include transfer usage to calculate vendor turns.
-
N - Do not include transfer usage. Only include sales order and work order usage.
Column Descriptions
Column Heading
Description
Vendor
The vendor number and description.
Actual Cost of Sales
The sum of the cost of products sold on sales orders, work orders, and transfers for the past 12 months, not including the current month, extended by the current average cost.
Inventory Dollars
The dollar amount of the vendor’s products currently in inventory in the warehouse, including linked and vendor consigned inventory, extended by average cost.
Target
The number of turns targeted for the year for the vendor.
Actual
The number of inventory turns for the vendor/warehouse. Turnover is calculated by dividing Actual Cost of Sales by Inventory Dollars.
Less than Target?
(unlabeled column)
Indicates if the actual turns meets target turns:
•
(*) asterisk - Actual turns are less than target turns.
•
Blank - Actual turns meet or exceed target turns.
