Turns Report By Product Line/Warehouse
The Turns Report By Product Line/Warehouse displays the average annual inventory turnover calculated for the total products in a product line for a warehouse. This report can help you to analyze how well a branch is maintaining inventory levels. The turnover is calculated by dividing the cost of stock sold in the last 12 months by the inventory value of the current on-hand quantity. The higher the turns the faster the products are selling, which usually indicates which products are more profitable to sell.
Menu Path: Purchasing > Reports > Vendor Turn Reports > Product Line Turns Report
Selection Criteria
Use the following criteria to limit the information to print on the report:
•
Division. The highest possible grouping in your company (e.g., Eastern Division, Western Division).
•
Region. A subset of Division - if Division is used (e.g., Southeast Region, Northwestern Region).
•
Selling Warehouse. The warehouse responsible for selling the material.
•
Department. A subset of warehouse. Typically used to separate expenses within a warehouse (e.g., accounting, sales, warehouse).
•
Cost Center. A subset of Department - if Department is used. Typically used to separate expenses within a warehouse or department (e.g., shipping, receiving, outside sales, inside sales).
•
Product Line. Groups products together for sales and purchasing functions.
•
Stocking Status. The stocking status of the products to include on the report:
-
S - Stock.
-
N - Non-stock.
-
B - Both stock and non-stock.
-
C - Consignment.
-
A - All (stock, non-stock, consignment).
•
Include Transfer Usage?. Determines if product usage from transfers is included to calculate usage value (cost of goods sold):
-
Y - Include transfer usage to calculate vendor turns.
-
N - Do not include transfer usage. Only include sales order and work order usage.
Column Descriptions
Column Heading
Description
Line
The product line.
Description
The description of the product line.
Actual Cost of Sales
The sum of the cost of products in this product line sold on sales orders, work orders, and transfers for the past 12 months, not including the current month, extended by the current average cost.
Inventory Dollars
The dollar amount of the product line currently in inventory in the warehouse, including linked and vendor consigned inventory, extended by average cost.
Turns
The number of inventory turns for the warehouse/product line. Turnover is calculated by dividing Actual Cost of Sales by Inventory Dollars.
