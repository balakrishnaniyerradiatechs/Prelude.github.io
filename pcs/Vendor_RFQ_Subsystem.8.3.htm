Starting or Editing a Vendor RFQ
To begin a vendor request for quote:
1
Select Purchasing > Vendor RFQ Subsystem > Vendor RFQ Entry to access the Vendor Request For Quote screen.
If you have access to more than one warehouse, the Location Retrieval screen appears first. Enter the appropriate Selling Warehouse, Department, and/or Cost Center.
2
To enter an RFQ Number, choose one of the following:
•
To start a new RFQ press ENTER to assign a new RFQ number and display the VRFQ Header Information screen. Go to step 3.
•
To edit an existing quote, enter the RFQ number or press F3 to search by Vendor RFQ number. The Vendor Request for Quote screen appears to allow you to add or edit items. Go to ”Entering Products on a Vendor RFQ.
3
VEnter the Expiration Date of this vendor RFQ.
Vrfq Expiration Days in Purchasing Parameters determines the number of days after the RFQ Date to calculate the Expiration Date.
4
Enter the Response Req Date, which is the date by which you would like for the vendors to reply.
5
Enter the Delivery Date, which is the date that the material needs to be delivered to your warehouse.
6
If applicable, enter a Reference Number for this request.
If the RFQ was generated in Quote Order Entry, the original quote number and customer number display in Document and Customer.
7
Under the Vendor column, enter the vendor names or numbers to include in the RFQ.
8
If freight is to be considered in the quote, enter the Frt Rate.
9
Press F2-Save to save the header information and to start entering products. See Entering Products on a Vendor RFQ.
Duplicating a Vendor RFQ
To copy the detail information from an existing RFQ onto a new RFQ, press F5-Dup From History when the cursor is on RFQ Date in the header screen. You can only duplicate the RFQ after you convert it into a purchase order or manually move it to history in Purchase Order Conversion Entry. Refer to ”Converting a Vendor RFQ to a Purchase Order.
