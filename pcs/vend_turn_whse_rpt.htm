Turns Report By Warehouse
The Turns Report By Warehouse displays the average annual inventory turnover calculated for the total products in a warehouse. This report can help you to analyze how well a branch is maintaining inventory levels. The turnover is calculated by dividing the cost of stock sold in the last 12 months by the inventory value of the current on-hand quantity. The higher the turns the faster the products are selling, which usually indicates which products are more profitable to sell.
Menu Path: Purchasing > Reports > Vendor Turn Reports > Warehouse Turns Report
Selection Criteria:
Use the following criteria to limit the information to print on the report:
•
Division. The highest possible grouping in your company (e.g., Eastern Division, Western Division).
•
Region. A subset of Division - if Division is used (e.g., Southeast Region, Northwestern Region).
•
Selling Warehouse. The warehouse responsible for selling the material.
•
Department. A subset of warehouse. Typically used to separate expenses within a warehouse (e.g., accounting, sales, warehouse).
•
Cost Center. A subset of Department - if Department is used. Typically used to separate expenses within a warehouse or department (e.g., shipping, receiving, outside sales, inside sales).
•
Stocking Status. The stocking status of the products to include on the report:
-
S - Stock.
-
N - Non-stock.
-
B - Both stock and non-stock.
-
C - Consignment.
-
A - All (stock, non-stock, consignment).
•
Include Transfer Usage?. Determines if product usage from transfers is included to calculate usage value (cost of goods sold):
-
Y - Include transfer usage to calculate vendor turns.
-
N - Do not include transfer usage. Only include sales order and work order usage.
Column Descriptions
Column Heading
Description
Whse
The warehouse where the products were sold.
Description
The description of the warehouse.
Actual Cost of Sales
The total cost products sold in the past 12 months.
Inventory Dollars
The dollar amount of inventory currently in the warehouse (average cost x on-hand quantity).
Turns
The number of inventory turns for the warehouse. The turnover is calculated by dividing the cost of stock sold in the last 12 months by the inventory value of the current on-hand quantity.
