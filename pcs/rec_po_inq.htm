Recommended P/O Inquiry screen
The Recommended P/O Inquiry screen allows you to review purchasing information for a product on the recommended purchase order.
Menu Path: Purchasing > Inquiries > Buyer Queue Inquiry > Recommended Purchase Orders > F5-Review > F5-Edit > F6-Rec Review
Field and Function Key Descriptions
Column
Description
Product Number/
Description
Line 1: Product number.
Line 2: Product description.
Package
The number of units in a package.
Ldt
The lead time for the product from Product Vendor Maintenance.
Net Available
The number of units available including on-order material.
On-Hand
The number of units on-hand.
Volume: (below column heading)
Volume discount multiplier from Product Vendor Maintenance, if the vendor gives a discount based on volume.
Stk/Buy: (below column heading)
The product’s stock-to-buy ratio.
Weight: (below column heading)
Weight for one unit of the product.
Committed
The number of units committed to sales orders, transfers, and work orders.
On Order
The quantity of this product currently on order.
Forecast
The forecast quantity from Product Purchasing Parameters Maintenance.
MTD-Usage
The current month-to-date usage.
Op/Min
The order point or minimum. When the available quantity falls below this number, a purchase order is recommended.
Line Point
Line point. When the available quantity for any item falls below the order point, the entire vendor line is reviewed for a possible line buy.
Reccmd Quantity
The Recommended Qty from Product Purchasing Parameters Maintenance. The letter after the dash indicates why the purchase order was recommended:
•
A - EOQ. Economic order quantity.
•
B - PDQ. Purchase discount quantity.
•
C - Classification order quantity.
•
D - Manually entered override replenishment quantity.
•
E - Price (refer to Including Future Price Increases).
•
F - Linked to a sales order.
•
G - Backorder.
•
H - Min/Max. The item is replenished according to min.max parameters.
Class
The product classification. Refer to “Classifying Products” in the Inventory Module.
Sea
Indicates if the item is seasonal:
•
N - Not seasonal.
•
Y - Seasonal.
Month
The months corresponding to the Usage column.
Usage
The usage for each corresponding month.
Exc
Indicates if exceptions exists:
•
U - Unusual usage. Monthly usage is greater than the total of the last 5 months of combined usage.
•
O - Out of stock. A product is out of stock for 2 weeks or more in one month.
•
S - Spike. Monthly usage is above or below the minimum or maximum levels set for the product by the percentage defined in Spike Exception - Min/Max set in Purchasing Parameters Maintenance > F5-Forecasting. Refer to “Entering Formulas/Parameters by Company” in the Forecasting Module.
Disc%
The discount percentage for the vendor line.
Cost
The cost for one unit of the product.
Extension
The extended dollar value of the recommended buy quantity.
U/M
The product’s unit of measure.
Replenish Quantity
The suggested purchase quantity based on the recommended quantity, stock adjustments, and rounding.
P/O Quantity
The current quantity defined on the temporary purchase order.
Message Line
(not labelled)
A message appears if the Replenish Quantity is greater than the Reccmd Quantity indicating the reason for the increase.
Frozen
Indicates if purchasing parameters are frozen:
•
Y - Purchasing parameters are not re-evaluated with end-of-month processing.
•
N - Purchasing parameters are re-evaluated with end-of-month processing.
Days to OP
The number of days until the product reaches its order point.
F5-Weekly Usage
Displays the weekly usage amounts for previous weeks.
F6-Usage Summary
Displays the Usage Summary screen to allow you to view usage and forecast information for the current year, previous year, and the year before last.
F7-Inventory Inquiry
Displays the Inventory Inquiry screen to allow you to view detailed product information including onhand, on order, linked, and available quantities.
sF5-Prev Group
Displays the previous block of records. The number of records that display is determined by Records Per Block in Company Maintenance > GUI Options.
sF6-Next Group
Displays the next block of records. The number of records that display is determined by Records Per Block in Company Maintenance > GUI Options.
