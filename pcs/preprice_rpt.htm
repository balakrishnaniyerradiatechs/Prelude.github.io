PrePrice Increase Report
The PrePrice Increase report lists products that you should consider purchasing due to a future price in their product record. Flags in Purchasing Parameters determine if you should purchase more of an item due to a future price increase (refer to Including Future Price Increases). The Recommended Purchase quantity on the report suggests a quantity of each item in a vendor’s line that would balance the savings gained by ordering before a price increase, against the cost of maintaining additional material in inventory. This recommended quantity also appears on the Recommended Purchase Order Action Report under the Replenish Quantity.
Required System Flag Settings
Perform Pre Price Update (Purchasing Controls > Purchase Parameters) must be set to Y.
Auto Recalc Prices (Option Parameters > Inventory Parameters) Must be set to Y
Menu Path: Purchasing > Reports > Nightly Processing Reports > Pre-Price Increase Report
Selection Criteria
Use the following criteria to limit the information to print on the report:
•
Warehouse. The warehouse where the products are stocked.
Column Descriptions
Column
Description
Product
The product that should be purchased due to a future price increase.
Description
The product description.
Whse
The warehouse where the product should be replenished.
Current/
Revised Cost
Line 1: The current cost of the product in the product record.
Line 2: The new future cost that will update the product on the Effective Date.
Effective Date/
Lead Time
Line 1: The date the future Revised Cost will go into effect.
Line 2: The lead time of the product from Product Vendor Maintenance.
Incr Pct
The percent increase in cost from the Current Cost to the Revised Cost.
Extra Months Stock
The extra number of month’s supply to stock due to the price increase.
(% Increase x 24)/ (ROAI x KCost). Refer to Including Future Price Increases for an explanation of the formula.
Usage Rate
The Forecast (monthly usage) quantity of the product from Product Purchasing Parameters Maintenance.
Ord Pt/
Ln Pt
Line 1: Order Point, If Purchase Method is EOQ and available stock falls below this quantity, the system recommends replenishment.
Line 2: Line Point. If Purchase Method is EOQ and available stock falls below this quantity, the product is evaluated as part of a line buy if another product in the line is below its Order Point (OP).
Refer to Product Purchasing Parameters Maintenance in the Inventory documentation for the explanation of how these are calculated.
On Order/
On Hand
Line 1: The quantity of the product due to arrive into the warehouse on purchase orders and transfers.
Line 2: The quantity of the product on-hand in the warehouse, regardless of whether or not it is available to sell.
Orig
Rec
The original Recommended Quantity of the product from Product Purchasing Parameters Maintenance. This would be the recommended buy quantity if there were no future price increase.
Recommended
Purchase
The quantity that you should consider purchasing due to the future price increase.
(% Increase x 24)/ (ROAI x KCost). Refer to Including Future Price Increases for an explanation of the formula.
