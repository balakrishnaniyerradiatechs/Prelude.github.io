Fill Rate by Product Report
The Fill Rate by Product report displays the percentage of stock products that were available when sales orders were first entered, not when invoiced. This report allows you to see how well you are maintaining service levels for a product.
Products That Will Not Appear on the Fill Rate Reports
If the following conditions exist for a product/warehouse, it will not update the fill rate:
•
The stocking status is other than S (stock).
•
Both the min and max quantities are set to zero.
•
It has an order point override of zero.
•
The calculated order point is zero and it does not have an override (other than zero).
Fill Rate in File Update Parameters must be set to Y to capture fill rate information.
 
Menu Path: Purchasing > Reports > Fill Rates at Order Entry Reports > Fill Rate by Product Number
Selection Criteria
Use the following criteria to limit the information to print on the report:
•
Warehouse. The warehouses to include on the report.
•
Order Date Range. The beginning and ending order entry dates to include.
•
Product Line. The product lines to include on the report. Fill rates for all products in each selected product line are listed.
•
Major Group. The major product groups to include on the report. Major groups are groups of products or product lines that group products together for sales analysis.
•
Product Number range: The beginning and ending product number to include on the report.
Column Descriptions
Column Heading
Description
Product
The product ordered by a customer.
Description
The description of the product.
Whse
The shipping warehouse where the product was ordered by a customer.
Ord Date
The date the product was ordered.
Order #
The customer’s order number.
OrdQty
The quantity ordered on each sales order.
PckQty
The quantity available to pick the first time the pick ticket printed.
Fill%
The fill rate (Pck Qty/Ord Qty).
#Lines
The number of lines on the sales order.
Filled
The number of lines filled complete the first time the pick ticket printed.
Fill %
The fill rate compared to the number of lines on the sales order (Filled/#Lines).
If a line is 100% shipped then the fill rate for that line is 100%. But if the line is less than 100% shipped then the fill rate for that line is zero.
