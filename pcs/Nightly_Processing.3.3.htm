Including Future Price Increases
Flag settings in Purchasing Parameters determine if the buyer should purchase more of an item due to a future price increase. Both the Recommended Purchase Order Action Report and the PrePrice Increase Report suggest a quantity of each item in a vendor’s line that would balance the savings gained by ordering before a price increase, against the cost of maintaining additional material in inventory.
The system compares the Effective Date for a new cost in Product Vendor Maintenance, Product Maintenance, or Product Warehouse Maintenance to the Review Cycle Days in the Vendor Line Target Parameters Screen. If the New Cost Effective Date precedes the next review cycle date, a recommended purchase quantity is calculated. The calculation is as follows:
24 x (% increase) / ROAI + KCost%
•
% increase = the percent change in cost from the current cost to the future cost.
•
ROAI = the return on additional investment (explained below).
•
KCost = carrying cost (i.e, the cost to carry the product in inventory).
Related Reports
Description
PrePrice Increase Report

Recommended Purchase Order Action Report

To set the flags that affect price increases:
1
Select Purchasing Controls > Purchasing Parameters.
2
For ROAI, enter the percent of cost to use as the return on additional investment (ROAI). This factor is used in the pre-price increase calculation. When you buy in advance of a price increase, you’re assuming that usage and price for the product will not decrease in the future. You would normally need a significant return on your investment to justify this risk.
3
For Perform Pre Price Update choose one of the following:
•
Y - Nightly Processing updates the temporary purchase orders on the Recommended Purchase Order Action Report with products that should be purchased due to future price increases. The reason code E (price) indicates that temporary purchase order was created due to a future price increase. These items also appear on the PrePrice Increase Report.
•
N - Nightly Processing does not create temporary purchase orders for items with future price increases.
4
Press F2-Save three times to save any changes.
