Processes
Job Billing
Job Billing processes are in Financial > Fixed Assets > Processes > Job Billing.
Job Billing Entry (/JBE)
Bills a customer for assets with an equipment record. Job billing entries can be changed or deleted.
•
Asset Number: Enter the asset number that is being billed.
•
Invoice Number:Enter the Invoice number for this job billing.
•
Customer Number: Enter the customer to be billed.
•
Billing Date: Enter the billing date for the job.
•
Billing Amount: Enter the amount to bill the customer.
•
F2- Save: Saves the job billing entry.
•
F4- Delete: Deletes the job billing entry.
•
F5- Billing Notes: Any notes regarding the billing or the customer.
Job Billing Register (/JBRU)
Generates a list of job billing entries since the last update and updates the equipment file. Beginning and ending invoice numbers can be entered, or leave blank to include all.
Repairs / Scheduled Maintenance
These features are in Financial > Fixed Assets > Processes > Repairs Scheduled Maintenance.
Repairs Maintenance Entry (/RE)
You can set up and track a maintenance schedule for items. The next repair date is automatically calculated. Charges can be added back to equipment maintenance.
•
Asset Number: Enter a valid asset number.
•
Repair Number: The user-defined repair number for the maintenance record.
•
Scheduled Maint?: Enter Y if this is a scheduled maintenance item.
•
Next Scheduled Maint: The next scheduled maintenance is automatically calculated.
•
Repair Date: Enter the date of the repair.
•
Charges: The amount charged for this repair, which can be added to Equipment Maintenance.
•
F2- Save: Saves the repair/maintenance information.
•
F4 - Delete: Deletes the repair/maintenance entry.
•
F5 - Repair Notes: Any notes regarding the asset or the repair.
Repairs Register Update (/RRU)
Prints a list of repairs and scheduled maintenance since the last update for Fixed Assets or Accounts payable. It also allows you to update Repairs/Maint Register Update Screen.
•
Enter Beginning Repair Number: Enter the first repair number to include in this register or leave blank to include all repairs.
•
Enter Ending Repair Number: Enter the last repair number to include in this register or leave blank to include all repairs.
•
And with Source Type Equal to: Enter the source code for which this register should be created or updated:
-
FA - Fixed Assets
-
AP - Account Payable

Lease Processing
Lease processes are in Financial > Fixed Assets > Processes > Lease Processing.
Select Lease For Billing (/LBS)
This process selects leases for billing as of a lease date and frequency. It only selects the leases that need to be billed. The billing is updated in the Billing Register/Update Process.
•
Enter As Of Lease Date: Enter the cut-off date for this billing period. Everything leased on or before this date is included in this period. Leave blank to include all.
•
Enter Lease Frequency: Enter the lease frequency to update:
-
A - Annually.
-
S - Semi Annually.
-
Q - Quarterly.
-
BM - Bi monthly.
-
M - Monthly.
-
BW - Bi weekly.
-
W - Weekly.
•
F2 - Accept: Performs the update to the lease date and frequency.
Manual Lease Charge Entry (/LBE)
Allows lease charges to be created for a asset with an equipment record.
Billing / Register Update (/LBRU)
Prints a list of lease charges since the last update and updates the equipment file.
End of Year (/FEOY)
Path: Financial > Fixed Assets > Processes > End of Year
This process purges files for a specified book code in preparation for a new fiscal year. All year to date fields for the equipment and asset files are reset to zero. The new opening reserve becomes last years Year to Date depreciation plus last years opening reserve. The bonus depreciation flag is reset to N if a bonus depreciation was taken for the year. The investment tax credit flag will be reset to T if a tax credit was taken for the year.
End of Period
End of Period processes are in Financial > Fixed Assets > Processes > End of Period.
The 3 methods of depreciation updating are Manual Depreciation Update, Units of Usage Depreciation Update and Depreciation Method Update, which determine when to update depreciation (e.g., weekly, monthly, yearly). The primary book posts to the G/L, but you may depreciate as many books as necessary. You cannot over depreciate. In V18+, the Serial Number History Inquiry with the code DPR (depreciation).
Manual Depreciation Update (/MDU.UPD)
This process updates depreciation for fixed assets with a depreciation method of M (in Assets Maintenance). Once all managed depreciation assets were entered, a depreciation report is printed containing the information entered, along with the previous General Ledger and Asset records.
•
Enter Book Code: Enter the book being used for the depreciation of this asset.
•
Enter Fiscal Month: Enter the fiscal period for which this update is being performed.
•
Enter Transaction Date: Enter the date of the depreciation.
Units Of Usage Depreciation Update (UUDU.UPD)
This process manually depreciates fixed assets with a method of U - Units of Usage (in Assets Maintenance).
•
Enter Book Code: Enter the book used for the depreciation of this asset.
•
Enter Fiscal Month: Enter the fiscal period for which this update is being performed.
•
Enter Transaction Date: Enter the date of the depreciation.
Depreciation Method Update (DMU.UPD)
Calculates and updates depreciation for assets with depreciation methods not based on M or U. This is the normal depreciation calculation update (in Assets Maintenance).
•
Enter Book Code: Enter the book being used for the depreciation of this asset. F3 for a listing.
•
Enter Fiscal Month: Enter the fiscal period for which this update is being performed.
•
Enter Transaction Date: Enter the date of the depreciation.
•
Enter Period Type (W/M):
-
W - To specify a weekly period.
-
M - To specify a monthly period.
•
Enter Number of Periods: Enter the number of periods (weeks or months) over which the depreciation should be calculated for all assets whose depreciation method is not M or U.
