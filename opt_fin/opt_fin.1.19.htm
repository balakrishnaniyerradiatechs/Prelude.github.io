Entry and Processing
Sales Order Entry (/SOE)
If Change Order Control flag is Y in Customer Job Maintenance and a job is entered on an order, the Job Accounting Control Entry screen appears.
•
Original Order: Enter a new order for the job.
•
Change Order: Shows a change order number that is tracked as a reference. You can add or delete lines but you cannot change prices and quantities.
If you edit the order, the following screen allows you to choose the appropriate type of update.
•
Orig Order: This order is the first for the job.
•
Change Order: You can add and delete lines but you cannot change prices and quantities. The Sales Order Log Inquiry references the change order number.
•
Adjustment: You can add line items with a zero price. The price of the job does not change, extra charges (e.g., freight) are tracked.
•
Correction: You can change all original data, including quantities and prices. Changes with this change order type affect the original estimated prices and costs of the customer job.
If you need to release products based on a schedule instead of availability, use the line items’ pick ticket date or the Tag and Hold order type. The Tag and Hold Release Entry can release a partial or complete quantity of an item. If you use change order control, you can only use order types 01, 02, 03, 04, 05, 06, 13, and 15.
When Inv Document Option and contact/email address information exist, the sales order defaults the appropriate contact and email address of the customer or customer ship-to entered.
Tag & Hold Release Entry (/RTHE)
If you created a Tag & Hold order, use Tag & Hold Release Entry to release products for invoicing to the customer. When the customer is ready for the merchandise, the products and quantities are flagged to print on a pick ticket and be shipped. You cannot edit product information.
Job Pick Ticket Printing (/JPTP)
You can print the pick ticket when you are ready to ship the product. This process is similar to regular Pick Ticket Printing and it also creates releases.
Release Confirmation Entry (/RCE)
After the pick tick prints and the material is shipped, this process confirms the quantities that shipped and sets the release up for internal billing.
Internal Invoice Printing and Processing (/IP)
This process selects all confirmed job releases and creates an internal invoice to credit inventory and debit job work in progress. The customer does not receive an invoice.
Orders with job change control are processed very differently. Invoicing relieves inventory for the products delivered to the customer, removes the completed lines from the open sales order, and creates job history data. An open A/R record is not created for change order control jobs. For the first billing, internal billing only credits the Inventory G/L account and debits the Jobs Work In Process account. For the second and subsequent billings, other accounts (e.g., A/R) are updated.
The A/R Inquiry uses the customer’s job history instead of regular invoice history. A separate invoice register lists customer job invoices because they do not appear on the invoice register.
Job Billing Entry (/CJBE)
This process bills a job to a customer. Enter the amount (lot billing, not a quantity) being released for each line is entered. For example, if a product was one-third installed, the billing amount is one-third of the extended price. Enter the current billing and stored materials amount. Both amounts are billed to the customer but affect the AIA Invoice Report differently. .
•
Scheduled Value: The net price multiplied by the quantity.
•
Work Completed – Previous: The value of line items invoiced before the date range.
•
Work Completed – Current: The value of line items invoiced during the date range. If a line item was partially shipped before the current period and partially shipped during the current period, both Work Completed columns have amounts.
•
Materials Stored: The value of material invoiced but not shipped to the job site.
•
A: Determines if all of the information on the line is correct.
-
Y - accept the information and move to the next line item.
-
N - edit data on this line item.
•
Tx?: Defaults to the setting for Job Line Item Tax Flag Default in Customer Job Maintenance.
•
Total Comp/Stored: The total value of the Work Completed columns and Material Stored.
•
Balance to Finish: The value of the remaining open balance for this line item.
•
Retainage: The Retainage Percentage for the job multiplied by the Total Completed/Stored.

You can also adjust and print the previously billed column if a partial amount was rejected on the previous month’s AIA billing. You must adjust the billing first or the current billing amount is processed again to the amount of the line item
Job Invoice Preview Report (/CUST.JOB.PREV.RPT)
The Job Invoice Preview Report lists jobs and invoice amounts ready for job invoice processing.
Job Invoice Printing and Processing (/CJIP)
This process selects the job for billing. Any line items with a billed amount are processed. A job invoice is transmitted the customer. An unposted transaction is created:
•
A/R is debited for the invoice amount and Job Work in Progress is credited for cost.
•
Cost of goods sold is debited for cost and Job Sales is credited for the invoice amount.
•
If tax jurisdictions are used, Job Use Tax Payable is credited and Job Use Tax Cost is debited.
•
F5-Job Invoice Reprint: Reprints customer job invoices that were processed and printed.
Invoice Jobs (B Update) (/CUST.JOB.BUPDATE.PROCESS)
This process updates Sales Analysis for the invoice jobs. A product number is created when the first billing process is completed. That product is updated from the job number and is used as a description for Sales Analysis. If any change orders exist, the change order number is listed after the job number when the product is updated in Sales Analysis. The quantity updated is the fraction of the total job invoiced in the current billing cycle (e.g., .5=50%; 1=100%). The amount is billed to the customer.
