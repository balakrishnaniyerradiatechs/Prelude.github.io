Assessing Fuel Charges
For rental products with fuel charges, the following screen appears in Rental Order Entry.
Path: Rental > Rental Order Entry
•
Fuel Type: the fuel type defaults to the type in Product Maintenance.
•
Fuel Level Out: the fuel level from Release Confirmation if Fuel Charge Applies is T.
•
Fuel Level In: the fuel level for the rental equipment to represent the 8th of a gallon at when the item is returned if Fuel Charge Applies is T (e.g., 0-8, E - empty, F - full).
•
Amount of Fuel: the amount of fuel used for the rental order. If Fuel Charge Applies is T, Fuel Level is used to calculate the number of gallons used on the rental order. The calculation is: Fuel Level Out (8th amount X tank) - Fuel Level In (8th amount X tank).
Example: Rental Item 9830 (Rental Information screen - Fuel Charge Applies = T)
Equipment Maintenance = 0000093 (Tank = 15)
Fuel Level Out = 4/8, Fuel Level In = 2/8
Amount of Fuel = 3.75 gallons (4/8 X 15) – (2/8 X 15)
•
Fuel Price: the fuel price defaults to the fuel price in Fuel Type Maintenance.
•
Extension: the extension is the amount of fuel charge to be added to the order. The amount of fuel is multiplied by the fuel price to determine this amount.
•
Bill For Fuel?: determines if a fuel charge service item is inserted on the rental order. Defaults to Y to automatically create a line on the order for fuel charges. The fuel charge line item is the one assigned in Product Maintenance. After the fuel charge is added Bill for Fuel is set to A. If the flag is N, a fuel charge is not added. You cannot complete a rental order without fuel charge information if Bill for Fuel is Y.
