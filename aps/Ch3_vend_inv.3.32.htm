Creating Letters of Credit
A commercial letter of credit is a contract between you and your bank to pay your vendor (typically an overseas vendor). At your request, the bank opens a letter of credit and promises to honor drawings on the letter of credit from your vendor(s). Essentially, the bank replaces you as the payee. To use a letter of credit to pay a vendor:
1
Enter the purchase order. Refer to the Purchasing documentation.
2
Get a letter of credit from your bank.
3
Enter the letter of credit from your bank and apply the open P/Os to it.
4
Your vendor redeems the letter of credit for the amount of the P/O.
5
Receive stock when it arrives (refer to the Inventory documentation).
6
Reconcile the invoice with the receiver (refer to Reconciling a Vendor Invoice to a Receiver).
7
If you pay the bank via automatic bank deduction, pay the invoice with a status of B (refer to Selecting Vendors/Invoices to Pay).
If you want to pay a vendor using a letter or credit, that vendor’s Pay To Vendor in Vendor Maintenance must be the bank that issued the letter of credit.
To create a letter of credit:
1
Select Financial > Accounts Payable > Processes > Letter Of Credit Entry to access the Letter of Credit Account screen.
2
Enter the appropriate number or press F3 to search for:
•
Div (Division), the highest possible grouping in your company.
•
Reg (Region), a subset of Division if Division is used.
•
Loc (Location), the physical location or warehouse.
•
Dep (Department), a subset of Warehouse. Typically used to separate expenses within a Warehouse.
•
Ccr (Cost Center), a subset of Department if Department is used.
•
Account # to use for the letter of credit.
3
Press F2-Save. The Letter of Credit Entry screen appears.
4
For Vendor, enter the vendor number for the bank that issued the letter of credit. The bank should be set up as a vendor and the vendor that the merchandise is purchased from should reference the bank as the Pay To Vendor (Vendor Maintenance > F6-A/P).
5
Enter the bank Document Number.
6
Enter the type of letter of credit in L/C Type. Choose one of the following:
•
R - Regular. The Letter of Credit Advance account is debited and the Letter of Credit Draw account is credited.
•
B - From Bank Credit Line. Reconciled through Vendor Invoice Reconciliation Entry and paid via automatic bank deduction.
7
Enter the letter of credit date in L/C Date. The fiscal period displays. This date is the posting date for regular (type R) letters of credit.
8
Enter the Expiration Date of the letter of credit.
9
Enter the total amount in L/C Amount.
10
Enter the P/O#, or press F3 to search for purchase orders the letter of credit covers. You can enter multiple purchase orders.
11
Press F2-Save to save the letter of credit information.
12
Letter of Credit Entry screen
