Reconciling a Vendor Invoice to a Receiver
To reconcile a receiver with a vendor invoice:
1
Select Financial > Accounts Payable > AP/PO Subsystem > Vendor Invoice Reconciliation > Invoice Reconciliation Entry to access the A/P Account Number screen.
2
Enter the appropriate number or press F3 to search for:
•
Div (Division), which represents the highest possible grouping in your company, if applicable.
•
Reg (Region), which is a subset of Division, if Division is used.
•
Loc (Location), which is the physical location or warehouse.
•
Dep (Department), which is a subset of Warehouse. Typically used to separate expenses within a Warehouse.
•
Ccr (Cost Center), which is a subset of Department if Department is used.
•
Account# to use for the vendor invoice. The description of the account appears. Defaults from the G/L Table Maintenance screen.
3
Press F2-Save to open the Vendor Invoice Reconciliation Entry screen.
4
For Receiver#, choose one of the following:
•
If the vendor invoice matches a single receiver, enter the receiver number (e.g., 123456-1) or press F3 to search for the receiver number. The receiver number is the purchase order with a suffix (e.g., -1, -2).
•
If the vendor invoice reflects part of a receiver, split the receiver (see Splitting a Single Receiver).
•
If the vendor invoice reflects two or more receivers, merge the receivers (see Merging Receivers).
•
If the invoice is for a direct ship P/O, receive the stock. Refer to the Inventory documentation for more information.
Pay-To Vendors
A warning message appears if the P/O is set up to pay a different vendor. Pay To Vendors default from Vendor Maintenance but can be overriding in Ovr Pay To Vendor on the P/O. You can also change the Pay To Vendor for an invoice.
5
Once you enter, split, or merge receivers, the Vendor and Payment Terms appear.
6
Enter the vendor’s Invoice Number and Invoice Date. You can only use an invoice number once per vendor. You cannot duplicate an invoice number.
Fiscal Period
A/P Receiver Fiscal Flag in Accounts Payable Parameters determines the fiscal period for received stock:
•
R - Default the fiscal period based on the date stock is received.
•
I - Default the fiscal period based on the date entered when the invoice is reconciled.
7
For Status, choose one of the following:
•
O - Open. Defaults if Vendor Payable Method is set to R.
•
H - Hold. Places the invoice on hold.
•
B - Bank deduction. Defaults if Vendor Payable Method is set to B.
•
E - Electronic transfer. Defaults if Vendor Payable Method is set to E.
•
C - Credit cards. Defaults if Vendor Payable Method is set to C.
Payment Terms defaults based on the P/O. Discount Date and Due Date are calculated based on the P/O’s terms and invoice date. For example, if the terms are 1% 10 Days Net 30, you must pay in 10 days to qualify for a 1% discount off the invoice amount. If you do not pay in 10 days, the full amount is due within 30 days.
8
Enter the Merch Amount from the invoice. This amount is for the merchandise only. If the Merch Amount does not match the Received Total from the P/O and the discrepancy exceeds your company’s limit, you must adjust the discrepancy (refer to Adjusting Discrepancies).
9
If the invoice lists additional charges, enter them in the following fields (the charges are allocated to the cost of the items based on your company settings):
•
Freight Amount.
•
Duty Amount.
•
Brokerage Amount.
•
Tax Amount.
10
For each amount entered in step 9, check Include? to include it in payment to the vendor. Leave blank if you will pay another vendor but still want to allocate the cost of freight to the cost of the goods.
11
Enter any other charges in Misc Amount (e.g, credits or debits for miscellaneous charges from the vendor).
G/L Account for Miscellaneous Charges
Misc Detail G/L in APPO in Accounts Payable Parameters determines if a G/L account is required when a Misc Amount is entered in Vendor Invoice Reconciliation Entry:
•
Y - Opens the APPO Miscellaneous Amount Entry screen to require a G/L account when a Misc Amount is entered.
•
N - Do not require a G/L account.
12
Change the Discount Amount if necessary. The Discount Amount defaults based on the Payment Terms.
13
Press F2-Save. Depending on system and user options set for reconciliation limits and discrepancy tolerances, one of the following occurs:
•
If the Merch Amount exceeds your reconciliation limit, the invoice goes on hold in the Accounts Payable Release Inquiry.
Reconciliation Limits
If you exceed the Vend Inv Rec Limit for your user ID in User ID Maintenance > F5-Administrative the invoice will go on hold and must be released by the A/P Manager. Refer to Accounts Payable Released Inquiry.
•
If the difference between the Merch Amount and the Received Total is less than the Invoice Margin in Accounts Payable Parameters, the following message appears:
Are You Sure You Wish To Update This Receiver?
Choose from the following:
-
Yes - Updates the receiver and flags it to be paid.
-
No - Suspends the receiver. Saves the information but does not flag the invoice to be paid. You must return to Vendor Invoice Reconciliation Entry to set it up to be paid.
Invoice Margin in Accounts Payable Parameters determines maximum discrepancy amounts allowed. Any discrepancy between a vendor’s invoice and the received amount that is less than this amount will automatically post to the general ledger discrepancy account.
•
If the difference between Merch Amount and Received Total is outside of the Invoice Margin in Accounts Payable Parameters, the following dialog box appears.
Discrepancy Amount of 259.25 Exists Between Original Merchandise Value Of Purchase Order And the Invoice Amount Entered. How Would You Prefer To Correct The Discrepancy?
14
Choose one of the following:
•
Suspend. Saves the information but does not flag the invoice to be paid. You must return to Vendor Invoice Reconciliation Entry and correct the discrepancy before the invoice will be paid.
•
Recost. Allows you to change cost on the purchase order. Refer to Recosting a P/O.
•
Discrep. Write the discrepancy off to a discrepancy account. The amount you can write off to this account is limited by Max Discrepancy Dollar and Max Discrepancy Percent in Accounts Payable Parameters. Refer to Setting up Accounts Payable Parameters. If the discrepancy exceeds the limit, you must choose the G/L Acct option.
•
G/L Acct. Allows you to write the discrepancy off to any G/L account. refer to Posting to a G/L Discrepancy Account.
•
Addl Inv. Allows you to generate another invoice to bill the difference back to the vendor. Refer to Creating an Additional Invoice.
•
Return. Allows you to go back to the Vendor Invoice Reconciliation Entry screen and edit it.
