Posting a Discrepancy to a G/L Account
If the difference between the Merch Total and the Received Amount in Vendor Claim Reconciliation Entry is more than the Max Discrepancy Dollar and Max Discrepancy Percent in Accounts Payable Parameters (refer to Setting up Accounts Payable Parameters), you must choose a General Ledger Account to post the write-off amount to.
Write-off Limits for a User
A/P Write-Off Limit in User Identification Maintenance > F5-Administrative determine the amount you can post to a write-off account. If you exceed this limits, the claim goes on hold and must be released by the A/P Manager (refer to the Accounts Payable Released Inquiry). If this field is blank, the user can write-off any amount.
To post to one or more G/L accounts:
1
In Vendor Claim Reconciliation Entry, choose G/L Acct. from the discrepancy warning dialog box. The G/L Accounts For Discrepancy screen appears.
2
Enter each G/L account you want to credit or debit:
•
Div #, (Division), which represents the highest possible grouping in your company, if applicable.
•
Reg# (Region), which is a subset of Division if Division is used.
•
Loc# (Location), which is the physical location or warehouse.
•
Dep# (Department), which is a subset of Warehouse. Typically used to separate expenses within a Warehouse.
•
Ccr# (Cost Center), which is a subset of Department if Department is used.
•
Account # to use for the vendor invoice. The description of the account appears.
3
Enter the discount amount applied in Disc. Amount.
You can enter as many lines as necessary, but the total of the Discrepancy Amount Applied must equal the amount in the Discrepancy Amount To Apply before you can exit the screen.
4
Press F2-Save to complete the G/L posting.
