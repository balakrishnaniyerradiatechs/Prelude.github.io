Defining A/P Terms
Accounts Payable terms define when payment is due for each vendor.
Due Date
Payment is due on a specific date (e.g., 4/15/2005).
Net Days Allowed
Payment is due a set number of days past the invoice date. For example, enter 15 if payment is due 15 days after the invoice date. If the invoice date is 4/14, the due date is 4/29.
Net Day of Month/Cutoff Date
The due date varies among 2 or 3 dates based on the invoice date and a cutoff date. If an invoice date is after the Cutoff Date, the due date is the Net Day of Month the following month. You can specify 1, 2, or 3 cutoff days and 2 or 3 due dates. You can also type a P before the Net Day of Month to indicate that payment is due the following month (prox dating).

Installment
Payment is due monthly, quarterly, or annually. You can specify how much to pay for the first and last payments and any payments in between.
Floor Plan
Payment is due when you sell an item on floor plan terms or on the due date, whichever is sooner. Refer to Tracking Floor Plan Inventory for more information on Floor Plans
