Entering a Batch of Recurring Invoices
To enter a batch of recurring invoices:
1
Select Financial > Accounts Payable > Recurring Vendor Invoices > Recurring Vendor Invoice Entry to access the A/P Invoice Batch Information screen.
2
For Batch Number, choose one of the following:
•
To get a new batch number, press ENTER over NEW.
•
To update a batch number, enter the batch number.
3
Enter the date on which the batch is created for Batch Date.
4
For Batch Amount, enter the total value of the invoices in the batch.
5
Press F2-Save. The Standard Account Entry screen appears.
6
Enter the appropriate number or press F3 to search for:
•
Div (Division), which represents the highest possible grouping in your company, if applicable.
•
Reg (Region), which is a subset of Division if Division is used.
•
Loc (Location), which is the physical location or warehouse.
•
Dep (Department), which is a subset of Warehouse. Typically used to separate expenses within a Warehouse.
•
Ccr (Cost Center), which is a subset of Department if Department is used.
•
Account# (defaults to your Accounts Payable G/L account number) for the vendor invoice. The description of the account appears.
7
Press F2-Save. The Recurring Vendor Invoice Entry screen appears.
8
Enter the Vendor.
9
Enter the vendor’s Invoice Number.
10
In Transaction Day, enter the day of the month on which the transaction will occur. If you enter 31 for a month that does not have 31 days, the invoice will post on the last day of that month (e.g., February’s invoice will post on the 28th).
11
Change Terms Code if necessary (defaults from Vendor Maintenance).
12
If you want to limit the number of recurring invoices that are generated, choose one of the following:
•
Maximum Payments - limits the number of invoices to generate.
•
Maximum Period - the last period during which invoices will be generated.
13
Enter the P/O Number, if available.
14
For Invoice Amount, enter the total amount to distribute.
15
For Merch Amount, enter the total amount of the invoice that is for merchandise (defaults to the Invoice Amount). Change it if necessary. This amount is used with the Terms Code to calculate the discount.
16
Change Discount Amount if necessary. The discount is calculated from the Merch Amount and Terms Code.
17
For each G/L account you need to distribute to, enter the following:
•
Div (Division), which represents the highest possible grouping in your company, if applicable.
•
Reg (Region), which is a subset of Division if Division is used.
•
Loc (Location), which is the physical location or warehouse.
•
Dep (Department), which is a subset of Warehouse. Typically used to separate expenses within a Warehouse.
•
Ccr (Cost Center), which is a subset of Department if Department is used.
•
Account (defaults to your Accounts Payable G/L account number) to use for the vendor invoice. The description of the account appears.
18
Enter the Dist Amount (total distribution must match the Invoice Amount).
19
Press F2-Save.
20
Repeat steps 8-19 for each recurring invoice.
