Letter of Credit Entry screen
The Letter of Credit Entry screen allows you to enter a letter of credit from your bank to honor a debt on a pending transaction. A commercial letter of credit is a contract between you and your bank to pay your vendor (typically an overseas vendor). At your request, the bank opens a letter of credit and promises to honor drawings on the letter of credit from your vendor(s). Essentially, the bank replaces you as the payee.
Refer to Creating Letters of Credit for more information.
Menu Path: Financial > Accounts Payable > Processes > Letter of Credit Entry
Field and Function Key Descriptions
Field/Function Key
Description
Vendor
The vendor number.
Document Number
The number the bank assigned that references the P/O for the letter of credit.
L/C Type
The type of letter of credit:
•
B - From bank credit line. Processed through Vendor Invoice Reconciliation Entry but does not generate payment although it does create a payment record.
•
R - Regular. G/L postings debit the Letter of Credit Advance account and credit the Letter of Credit Draw account.
L/C Date
The date the letter of credit goes into effect.
Expiration Date
The date the letter of credit expires.
L/C Amount
The value of your Letter of Credit at the bank.
Received Balance
The balance of the received dollar amounts of the purchase orders on the Letter Of Credit. Updated when a P/O referenced on the Letter of Credit is received.
Posted Balance
The balance of the posted amounts of the purchase orders on the Letter of Credit.
Ln# (not labeled)
The line count for accounting distribution.
P/O#
The purchase order number.
P/O Date
The purchase order date.
P/O Dollars
The dollar amount of the purchase order.
L/C Amount
The letter of credit amount for the purchase order that will post to the G/L account.
Posted
Indicates that each distribution line has posted to the general ledger. The P appears when the invoice is reconciled.
F2-Save
Saves the letter of credit information.
F4-Del
Deletes the letter of credit information.
