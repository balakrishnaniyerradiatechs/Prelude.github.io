Reconciling a Vendor Claim with a Credit Memo
When you receive a credit memo from a vendor, you must reconcile it with a vendor claim.
To reconcile a claim with a credit memo:
1
Select Financial > Accounts Payable > AP/PO Subsystem > Vendor Claim Reconciliation > Claim Reconciliation Entry to access the A/P Account Number screen.
2
Enter the appropriate number or press F3 to search for:
•
Div (Division), which represents the highest possible grouping in your company, if applicable.
•
Reg (Region), which is a subset of Division if Division is used.
•
Loc (Location), which is the physical location or warehouse.
•
Dep (Department), which is a subset of Warehouse. Typically used to separate expenses within a Warehouse.
•
Ccr (Cost Center), which is a subset of Department if Department is used.
•
Account# to use for the vendor invoice. The description of the account appears.
3
Press F2-Save. The Vendor Claim Reconciliation Entry screen appears.
4
For Receiver#, choose one of the following:
•
If the credit memo matches a single vendor claim, enter the claim number (e.g., 123456-1) or press F3 to search for the claim number.
•
If the credit memo reflects part of a vendor claim, split the claim (see Splitting a Single Claim).
•
If the credit memo reflects two or more vendor claims, merge the claims (see Merging Vendor Claims).
5
Once you enter, split, or merge claims, the Vendor and Payment Terms appear.
6
For Invoice Number, enter the vendor’s invoice/credit memo number.
7
For Status, choose one of the following:
•
O - Open. Defaults if Vendor Payable Method is set to R.
•
H - Hold. Places the claim on hold.
•
B - Bank deduction. Defaults if Vendor Payable Method is set to B.
•
E - Electronic transfer. Defaults if Vendor Payable Method is set to E.
•
C - Credit cards. Defaults if Vendor Payable Method is set to C.
Change it if necessary. Terms Codes default based on the Payment Terms on the original purchase order. If the Payment Terms is not changed on the purchase order header, the Payment Terms in Vendor Maintenance is the default. For more information, refer to the Purchasing documentation.
8
Discount Date and Due Date default based on A/P terms and claim date. Change if necessary.
9
Enter the Merch Amount from the credit memo. This amount is for the merchandise only. If the Merch Amount does not match the Received Total from the claim and the discrepancy exceeds your company’s limit you must adjust the discrepancy. Refer to Adjusting Discrepancies.
10
If any of the following charges are listed on the invoice, enter them in the following fields (the charges are allocated to the cost of the items based on your company settings):
•
Freight Amount.
•
Tax Amount.
Duty Amount and Brokerage Amount do not apply to vendor claims.
11
If you entered amounts in step 10, choose one of the following for Include?:
•
N - Do not include the charges in the payment to this vendor.
•
Y - Include the charges in payment to the vendor.
12
Enter any other charges in Misc Amount (e.g, credits or debits for miscellaneous charges from the vendor).
13
Change the Discount Amount if necessary. The Discount Amt defaults based on the Payment Terms.
14
Press F2-Save. Depending on system and user options on reconciliation limits and discrepancy tolerances, one of the following occurs:
•
If the Merch Amount exceeds your reconciliation limit, the invoice goes on hold in the Accounts Payable Release Inquiry.
Reconciliation Limits
If you exceed the Vend Inv Rec Limit for your user ID in User ID Maintenance > F5-Administrative the invoice will go on hold and must be released by the A/P Manager. Refer to Accounts Payable Released Inquiry.
•
If the difference between the Merch Amount and the Received Total is less than the Invoice Margin in Accounts Payable Parameters, the following message appears:
Are You Sure You Wish To Update This Receiver?
Choose from the following:
-
Yes - Updates the receiver and flags it to be paid.
-
No - Suspends the receiver. Saves the information but does not flag the invoice to be paid.
Invoice Margin in Accounts Payable Parameters determines maximum discrepancy amounts allowed. Any discrepancy between a vendor’s invoice and the received amount that is less than this amount will automatically post to the general ledger discrepancy account.
•
If the difference between Merch Amount and Received Total is outside of the Invoice Margin in Accounts Payable Parameters, the following dialog box appears.
Discrepancy Amount of 58.04 Exists Between Original Merchandise Value Of Vendor Claim And the Invoice Amount Entered. How Would You Prefer To Correct The Discrepancy?
15
Choose one of the following:
•
Suspend to save the information. You must return to Vendor Claim Reconciliation Entry and correct the discrepancy before the credit memo will be updated.
•
Recost to change cost on the credit memo. Refer to Recosting a Vendor Claim.
•
Discrep to write the discrepancy off to a discrepancy account. The amount you can write off to this account is limited by Max Discrepancy Dollar and Max Discrepancy Percent in Accounts Payable Parameters. Refer to Setting up Accounts Payable Parameters. If the discrepancy exceeds the limit, you must choose the G/L Acct option.
•
G/L Acct to write the discrepancy off to any G/L account. Refer to Posting a Discrepancy to a G/L Account.
•
Addl Inv to generate another invoice to bill the difference back to the vendor or pay extra to the vendor. Refer to Creating an Additional Debit or Credit Memo.
•
Return to go back to the Vendor Claim Reconciliation Entry screen and update it.
