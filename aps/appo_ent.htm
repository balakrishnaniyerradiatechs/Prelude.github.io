Vendor Invoice Reconciliation Entry screen
The Vendor Invoice Reconciliation Entry screen allows you to reconcile a receiver with a vendor invoice. Receivers are created when a purchase order is received. Refer to Reconciling a Vendor Invoice to a Receiver for step by step information how to reconcile an invoice and handle payment discrepancies.
Menu Path: Financial > Accounts Payable > AP/PO Subsystem > Vendor Invoice Reconciliation > Invoice Reconciliation Entry
Field and Function Key Descriptions
Field/Function Key
Description
Receiver#
The receiver number to reconcile, which is the P/O number followed by a dash (e.g., 10000621-1).
Vendor
The vendor number from the original purchase order.
Invoice Number
The vendor’s invoice number. Invoice numbers can only be used once per vendor.
Invoice Date
The vendor’s invoice date.
Fiscal Period (not labeled)
The fiscal period associated with the invoice date.
A/P Receiver Fiscal Flag in Accounts Payable Parameters determines the date to use to determine the fiscal period for received stock:
•
R - Default the fiscal period based on the date stock is received.
•
I - Default the fiscal period based on the date entered when the invoice is reconciled.
Status
The status of the vendor’s invoice:
•
O - Open.
•
H - On Hold.
•
B - Automatic Bank Deduction. Select B if a bank credit line letter of credit is attached to the receiver.
•
E - Electronic Funds Transfer.
•
C - Credit Cards.
Payment Terms
The A/P terms code.
Discount Date
The discount date associated with the terms code.
Due Date
The due date of the invoice calculated from the A/P terms code.
Merch Amount
The merchandise amount of the invoice. This should match the Received Total. If the totals don’t match, you can recost the invoice, place the invoice on buyer hold, split, or merge the invoice, or create an additional invoice.
Freight Amount
The freight amount of the invoice (if any).
Include?
Determines if the Freight Amount is included in the payment to the vendor:
•
Checked - Includes the freight amount.
•
Not Checked - does not include the freight amount.
Duty Amount
The duty amount for the invoice (if any).
Include?
Determines if the Duty Amount is included in the payment to the vendor:
•
Checked - Include the duty amount.
•
Not Checked - Do not include the duty amount.
Brokerage Amount
The brokerage amount for the invoice (if any).
Include?
Determines if the Brokerage Amt is included in the payment to the vendor:
•
Checked - Includes the brokerage amount.
•
Not Checked - Do not include the brokerage amount.
Tax Amount
The tax amount for this invoice (if any). The tax amount is based on the landed cost of the merchandise.
Include?
Determines if the Tax Amt is included in the payment to the vendor:
•
Checked - Include the tax amount.
•
Not Checked - Do not include the tax amount.
Misc Amount
Any miscellaneous charges the vendor may debit or credit that are not already included in this entry (e.g., charges other than merchandise and freight). You can determine if the miscellaneous amount goes to the A/P Miscellaneous Amount G/L Account number, or prompts for G/L account numbers.
Discount Amount
The discount amount associated with the terms code.
Invoice Amount
The total amount of the invoice.
Received Total
The total amount of the receiver.
Revised Total
The revised total if you change the cost of the receiver.
Warranty Total
The warranty total if any.
Receipt Date
The date the product was received.
Fiscal Period (not labeled)
The fiscal period associated with the receipt date.
Ship Via
The ship via codes and description from the purchase order.
Emergency P/O
Indicates if this purchase order was generated as an emergency purchase order:
•
Y - Yes.
•
N - No.
Freight Flag
Indicates how Freight Flag was set on the original purchase order:
•
Y - You are responsible for freight charges.
•
N - You do not have to pay any freight charges.
Buyer
The name and number of the buyer.
F2-Save
Reconciles the invoice and receiver and sets the invoice up for payment if no discrepancies exist. Refer to Adjusting Discrepancies for information on how to handle any payment discrepancies.
F4-Del
Deletes the invoice.
F5-Hdr
Opens the Purchase Order Header screen to view the original P/O header.
F6-Credit Memo
Opens the AP/PO Credit Memo Entry screen to create a credit memo.
F7-Recost
Opens AP/PO Cost Adjustments screen to enter new costs for the receiver.
F8-Buyer Hold
Opens the Buyer Hold Notes screen to place the receiver on hold. The buyer can resolve the discrepancy between the merchandise and received totals.
Action Bar
User Fields
See your System Administrator.
Notes
Opens the notes screen to allow you to enter, edit, or view notes.
Multi Dsc
Opens the Multi Discounts screen to allow you to enter multiple discounts for the receiver.
Remit Comm
Opens the Remittance Check Comment screen to allow you to enter comments for payment.
Import Cost
Displays the APPO Import Costs screen to display importing cost information.
