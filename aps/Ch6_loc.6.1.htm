Using Letters of Credit
A commercial letter of credit is a contract between you and your bank to pay your vendor (typically an overseas vendor). At your request, the bank opens a letter of credit and promises to honor drawings on the letter of credit from your vendor(s). Essentially, the bank replaces you as the payee. To use a letter of credit to pay a vendor:
1
Enter the purchase order. Refer to the Purchasing documentation.
2
Get a letter of credit from your bank.
3
Enter the letter of credit from your bank and apply the open P/Os to it (refer to Creating Letters of Credit).
4
Your vendor redeems the letter of credit for the amount of the P/O.
5
Receive stock when it arrives (refer to the Inventory documentation).
6
Reconcile the invoice with the receiver (refer to Reconciling Inventory Invoices).
7
If you will pay the bank via automatic bank deduction, pay the invoice with a status of B (refer to Creating a Batch of Invoices to Pay).
