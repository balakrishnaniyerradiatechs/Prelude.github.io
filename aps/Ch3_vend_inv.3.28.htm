Posting Manual Invoices
Once you create a batch (refer to Starting a Batch of Manual Invoices) or choose Accounts Payable Manual Invoice Entry, the Accounts Payable Manual Invoice Entry screen appears.
If W/O Required Flag in Chart of Accounts Maintenance is Y, you can enter a Work Order Number and Burden Code for the manual invoice.
For each invoice:
1
Enter the number for the Vendor or enter M for miscellaneous vendor. Enter address and contact information for the vendor.
2
Enter the Invoice Date.
3
Change Payment Terms if necessary (defaults from Vendor Maintenance).
4
Change the Due Date and Discount Date if necessary (default based on the Invoice Date and Terms Code).
5
Enter a P/O Number, if available.
6
For Status, indicate the status of the invoice:
•
O - Open. Defaults if Vendor Payable Method is set to R.
•
H - Hold. Places the vendor on hold.
•
B - Bank deduction. Defaults if Vendor Payable Method is set to B.
•
E - Electronic transfer. Defaults if Vendor Payable Method is set to E.
•
C - Credit cards. Defaults if Vendor Payable Method is set to C.
7
In Invoice Amount, enter the total amount to distribute.
8
For Merch Amount, enter the total amount of the non-inventory items. This amount is used to calculate the discount (if any).
Distributing A/P Tax
Post Manual Invoice Tax in Accounts Payable Parameters (Systems Menu) indicates if the tax amount for a manual invoice is automatically distributed to the A/P tax account in the general ledger table:
•
Y - Automatically distribute tax to the A/P tax account. The merchandise amount will be the distribution amount.
•
N - Do not post tax to the general ledger. The invoice amount in Accounts Payable Manual Invoice Entry will be the distribution amount, whether or not there is a sales tax.
9
Change Disc. Amount if necessary (defaults based on the Merch Amount). If the vendor has G/L accounts, the following screen appears.
Do You Wish To Use This Vendor’s G/L Distribution Accounts And Percentages For The Invoice?
If the vendor does not have G/L accounts established or you wish to use different G/L accounts, you can select the accounts you wish to post to.
10
Choose one of the following:
•
Yes - Use vendor’s G/L accounts and percentages for the invoice.
•
No - Manually enter G/L accounts and percentages.
11
For each G/L account you need to distribute to, enter the:
•
Div (Division), which represents the highest possible grouping in your company, if applicable.
•
Reg (Region), which is a subset of Division if Division is used.
•
Loc (Location), which is the physical location or warehouse.
•
Dep (Department), which is a subset of Warehouse. Typically used to separate expenses within a Warehouse.
•
Ccr (Cost Center), which is a subset of Department if Department is used.
•
Account# (defaults to your A/P account) to use for the manual invoice. The description of the account appears. For asset accounts, refer to Posting Manual Invoices for Assets.
12
Enter the distribution amount in Dist Amt. Total Distribution must match the Invoice Amount.
Project IDs track changes to G/L accounts and amounts. If Project Id Required in Chart of Accounts Maintenance is Y, you must enter a project ID.
13
If you want to enter comments that appear on the Detail General Ledger Report, enter Y for C. The Distribution Comments screen appears.
14
Enter comments.
15
Press F2-Save. The manual invoices are posted.
