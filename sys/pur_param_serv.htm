Service Factors Screen
If you want to further define safety allowance quantities to maintain more accurate inventory levels, you can use the Service Factors screen to enter a service level factor for each service level percent. For example, if all you want is a 50% service level, you may not need to carry any safety stock. But, if you want a 99% service level, you may need to carry more than 2 months of safety stock. The Inventory month-end process checks these service level factors to calculate leadtime.
Service level percent is defined by class in S/A% in the Product Class Settings screen. You can override this value for a product in Product Purchasing Parameters Maintenance > Safety Allowance %.
The Service Level Method calculates safety quantity as follows:
Safety Quantity = STDEV(monthly demand) x SQRT (monthly leadtime) x Service Level Factor
•
STDEV (monthly demand): The standard deviation of monthly demand over the forecast months.
•
SQRT (monthly leadtime): Simple square root of the leadtime expressed in a monthly time unit (i.e., leadtime days/30).
•
Service level factor: A constant which reflects the amount of safety stock required to achieve a given service level.
The Service Level Factor constant is derived from finding the value for the desired Service Level Percent from a standard normal cumulative distribution curve. A statistical Z-Table is generally used to calculate service level factor.
 
Menu Path: Systems > System Parameters > Maintenance > Purchasing Controls > Purchasing Parameters > Safety Allowance Method = S
Field and Function Key Descriptions
Field/Function Key
Description
Service%
The service level percent. Use the S/A% field in the Product Class Settings screen to locate the service level factor to calculate safety stock.
You can override this value for a product in the Safety Allowance % field in Product Purchasing Parameters Maintenance.
Factor
The service level factor for the corresponding service level percent goal. This factor is used to determine how much safety stock to keep on hand. For products that do not require a high level of service, you may not need to keep any safety stock. For products that require a higher level of service, you may need to keep a larger quantity of safety stock. For example, a factor of 2.5 would indicate that products at this service level should have a two and a half month supply of inventory in safety stock.
F2-Save
Saves the service factor information.
