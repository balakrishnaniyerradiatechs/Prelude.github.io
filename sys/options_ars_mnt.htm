Accounts Receivable Parameters screen
The Accounts Receivable Parameters screen allows you to set flag and option parameters to control how Prelude operates for A/R functions.
Menu Path: Systems > System Parameters > Maintenance > Option Parameters > Accounts Receivable Parameters
Field and Function Key Descriptions
Field/Function Key
Description
Credit Hold/# Mo Last Sale
Determines if orders should go on credit hold if a customer has not placed any orders in the number of months indicated:
•
Y - Orders go on credit hold if no sales activity occurs for a customer in the number of months that you enter in the next (unlabeled) field.
•
N - Sales activity is not a criteria for credit hold.
Number of Months (unlabeled)
The number of months without activity that determines if an order goes on credit hold (if Credit Hold/# Mo Last Sale is set to Y).
Credit Hold/Confirm Order
Determines if type 06 (confirmation) sales orders are included in the credit limit check:
•
Y - Confirmation sales orders are included in the credit limit check.
•
N - Confirmation sales orders are not included in the credit limit check.
Credit Hold/A/R Terms Change
Determines if a sales order goes on credit hold if the Payment Terms are changed on the Sales Order Header:
•
Y - Sales orders go on credit hold if the Payment Terms are changed.
•
N - Sales orders do not go on credit hold if the Payment Terms are changed.
Credit Hold/COD-CASH Orders
Determines if a sales order with Payment Terms for cash or COD is included in the customer’s credit limit check:
•
Y - Include cash and COD orders in the credit limit check. In the next (unlabeled) field, enter a value so that all cash and COD orders over this amount go on credit hold regardless of the customer’s credit standing.
•
N - Do not include cash or COD orders in the credit limit check.
Cash/COD value
(Unlabeled)
Cash and COD orders over this amount will go on credit hold regardless of the customer’s credit standing (if Credit Hold/COD-CASH Orders is Y).
Credit Hold/Unallocate Inv
Indicates if sales orders on credit hold allocate inventory:
•
Y - Sales orders on credit hold do not allocate inventory. Pickable line items are backordered. When the sales order is released from credit hold, the items are allocated.
Linked products are an exception; when linked items are received, they still allocate to the sales order on hold.
•
N - Sales orders on credit hold allocate inventory.
COD Inv Upd Cust ‘000COD’
Determines if COD invoices are consolidated under customer 000COD:
•
Y - Consolidate COD invoices for customer 000COD. This option can make cash application for freight invoices easier.
•
N - Do not consolidate COD invoices.
Auth Cash Terms Chg To COD
If a customer is set up with default payment terms for cash, indicate if the payment terms on a sales order can be changed to COD:
•
Y - Allow the Payment Terms on a sales order to be changed from cash to COD, if the customer’s default terms are cash.
•
N - If the customer’s default Payment Terms is cash, the A/R terms code on a sales order cannot be changed.
Open A/R Aging Method
Determines how open accounts receivable are aged:
•
D - Age open accounts receivable by date.
•
P - Age open accounts receivable by fiscal period.
Include Discount Aging
Determines whether net or gross amounts are displayed in aging columns on reports and inquiries when the discount date is not yet reached:
•
Y - Display invoice net amounts with aging information.
•
N - Display invoice gross amounts with aging information.
Days Past Due
The number of days past due a customer’s account must be to put the customer on automatic credit hold. This value is checked by the Auto Customer Credit Hold procedure. The customer will go on credit hold if they have invoices this number of days overdue. The credit hold settings at the customer level override the settings at the system level. Refer to “Credit Holds” in the Accounts Receivable documentation for more information.
Dollars Past Due
The maximum dollar amount past due a customer account can be before going on credit hold. For example, if you enter 1000, customers with a past due balance of $1000 or less will not go on credit hold. This flag is used with Days Past Due to determine if the Auto Customer Credit Hold procedure places a customer on hold. The Auto Customer Credit Hold procedure totals the invoice amount for all invoices that are the number of days overdue indicated in Days Past Due. If the amount is greater than or equal to the Dollars Past Due, the customer goes on credit hold. The credit hold settings at the customer level override the settings at the system level. Refer to “Credit Holds” in the Accounts Receivable documentation for more information.
Min.Stmt Debit Amount
The minimum amount a customer must owe to receive a statement. Statements will generate for customers that owe this amount or more (balance forward statements not included).
Min. Stmt Credit Amount
The minimum amount of credit owed to a customer that will generate a statement for this customer. Statements will generate for customers that are due this amount or greater.
Min. Service Charge
The minimum service charge calculated that will create a service charge invoice. This value can be used differently depending on how Skip Charge if < Minimum? is set in the Auto Service Charge Entry screen (refer to ”Automatically Assessing Service Charges” in the Accounts Receivable documentation).
Max Disc. Dollar
The largest amount allowed for a cash discrepancy write-off when paying invoices in Cash Receipts Entry. You can override this for a user in C/R Write-Off Limit in the User Id Maintenance Administrative Settings screen.
Max Disc. Percentage
The largest percentage difference between the paid amount and the invoice amount that is allowed for a cash discrepancy write-off when paying invoices in Cash Receipts Entry. You can override this for a user in C/R Write-Off Limit in the User Id Maintenance Administrative Settings screen.
Min Cred Coll Amt
Any customer whose past due A/R balance is below this amount is not included in Credit Collections Nightly Processing. You can override this value for a customer in Customer Maintenance > F6-A/R > F5-Add Info > Credit Collections Min $.
Refer to the “Credit Collections Subsystem “in the Accounts Receivable documentation for more information.
Save M/E A/R Stmt Detail
Determines if customer statements are saved to the customers’ A/R history during Month-End Customer A/R Aging Save:
•
Y - Customer statement history is updated (necessary if you need to reprint customer statements).
•
N - Customer statement history is not updated. This option is recommended to keep your files from getting too big.
Charge Back Invoice By Cust P/O?
Determines if a customer P/O number is required for each Chargeback Reason Code in Cash Receipts Entry:
•
Y - A customer P/O is required for each charge back invoice. In this case, you cannot consolidate charge back invoices (Consol. Chargeback Invoices must be set to N).
•
N - Customer P/O numbers are not required for each charge back invoice.
Refer to “Issuing Chargeback Invoices” in the Accounts Receivable documentation.
High Volume C/R Method
Defines the criteria required to find a customer invoice in High Volume Cash Receipts:
•
I - Invoice. Match on invoice number only.
•
R - Reference. Match on any reference number (e.g., invoice, order, customer P/O, release number).
Refer to “Applying EDI Payments in High Volume Cash Receipts” in the Accounts Receivable documentation.
Bypass Security Access in Coll?
Indicates if security access codes are required in Credit Collections Schedule Entry:
•
Y - Do not use security access in the collection subsystem. All customers appear in the activity collection schedule, not just those associated with a security access code.
•
N - Require security codes. Only customers with a matching security code appear in the activity collection schedule. Security codes are set up in Customer Maintenance > F6-A/R > Action Bar > Security Access.
Consol. Chargeback Invoices
Determines if charge back invoices are consolidated on one invoice:
•
Y - Charge back invoices are consolidated into one invoice per reason code for a customer in Cash Receipts Entry.
•
N - Each charge back invoice is on a separate invoice for a customer in Cash Receipts Entry.
Refer to “Issuing Chargeback Invoices” in the Accounts Receivable documentation.
Collections Evaluation Method
Determines how customers pass through activities in Credit Collections Schedule Entry:
•
A - Action method. Moves customers through activities only if they were reviewed (for the call queue) or had a letter generated (for a correspondence activity).
•
T - Time method. Moves customers based on the number of days that passed, regardless of whether or not they were reviewed. For example, if the customer is in a queue but no action is taken, they will still appear in the next queue in the number of days assigned to their credit collections type.
Avg Days To Pay Months
The number of months (1-24) to use to calculate average days to pay in the Accounts Receivable Inquiry. For example, if you enter 3, the amount of cash applied in the last 3 months (including this month) is used to calculate average days to pay.
If blank, the total cash applied since the customer was created is the basis to calculate average days to pay.
Refer to “Calculating Average Days to Pay” in the Accounts Receivable Maintenance Setup documentation.
Delay SC Post Until Collect?
Determines if service charges post to the general ledger when the invoice is generated or when the customer pays the invoice:
•
N - The service charge posts to the general ledger when the service charge invoice is generated.
•
Y - When the invoice is generated, both a debit and a credit post to the Service Charge Account, with no net effect on the general ledger.
Edit Credit Comments
Indicates how notes are entered and viewed in Accounts Receivable Inquiry, Customer Invoice Inquiry, and Customer Maintenance:
•
Y - Enter free form credit comments.
•
N - Use the standard Notes Entry screen for credit comments.
F2-Save
Saves the accounts receivable parameter information.
