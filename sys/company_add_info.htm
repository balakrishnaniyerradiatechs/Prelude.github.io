Additional Information screen
Menu Path: Systems > System Parameters > Maintenance > Company Maintenance > F5-Add. Info
Field and Function Key Descriptions
Field/Function Key
Description
Order Processing
Bin Loc Sort - S/O
Determines the sort sequence for pick tickets if you use bins:
•
Y - Print pick tickets in sequence by bin. Kit components print in bin order if using continuous printing.
•
N - Print pick tickets by product number.
You can override this setting for a warehouse in Bin Loc Sort - S/O or in Bin Loc Sort - W/O (for work orders) in the Warehouse Maintenance Additional Information screen.
G/L Cost
Sets the company’s accounting cost:
•
A - Average.
•
F - FIFO. Opens the FIFO Method Info screen to allow you to determine if FIFO layers are tracked at the company level or the warehouse level.
•
L - LIFO.
•
R - Replacement.
•
S - Standard. Opens the Standard Cost Info screen to allow you to indicate if standard cost is maintained at the product level or the product warehouse level and if standard cost can be changed in product maintenance. You can also determine whether you will use standard cost or linked cost for sales orders, work orders, and transfers.
GL Rep Cst Default
If the accounting cost of a product is zero, determine if replacement cost is used:
•
Y - Use replacement cost.
•
N - Leave the cost as zero.
•
C - Use replacement cost for credit memos only.
Price Matrix
Determines if prices matrices are used to price sales orders:
•
Y - Price matrices are used.
•
N - Price matrices are not used. If you don’t use price matrices, set this flag to N to help speed up price searches on sales orders.
Refer to “Prices Matrices” in the Order Processing documentation for more information.
Commission Matrix
Determines if the system checks for commission matrices to calculate commission:
•
Y - Use commission matrices from Commission Matrix Maintenance and the commission rates from Sales Representative Maintenance.
•
N - Do not use commission matrices to calculate commission.
Commission matrices are used to vary the commission rate or salesrep for customers and/or products. Refer to ”Creating a Commission Matrix” in the Order Processing documentation.
Utilize SalesRep#2
Indicates if a second sales representative (Salesrep Two) can be assigned to a customer in Customer Maintenance:
•
Y - You can assign a Salesrep Two in Customer Maintenance.
•
N - Salesrep Two is not accessible in Customer Maintenance.
Credit Check
Determines if customer credit limits are checked as sales orders are entered:
•
H - Sales orders go on credit hold when a customer is over their credit limit or has past due invoices. Nightly Processing checks the total amount of open invoices to determine if the customer should go on credit hold the next time an order is entered.
•
N - Sales orders never go on credit hold.
Refer to “Credit Holds” in the Accounts Receivable documentation.
Minimum Invoice Amt
The minimum invoice amount allow for sales orders. If an order for a customer is less than this amount, then a Minimum Order Charge is added to the invoice. You can override this amount in Customer Maintenance > F7-Order Info, or Ship Via Maintenance.
Restock Charge %
The restocking charge percent to use to calculate a restocking charge on credit memos and RGAs.
Soe Restock Charge Method in the Order Processing Parameters Additional Options screen determines if restocking charges are added to returned goods:
Restocking Charge (Customer Maintenance > F7-Order > F5-Add Info) determines if a restocking charge is calculated as each line item is entered on a credit memo or credit lines on a sales order.
Ucc Mfg Number
The UCC (Uniform Code Council) manufacturer number assigned to your company. The code is used as part of the serial shipping container code (SSCC) identification number. This number is not used if a UCC Mfg Number is defined for the warehouse in the Transfer Information screen.
Refer to “Using Serial Shipping Container Codes” in the Release Confirmation & Shipping section of the Order Processing documentation, or “Using Serial Shipping Container Codes for Transfers” in the Inventory documentation.
Accounts Payable
AP/PO Interface
Indicates if Vendor Invoice Reconciliation Entry must match the received items on a purchase order.
•
Y - The vendor invoice is reconciled with the items on the receiver.
•
N - The receiver number does not display when reconciling a vendor invoice. The invoice amount is not applied to the amount received.
Inventory Control
LIFO 1st Acq Cost
Determines if the system captures LIFO first acquisition cost:
•
Y - Use the first received cost of an item if G/L Cost is set to L.
•
N - Use chain link cost for first acquisition cost.
Maintain FIFO Layers
Determines if you want to keep FIFO information for reporting purposes:
•
Y - Maintain FIFO layers for all incoming material. Enough layers are stored to support the current on-hand quantity of a product. The date, document number, reference number, quantity, cost, freight, duty, and brokerage are all saved for reports and inquiries.
•
N - Do not store FIFO layers.
Maintain LIFO Layers
Determines if you want to keep inventory valuation by LIFO:
•
Y - Keep inventory valuation by LIFO.
•
N - Do not use LIFO for inventory evaluation.
Freight Allocation - P/O
Determines how freight charges are allocated to the average cost of individual items received on a purchase order:
•
Y - Stock Receipts Entry and Vendor Invoice Reconciliation Entry allocate inbound freight charges to average cost and debit the general ledger inventory account for the freight amount.
•
I - Inbound freight charges for non-direct ship purchase orders allocate to average cost and debit the general ledger inventory account for the freight amount. Direct ship purchase orders post to the general ledger freight account.
•
N - All inbound freight charges entered in Vendor Invoice Reconciliation Entry debit the general ledger freight account.
If freight charges are not known when stock is received, the Freight Allocation method in Product Line Maintenance determines how the freight estimate is calculated.
Refer to “Freight” in the Purchasing documentation.
Freight Allocation -T/R
Determines how freight charges are allocated to the average cost of items received on transfers:
•
Y - Allocate freight to the product’s average cost when a transfer is received. Freight is calculated on branch transfers by using a Freight Matrix (refer to “Entering a Freight Matrix for a Product Line” in the Freight Chapter of the Purchasing documentation). Freight cost is recalculated upon receipt and added to Inventory in the receiving branch.
•
N - Do not allocate freight to the product’s average cost when a transfer is received.
Document Sort - T/R
The sort sequence for transfer pick tickets:
•
Y - Sort by bin number. The system checks the Sort Sequence Code in Bin Location Maintenance when the bin allocation takes place for sorting the products on the transfer.
•
P - Sort by product number.
•
L - Sort by product line.
•
N - Do not sort.
General Ledger
# of Fiscal Years
The maximum number of fiscal years to keep on-hand (3 to 10 years).
# of Fiscal Periods
The number of fiscal periods to use:
•
12 - One fiscal period for each month.
•
13 - The 13th period is for year-end adjustments. The 12th period must be closed prior to posting in the 13th.
Multiple Fiscal Periods?
Determines if more than one fiscal period is used for the company:
•
Y - Use multiple fiscal periods (If you have multiple locations that share inventory but post to different fiscal periods).
•
N - Do not use multiple fiscal periods.
Refer to “Using Multiple Fiscal Periods” in the General Ledger documentation.
G/L Acct # Length
The length of your general ledger account numbers (must be the same for all companies). Twelve is the maximum amount.
G/L# Suppress Zero Fill
Determines if zeros are added to general ledger account numbers to the G/L Acct# Length.
•
Y - Do not fill in zeros for general ledger account numbers.
•
N - Add zeros for general ledger account numbers to make it the G/L Acct# Length. For example, if G/L Acct# Length is 6 and you enter an account number of 2222, it automatically adjusts to 222200.
F2-Save
Saves the information on the Company Maintenance Additional Information screen.
