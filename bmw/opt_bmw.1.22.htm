Band Saw Blade Products
Band Saw Blade products have special requirements which must be established so that they can be entered and priced correctly on a sales order. Once the master product is set up in Bill of Material Maintenance, the length is prompted for when it is entered on a sales order. Each new length creates a new bill of material.
Order Processing Parameters (/OPTIONS.OIS.MNT)
Path: System Parameter > Maintenance > Option Parameters > Order Processing Parameters
•
Commission Cost:
-
R - Replacement Cost.
-
C - Cost for Sales.
Product Maintenance (/PM > F5)
Path: Inventory > Maintenance > Product Maintenance >Product Maintenance > F5-Add Info
The following flag determines if lengths are used for this product. The base product must be established as a non stock item (Status Code field set to N in Product Warehouse Maintenance).
•
Special Operation: L - To use length when entering Band Saw Blade products. This brings up the following screen to allow you to establish the length pattern and length rounding information used in Sales Order Entry.
•
Length Pattern: Determines the pattern for this product if the generic length pattern of ‘XXXX is not being used. Example: Product 401605 has a length pattern of 999.999. In Sales Order Entry, a length of 50 inches is entered. The 50 is then zero filled to the length pattern 050.000 and the result is appended to the product 401605 (401605050.000). If this product exists, it is processed as normal. If not, a new product and product warehouse record are created for the new product 401605050.000.
•
Length Rounding: Determines the rounding for the length entered. Example: If .50 is entered, the length is rounded up to the next half foot.
Bill of Material Maintenance (/BOMM)
Path: BMW/Work Order > Maintenance > Bill of Maintenance
This program is used to create or maintain a Bill of Material. Below is an example of the master band saw blade product that must be established to update pricing and costs for the new bill of material/product numbers created in Sales Order Entry.
In the following example, a band saw blade bill of material is created when a length of 50 is entered for product 401605 (master bill of material) in Sales Order Entry.
The product 401605 was established with a Length Pattern of 999.99 and Length Rounding of .50. A length of 50 was entered in Sales Order Entry. The bill of material was created as 401605050.000. The Qty-Asm calculated 4.50 (50 / 12 = 4.16), rounded up to the nearest half foot). The pricing and costs of product 401605050.000 can be displayed in Product Maintenance and are calculated by adding the individual pricing and costs of the products established in the master bill of material. In the above example, products 401605001 and WELD CHARGE pricing and costs are used.
Sawblade Mass Price/Cost Update (/BOM.SAWBLADE.MASS.UPD)
Path: BMW/Work Order > Processes > Sawblade Mass Price/Cost Update
This process selects all bill of material items that have the Special Operations flag in Product Maintenance set to L for the base product number. All prices, replacement costs, standard costs, and cost for sales are updated based on the rollup of the components in the bill of material record. If the service item is being processed as a burden, the cost from the burden is used. Otherwise, the cost of the service item is pulled from the product file.
Example of the price calculation and update:
•
Price 5 for product 401605001 is updated to $1.75.
•
The Sawblade Mass Price/Cost Update is performed.
•
Price 5 for product 401605050.00 is updated with the following calculation:
-
Qty Asm = 4.50
-
Price 5 = 1.75
-
Price 5 weld charge = 7.48
-
(4.50 X 1.75 = 7.875) + 7.48 = 15.355.
-
Price 5 for product 401605050.000 is now 15.355.
Sales Order Entry (/SOE)
Path: Order Processing > Sales Order Entry
When a Sawblade Bill of Material item is entered, (ex: 401605) you will be prompted for the length. Once the length is entered, the new product number is created using the length pattern established in product maintenance (ex: 401605050.000). If this number has not be entered before, you are given the opportunity to create a new bill of material.
