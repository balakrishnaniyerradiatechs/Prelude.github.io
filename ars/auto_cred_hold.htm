Automatically Placing Customers on Credit Hold
The Auto Credit Hold Procedure places customers on credit hold that have overdue A/R invoices. You should run this procedure nightly to check customer balances and to determine which customers should go on credit hold. The following Maintenance flags determine if and when customers’ sales orders go on credit hold:
•
Days Past Due and Dollars Past Due in Accounts Receivable Parameters determine if customers go on credit hold if they have overdue invoices greater than a pre-determined value. Refer to Accounts Receivable Parameters for a detailed explanation of how these flags affect credit hold.
•
Auto Credit Review and Auto Credit Days in Customer Maintenance override the Auto Credit Hold Procedure and/or determine the number of overdue days a customer’s invoice can be to go on credit hold. Refer to Customer Maintenance for a detailed explanation of how these flags affect credit hold.
To use automatic credit hold:
1
Select Financial > Accounts Receivable > Processes > Auto Credit Hold Procedure. The following message appears:
This Procedure Will Place All Customers On Credit Hold That Have Open A/R Invoices ‘XX’ Number Of Days Overdue And The Sum Of Those Overdue Invoices Is Equal To Or Greater Than A Set Dollar Amount. The Number Of Days Is Retrieved From Each Customer Record Independently. If The Number Of Days Does Not Exist In The Customer Record, The Number Of Days Is Retrieved from The System Wide Default From The Option Parameters, Continue?
2
Choose from the following:
•
No - Cancels the Auto Credit Hold Procedure.
•
Yes - The following message appears:
Do You Wish To ....
3
Choose from the following:
•
Execute - Begins the Auto Credit Hold Procedure. When complete, a message appears to indicate how many customers were put on credit hold.
•
Schedule - Opens the Scheduler Job Parameters screen to allow you to schedule the procedure.
•
Exit - Cancels the Auto Credit Hold Procedure.
