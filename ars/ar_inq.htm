Accounts Receivable Inquiry
The Accounts Receivable Inquiry displays detail accounts receivable and aging information for a customer account. You can also use this inquiry to add credit related comments for the customer.
Displaying Customer A/R Information
To view accounts receivable information for a customer:
1
Select Financial > Accounts Receivable > Inquiries > Accounts Receivable Inquiry to open the Accounts Receivable Inquiry screen.
2
Enter the Customer to view.
Sub Accounts
If this customer is a master account (Master Account Flag set to Y in Customer Maintenance > F6-A/R), you have the option to include aging information for all subsidiary accounts.
 
Aging Information
Open A/R Aging Method in Accounts Receivable Parameters determines if open accounts receivable are aged by date or fiscal period.
Include Discount Aging in Accounts Receivable Parameters determines if net or gross amounts are displayed with aging information.
Field & Function Key Descriptions
Field/Function Key
Description
Contact
The customer contact name from Customer Maintenance who is responsible for paying the bills.
Phone
The contact’s phone number.
Fax
The contact’s fax number.
Master
The master account number if this customer is a sub-account for a master account.
Payment Terms
The accounts receivable payment terms code for orders and quotes that indicates when payment is due, payment conditions, and any applicable discounts. These codes are set up in A/R Payment Terms Maintenance. Refer to Using A/R Payment Terms and assigned to each customer in Customer Maintenance.
Salesrep
The primary sales representative assigned to the customer as Salesrep One in Customer Maintenance.
Collect Type
The credit collections type that groups customers for collections. These are created in Credit Collections type Maintenance (refer to Defining Credit Collections Types) and assigned to customers in Customer Maintenance.
Credit Code
The Credit Code assigned in Customer Maintenance > F6-A/R that determines if and when orders can be entered for the customer:
•
A - An Authorization Code must be entered in Sales Order Entry after the customer number and before the header information. The authorization codes are set up in Accounts Receivable Controls.
•
E or blank - Sales orders are subject to the customer’s Credit Limit. If the customer is over their credit limit, the pick ticket will not print until the order is released from credit hold.
•
H - Sales orders can be entered but are always held until the credit manager releases them. You must enter the credit reason abbreviation code (established in Valid Response Maintenance).
•
N - No sales orders or quotes can be entered for this customer.
Credit Limit
The customer’s credit limit assigned in Customer Maintenance > F6-A/R. When you enter a sales order for this customer, if the total value of the customer’s A/R balance and open sales orders (excluding disputed and long-term future orders) exceeds the credit limit, the order goes on credit hold.
Credit Balance
The value of orders that the customer can still place and not exceed their credit limit.
Credit Limit - (Order Balance + A/R Balance).
Order Balance
The total value of orders that the customer placed that are not invoiced.
Excluded Order Balance (Unlabeled field to the right of Order Balance)
The excluded sales order amount. The value of orders that are excluded from the customer’s credit limit test. Refer to Excluding Sales Orders From the Credit Limit.
A/R Balance
The open account receivable amount that the customer owes for invoiced sales orders.
Excluded Invoices (Unlabeled field to the right of A/R Balance)
The excluded invoice amount. The value of invoices that are excluded from the customer’s credit limit test. Refer to Excluding Sales Orders From the Credit Limit.
Start Date
The date the customer’s account was created.
Currency (unlabeled field below Start Date)
The currency indicator assigned to the customer, if you use multiple currency.
Last Sales Date
The last date a sales order was invoiced to this customer.
Last Quote Date
The last date a quote was entered for this customer.
Last Cash Amount
The last payment amount that was applied to this customer’s account in Cash Receipts Entry.
Last Cash Date
The last date payment was applied to this customer’s account in Cash Receipts Entry.
Last Check#
The check number from the last date payment was received from this customer.
High Balance
The highest amount that the customer’s open A/R balance rose to. When the balance goes above this amount, the High Balance is updated.
High Date
The date the last time the customer’s open A/R balance hit the High Balance
Oldest Invoice
The oldest open invoice for this customer.
AvgPay+
The average number of days it takes for this customer to pay an invoice. Refer to Calculating Average Days to Pay for a detailed explanation and the formulas used to calculate this value.
AvgOvr
The average number of day overdue that this account is past due. Refer to Calculating Average Days to Pay for a detailed explanation and the formulas used to calculate this value.
DaysSO
 
Days Sales Outstanding (Total A/R Balance / total sales over the past rolling 3 months) X 91.
Total A/R Balance is the open account receivable amount that the customer owes for invoiced sales orders.
91 is the average number of days in a quarter.
This field updates when the Month End Customer A/R Aging Save runs.
#Inv
The number of open A/R invoices not paid.
D
Displays a D if at least one invoice for the customer is marked as disputed. You can mark the invoice status of an invoice as disputed in Manual Invoice Entry (refer to Manually Entering an Invoice) or in Accounts Receivable Maintenance (refer to Editing an A/R Invoice).
Future
Any invoice due more than 30 days in the future.
Current
The value of customer invoices that are not past due.
1-30
The value of customer invoices that are 1-30 days past due.
31-60
The value of customer invoices that are 31-60 days past due.
61-90
The value of customer invoices that are 61-90 days past due.
Over 90
The value of customer invoices that are over 90 days past due.
OK
Closes the inquiry screen.
F5-Invoices
Opens the Customer Invoice Inquiry to display detail information for each customer invoice.
F6-Orders
Opens the Sales Order Inquiry to list open (not invoiced) sales orders for a customer. You can access detail product and shipping information for each order.
F7-Cred Comment
Opens the Customer Credit Comments and Review screen to allow you to enter or review any A/R comments for this customer.
Edit Credit Comments in Accounts Receivable Parameters indicates how notes are entered and viewed in Accounts Receivable Inquiry, Customer Invoice Inquiry, and Customer Maintenance.
•
Y - Enter free form credit comments.
•
N - Use the standard Notes Entry screen for credit comments.
F8-Det Aging
Opens the Detail Aging Inquiry to display current and past due invoice amounts for the customer.
F9-Stats
Opens the Customer Statistics screen to display sales, profit, and payment information for a customer, broken down by month.
Action Bar
Address
Opens the Customer Address Information screen to display the customer’s address.
Inquiries
Provides access to the following:
•
Monthly Cash Receipts History screen to list fiscal periods in which checks were received from a customer.
•
Credit Collection Notes Inquiry to list collection notes entered for the customer. For more information, refer to the Credit Collections History Inquiry.
•
Month-End A/R Aging screen.
•
Customer Invoice Core Inquiry. Refer to the Inventory and the Order Processing documentation for information on Pending Cores
Images
If your company uses CoStar ® imaging, you can access images associated with this invoice and customer.
Graphs
Displays the customer’s A/R aging information in a graph format.
