Cash Receipts Invoice Detail Entry screen
The Cash Receipts Invoice Detail Entry screen allows you to apply cash to customer invoices (refer to Applying Cash to Invoices). You can view the customer’s invoice information and determine how much to apply to each invoice, to charge back to the customer, to adjust for tax and freight, or to write off. You can apply cash one invoice at a time or use the auto-pay feature to pay groups of invoices at once. Refer to the following:
•
Manually Applying Cash to Individual Invoices.
•
Automatically Applying Cash to Invoices.
Menu Path: Financial > Accounts Receivable > Cash Receipts Entry > F5-Enter Detail
Field and Function Key Descriptions
Field/Function Key
Description
Post Date
The date to post to the customer’s account and the general ledger. Defaults to the Deposit Date from the Cash Receipts Entry screen. If you change this date, a warning message displays but allows you to change or accept the date. This date is used to calculate earned discounts and to track payment history.
Batch Total Applied
The total amount applied in the batch so far. This number increases as you apply cash to each invoice for each customer in the batch.
Customer
The customer whose payment you are entering.
A/R Balance
The customer’s open accounts receivable balance. The total of sales orders billed to the customer but not yet paid.
Check Number
The customer’s check number.
Check Amount
The amount of the customer’s check.
Invoice List
Indicates how you want to list the customer’s invoices:
•
O - List all invoices with an open balance.
•
A - List open and paid invoices. Use this option to edit previous entries.
•
M - Manually enter an invoice number.
Cmt
Indicates if a cash receipt comment was entered for this batch:
•
Y - Comments exist for the batch.
•
N - Comments do not exist for the batch.
Invoices
The selected invoices for the customer.
Curr
The currency used by this customer (if you use multiple currency).
Inv Date
The date the order was invoiced.
S
The status code that indicates if any of the following conditions exist for the invoice:
•
Blank - None.
•
B - Bankruptcy.
•
C - Collections.
•
D - Disputed. You can mark an invoice as disputed in Manual Invoice Entry (refer to Manually Entering an Invoice) or in Accounts Receivable Maintenance (refer to Editing an A/R Invoice). Include Disputed Invoices in Collections in Additional Option Parameters determines if disputed invoices are included in the Credit Collections Subsystem.
•
L - Litigation.
•
N - Insufficient funds.
•
R - Rebate.
•
W - Write-off.
Dsc Date
The date by which the customer must pay the invoice in order to receive the discount. This date is determined by the sales order’s payment terms (refer to Using A/R Payment Terms).
Invoice Amt
The total amount billed to the customer on this invoice.
Balance
The amount still due from the customer for this invoice.
Disc Avail
The discount amount available to the customer if they pay the invoice by the Dsc Date. This amount is calculated according to the sales order’s payment terms (refer to Using A/R Payment Terms).
Disc Appl
The discount applied to this invoice. If the date is past the Dsc Date, the default is zero. If you want to give the customer the discount anyway, you can change it and enter an unearned discount reason code (refer to Overriding an Unearned Discount).
Cash Applied
Defaults to the amount of the invoice if the Amount Remaining on the check is enough to cover the invoice. Otherwise, Cash Applied defaults to Amount Remaining. If the balance is not paid, select from the following:
•
If the customer underpaid an invoice, a warning message appears. Refer to Reconciling Partial Payments.
•
If the customer overpaid an invoice, refer to Reconciling Over Payments.
•
If there is an error in the invoice refer to Editing an A/R Invoice.
M/C
If the amount that you enter in Cash Applied is less than the Invoice Amt, choose from the following:
•
A - Adjust to Existing Invoices (refer to Adjusting Tax and Freight).
•
C - Charge Back Entry (refer to Issuing Charge Back Invoices).
•
M - Miscellaneous Entry (refer to Writing off the Unpaid Balance).
•
N - No Adjustment (default).
Currency (unlabeled field)
The currency indicator for this customer if you use multiple currency.
Amount Applied
The total amount applied so far for this check.
Amount Remaining
The amount still available on the check to apply to invoices.
F2-Save
Saves the applied cash information and allows you to either enter the next customer in the batch or complete the batch.
F5-Reset Entry
Clears the customer/invoice information currently on the screen to allow you to start over.
F6-Auto Pay
Allows you to choose from the following auto-pay options to apply cash to multiple invoices:
•
Pay From Invoice Down - Applies the available cash to each invoice down starting from the highlighted invoice.
•
Invoice Number Range - Opens the Invoice Number Range Entry screen so you can enter beginning and ending invoice numbers to apply cash to.
•
Consolidated Invoice - Opens the Consolidated Invoice Number screen. If you use consolidated invoicing (refer to the Order Processing documentation for more information) you can enter the Consolidated Invoice Number to pay matching invoices by ascending date.
•
Customer P/O Number - Opens the Customer P/O Number screen to allow you to pay invoices using the customer’s purchase order number.
Refer to Automatically Applying Cash to Invoices.
F7-Cumm Bal
Opens the Cumulative Balance Inquiry screen to display the individual balance for each invoice along with the cumulative A/R balance.
F9-Goto Line
Allows you to enter a line number, page number, or reference number to go directly to.
Action Bar
Invoice Func
Allows access to the following:
•
Examine Invoice - Opens the Invoice Detail Inquiry to display detail payment information for the invoice.
•
Invoice Maintenance - Opens the Accounts Receivable Maintenance screen to allow you to edit the invoice.
Cash Receipts Func
Allows access to the following:
•
Additional Comments - Opens the Cash Receipts Comments screen to allow you to enter any notes regarding the batch. If comments are entered, a Y displays under Cmt on the Cash Receipts Invoice Detail Entry screen.
•
Opens the Unearned Discount screen to allow you to view or edit the unearned discount code for an invoice. Refer to Creating Unearned Discount Reason Codes.
