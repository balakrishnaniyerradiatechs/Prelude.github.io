Accounts Receivable Parameters
To determine the accounts receivable criteria that places orders on credit hold:
1
Select Option Parameters > Accounts Receivable Parameters to open the Accounts Receivable Parameters screen.
2
For Cred Hold/# Mo Last Sale, determine if orders should go on credit hold if a customer does not place any orders in the number of months indicated:
•
Y - Orders go on credit hold if no sales activity occurs for a customer in the number of months that you enter in the next (unlabeled) field. If a customer does not purchase anything in the number of months indicated, any new sales orders for the customer automatically go on credit hold.
•
N - Sales activity is not a criteria for credit hold.
3
For Cred Hold/ Confirm Order, determine if 06 (confirmation) sales orders are included in the credit limit check:
•
Y - 06 (confirmation) sales orders are included in the credit limit check.
•
N - 06 (confirmation) sales orders are not included in the credit limit check.
4
For Credit Hold A/R Terms Change, determine if a sales order goes on credit hold if the Payment Terms are changed on the Sales Order Header:
•
Y - Sales orders go on credit hold if the Payment Terms are changed.
•
N - Sales orders do not go on credit hold if the Payment Terms are changed.
5
For Credit Hold COD/CASH Orders, determine if a sales order with Payment Terms for cash or COD is included in the customer’s credit limit check:
•
Y - Include cash and COD orders in the credit limit check. In the next (unlabeled) field, enter a value so that all cash and COD orders over this amount will go on credit hold regardless of the customer’s credit standing.
•
N - Do not include cash or COD orders in the credit limit check.
6
For Credit Hold/Unallocate Inv, determine if sales orders on credit hold allocate inventory:
•
Y - Sales orders on credit hold do not allocate inventory. Pickable line items are updated as backordered. When the sales order is released from credit hold, the items are allocated.
An exception to this would be a line item linked to a purchase order or transfer. When linked items are received, they still allocate to the sales order on hold.
•
N - Sales orders on credit hold will still allocate inventory. All items on the order will commit inventory.
If stock is received, the system does not allocate the inventory to the open sales order on credit hold since inventory is allocated when the sales order is released from credit hold (Y flag setting).
7
For Days Past Due, enter the number of days past due a customer’s account must be to put the customer on automatic credit hold. This value is checked by the Auto Customer Credit Hold procedure (refer to Automatically Placing Customers on Credit Hold). The customer will go on credit hold if they have invoices this number of days overdue.
8
For Dollars Past Due, define the maximum dollar amount past due a customer account can be before going on credit hold. For example, if you enter 100, customers with a past due balance of $100 or less will not go on credit hold. This flag is used with Days Past Due to determine if the Auto Customer Credit Hold procedure places a customer on hold (refer to Automatically Placing Customers on Credit Hold). The Auto Customer Credit Hold procedure totals the invoice amount for all invoices that are the number of days overdue indicated in Days Past Due. If the amount is greater than or equal to the Dollars Past Due, the customer goes on credit hold.
9
Press F2-Save to save the A/R parameter credit hold information.
