Creating Credit Collections Activity Codes
To follow-up on all activity regarding credit collections for a customer, you can set up Credit Collection Activity codes to identify each type of activity used to collect on past due invoices (e.g., phone call, collection letter). You can use these codes in Credit Collections Follow-Up Maintenance to define the sequence of collection activities to follow when invoices are past due. (refer to Sequencing Follow-Up Collection Activities).
To create credit collection activity codes:
1
Select Financial > Accounts Receivable > Collections Subsystem > Maintenance > Credit Collections Activity Maintenance to open the Credit Collections Activity Maintenance screen.
2
For Activity Code, enter the alphanumeric code to identify an activity.
3
Enter a Description of the activity (e.g., First Phone Call, Warning Letter).
4
For Correspondence Document?, indicate if this activity involves a letter:
•
N - This activity does not generate a letter. The activity appears in Credit Collections Schedule Entry (refer to Entering Scheduled Credit Collection Activities). Press F2-Save.
•
Y - This activity generates a letter (refer to Generating Scheduled Collection Letters). Continue to step 5.
5
Indicate the Number of Copies of the letter to send.
6
For Salesrep Letter?, indicate if a copy of the letter should print/fax/or email to the customer’s sales representative:
•
Y - Send a copy of the letter to give to the customer’s sales representative. The letter will print, fax, or email depending on how the Collections Method is set in Customer Maintenance.
•
N - Do not send a copy of the letter to the customer’s sales representative.
7
To enter or edit a letter, press F5-Document Text. The Credit Collections Correspondence Definition screen appears.
8
Enter a new letter or edit the existing letter. To include a list of all undisputed past due invoices that qualify for collections, type && in the body of the letter where the list should appear.
9
Press F2-Save to save the letter.
10
Press F2-Save again to save the activity.
