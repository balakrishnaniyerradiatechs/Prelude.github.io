Automatically Assessing Service Charges
Once you determine how service charges are calculated (refer to Setting Maintenance Options for Service Charges), you can generate service charges for past due invoices.
To generate service charges for past due invoices:
1
Select Financial > Accounts Receivable > Processes > Service Charge Processing > Auto Service Charge Entry to open the Auto Service Charge Entry screen.
2
For Current Invoice Date, enter the date to appear on the service charge invoices. Last S/C Run Date displays the last date the service charge process ran. Normally, service charges are generated once a month. If you run it more than once a month, a warning message appears.
3
For Current Days Limit, enter the days past due an invoice must exceed before service charges are calculated. For example, if you enter 120, only invoices over 120 days past due are included in the service charge calculation. This value is overridden by Service Charge Days in Customer Maintenance.
4
For Include Credits, determine if credit memos are included in service charge calculations:
•
Y - Deduct credit memos from invoices before calculating service charges.
For example, a customer is set up with a Service Charge % of 2.00. The customer’s invoices over 120 days past due total $1000.00. Credit memos for the customer total $100.00. The base value used to calculate the service charge is $900 (1000 - 100) and the service charge amount is $18 (900 x .02).
•
N - Do not use credit memos in service charge calculations.
For example, a customer is set up with a Service Charge % of 2.00. The customer’s invoices over 120 days past due total $1000.00. Credit memos for the customer total $100.00. The base value used to calculate the service charge is $1000 (credit memos are ignored) and the service charge amount is $20 (1000 x .02).
5
For Skip Charge if < Minimum?, determine if the calculated service charge is invoiced to the customer if it is below the Min. Service Charge set in Accounts Receivable Parameters:
•
Y - If the calculated service charge is below the Min. Service Charge amount, the customer is not billed a service charge for past due invoices.
•
N - If the calculated service charge amount is below the Min. Service Charge amount, the service charge is increased to meet the Min. Service Charge.
6
For Include Prev Service Chrgs?, determine if previously calculated service charges are included in the new service charge calculation:
•
Y - Previous service charges that meet the Current Days Limit are included in the base invoice amount used to calculate the current service charge.
•
N - Previous service charges are excluded from service charge calculations.
7
For Create Invoice for Each Whse?, determine if you want to create service charge invoices by customer or by warehouse by customer:
•
Y - Create service charge invoices by warehouse by customer (e.g., one service charge invoice is created for each customer/warehouse combination). Warehouse is determined by the sales order’s selling warehouse.
•
N - Create service charge invoices by customer (e.g., one service charge invoice is created for each customer).
8
Press ENTER. The following message appears:
WARNING: The program will update all warehouses RUN ONLY ONCE!
9
Choose from the following:
•
Continue - Service charges calculate on past due invoices for customers with a Service Charge % in Customer Maintenance. The general ledger is not yet updated (refer to Accounts Receivable Parameters). You can modify the calculated service charge in Manual Service Charge Entry if necessary (refer to Manually Assessing or Editing a Service Charge).
•
Quit - Cancels the service charge calculation.
You can modify any service charge calculations in Manual Service Charge Maintenance (refer to Manually Assessing or Editing a Service Charge).
To print, email, or fax the service charge invoice to the customer, refer to Printing Service Charge Invoices.
