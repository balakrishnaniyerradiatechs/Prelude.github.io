A/R Payment Terms Maintenance screen
The payment terms assigned to a customer or a sales order used to determine the date the customer’s invoices are due, the date by which they must pay the invoice to receive a discount, and the discount amount (refer to Understanding Payment Terms). Once you set up the payment terms codes, you can assign them to each customer in Customer Maintenance or Customer Ship-To Maintenance.
Menu Path: Financial > Accounts Receivable > Maintenance > A/R Payment Terms Maintenance
Field and Function Key Descriptions
Field/Function Key
Description
Payment Terms
The code that identifies the customer due dates and payment information.
Description
The description of the payment terms code.
Terms Percent
The discount percentage for this terms code. For example, if Terms Percent is 2% and is applied to an invoice totaling $100.00, the discount is $2.00. Leave blank if the customer is not to receive a discount.
Refer to Setting Up A/R Payment Terms for information on how Due Date, Net Days Allowed, Net Day-Of-Month, and Cutoff Day are used together to determine a due date.
Due Date
The date invoices are due. This field only applies if the payment terms indicate that all payments are due on the same day of the month.
Net Days Allowed
The number of days after the invoice date when payment is due. For example, if the invoice date is 5/10 and Net Days Allowed is 15, payment is due by 5/25. If Net Days Allowed is P15 (Prox Terms), payment is due by 6/25.
Status
Indicates if the payment terms type is active:
•
A (or blank) - Active.
•
I - Inactive. You cannot enter a sales order using an inactive payment terms.
Net Day-Of-Month
The day of the month when payment is due. Use Prox Terms if payment is due the following month. Net Day-Of-Month works with Cutoff Day. For example, if Net Day-Of-Month is 25 and Cutoff Day is 15, invoices dated before the 16th are due on the 25th of the month. If Net Day-Of-Month is P25 and Cutoff Day is 25, invoices dated before the 16th are due on the 25th of the next month.
Cutoff Day
The last day on which invoices dated before Net Day-Of-Month are due. See Net Day-Of-Month for an example.
Net Day-Of-Month
If this terms code has 2 cutoff days, the second due date.
Cutoff Day
The cutoff day for the second due date.
Net Day-Of-Month
If the terms code has 3 cutoff days, enter the third due date.
Cutoff Day
The cutoff day for the third due date.
Discount Date, Disc. Days Allowed, or Disc. Day-Of-Month are only required if you entered a Terms Percent and offer a discount to customers who pay invoices early. Refer to Setting Up A/R Payment Terms for more information on how to choose a discount method.
Discount Date
The date by which the customers should pay to receive a discount, if it is the same every month. This date overrides the calculated discount date.
Disc. Days Allowed
The number of days after the invoice date when a discount no longer applies. For example, if an invoice is dated 5/25, and the Discount Days Allowed is 15, the customer needs to pay before 6/09 to receive the discount.
Disc. Day-Of-Month
The day of the month on which payment is due for your customers to receive a discount. Use prox terms if payment is due the following month. Disc. Day-Of-Month works with Cutoff Day. For example, if Disc. Day-Of-Month is 25 and Cutoff Day is 15, invoices dated before the 16th are due on the 25th of the month to receive a discount. If Disc. Day-Of-Month is P25 and Cutoff Day is 25, invoices dated before the 16th must be paid by the 25th of the next month to receive a discount.
Cutoff Day
The last day on which invoices dated before the Disc. Day-Of-Month are due. See Disc. Day-Of-Month for an example.
Disc. Day-Of-Month
If the terms code has 2 cutoff days, the second discount date.
Cutoff Day
The cutoff day for the second discount date.
Disc. Day-Of-Month
If the terms code has 3 cutoff days, enter the third discount date.
Cutoff Day
The cutoff day for the third discount date.
Cash Type
Indicates if the terms apply to cash sales:
•
Y - The sale is processed as a cash sale. The Sales Order Completion screen appears on completion of the sales order. When a sales order is entered for a customer, they cannot pay on credit and they will not receive a discount for early payment.
•
N - The sale is not a cash sale.
Credit Card Type
Indicates if the terms apply to credit card sales:
•
Y - The sale is processed as a credit card sale. The Sales Order Completion screen appears on completion of the sales order so you can enter credit card information.
•
N - The sale is not a credit card sale.
Refer to the “Credit Cards” documentation in the Order Processing module for more information.
C.O.D. Type
Indicates if the terms require cash on delivery (C.O.D.):
•
Y - The sales order requires cash upon delivery of the order. The Sales Order Completion screen appears on completion of the sales order. The customer cannot pay on credit and they will not receive a discount for early payment.
•
N - The sales order does not require C.O.D.
Cash Equivalent
If C.O.D is Y, the customer must provide cash or cash equivalent (such as a money order or cashier’s check) at the time of delivery.
Installments
Determines if multiple A/R invoices are created based on an installment payment schedule:
•
Y - Opens the A/ R Installment Terms screen to allow you to define a payment schedule for multiple invoices. A single invoice is created for each payment with a new calculated discount and due date for each invoice. All invoices are created at the same time.
•
N - Installment type invoices are not used.
Pay In Advance
Indicates if a customer must pay in advance:
•
Y - The customer must pay in advance, upon completion of the sales order. Even for backordered merchandise.
•
N - The customer does not have to pay in advance.
Lease Type
Indicates if the payment type is for leased material:
•
Y - Opens the A/R Lease Terms screen to allow you to define a payment schedule for multiple invoices. A single invoice is created for each payment with a new calculated discount and due date for each invoice.
•
N - The payment terms is not for leased material.
Deposit Required
Indicates if a deposit is required from customers:
•
Y - A deposit is required on sales orders, even if the material is backordered.
•
N - A deposit is not required.
Credit Card Entry Mode
Indicates if customer credit card information is automatically copied from the customer’s account:
•
Y - The customer’s credit card information is used on the sales order.
•
N - No credit card information is used.
F2-Save
Saves the A/R term information.
F4-Delete
Deletes the A/R terms.
F5-Multiple Discount Levels
Opens the Multiple Discount Levels screen to allow you to view any additional percentages set up for the terms code.
