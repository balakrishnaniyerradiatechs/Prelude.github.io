Calculating Average Days to Pay
To calculate Average Days to Pay, the system uses the average number of days it takes a customer to pay an invoice and the average number of days a customer’s account is overdue. Average Days to Pay uses the cash amount applied to place an emphasis on invoices of greater value. The larger the invoice, the higher the Average Days to Pay. Disputed invoices are excluded from this calculation.
Marking an Invoice as Disputed
You can mark an invoice as disputed in Manual Invoice Entry (refer to Manually Entering an Invoice) or in Accounts Receivable Maintenance (refer to Editing an A/R Invoice).
Average Days to Pay Calculations
Term
Calculation
Days to Pay
Cash Receipts Post Date - Invoice Date
Days Overdue
Cash Receipts Post Date - Due Date
Weight of New Cash Applied
(Wn)
New Cash Applied/
(New Cash Applied + Sum Of Previously Applied Cash)
Weight of Previous Cash Applied (Wp)
1 – Wn
Average Days
(Wn x Days to Pay) + (Wp x Previous Average Days to Pay)
Average Days Overdue
(Wn x Days Overdue) + (Wp x Previous Days Overdue)
Number of Months to use in Average Days to Pay
Avg Days To Pay Months in Accounts Receivable Parameters sets the number of months to use to calculate average days to pay.
Both the Average Days to Pay and Average Days Overdue are calculated when a cash receipts batch is updated (refer to Completing the Batch).
