Entering Scheduled Credit Collection Activities
Credit Collections Schedule Entry allows you to view A/R information for customers that are scheduled for follow-up credit collection activities and to perform and record the next scheduled activity. These activities are generated by Nightly Processing (refer to Running Credit Collections Nightly Processing).
You may find it easier to run the Credit Collections Activity Report before beginning collection activities to determine which group of customers you want to contact or which type of activities you want to perform.
Collections Evaluation Method in Accounts Receivable Parameters determines how customers pass through activities in Credit Collections Schedule Entry.
•
A - Action method. Moves customers through activities only if they were reviewed (for the call queue) or had a letter generated (for a correspondence activity).
•
T - Time method. Moves customers based on the number of days that passed, regardless of whether or not they were reviewed. For example, if the customer is in a queue but no action is taken, they will still appear in the next queue in the number of days assigned to their credit collections type.
To review and follow-up on past due customer accounts:
1
Select Financial > Accounts Receivable > Collections Subsystem > Credit Collections Schedule Entry to open the Credit Collections Schedule Entry screen.
2
Enter the Credit Collections Type to determine the customers to include. Credit collections types are set up in the Credit Collections Type Maintenance screen and assigned to customers in Customer Maintenance.
3
If applicable, enter the Security Access Code (if Bypass Security Access Code is set to N in Accounts Receivable Parameters).
4
Enter the Activity Code to list all customers that have this code as their next scheduled activity. These codes are established in the Credit Collections Activity Maintenance screen. You can only enter activity codes that do not generate a document. Activities that generate a letter are scheduled by Credit Collections Nightly Processing (refer to Generating Scheduled Collection Letters).
All customers that have this code as their next scheduled activity appear.
5
Highlight the customer to review and/or to record an activity for and press F5-Review. The Credit Collections Customer Entry screen opens to display A/R information for the customer including open balances and credit collection notes.
Before you contact the customer, you can review detail information regarding the customer’s open balance by selecting one of the following:
•
Action Bar > Add Info to open the Additional Info screen to view override follow-up activities scheduled for the customer.
•
Action Bar > Inv Maint to open the Accounts Receivable Maintenance screen (refer to Editing an A/R Invoice).
•
Action Bar > Hist Inq to view a copy of the Credit Collections History Inquiry report.
•
F5-Invoice List to open the Customer Invoice Inquiry to view open invoices for the customer.
•
F6-A/R Inquiry to open the Accounts Receivable Inquiry.
•
F7- Collection Notes to open the Credit Collection Notes Inquiry. Collection notes are entered in Credit Collections Notes Maintenance (refer to Entering Credit Collections Notes). This data is also updated when you manually enter a credit collection letter on demand (refer to Generating Collection Letters on Demand).
6
Once you perform the activity (e.g., make the phone call), you can enter notes regarding the phone call. To enter notes:
•
Enter the Date for each line of notes.
•
For Type, choose from the following:
-
P - Permanent. The notes stay in the customer’s credit collections history indefinitely.
-
G - General. The notes have an expiration date. You must enter the Expiration Days which is the number of days until the notes are removed from the customer’s credit collections history. The number of Expiration Days is determined in Additional Option Parameters.
-
T - Temporary. The notes are associated with a customer invoice. You must enter the Invoice Number. When the invoice is paid, the note is removed from the customer’s collection history.
•
For Manual Notes, enter any notes regarding this activity.
7
For Reviewed, indicate if the customer was sufficiently reviewed and should be removed from the current collections queue:
•
Y - The customer is removed until the next action date, if they are still past due.
•
N - The customer remains in the queue. An asterisk appears on the Credit Collections Schedule Entry screen to indicate that the customer was reviewed but should not be removed from the queue.
8
For Next Action, choose from the following:
•
Accept the default to allow the next scheduled activity to occur for this customer as defined in Credit Collections Follow-Up Maintenance (refer to Sequencing Follow-Up Collection Activities) for each Credit Collections Type. Nightly Processing for Credit Collections will schedule the next activity if the customer does not become current.
•
Override the next scheduled activity with a new activity code (refer to Creating Credit Collections Activity Codes). The Additional Info screen appears to allow you to enter additional follow-up activities and dates, if applicable.
9
If you entered an override activity for Next Action, enter the date the activity should take place in Next Date.
10
Press F2-Save to close the screen and return to the Credit Collections Schedule Entry screen.
11
Repeat steps 5-10 for each customer to review.
Credit Collection Standalone Entry
To record a credit collection activity that was not generated by Nightly Processing, select Financial > Accounts Receivable > Collections Subsystem > Credit Collections > Standalone Entry. This program works the same as Credit Collections Schedule Entry, except that the customer does not have to exist in a current credit collections queue. A warning message appears if you enter a customer that is already scheduled for a credit collection activity.
