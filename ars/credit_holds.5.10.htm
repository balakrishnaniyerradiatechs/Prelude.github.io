Customer Maintenance
Credit hold options for each customer in Customer Maintenance override any settings at the warehouse or system level.
To set customer credit hold options:
1
Select Order Processing > Maintenance > Customer Maintenance.
2
Enter the Customer number.
3
Press F6-A/R to open the A/R Information screen.
4
For Auto Credit Review, indicate if this customer is placed on credit hold by the Automatic Credit Review Process, which is run periodically:
•
Y - The customer is placed on or off credit hold by the Auto Credit Hold Procedure (refer to Automatically Placing Customers on Credit Hold). If an invoice is more than the number of days past due indicated in Auto Credit Days and not flagged as disputed, then this flag is automatically changed to H (automatic credit hold). You can temporarily take the customer off hold by changing the H back to a Y until the Auto Credit Hold Procedure runs again. Other settings such as credit limits may affect this flag also.
•
H - The customer is on automatic credit hold.
•
N - The customer is not included in the Auto Credit Hold Procedure.
5
For Auto Credit Days, enter the number of days past due the customer’s payments must be before they are subject to the Auto Credit Hold Procedure (Auto Credit Review must be set to Y). This number overrides the Days Past Due set in Accounts Receivable Parameters.
6
Enter the Credit Limit for the customer. When you enter a sales order for this customer, if the total value of the customer’s A/R balance and open sales orders (excluding long-term future orders) exceeds the credit limit, the order goes on credit hold.
7
For Credit Code, enter the credit control code that determines if and when orders can be entered for the customer:
•
A - An Authorization Code must be entered in Sales Order Entry after the customer number and before the header information. An authorization code refers to any user ID set up as an authorization code. Refer to Credit Authorization Code Maintenance for information on setting up authorization codes.
•
E or blank - Sales orders are subject to the customer’s Credit Limit. If the customer is over their credit limit, the pick ticket will not print until the order is released from credit hold. Refer to Releasing Orders From Credit Hold.
•
H - Sales orders can be entered but are always held until the credit manager releases them. You must enter the credit reason abbreviation code (established in Valid Response Maintenance) that explains why this customer’s orders always go on hold. If no codes is entered, the code MAN (manual) is used.
•
N - No sales orders or quotes can be entered for this customer.
8
Enter the Credit Manager ID override for this customer. Leave blank to use the credit manager assigned to the warehouse in Warehouse Manager Maintenance.
9
For Oversized Order Value, enter the order value that is too large for this customer. Any orders over this amount will go on credit hold. This value overrides the oversized order value set inWarehouse Maintenance or in Order Processing Parameters.
10
Press F2-Save twice to save the customer’s credit information.
Refer to “Customer Maintenance” in the Order Processing documentation for a detailed explanation of each field in Customer Maintenance.
