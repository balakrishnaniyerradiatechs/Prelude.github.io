Daily Credit Status Change Report
The Daily Credit Status Change Report lists changes to customers’ credit status (e.g., taken off or put on credit hold, credit limit changed, terms code changed). Refer to Credit Holds for more information.
After you run the report, you can choose to purge the information so that it does not appear the next time you run the report. If this report is run on the scheduler, the data is automatically purged.
Credit Statistics in File Update Parameters must be set to Y to capture credit status change information.
Menu Path: Financial > Accounts Receivable > Reports > Credit Status Change
Selection Criteria
Use the following criteria to limit the information to print on the report:
•
Entry Date <. The ending date for all credit status changes that you want to include.
Column Descriptions
Column Heading
Description
User
The user ID of the person that changed the customer’s credit status.
Customer
The customer number and description.
Tran Type
The type of transaction that changed the customer’s credit status:
•
FM - File maintenance. The customer’s credit limit or payment terms were changed in Customer Maintenance or Customer Ship-To Maintenance.
•
AUTO - The customer was put on or off credit hold using the Auto Credit HOld Procedure (refer to Automatically Placing Customers on Credit Hold).
Credit Hold Flag Old/New
If the customer’s credit hold status was changed, the old and new credit hold status displays:
•
Y - The customer is placed on or off credit hold by the Auto Credit Hold Procedure (refer to Automatically Placing Customers on Credit Hold). If an invoice is more than the number of days past due indicated in Auto Credit Days, and not flagged as disputed, then this flag is automatically changed to H (automatic credit hold). You can temporarily take the customer off hold by changing the H back to a Y until the Auto Credit Hold Procedure runs again. Other settings such as credit limits may affect this flag also.
•
H - The customer is on automatic credit hold.
•
N - The customer is not included in the Auto Credit Hold Procedure.
Credit Code
Old/New
If the customer’s credit code was changed, the old and new credit control code assigned to the customer. Credit control codes determine if and when orders can be entered for the customer:
•
A - You must enter an Authorization Code in Sales Order Entry after you enter the customer number and before you enter the header information. Refer to Credit Authorization Code Maintenance for information on setting up authorization codes.
•
E or blank - Sales orders are subject to the customer’s Credit Limit. If the customer is over their credit limit, the pick ticket will not print until the order is released from credit hold. Refer to Releasing Orders From Credit Hold.
•
H - Sales orders can be entered but are always held until the credit manager releases them. You must enter the credit reason abbreviation code (established in Valid Response Maintenance) that explains why this customer’s orders always go on hold. If no code is entered, the code MAN (manual) is used.
•
N - No sales orders or quotes can be entered for this customer.
Credit Limit
Old/New
If the customer’s credit limit was changed, the old and new credit limit assigned to the customer.
A/R Terms
Old Code/Description
New Code/Description
If the customer’s payment terms were changed, the old and new payment terms assigned to the customer. Refer to Using A/R Payment Terms for more information.
Entry Date/Time
The date and time that the customer’s credit information was changed.
