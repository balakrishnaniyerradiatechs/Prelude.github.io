Beginning a Cash Receipt Batch
You can accumulate checks from one or more customers to create a cash receipts batch. The batch amount represents the total of cash and checks included in a bank deposit.
High Volume Cash Receipts
If a customer has a large volume of payments per month and you receive payment via EDI, you can use High Volume Cash Receipts to expedite the cash application process. Refer to Applying EDI Payments in High-Volume Cash Receipts.
To begin the cash receipts batch:
1
Select Financial > Accounts Receivable > Cash Receipts Entry to open the Cash Receipts Entry screen.
If you have access to more than one warehouse, the Location Retrieval screen appears first. Enter the appropriate Selling Warehouse, Department, and/or Cost Center and press ENTER.
2
For Batch Number, choose one of the following:
•
Type NEW and press ENTER to let the system generate a new batch number.
•
Press F3 to search for a suspended batch.
•
Enter the exact batch number, if known.
Press F6-Status Display to view check numbers and amounts already applied for a batch.
3
For Deposit Date, enter the date the cash will be deposited in the bank. The fiscal period appears.
4
Enter the Bank Code to designate where the money is being deposited.
5
For Deposit Total, enter the total amount of cash and checks in the deposit.
6
If necessary, enter any comments regarding the deposit in Deposit Comments.
7
Press F5-Enter Detail. The Cash Receipts Invoice Detail Entry screen appears.
8
Accept the default Post Date of today’s date. You can change the date if necessary, but a warning message appears.
This date is used to post to the customer’s account and to the general ledger and is also used to calculate earned discounts and track payment history.
9
Enter the first Customer in the batch. The customer’s open A/R Balance appears. This is the total amount of unpaid invoices for the customer.
10
Enter the Check Number for the first check in the batch. If the check number was previously used for this customer, a warning message appears, but you can still use the number.
11
Enter the Check Amount. The Batch Total Applied defaults to the amount of the check.
12
For Invoice List, determine how to display the invoices. Choose from the following:
•
O - List all invoices with an open balance.
•
A - List all open and paid invoices. Use this option to edit previous entries.
•
M - Manually enter an invoice number.
The invoices for the customer display; including the invoice date, amount, remaining balance, and available discounts. The Disc Avail (discount available) is the discount the customer receives if they pay the invoice by the discount date. If the Post Date that you enter is past the customer’s discount date, the Disc Appl (discount applied) defaults to zero.
Invoice Status
The S (Status) Column indicates if any of the following conditions exist for the corresponding invoice:
•
Blank - None.
•
B - Bankruptcy.
•
C - Collections.
•
D - Disputed.
•
L - Litigation.
•
N - Insufficient funds.
•
R - Rebate.
•
W - Write-off
To enter or change the status, from the Action Bar, select Invoice Func > Invoice Maintenance > Invoice Status
13
Once the invoices display, choose from the following:
•
To continue with the cash application refer to the Applying Cash to Invoices.
•
To clear the list and start over with a new customer number, press F5-Reset Entry.
•
To examine an invoice, highlight the invoice and select the Action Bar > Invoice Func > Examine Invoice to open the Invoice Detail Inquiry.
•
To edit invoice due dates, discount dates, invoice status or other A/R information, from the Action Bar, select Invoice Func > Invoice Maintenance to open the Accounts Receivable Maintenance screen. Refer to Editing an A/R Invoice.
