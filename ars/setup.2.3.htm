Understanding Payment Terms
Accounts Receivable payment terms are defined by due date, net days allowed, or by net day of month cutoff day. Refer to the following definitions and examples.
Due Date:
Set a specific date for the customer to pay (e.g. 5/25/2008).
Net Days Allowed:
Payment is due a set number of days past the invoice date. For example, enter 15 if the payment is due 15 days after the invoice date. If the invoice date is 5/14, the due date is 5/29.
Net Day of Month / Cutoff Day:
The due date varies among 2 or 3 dates based on the invoice date and a cutoff date. If an invoice date is after the Cutoff Date, the due date is the Net Day of Month the following month.You can specify 2 or 3 due dates and 1, 2, or 3 cutoff days. You can also enter a P before the Net Day-Of-Month to indicate that payment is due the following month (prox dating).
