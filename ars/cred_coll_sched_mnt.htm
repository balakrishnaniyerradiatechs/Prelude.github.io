Credit Collections Customer Entry screen
The Credit Collections Customer Entry screen in Credit Collections Schedule Entry displays A/R information for a customer including open balances and credit collection notes. You can enter notes regarding credit collection activities and/or schedule future credit collection activities.
Menu Path: Financial > Accounts Receivable > Collections Subsystem > Credit Collections Schedule Entry > F5-Review
Field and Function Key Descriptions
Field/Function Key
Description
Customer
The customer with the past due balance.
Contact
The customer’s contact person (from Customer Maintenance) responsible for paying invoices.
Phone
The contact’s phone number.
Cust Type
The customer type code (Customer Maintenance > F5-Add Info) used to group customers for custom reports.
Coll Type
The collection type code. These codes are created in Credit Collections Type Maintenance (refer to Defining Credit Collections Types) to group similar customers for collections (e.g., slow paying customers, good customers) and assigned to customers in Customer Maintenance > F6-A/R > F5-Add Info.
Fax
The fax number for the customer contact.
A/R Bal
The customer’s accounts receivable balance. Orders invoiced but not yet paid.
Credit Limit
The customer’s credit limit assigned to a customer in Customer Maintenance > F6-A/R.
High Bal
The highest A/R Balance that customer has had.
Started
The date that credit collection activity started for this customer.
Avg Days Pay
The average number of days it takes for the customer to pay an invoice. Refer to Calculating Average Days to Pay for more information.
Last Pmt
The amount of the last payment received from the customer.
Last Pmt Date
The last date payment was received from the customer.
Invoice
The invoice numbers that are past due.
Date
The date the order was invoiced.
Order
The sales order number for the corresponding invoice.
P/O Number
The customer’s purchase order number for the corresponding invoice.
Rep
The primary salesrep assigned to the sales order.
Invoice Amount
The total amount of the invoice.
Balance
The balance still due from the customer for the invoice.
Date
If notes exist for the past due account, the date notes were entered.
Type
The type of notes previously entered:
•
P - Permanent. The notes stay in the customer’s credit collections history indefinitely.
•
G - General. The notes have an expiration date. You must enter the Expiration Days, which is the number of days until the notes are removed from the customer’s credit collections history.
•
T - Temporary. The notes are associated with a customer invoice number that you specify. When the invoice is paid, the note is removed from the customer’s collection history.
Historical Notes
The notes entered for this customer.
The following fields record information after an activity is performed (e.g. phone call made):
Date
The date of the activity that you are recording.
Type
The type of notes that you are entering regarding this activity:
•
P - Permanent. The notes stay in the customer’s credit collections history indefinitely.
•
G - General. The notes have an expiration date. You must enter the Expiration Days, which is the number of days until the notes are removed from the customer’s credit collections history.
•
T - Temporary. The notes are associated with a customer invoice number that you specify. When the invoice is paid, the note is removed from the customer’s collection history.
Manual Notes
Any notes regarding this customer and activity.
Reviewed
Indicates if the customer has been sufficiently reviewed and should be removed from the current collections queue:
•
Y - The customer is removed until the next action date (if it remains past due).
•
N - The customer remains in the queue. An asterisk appears on the Credit Collections Schedule Entry screen to indicate that the customer was reviewed but should not be removed from the queue.
Next Action
The next scheduled activity to occur for this customer as defined in Credit Collections Follow Up Maintenance (refer to Sequencing Follow-Up Collection Activities) for each Credit Collections Type. Nightly Processing for Credit Collections will schedule the next activity if the customer does not become current. You can override the default with a new activity code (refer to Creating Credit Collections Activity Codes). The Additional Info screen appears to allow you to enter additional follow-up activities and dates, if applicable.
Next Date
The date that the next activity should take place.
F2-Save
Saves the activity information and returns to the Credit Collections Schedule Entry screen.
F5-Invoice List
Opens the Customer Invoice Inquiry to display open invoices for the customer.
F6-A/R Inquiry
Opens the Accounts Receivable Inquiry to display detail information for the customer’s invoices.
F7-Collection Notes
Opens the Credit Collection Notes Inquiry. Collection notes are entered in Credit Collections Historical Notes Maintenance. This data is also updated when you manually enter a credit collection letter on demand (refer to Generating Collection Letters on Demand).
Action Bar
Add Info
Opens the Additional Info screen to display override follow-up activities scheduled for the customer.
Inv Maint
Opens the Accounts Receivable Maintenance screen to allow you to edit the invoice (refer to Editing an A/R Invoice).
Hist Inq
Allows you to print or view a copy of the Credit Collections History Inquiry report.
Images
If your company uses CoStar imaging, you can access images associated with this customer.
