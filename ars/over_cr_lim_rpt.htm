Customer Over Credit Limit Report
The Customer Over Credit Limit report lists customers who are over their credit limit. Refer to Credit Holds for more information.
Menu Path: Financial > Accounts Receivable > Reports > Customer Over Credit Limit
Selection Criteria
Use the following criteria to limit the information to print on the report:
•
Selling Warehouse. The selling warehouse that receives credit for a customer’s orders, regardless of which warehouse shipped the material.
Column Descriptions
Column Heading
Description
Customer
The customer number.
Description
The customer description.
Whse
The selling warehouse assigned to the customer.
Avg Days
The average number of days it takes for this customer to pay their invoices (refer to Calculating Average Days to Pay).
Csh Date
The last time payment was applied to this customer’s account.
Cash Amount
The last payment amount that was applied to this customer’s account.
Cde
 
The credit code assigned to the customer in Customer Maintenance that determines if and when orders can be placed for the customer:
•
A - You must enter an Authorization Code in Sales Order Entry after the customer number and before the header information. Refer to Credit Authorization Code Maintenance.
•
E or blank - Sales orders are subject to the customer’s Credit Limit. a pick ticket will not print until the order is released from credit hold. Refer to Releasing Orders From Credit Hold.
•
H - Sales orders can be entered but are always held until the credit manager releases them. You must enter the credit reason abbreviation code (established in Valid Response Maintenance) that explains why this customer’s orders always go on hold. If no code is entered, the code MAN (manual) is used.
•
N - No sales orders or quotes can be entered for this customer.
Credit Limit
The credit limit assigned to the customer in Customer Maintenance > F6-A/R. When you enter a sales order for this customer, if the total value of the customer’s A/R balance and open sales orders (excluding disputed and long-term future orders) exceeds the credit limit, the order goes on credit hold.
A/R Balance
The total open accounts receivable balance due by the customer (i.e., sales orders billed but not yet paid).
Ord Balance
The total open sales order balance for the customer (i.e., sales orders placed but not yet invoiced).
Credit Bal
The customer’s credit balance
Credit Limit - (A/R balance + Ord Balance).
