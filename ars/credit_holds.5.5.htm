Order Processing Parameters
To determine Sales Order Entry credit hold options:
1
Select Option Parameters > Order Processing Parameters > F5-Add Info to open the Additional Information screen.
2
For Exclude Install Terms From Cred Hold, choose from the following:
•
Y - Orders do not go on automatic credit hold when a sales order’s Payment Terms are changed to installment terms.
•
N - Sales orders go on automatic credit hold when the Payment Terms are changed to installment terms.
Refer to ”Setting Up A/R Payment Terms” on page 2-4.
3
For Inc Fut Bal/Cred Check, determine if future orders (orders with a future ship date) are included in the credit limit check:
•
Y - Future orders are included when calculating a customer’s credit balance for credit hold purposes.
•
N - Future orders are excluded from credit balance checking for credit holds.
4
For Exclude From Credit Limit, determine if the credit manager can approve a large sales order and exclude it from the credit limit calculation so that additional sales do not go on credit hold.
•
Y - After a sales order is released in Credit Release Inquiry, a message appears to allow the credit manager to exclude the order from the credit limit check (refer to Excluding Sales Orders From the Credit Limit).
•
N - The credit manager cannot exclude an order from the credit limit check while in Credit Release Inquiry.
5
For Suppress Credit Messages, determine if the sales person is notified in Sales Order Entry when a sales order goes on credit hold. Choose from the following:
•
Y - Messages regarding the credit status will not appear in Sales Order Entry when the sales order goes on credit hold. The person entering the order will not know if it is on hold.
•
N - When a sales order is entered for a customer on credit hold, a message appears to let the salesperson know that the customer is on credit hold. The salesperson can send a note to the credit manager upon completion of the order.
6
Press F5-Add Info to open the Additional Options screen:
7
For Oversize Order Value, define the amount above which sales orders go on credit hold. For example, enter 50,000 if orders over 50,000 should go on credit hold. You can override this value for a customer (Customer Maintenance > F6-A/R > Oversize Order Value) or for a warehouse (Warehouse Maintenance > F5-Add Info > Oversized Order Value).
8
For Suspend Orders From Activities, determine if suspended sales orders can go on credit hold or administrative hold. Choose from the following:
•
Y - Do not place a suspended order or quote on hold, even if it meets the criteria for credit hold or administrative hold.
•
N - If a sales order or quote meets the criteria for credit hold or administrative hold, it will go on hold even if it is suspended.
9
Press F2-Save 3 times to save the Order Processing Parameter information.
