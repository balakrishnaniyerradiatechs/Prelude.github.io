Entering a High Volume Cash Receipts Batch
To start a high-volume cash receipt batch:
1
Select Financial > Accounts Receivable > High Volume Cash Receipts > Batch Entry to open the High Volume Cash Receipts Entry screen.
2
Enter a Batch Number to edit or press ENTER to start a new batch.
3
Enter the Batch Amount, which is the total amount of all payments in the the batch.
4
Enter the Deposit Date to calculate the fiscal period for the general ledger postings.
5
Specify the Bank Code to designate the bank where this batch was deposited.
6
Enter any comments about this batch in Deposit Comments.
7
Press ENTER to open the High Volume Cash Detail Entry screen.
8
Press ENTER to get a new Transaction Number or enter an existing transaction number to edit. Each check requires a separate transaction.
9
Enter the Customer Number, Check Number, Check Amount and the Check Date.
10
Enter the Reference Number from the customer’s list of payments. Depending on how High Volume C/R Method is set in AP Option Parameters, choose one of the following:
•
Invoice number (High Volume C/R Method = I).
-
You must enter the exact invoice number. The gross Amount, Discount, and Net Amount appear.
-
If an invoice number is not accepted, you may have to manually search for it in High Volume Cash Receipts Maintenance (refer to Manually Matching and Editing Invoices).
•
Reference numbers (High Volume C/R Method = R).
-
Enter an invoice, order, customer P/O, or release number.
-
Enter the Amount and/or Discount of the invoice. If you only have a discount for the check, instead of each invoice, enter the entire amount in the first Discount.
11
Repeat step 10 for each invoice to apply cash to. You can save this batch entry even if it is out of balance. However, the data must balance before you can update the batch (refer to Updating a Batch).
12
Press F2-Save.
Once all the invoices are identified, you must match each entry to an open invoice. Refer to Matching Cash Receipts with Open Invoices.
