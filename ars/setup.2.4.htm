Setting Up A/R Payment Terms
The payment terms assigned to a customer determine the date the customer’s invoices are due, the date by which they must pay to receive a discount, and the discount amount (refer to Understanding Payment Terms). Once you set up the payment terms codes, you can assign them to each customer in Customer Maintenance, Customer Ship-To Maintenance, or Customer Job Maintenance.
To create Payment Terms codes:
1
Select Financial > Accounts Receivable > Maintenance > A/R Terms Maintenance to open the A/R Payment Terms Maintenance screen.
2
For Payment Terms, enter the 1 or 2 digit alphanumeric code to identify customer payment criteria.
3
Enter the Description of the payment terms (e.g., 2% 10 Days Net 30).
4
Assign a Terms Percent to give the customer a discount for early payment. For example, if the Terms Percent is 5% and is applied to an invoice total of $100.00, the discount amount is $5.00. Leave blank if the customer should not receive a discount.
5
Determine the Status of the of the terms code. Choose from the following:
•
A (or blank) - The terms code is active.
•
I - The terms code is inactive. You can not enter a sales order for a customer if the payment terms is inactive.
6
Choose one of the following methods to determine the due date:
•
Enter the Due Date of each month when invoices are due.
•
Enter the Net Days Allowed, which is the number of days after the invoice date that payment is due. For example, if the invoice date is 5/10 and Net Days Allowed is 15, payment is due by 5/25.
•
Enter the Net Day-Of-Month, which is the day of the month when payment becomes past due. To use prox terms, enter a P in front of the number if payment is due the following month.
This field works with Cutoff Day. For example, if Net Day-Of-Month is 25 and Cutoff Day is 15, invoices dated before the 16th are due on the 25th of the month. If Net Day-Of-Month is P25 and Cutoff Day is 25, invoices dated before the 16th are due on the 25th of the next month. You can enter up to 3 dates and cutoff days. For more information, refer to Understanding Payment Terms.
7
If you entered a Terms Percent and the customer should receive a discount for paying early, choose one of the following:
•
For Discount Date, enter the date of each month when invoices with this terms code are eligible for an early pay discount.
•
For Disc. Days Allowed, enter the number of days past the invoice date after which the invoice is not eligible for a discount. For example, if an invoice is dated 5/25 and the Discount Days Allowed is 15, the customer must pay before 6/09 to receive the discount.
•
For Disc. Day-Of-Month, enter the day of the month after which discounts are no longer available for the invoice. Use prox terms if payment is due the following month.
This field works with Cutoff Day. For example, if Disc. Day-Of-Month is 25 and Cutoff Day is 15, invoices dated before the 16th are due on the 25th of the month to receive a discount. If Disc. Day-Of-Month is P25 and Cutoff Day is 25, invoices dated before the 16th are due on the 25th of the next month to receive a discount.
8
For Cash Type, indicate if the terms apply to cash sales:
•
Y - The sale is processed as a cash sale. The Sales Order Completion screen appears upon completion of the sales order. No discount is available for early payment.
•
N - The sale is not a cash sale.
9
For Credit Card Type, indicate if the terms apply to credit card sales:
•
Y - The sale is processed as a credit card sale. The Sales Order Completion screen appears upon completion of the sales order so you can enter credit card information.
•
N - The sale is not a credit card sale.
Refer to “Credit Cards” in the Order Processing documentation for more information.
10
For C.O.D. Type, indicate if cash upon delivery is required.
•
Y - The sales order requires cash upon delivery of the order. The Sales Order Completion screen appears upon completion of the sales order. The customer cannot pay on credit and they will not receive a discount for early payment.
•
N - The sales order does not require C.O.D.
11
If C.O.D. Type is set to Y, for Cash Equivalent, indicate if the customer must have a cash equivalent (e.g., money order or cashier’s check). Choose from the following:
-
Y - The customer must have a cash or a cash equivalent (e.g., money order or cashier’s check) at the time of delivery.
-
N - The customer can pay by other means than cash (e.g., personal check, credit card).
12
For Installments, indicate if the this terms code allows payments to be made using an installment plan. Choose from the following:
•
Y - Opens the A/ R Installment Terms screen to allow you to define a payment schedule for multiple invoices. A single invoice is created for each payment with a new calculated discount and due date for each invoice. Enter the following:
-
Number of Payments. The total number of payments for this plan.
-
Frequency - The frequency of payments (e.g., O - Monthly, Q - Quarterly, Y - Yearly).
-
Initial Payment% - The percent of the total payment due for the first payment.
-
Final Payment% - The percent of the total payment due for the final payment.
-
Periodic Payment % - The percent of the total payment required for each periodic payment. This value is automatically calculated after you enter the Initial Payment% and the Final Payment %.
•
N - The payment terms is not for installment payments.
13
For Pay In Advance, determine if payment is required in advance:
•
Y - This terms code requires the customer to pay in advance, even for backordered material.
•
N - No advance payment is required for this terms code.
14
For Lease Type, indicate if this terms code is for leased material.
•
Y - Opens the A/R Lease Terms screen to allow you to define a payment schedule for multiple invoices. A single invoice is created for each payment with a new calculated discount and due date for each invoice. Enter the following:
-
Number of Payments. The total number of payments for this lease plan.
-
Frequency - The frequency of payments (e.g., O - Monthly, Q - Quarterly, Y - Yearly).
-
Down Payment% - The percent of the total payment due for the down payment.
-
Final Payment% - The percent of the total payment due for the final payment.
-
Periodic Payment % - The percent of the total payment required for each periodic payment.
-
Depreciation% - The percent of the sale to calculate as depreciation.
•
N - This payment terms does not apply to lease orders.
15
For Deposit Required, determine if a deposit is required on sales orders with this terms code:
•
Y - A deposit is required for the entire amount when the order is entered. The deposit amount can be overridden on the order.
•
N - No deposit is required for this terms code.
16
For Credit Card Entry Mode, indicate if the cash application screen automatically appears when completing the sales order:
•
Y - When you enter a sales order for the customer, you are not required to enter the customer’s credit card information in the sales order header. You are automatically brought to the Cash Application screen when completing the order and the customer’s credit card information is automatically defaulted. The pick ticket prompt is suppressed.
•
N - If you enter a credit card order type order, you must fill in the credit card information in the sales order header.
Refer to the “Credit Cards” chapter in the Order Processing documentation.
If you want to enter more than one set of term percentages and discount dates, press F5-Multi Disc Levels to open the Multiple Discount Levels screen.
17
Press F2-Save to save the A/R Terms information.
