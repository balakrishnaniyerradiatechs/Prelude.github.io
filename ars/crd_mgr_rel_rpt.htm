Credit Manager Release Report
The Credit Manager Release Report displays all orders released from credit hold for a credit manager ID (refer to Releasing Orders From Credit Hold).
After you run the report, you can choose to purge the information so that it does not appear the next time you run the report. If the report is run using the scheduler, the data is automatically purged.
Credit Mgr Release in File Update Parameters must be set to Y to keep credit manager release information.
Menu Path: Financial > Accounts Receivable > Reports > Credit Manager Release
Selection Criteria
Use the following criteria to limit the information to print on the report:
•
Division. The highest possible grouping in your company (e.g., Eastern Division, Western Division).
•
Region. A subset of Division - if Division is used (e.g., Southeast Region, Northwest Region).
•
Department. A subset of warehouse. Typically used to separate expenses within a warehouse (e.g., accounting, sales, warehouse).
•
Cost Center. A subset of Department - if Department is used. Typically used to separate expenses within a warehouse or department (e.g., shipping, receiving, outside sales, inside sales).
•
Warehouse. The selling warehouse. The selling warehouse is the warehouse that receives credit for the sale, regardless of which warehouse ships the merchandise.
•
Credit Manager. The credit managers to include.
•
Release Date range. The beginning and ending credit hold release dates to include.
Column Descriptions
Column Heading
Description
Whse
The selling warehouse assigned to the sales order.
Customer
Line 1: The customer number.
Line 2: The customer description.
Order #
The sales order number.
Amt Released
The value of the order that was released.
Rel-Date
The date the sales order was released.
Time
The time at which the sales order was released.
Released By
The name of the employee that released the sales order from credit hold.
Rsn
The credit reason code that indicates why the order went on Credit Hold. Refer to the Credit Hold Reason Codes for an explanation of each code.
Credit Bal
The customer’s credit balance when this order went on credit hold. The credit balance is calculated as (Credit Limit - A/R balance - Ord Balance).
Cde
The Credit Code assigned to the customer in Customer Maintenance that determines if and when orders can be placed for the customer:
•
A - You must enter an Authorization Code in Sales Order Entry after you enter the customer number and before you enter the header information. Refer to Credit Authorization Code Maintenance for information on setting up authorization codes.
•
E or blank - Sales orders are subject to the customer’s Credit Limit. If the customer is over their credit limit, the pick ticket will not print until the order is released from credit hold. Refer to Excluding Sales Orders From the Credit Limit.
•
H - Sales orders can be entered but are always held until the credit manager releases them. You must enter the credit reason abbreviation code (established in Valid Response Maintenance) that explains why this customer’s orders always go on hold. If no code is entered, the code MAN (manual) is used.
•
N - No sales orders or quotes can be entered for this customer.
AH
The customer’s automatic credit review hold status:
•
Y - The customer is placed on or off credit hold by the Auto Credit Hold Procedure (refer to Automatically Placing Customers on Credit Hold). If an invoice is more than the number of days past due indicated in Auto Credit Days, and not flagged as disputed, then this flag is automatically changed to H (automatic credit hold). You can temporarily take the customer off hold by changing the H back to a Y until the Auto Credit Hold Procedure runs again. Other settings such as credit limits may affect this flag also.
•
H - The customer is on automatic credit hold.
•
N - The customer is not included in the Auto Credit Hold Procedure.
SaleDate
The last time an order was entered for this customer, as of the date this order went on credit hold.
PymtDate
The last time payment was applied to this customer’s account, as of the date the order went on credit hold.
