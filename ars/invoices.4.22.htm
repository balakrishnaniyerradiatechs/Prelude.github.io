Printing Statements
You can generate statements for one or more customers depending on the selection criteria.
In Prelude, the term Printing can also refer to faxing or e-mailing, depending on customer settings.
To print customer statements:
1
Select Financial > Accounts Receivable > Customer Statement Printing to open the Customer Statement Printing screen.
2
Enter the Selling Warehouse for which you want to print statements or leave blank to print statements for all warehouse.
3
Enter a Customer to print statements for a single customer, or leave blank to print statements for all customers.
4
For Statement Type, choose one of the following:
•
Y - Print open balance statements. Enter a Statement Date.
•
B - Print balance forward statements for customers who are set up to print balance forward statements. Enter a Statement Period (fiscal period).
Print Statement in Customer maintenance determines the type of statements to print for each customer (refer to Maintenance Options for Statements).
5
Document Option, is only accessible if you enter a customer. Indicate how statements will transmit to the customer:
•
B - Mail and fax.
•
E - Email.
•
M - Mail and email.
•
N - Mail.
•
Y - Fax.
6
For Printer Form Queue, enter the printer where the statements should print.
7
For Statement Message, if applicable, enter text to appear at the footer of each statement.
8
For Print Zero Balances, choose one of the following:
•
Yes - Include invoices with a zero dollar balance.
•
No - Do not include invoices with a zero dollar balance.
To include invoices with a zero-dollar balance, don’t purge the zero balance A/R records until after the invoices print. Refer to Purging Zero Balance A/R Records.
9
Press F2. The following message appears:
Load Statement Forms in Printer & Align Visually
10
Choose one of the following:
•
Print Statements - Prints the customer statements.
•
Mask Print Test - Tests the alignment of the statement forms in the printer. Not necessary for laser printers. After the forms are correctly aligned, select Print Statements.
•
Abort Printing - Cancels the print process.
If Statement Type is B, the following dialog box appears:
Process Balance Forward Customers?
Answering Yes Will Roll Balances And Move
Current Transactions To Prior Period!
11
Choose one of the following:
•
Yes - Moves current transactions to the previous fiscal period for balance forward customers.
•
No - Leaves the transactions in the current fiscal period.
Month-End Customer A/R Aging Save
(General Ledger > Processes > End of Month Closing)
Saves all customer A/R aging balances. You can run the A/R reports for a period after this process is completed. Customer statements (excluding Balance Forward) use this information to print statements. Balance forward statement history updates when the original statement prints. Run this process only at month-end and only once per fiscal period. Refer to Maintenance Options for Statements.
