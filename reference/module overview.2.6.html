Entering Products
You can set up your products with special requirements or stocking status information so that when you enter a product on a sales order, specific information is required or displayed. A product can be set up as one or more of the following:
•
Stock: Products that you keep in your warehouse and for which you can maintain replenishment levels.
•
Non-stock: Products that you only special order for a customer.
•
Consignment: Products removed from on-hand inventory and shipped or reserved for a customer but not invoiced until later.
•
Miscellaneous: If you need to enter a charge on a sales order that is not a stock product (e.g., gift certificate, charge for liability insurance, rebate credit, trade show billing), you can use a miscellaneous product number. Miscellaneous products can be identified as M (taxable if the order is taxable) or MN (non-taxable, even if the order is taxable). The disadvantage of a using a miscellaneous charge is that is it billing only, no cost is entered. Also, a miscellaneous product does not show up on product searches.
•
Upsell: A more expensive product or list of products that you may want to offer when a customer asks for an item. You can review the upsell products and replace the original product if the customer agrees to buy the more expensive product.
•
Substitute: Products that can be substituted for another product. You can choose to either update usage for the original product or the substitute product.
•
Alternate: Additional product numbers that can be used to access a product.
•
Product Complements: Helpful information that can help a salesperson sell a product.
•
Product Accessories: Additional products that can be sold with the another product. The salesperson is prompted to recommend the accessories to the customer.
•
Kit/Components: You can set up a kit parent that consists of a list of components. You can choose to sell the product as a kit or as individual components.
•
Rationed: Sales order rationing allows you to limit the quantity of a product or product line that a customer can buy within a designated number of days. Ration comments appear on the sales order when the ration quantity is exceeded.
•
Control: When you designate a product as a control product, the other products on the sales order will not ship until the control product is available to ship. You may want to use this if you sell a product that has accessories and the customer cannot use the accessories until the main product is available.
•
Prescription: If you sell products prescribed by doctors, you can set up a product as a prescription so that when a salesperson enters the product on a sales order, they must enter the prescription number. You can also set up and track the number of refills, expiration dates, and special instructions to include with each prescription.
•
Gasket: If you use the Gasket/Hose Subsystem, you can establish components for material and labor to use on sales orders and quotes, and you can set up the questions to ask when the customer purchases a hose product. When you enter the product on a sales order, you can assign components and options.
•
Base/Child: If you sell a product in multiple sizes, colors, lengths, or other options, you can set up one product as the base product and then create child products from all of the available configurations of the product. For example, if you sell a t-shirt that comes in many sizes and colors, the base product is the t-shirt and the child products are all of the size and color combinations available for sale.
