Using Letters of Credit
A commercial letter of credit is a contract between you and your bank to pay your vendor (typically an overseas vendor). At your request, the bank opens a letter of credit and promises to honor drawings on the letter of credit from your vendor(s). Essentially, the bank replaces you as the payee.
