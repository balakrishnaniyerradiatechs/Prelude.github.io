Searching for Products
When you search for a product from a transaction entry screen (e.g., Sales Order Entry, Transfer Entry, Purchase Order Entry) a Product Lookup screen or Product Tagging Entry screen appears based on the partial information entered to search for a part number. You can view the available quantity of each item and enter the quantity to add to your order.
 
Product Tagging Screen - Field and Function Key Descriptions
.
Field/Function Key
Description
Product
The product number.
Description
The description of the product.
Available
Depending on the type of product, one of the following displays:
•
If the product is a service item, SERVICE displays.
•
If the product is a service item with an SRT (standard repair time), the repair time displays. The SRT is set up in the Shop Repair Information screen in Product Maintenance.
•
If the product is a regular stock or nonstock product, the available quantity displays.
Stat
The inventory status of the product:
•
N - Non-stock item.
•
B - BOM parent.
•
K - Kit parent.
•
R - Rental item.
•
S - Stock item.
•
X - Obsolete item.
•
Null - No warehouse record exists.
Quantity
Allows you to enter the quantity of each item to place on your order.
F2-Save
Saves the quantities entered and places them on your order.
F5-Whse Avl
Opens the Product Warehouse Availability Inquiry screen to allow you to view the available quantity of the product in each warehouse.
F6-Inv-Inq
Opens the Inventory Inquiry screen to allow you to view detailed information for the on-hand, on-order, and committed quantities of the product.
F7-Sort
Allows you to resort the list according to Description or Product Number.
F8-Switch Mode
Allows you to switch between placing the cursor in Quantity or Product Number. The default is set in Default Parameters Maintenance in the Product Tagging Entry Mode field.
F9-Goto Line
Allows you to enter a product number or reference number to go directly to.
 
Product Tagging Flags
Product Tagging in Inventory Parameters determines how you can look up and enter part numbers when searching by keyword:
•
N - Product tagging is not activated. You must enter 1 item at a time on the order, then enter the quantity in the detail information screen.
•
Y - Activates the Product Tagging Entry screen for entry programs to allow you to enter quantities for multiple products to go directly on the order.
•
A - The same as Y, except that the Product Tagging Entry screen also displays for inquiries, but you can only select one product at time in the inquiries.
•
I - The Product Tagging Entry screen displays for both entry programs and inquiries, but you can only select one product at time.for both.
Availability Flag (Inventory Parameters > F5-Add Info) determines if the availability that displays in the Product Tagging Entry screen is based on the current warehouse or all warehouses combined.
•
W - Display availability and on-order quantities for the selected warehouse.
•
C - Display availability and on-order quantities for all warehouses.
Product Tagging Entry Mode in Default Parameters Maintenance determines where the cursor starts in the Product Tagging Entry screen for product entry programs:
•
P - Product. The cursor starts on the Product field and each time you press ENTER it goes back and forth between the Product and the Quantity.
•
Q - Quantity. The cursor starts on the Quantity field and each time you press ENTER, it goes to the next Quantity.
When in the Product Tagging screen, you can press F8-Switch Mode to change between the P and the Q options.
Product Tagging Default Sort in Default Parameters Maintenance determines the default sort sequence for viewing products in the Product Tagging Entry screen:
•
P - Display products in ascending order by product number.
•
D - Display products in ascending order by product description.
To use the Product Tagging Entry screen:
1
For each item to add to the order, enter a Quantity.
2
Press F2. The selected items appear on the order entry screen.
3
To view the detail information screen for a product, put your cursor on that product and press ENTER.
This example refers to Sales Order Entry, but product tagging works the same for other product entry programs (e.g., Purchase Order Entry, Transfer Entry, Vendor Claim Entry).
