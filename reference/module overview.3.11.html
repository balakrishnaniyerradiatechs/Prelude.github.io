Using Administrative Holds for Sales Orders
If you want management to review sales orders or transfers that do not meet gross profit, credit limit, or quantity requirements, you can set up system parameters to hold unacceptable orders. The orders must be released by management before pick tickets can print. The following types of holds are available:
•
Branch Hold: Controls sales orders and transfers to make sure that other branches can not deplete your inventory without your authorization. No pick ticket prints until it is released.
•
Administrative Order Hold: Allows you to hold orders that do not meet pricing requirements or other ordering restrictions defined by your company. No pick ticket or acknowledgement prints until it is released.
•
Administrative Invoice Hold: Allows you to prevent orders from being invoiced because of pricing changes, credit card violations, tax errors, or other ordering restrictions defined by your company.
•
Administrative Quote Hold: Allows you to prevent a quote from going to a customer if it does not meet pricing or other standards defined by your company.
•
Administrative Credit Hold: Allows you to prevent an order from printing if the customer is over their credit limit.
