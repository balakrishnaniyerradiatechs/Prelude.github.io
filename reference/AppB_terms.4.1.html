Appendix A - Glossary
accessory
Products that you can sell to go with another product. When you sell an item that has accessory products, you can view the list of accessories and select the ones you want to add to the order.
acknowledgement
After you enter a sales order, you can send an acknowledgement to the customer to let them know that the order was received and entered. The acknowledgement lists the line items on the sales order and a total for the sales order.
action bar
A menu bar at the top of some screens. If you use the character mode, press F10 to view action bar options. If you use the GUI mode, the action bar displays at the top of the screen.
admin invoice hold
Administrative invoice hold contains orders put on hold for pricing changes, credit card violations, tax errors or other ordering restrictions defined by your company. The invoice must be released by the Administrative Invoice Manager before an invoice can generate.
admin order hold
Administrative order hold control sales orders to make sure they meet company standards (e.g, pricing requirements, order size, freight information). The orders must be released by the Administrative Order Manager before the pick tickets will print.
aging period
Time periods used to keep track of customer’s past due payments.
alphanumeric fields
Fields in which you can enter both letters and numbers.
A/P
Accounts Payable
A/R
Account Receivable
available
Available inventory is stock that is ready to be committed to an order.
average weekly usage (AWU)
Purchase method that calculates recommended purchase quantities for stock products that have an extremely high sales volume for certain periods of the year.
AWU
See average weekly usage.
backorder
If a product is not available in a warehouse for picking, it is backordered. Backordered products may be replenished in your warehouse via transfers, purchase orders, or work orders.
batch printing
Many of the documents in Prelude can be printed, faxed, or e-mailed by batch or continuously. If a document is set to batch print, the documents wait in a queue until a process is run to print the documents. For example, if pick tickets are set to batch print, the Pick Ticket Printing process must be run for the pick tickets to print.
bill of lading
A document that lists all of the products in a shipment. A bill of lading is typically used by third-party carriers to identify any hazardous products.
bill of materials
A bill of materials (BOM) is a combination of products that are used to build a finished good using the Work Order module.
B/O
See backorder.
BOM
See bill of materials.
branch hold
Controls sales orders and transfers to make sure that other warehouses can not deplete your inventory without your authorization. A Branch Manager must release the hold before a pick ticket can print.
buyer
The employee responsible for replenishing stock and purchasing products from a vendor or vendor line.
character
Text based screens. Prelude screens can be viewed in either the character or the GUI mode.
charge back
An amount billed to a customer that is associated with an invoice (e.g., returned check fee).
class
Product classifications are based on the total value of a product that moves through inventory each month. Past sales and the cost of each stock item calculate a usage value. Classifications range from 1-13; class 1 is assigned to items with the greatest turns and value and class 13 is assigned to items with no sales (dead stock). New items are assigned class 0. You can set purchase parameters to determine which products are assigned to each class.
committed
Stock that is allocated to a sales order. Committed inventory is part of on-hand inventory, but is not part of available inventory.
complement
Complement information provides the salesperson with helpful information regarding a product. Once complements are set up for a product in Product Maintenance, any time you enter the product on a sales order, COMP displays on the Line Item Detail screen to let you know that complements exist for the product.
continuous printing
If a document is set to print, fax, or email continuously, as soon as all criteria are met, the document prints. A separate process does not have to be run as in batch printing. System settings for each print program determine if it prints, faxes, or emails continuously or by batch.
credit
An amount subtracted from a customer’s account (e.g., for returned goods, pricing adjustment).
credit hold
You can set credit hold options to control sales orders to make sure that a customer’s open or overdue balance does not get too high. The orders must be released by the credit manager before pick tickets print or acknowledgements are sent.
credit limit
Determines how high a customer’s open accounts receivable amount can be. When you enter a sales order for a customer, if the total value of the customer’s A/R balance and open sales orders (excluding long-term future orders) exceeds the credit limit, the order goes on credit hold.
credit memo
A document that gives credit to customers for returned items or pricing errors.
daily processing
Performs the same calculations and recommendations as Nightly Processing, except that you must specify each vendor and warehouse. All recommended transactions for the vendors listed are re-evaluated and regenerated to update the buyer queues. You may want to use Daily Processing to generate recommended purchase orders during the day, after you have corrected any errors generated from Nightly Processing (e.g., assigning primary vendors).
debit memo
A transaction that records an amount owed to you by your vendor.
direct ship
When a product is shipped directly from your vendor to your customer.
distribution requirements planning
DRP is used in the Advanced Forecasting module to help you to optimize forecasting for products that have extended lead times, scheduled deliveries from the vendor, or when a single purchase order is issued to cover an extended period of time.
DRP
See distribution requirements planning.
economic order quantity
A type of purchasing method that uses an order point and a line point to determine replenishment quantities.
EOQ
See economic order quantity.
field
An area on a screen that either displays or accepts information. Fields have labels or a title that identifies the type of information.
forecast
An estimation of future demand based on previous usage, seasonal demand, and trends.
function buttons
The F keys at the top of the keyboard that correspond to the buttons at the bottom of the screen.
general ledger
The Prelude general ledger allows you to post all financial transactions, balance bank records, track projects, and create financial reports.
G/L
See general ledger.
GRIM
General Report Interface Maintenance. Most reports and many processes in Prelude use this interface to allow you to edit the report or process parameters to save and use later.
GUI
Graphical User Interface. Prelude can be viewed in either character or GUI mode. GUI mode takes on the appearance of a windows-based screen. You navigate through the system using the mouse or keyboard. GUI mode also displays toolbars that are not available in character. Refer to Using the Toolbar.
inventory
The set of products or stock on-hand at a location.
invoice
A bill that lists the price of the items sold to the customer.
invoice number
The number the system assigns to identify an invoice.
kit
A list of components that make up a part number.
kit parent
A product number that represents multiple components that are sold together.
lead time
The time required to receive a product from the time it is requested.
line item
A product entered on a transaction/order.
line point
If the purchase method is EOQ and available stock falls below this quantity, the product is evaluated as part of a line buy if another product in the line is below its order point.
lost sale
A product that you did not sell, but could have sold if conditions had been right (e.g., enough quantity in stock, shorter delivery time). Products recorded as lost sales can update usage.
lot billing
Billing a sales order at the order level, not the line level. Line item prices do not display.
min/max
A method of replenishing stock. If the quantity of a product falls below the minimum quantity, an amount is purchased to bring it up to the
maximum quantity.
negative receiver
A receiver entered to correct an over-receipt of stock. For example, you record that you received 10 units of a product, but you only received 8. You must enter a negative receiver to deduct the two that were received in error.
Nightly Processing
Nightly Processing evaluates the replenishment status of products in each warehouse and generates recommendations to fill the replenishment requirements for the next business day (e.g., purchase orders, work orders, transfers) . The results appear in the buyers’ work queues and on supporting reports.
on-hand
The total quantity of a product in a warehouse, including quantities committed to sales orders or vendor claims but not yet shipped out of the warehouse.
order point
If the purchase method is EOQ (economic order quantity) and available stock falls below this quantity, the system recommends replenishment.
packing list
A document that ships with an order and lists each product on the order. The packing list can be used to receive items against.
password
A set of characters used to access a program, process, field, or to logon to the system. Passwords are used to help prevent unauthorized access to the system or an individual program.
payment terms
Used to define payment rules and discount dates and amounts for each customer/vendor. In Prelude, you can set up as many payment terms codes as necessary to accommodate any variation of due dates, payment dates, and payment methods.
physical inventory
A physical count of every product in a warehouse.
pick ticket
A document that lists items that are committed to an order and are available in the warehouse. These items will be taken from the shelves and either placed in a staging area or shipped to the customer. Multiple pick tickets may be generated for an order. See release.
P/O
See purchase order.
point of sale
Point of sale equipment (e.g, cash drawers, pole displays, receipt printers) in which counter employees can input sales, returns, and exchanges.
primary vendor
The vendor from which a product is usually purchased. Primary vendors are established for a product/warehouse combination. If a primary vendor is established, that vendor is used for recommended purchase orders created during Nightly Processing.
product class
See class.
product line
Every product must be associated with a product line. The product line determines freight allocation, seasonal demand, forecast formulas, and override G/L accounts. Product lines can also be used to create discounts in Price Matrix Maintenance.
purchase method
Identifies the purchasing method for a product:
-
EOQ - Economic Order Quantity.
-
CLS - Class.
-
AWU - Average Weekly Usage.
-
DRP - Distribution Requirements Planning.
-
DS - Days Supply.
purchase order
A transaction that lists products that you are purchasing from a vendor.
quantity break
The quantity of a product that must be bought to receive a quantity break price.
quantity break price
A price or cost based on the quantity sold. You can set up multiple quantity break prices so that the more a customer buys, the better the price.
quote
A transaction that lists products that a customer wants to purchase. Inventory is not committed to a quote. The quote lists prices that are valid until the quote’s expiration day. An entire quote or line items from a quote can be converted to a sales order.
receiver
A transaction that records stock that was received. You can create multiple receivers against a P/O, one for each time stock was received against a P/O. Each receiver is noted with a -<#>. For example, the first receiver for P/O 123456 is 123456-1. The second receiver is 123456-2.
record
An organized collection of information (e.g., for a vendor, customer, product, warehouse).
reference number
A number that identifies a transaction (e.g., invoice number, original document number).
release
A transaction that records what products were picked and shipped for an order. An order can have multiple releases, one for each time products were picked and shipped. Each release is noted with a -<#>. For example, the first release for order 123456 is 123456-1. The second release is 123456-2.
replenishment method
Identifies how the product will be replenished for a product/warehouse combination. Possible replenishment methods are:
•
P - Purchase order.
•
D - Direct.
•
T - Transfer.
•
M - Manufacture.
•
O - Outplant.
returned goods
See RGA.
review cycle
The frequency with which products in a vendor line are reviewed by Nightly Processing for replenishment.
RF
Radio frequency. Technology used (e.g., RF guns) with the warehousing module to expedite picking, packing, and shipping.
RGA
Return goods authorization which authorizes a customer to return merchandise. When you enter the RGA, inventory is not affected but the information is recorded. When the customer returns the merchandise, you can turn the RGA into a credit memo to credit the customer and adjust inventory.
safety stock
The quantity of a product that is kept on-hand to meet unanticipated changes in usage or lead time.
sales order
A transaction that records what products a customer ordered.
scheduler
Part of GRIM. The scheduler allows you to schedule a report or process to run daily, weekly, monthly, or annually. You can print, email, or fax the report.
security
A method of password protecting programs within Prelude and disallowing access to custom menus, so that employees can only access programs to which they are authorized.
slash command
A shortcut to access a process in Prelude. For example, to access Sales Order Entry, you can use the menu path Order Processing > Sales Order Entry, or you can type its slash command - /SOE.
S/O
See sales order.
statement
A document that lists open accounts receivable balances for a customer.
stock transfer
See transfer.
substitute
A product that can be sold in the place of another product.
terminal
An input/output device for a computer system. A keyboard and monitor, that which are connected to a host computer directly, through a cable link, or indirectly through a cable link to a terminal server.
terms
See payment terms.
toolbar
A row of icons at the top of the screen.
T/R
See transfer.
transaction
A record of an event (e.g., sales order, transfer, purchase order) in the system.
transfer
A transaction that records products that were shipped from one warehouse to another.
U/M
See unit of measure.
unit of measure
How a product’s quantity is tracked. A product may have different buy, stock, and price units of measure (e.g., each, per hundred, by case, by pallet, and by foot).
upsell
A more expensive product or list of products that you may want to offer when a customer asks for an item. When you enter the original product on a sales order, a dialog box appears to inform you that the product has up-sell products to offer. You can review the products and replace the original product if necessary.
usage
Monthly usage is the primary statistic used to calculate purchasing parameters for a product. To calculate parameters correctly, you must record a product’s usage in the correct warehouse, and for the correct product number.
user
A person who uses Prelude.
user ID
A unique set of characters used to identify a user when he/she logs onto the system. The user ID and password are entered on the login screen. Passwords and user IDs help prevent unauthorized access to the system.
vendor claim
A transaction that records products that are being sent back to the vendor or products that were scrapped for which you will receive credit or a replacement product.
vendor line
Used to group products to establish target information for purchasing requirements (e.g., minimum order, free freight, discounts). These targets are included in Nightly Processing calculations. Vendor lines are established for a product/warehouse/vendor combination.
version
The software release number (e.g., Prelude V19).
W/O
See work order.
warranty
Inventory for which the vendor will send you credit or a replacement. You can place stock in warranty inventory through a credit memo, inventory adjustment, or stock receipts rejection.
work order
A transaction that records a bill of material (BOM) that must be built to create a finished good.
