Entering Vendor Claims and Warranty Replacements
When you return inventory to a vendor, you must indicate a reason for return (e.g., overstocked, damaged, defective, incorrectly shipped). The reason for return determines how to adjust inventory and the general ledger.
The following vendor claim and warranty replacement procedures allow you to identify products to return to your vendors and to track the status of replacements and reimbursements:
1
Move the product into warranty inventory: If you have a product to return to the vendor that is defective, damaged, or incorrectly shipped, you can designate the product as warranty inventory using a tag number. The product moves from regular inventory into warranty inventory so that it cannot be sold. You can tag warranty inventory by:
•
Entering a credit memo to the customer.
•
Adjusting the quantity in Inventory Adjustment Entry.
•
Rejecting the item in Stock Receipts Entry.
2
Enter a vendor claim: Vendor Claim Entry identifies the vendor and the material to return (whether it is in warranty inventory or regular inventory). You can also reference the vendor RMA number, the vendor invoice number, and shipping information, if available.
3
Print the vendor claim: Vendor Claims Printing allows you to print, email, or fax the claim. You can also use the printed copy as a packing list to ship the material to the vendor.
4
Assign serial shipping container codes: Optional if your company prints SSCCs on barcode labels to track containers and contents.
5
Update the vendor claim: Vendor Claim Update Processing confirms the shipment and relieves the quantities from inventory.
6
Enter a warranty replacement: Warranty Replacement Entry receives the vendor’s replacement for the defective product and updates inventory and the general ledger.
