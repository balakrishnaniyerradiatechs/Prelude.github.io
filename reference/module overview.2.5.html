Identifying Sales Order Types
When you enter a sales order, you must enter a sales Order Type to define the shipping and backorder conditions for the order (e.g, delivery, counter, direct, ship complete). For each customer, you can set a default order type and any disallowed order types. You can also restrict employees by user ID to limit the types of sales orders they can enter.
The valid sales order types in Prelude are:
•
01 - Regular: A regular order prints a pick ticket if inventory is available, the order is not on hold, and the pick ticket is scheduled to print that day.
•
02 - Ship Complete: Material does not ship until all lines on the order are available.
•
03 - Tag and Hold: The material is held until the customer calls with shipping or other additional instructions. You can release only what the customer needs (e.g., customer is building a house and needs items in stages).
•
04 - Direct Ship: Material is shipped directly from the vendor to the customer. Each direct ship line item, regardless of its stock status, is backordered since it is shipped directly from the vendor and not from stock.
•
05 - Over the Counter: Creates a counter invoice when the customer purchases an item at your counter. As you enter each line item, the quantity available is allocated and confirmed as shipped. At the end of the order, you can either print a pick ticket or complete the order and print a counter invoice.
•
06 - Confirmation: The order is created and confirmed in one step. As you enter each line item, the quantity is picked and shipped. You can use this type to correct billings and for credit memos when material is returned. Other order types (e.g., 01, 02) become 06 when confirmed.
•
07 - Consignment: Material is removed from on-hand inventory and shipped but invoiced later. You can track inventory based on the type of consignment. When the pick ticket release is confirmed, a zero dollar invoice generates and the inventory moves from on-hand to consigned inventory. An 07 becomes an 08 order type when invoiced. The line items accumulate on the 08 order according to Accumulate on the 07 order.
•
08 - Confirmation Consignment: An 08 order type is automatically created when the 07 order is invoiced. The 08 is used to invoice/bill the customer when they purchase the consigned material or return it to stock.
•
09 - RGA: Returned goods authorization. Identifies products that a customer wants to return and gives authorization for the return. When you receive the material, you can change the order type from 09 to 06 to create a credit memo.
•
10 - Repair: Repair orders interface with the Work Order module to accumulate material, labor, and other costs associated with the repair. If you do not have the Work Order module, you can still choose one of the following repair options:
-
01 - Warranty: The vendor, distributor, and/or customer are charged all, part, or none of the charges depending on the product’s warranty policy.
-
02 - Time and Material: The customer is charged for material and labor expended on the repair order based upon standard pricing logic and parameters for the customer.
-
03 - Fixed Fee: The customer is charged a fixed amount, usually based on an estimate related to the condition of the repaired product, regardless of the materials and labor.
For a more complete shop and repair order type, you can use the Shop Repair module to interface with sales orders.
•
12 - Invoice When Complete: The customer is not billed for an order until all releases for the order are shipped.
•
13 - Ship Backorder Complete: Ships all available inventory for the first release and then changes the order type to an 02 so that the remaining items ship complete. The customer receives 2 shipments and 2 invoices. However, the following conditions must exist:
-
All remaining lines must be for a single warehouse.
-
The first release must be confirmed.
-
No line item can have a D (direct) supply code.
•
14 - Ship Line Item Complete: A line item is not shipped until the full quantity is available. Similar to an 01 order type, except that for each line item, Ship Line Item Comp in the Line Item Detail Additional Information screen is set to Y. If all of the material is available, a pick ticket is printed. If inventory is not available, the available inventory is allocated to the line item and a staging document prints if your staging flag is set to Y.
•
15 - Show: Used at trade shows and includes special prices. A show order is similar to the campaign system because it prompts for the show number, which is stored on the order. The orders taken at the show are sent back to the office to be entered.
•
16 - Order Projection: A projection of what the customer will order during the year. All products on the order are backordered and you must manually allocate them (i.e., you must manually enter a pick quantity).
•
17 - Manufacturer Rep: Tracks manufacturer’s rep commission (e.g., a vendor pays commission for orders shipped directly to the customer).
•
18 - Crib Replenishment: Allows you to maintain inventory that your customer holds at their site. Cribs are replenished by moving inventory from one warehouse to another without a stock transfer. The customer number must be established in the Crib Customer Number in Warehouse Maintenance.
