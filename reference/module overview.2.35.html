Setting up Products
Each product in your warehouse must exist in Product Warehouse Maintenance before you can enter it on sales orders, purchase orders, work orders, transfers, or other transactions. The following information is required for each product:
•
Products: You can set up a product and then use the same basic information to create the product in other warehouses while accommodating the unique characteristics of each warehouse and vendor. Every product interacts with all Prelude modules so that data (e.g., usage, pricing, cross reference information) is accurately updated.
•
Product Lines: Product lines group similar product for selling purposes (e.g., sales analysis, discounts, price matrices, product campaigns). They also allow you to set gross profit limits, freight allocation methods, and handling charges to assign to a group of products. Product lines only determine how you sell a product to your customers and do not affect vendor or purchasing information. Every product set in Product Maintenance or Quick Product Maintenance must have a product line assigned.
•
Product Purchasing Parameters: Nightly Processing uses the information in a product’s purchasing parameters to determine when the product should be replenished. End-of-month processing re-evaluates the purchase parameters for each product in each warehouse to determine if they should be adjusted to accommodate product usage. In some cases (e.g., new products), you may want to manually override the recommended purchase quantities and then freeze purchasing controls for a product, so that the purchasing parameters calculated by end-of-month processing are ignored.
You can also enter the date to remove the frozen controls, which is when usage begins to factor into the replenishment quantity recommended by Nightly Processing. If you want to thaw products before the set date, you can either thaw just one product at a time or remove frozen controls for all products up to the date you indicate.
•
Product Pricing: Prelude’s price/cost capabilities provide a flexible pricing structure to accommodate the unique pricing and costing requirements and pricing contracts for your company. You can maintain prices and costs manually or automatically at the product level, product warehouse level, product vendor level, or by product line or price group.
