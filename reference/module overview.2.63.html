Balancing the General Ledger
The integrity of your general ledger depends on:
•
When to Collect Data. The data that you collect from reports and inquiries is the most crucial part of balancing. To make sure that the data is accurate, schedule the reports to run at the same time every day, after posting to the general ledger and when no transactions that affect balancing might be entered (e.g., stock receipts, invoicing). If you run the reports or inquiries at different times of the day or when transactions are processed, you cannot get an accurate picture of the perpetual figures or the general ledger.
•
How Often to Balance. You must balance frequently so that inventory is accurately represented. Activant recommends that you balance daily until you are comfortable with figures, reports, and balances, then you can balance weekly. The more frequently you balance, the easier it is to research discrepancies.
You can automatically post transactions to the general ledger or use the G/L Balances Postings Routine to post transactions from each module to the general ledger. You can post one module at a time or all at the same time. All transactions that occurred since the last time the process ran are posted. You can use the scheduler to post at night and on a recurring basis so that balances are updated when no one is entering any transactions.
Depending on how you set up your general ledger and how you track inventory, you can use the Prelude reports and inquiries to help balance the general ledger.
