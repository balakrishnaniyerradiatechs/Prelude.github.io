Searching for Information
You can search for items (e.g., customers, prospects, vendors) by:
•
Pressing F3 and entering search criteria
•
Entering criteria in a field (for customers, vendors, and prospects) and pressing F3 or ENTER. Refer to Searching for Products for information on product searches.
 
When searching in a maintenance screen, use a period (.) before the criteria. For example, to find DALLAS, enter .DALLAS. If you do not use the period, Prelude assumes that you are entering a new record.
When you press F3, a search screen may appear (if you are on a date field, a calendar appears, refer to Entering Dates). The search screen typically has only one field, but may have more.
1
Choose from the following:
•
Keyword. For example, to search for a customer or vendor, use the standard keywords Description, City, State, phone number and/or ZIP. Your company may also use additional keywords. You can enter the whole keyword or just the first few letters. You can also combine keywords. For example, if you’re looking for a restaurant supply customer in Georgia, you could enter REST GA.
•
Description. For example, if you’re searching for a code for a salesperson and you know the salesperson’s name is Jane Smith, you can enter JANE.
2
Press ENTER to see all values included in the search.
 
You can only search for characters at the beginning of a word or code. For example, if you enter DRIVE, SCREWDRIVER does not appear. To find the keyword SCREWDRIVER, you have to enter text from the beginning of the word (e.g., SCR).

To select an item, scroll through the list, highlight the item you want, and press F2 or ENTER.
4
If you want to select multiple items (e.g., reports and some screens) you can use item tagging:
•
To tag items, hold CTRL and use your arrow keys to move up and down the list. Continue holding CTRL and press F5 or SPACE on each item you want to select.
•
To un-tag items, hold CTRL and use your arrow keys to move up and down the list. Without holding CTRL, press SHIFT+F5 or SHIFT+SPACE on each item you want to un-tag.
•
To tag all items, press F9.
•
To un-tag all items, press SHIFT+F9.
•
To tag only items that contain specific text, press F8. In the screen that appears, enter a text string that you want to select and press ENTER. Items with text that match that string are selected.
•
To un-tag only items that contain specific text, press SHIFT+F8. In the screen that appears, enter a text string that you want to search for and press ENTER. Items with text that match that string are un-tagged.
 
After you choose an item from a selection screen, you can look at the next and previous items on the list without repeating the search. Type NN to look at the next item or BB to look at the previous item. Use .NN and .BB on maintenance screens.
