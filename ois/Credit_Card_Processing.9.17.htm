Post-Authorizing Credit Card Transactions
At the end of the business day, you must post-authorize the credit card transactions in Protobase before you can close out the batch. This pre-authorization/post-authorization procedure is required by the law since transactions cannot be post-authorized until the merchandise ships. When an order is pre-authorized, the money is not actually billed to the customer's card, but the available-to-buy is adjusted. Once the batch is closed (refer to Closing a Credit Card Batch) the transactions become valid and the money is transferred to your bank.
Before you post-authorize credit card transactions, you must:
•
Generate Invoices: You must confirm and invoice all credit sales orders to set them up for post-authorization. Counter type (05) orders invoice upon completion of the order. Regular type (01) orders must be confirmed in Release Confirmation Entry to be released for invoicing. Any orders on Admin Invoice Hold must be authorized and released (refer to the Administrative Invoice Inquiry).
•
Run the Daily Credit Card Sales Register: The Daily Credit Card Sales Register lists all credit card transactions for the day, according to what your general ledger says you pre-authorized. The total on this report should match the combined total of the Credit Card Post Authorization Approval Report and the Credit Card Post Authorization Rejection Report that generate when you post-authorize the batch.
To post-authorize a credit card batch:
1
Select Order Processing > Processes > Credit Card Processes > Post Authorization to open the Credit Card Post Authorization screen.
2
Enter the Sell Warehouse for the post-authorization.
3
Enter the Transaction Date to post to the general ledger. If you post-authorize in the morning for the transactions from the previous day, enter yesterday’s date.
4
For Post all Branches?, choose from the following:
•
Y - If you have more than one merchant ID per branch, all branches post instead of just the one entered.
•
N - Just the branch indicated in Sell Warehouse will post.
5
Once you choose Execute, the following 2 reports generate:
•
Credit Card Post Authorization Approval Report. Lists post-authorized transactions that were approved.
•
Credit Card Post Authorization Rejection Report. Lists rejected transactions and the reason for rejection. You can still close the batch if items exist on the rejection report, as long as you know why it was rejected. To remove items from the rejection report, refer to Removing Rejected Credit Card Transactions.
Once you identify all the rejections , follow the procedures for Closing a Credit Card Batch.
