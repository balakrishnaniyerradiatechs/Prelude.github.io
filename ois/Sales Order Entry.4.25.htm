Entering Rationed Products
Sales order rationing allows you to limit the quantity of a product or product line that a customer can buy within a designated number of days. For example, if the customer’s ration quantity of an item is 100 units and the ration days for that item is 30, the customer can purchase up to 100 units of that item within the rolling 30-day time frame. Ration comments appear on the sales order when the ration quantity is exceeded.
Ration types are set up in Rationing Maintenance (refer to “Customer Ration Types” in the Inventory documentation) and assigned to customers in the Customer Maintenance Order Information Screen.
The Customer Rationing Inquiry displays rationed products and quantities for a customer.
Option Parameters
Sales Order Rationing in Order Processing Parameters > F5-Add Info must be set to Y to use Rationing Maintenance.
When you enter a rationed product on a sales order, if the customer has not exceeded their ration quantity for the product, a comment line appears after the product to let you know that the product is rationed.
Once the customer has exceeded their ration quantity, you cannot enter the product on a sales order; instead, a comment line appears with any ration comments to explain why the product cannot be sold to this customer (refer to “Setting up Ration Comments” in the Inventory documentation).
