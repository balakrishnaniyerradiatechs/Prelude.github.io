Creating Credit Reason Codes
When a customer returns material and you issue credit, inventory and the general ledger are updated depending on the reason the material is returned. Credit reason codes define why the customer is returning material, how inventory is updated, and the appropriate general ledger account to post to.
Related Reports
Description
Monthly Credit Reason Report
Total credit memos issued for each product line for a month, sorted by credit reason code.
Historical Percentage Credit Reason Report
Total amount of credit issued to customers for each month.
To create credit reason codes:
1
Select Order Processing > Maintenance > Misc Code Files > Credit Reason Codes to open the Credit Reason Maintenance screen.
2
Enter a 1 or 2 digit Credit Reason Code for credit memos and RGAs to identify why a product is returned. Refer to the following examples:
•
01 - Shipped wrong product.
•
02 - Defective product. Refer to Creating Warranty Tags for Returned Products.
•
03 - Policy credit.
•
04 - Customer did not need.
•
05 - Pricing error.
•
CE - Core exchange credit.
•
EQ - Equipment trade-in credit.
3
Enter a Product Line if you want to set up a general ledger account just for this credit reason/product line combination. Leave blank to use the general ledger account assigned to the product line or to use a general ledger table.
4
Enter the Description of the credit reason.
The Account Number Entry screen appears.
You can also access this screen by pressing F5-G/L Accounts.
5
Enter the Account# information for the appropriate G/L Table Headings. These G/L accounts override the G/L accounts from Customer Maintenance, Product Maintenance, Product Line Maintenance, and General Ledger Table Maintenance.
6
To determine the percent to charge for restocking fees for this credit reason code, choose one of the following:
•
For Restock Charge Percent, enter the percent to deduct from the credit as a restocking charge per line item.
•
Press F7-Restock to open the Restocking Fee Ranges screen. You can enter multiple restocking charges depending on the number of days since the invoice date. For example, you can have a 5 percent restock charge for a product returned less than 30 days since the invoice date, a 10 percent restock charge for a product returned 30 to 60 days since the invoice date, etc.
This percent entered in either screen overrides a restocking percent at the company level, but you can override it on a credit or RGA.
Restocking Charge Flags
Restock Charge % (Company Maintenance) determines the default restocking charge to add to returned products.
Soe Restock Charge Method (Order Processing Parameters > F5 > F5). Determines if restocking charges are added to returned goods:
•
S (or blank) - Only add restocking charges to material returned to stock.
•
A - Add restocking charges to all types of returned goods.
Restocking Charge (Customer Maintenance > F7 > F5) determines if a restocking charge is calculated as each line item is entered on a credit memo:
•
Y - Calculate a restocking charge for each line item when credit is issued.
•
N - Do not calculate a restocking charges for each line when credit is issued.
7
For Default Inventory Type, identify the type of inventory that represents returned products for credit reason code. Choose from the following:
•
D - Damaged.
•
R - Return to stock.
•
S - Scrapped.
•
W - Warranty/scrapped.
If this field is blank, then the inventory type defaults to the type for the product’s primary vendor. If no inventory type exists for the primary vendor, then it defaults to the Default Quantity Type For Credits in Additional Option Parameters. If that is blank then the default is Return to Stock.
8
For Update Credit Rsn Stats, determine if credit reason statistics are updated when this reason code is used. Choose from the following:
•
Y - Update credit reason statistics.
•
N - Do update credit reason statistics.
9
For Update Customer Stats, determine if customer statistics (YTD Returns) are updated when a credit memo with this credit reason code is invoiced. Choose from the following:
•
Y - Update customer statistics to appear on the Monthly Credit Reason Report.
•
N - Do not update customer statistics.
10
For Update Product Usage, determine if product usage is updated when a credit memo with this credit reason code is invoiced. Choose from the following:
•
Y - Update product usage (e.g., out of stock).
•
N - Do not update product usage (e.g., mistake).
11
For Exclude from Quality Reporting?, determine if the credits using this credit reason code are excluded on the Customer On-Time Shipments/Quality Report when the credit memo is invoiced. Choose from the following:
•
Y - Exclude credits using this credit reason code. You may want to choose this option so that returns are not factored in to on-time shipment calculations.
•
N - Include the credit reason code.
12
For Equipment Trade In, determine if the trade-in serial number information is required on credit memos that use this credit reason code. Choose from the following:
•
Y - Require equipment trade-in information to update the make, model, price, and cost for the new serial number.
•
N - Do not require trade-in information.
13
For Update Picker Stats, if you use the Warehousing Module, determine if pick error information is updated for the Picker Statistics report when a credit memo with this credit reason code is invoiced:
•
Y - Update pick error information (e.g., shipping error).
•
N - Do not update pick error information (e.g., customer mistake).
F5-G/L Accounts: Allows you to update general ledger account information for the credit reason code.
F6-Disposition Inst: Allows you to enter instructions for disposing of the material for this credit reason code.
14
Press F2-Save to save the credit reason code information.
