Consignment Review Date Report
The Consignment Review Date Report lists type 08 consignment sales orders that are past the consignment review date. Consignment orders contain products that are on loan to the customer. If the customer sells the product, then the customer is invoiced for the products sold. If the customer does not sell the products, they may be returned to stock. Refer to Using Consignment Sales Orders for more information.
Menu Path: Order Processing > Reports > Daily Order Detail Reports > Consignment Reports > Consignment Review Date Report
Selection Criteria
Use the following criteria to limit the information to print on the report:
•
Division. The highest possible grouping in your company (e.g., Eastern Division, Western Division).
•
Region. A subset of Division - if Division is used (e.g., Southeast Region, Northwestern Region).
•
Department. A subset of warehouse. Typically used to separate expenses within a warehouse (e.g., accounting, sales, warehouse).
•
Cost Center. A subset of Department - if Department is used. Typically used to separate expenses within a warehouse or department (e.g., shipping, receiving, outside sales, inside sales).
•
Warehouse. The selling warehouse. The selling warehouse is the warehouse that receives credit for the sale, regardless of which warehouse ships the merchandise.
•
Customer. The customer holding the consigned merchandise.
•
Consignment Review Date <. The date by which the consignment orders should be reviewed, as entered in Review Date in the sales order header for 08 consignment type orders. Refer to Using Consignment Sales Orders.
Column Descriptions
Column Heading
Description
Cust#/Name
Line 1: The customer number.
Line 2: The customer description.
Slsm
The sales representative assigned to the sales order.
Rev-Date
The date the consignment order should be reviewed, as entered in Review Date in the sales order header for 08 consignment type orders.
Order#
The sales order number.
#
The number of times the order went through the invoice cycle.
P
Indicates the pick ticket print status:
•
S - The order was suspended and no pick ticket printed.
•
P - The product is available and a pick ticket will print during the next print cycle or when the date to print is reached.
•
Blank - The product is not yet available so the pick ticket is not ready to print.
I
Indicates the order’s invoice status:
•
Y - The order is flagged for invoicing.
•
H - The order is on hold and cannot be invoiced until released. Refer to the Administrative Invoice Inquiry.
•
Blank - The order is not yet flagged for invoicing.
Product Number
The consigned product number.
Description
The description of the product.
C
Indicates if the line item is part of a kit:
•
K - Kit parent.
•
C - Kit component.
•
Blank - The item is not part of a kit.
S
Line 1: The line item’s supply code if the product is on backorder and varies depending on the product’s replenishment path:
•
B - Purchase order.
•
E - Emergency purchase order.
•
D - Direct ship purchase order.
•
R - Emergency stock transfer.
•
T - Stock transfer.
•
W - Work order.
Line 2: The linked document number, if the product is linked to a purchase order, transfer, or work order.
Qty-Opn
The quantity of the item on the sales order.
U/M
The unit of measure of the item as entered on the sales order.
Price/Ext.
Line 1: The sales price of the product.
Line 2: The extended value of the total quantity of the product (Qty-Opn x Price).
