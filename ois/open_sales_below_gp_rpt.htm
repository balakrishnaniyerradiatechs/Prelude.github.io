Gross Profit Exception Report
The Gross Profit Exception Report lists orders that are below or above a specified gross profit amount. The selection criteria allows you to determine whether to use the minimum gross profit settings from Order Processing Parameters, the product line gross profit level settings, or your own minimum or maximum gross profit requirements. The report excludes direct orders, consignment orders, and credit memos. Once an order appears on the report, it will not appear again.
Menu Path: Order Processing > Reports > Daily Order Detail Reports > Gross Profit Exception Report
Selection Criteria
Use the following criteria to limit the information to print on the report:
•
Division. The highest possible grouping in your company (e.g., Eastern Division, Western Division).
•
Region. A subset of Division - if Division is used (e.g., Southeast Region, Northwest Region).
•
Department. A subset of warehouse. Typically used to separate expenses within a warehouse (e.g., accounting, sales, warehouse).
•
Cost Center. A subset of Department - if Department is used. Typically used to separate expenses within a warehouse or department (e.g., shipping, receiving, outside sales, inside sales).
•
Selling Warehouse. The warehouse that receives credit for the sale, regardless of which warehouse ships the merchandise.
•
Customer. The customers for which you want to view gross profit exceptions.
•
Order Type. The sales order types to include on the report. Choose one or more of the following:
-
01 - Regular.
-
02 - Ship complete.
-
03 - Tag & hold.
-
04 - Direct ship.
-
05 - Counter invoice.
-
06 - Confirmation.
-
07 - Regular consignment.
-
08 - Confirmation consignment.
-
09 - Return goods.
-
10 - Repair orders.
-
12 - Invoice when complete.
-
13 - Ship backorder complete.
-
14 - Ship line item complete.
-
15 - Show order.
-
16 - Order projection.
-
17 - Manufacturer’s representative.
-
18 - Crib replenishment.
•
Use Product line G/P Min/Max. Indicates if you want to use the gross profit levels set up by product line, to determine which orders to include on the report:
-
N - Use the S/O-G/P Percent and Min S/O-G/P Dollars set in Order Processing Parameters.
-
Y - Use the gross profit levels for the product line (Product Line Maintenance > Gross Profit Test? = Y). You can override the minimum and maximum gross profit levels in the following selection criteria.
•
Override Minimum G/P%. The override to the minimum gross profit percent allowed for the product line as set up in Product Line Maintenance.
•
Override Maximum G/P%. The override to the maximum gross profit percent for the product line as set up in Product Line Maintenance.
•
Is G/P Range Inclusive?. If you are using the minimum and maximum gross profit percentage limits by product line, indicate if you want to include all products between those 2 parameters:
-
Y - Only include orders that have a gross profit percent and gross profit dollars between the minimum and maximum G/P values.
-
N - Only include orders with a gross profit percent and gross profit dollars above the maximum value or below the minimum value.
Column Descriptions
Column Heading
Description
Cust#/Name
Line 1: The customer number.
Line 2: The customer description.
Slsm
The sales representative assigned to the customer.
Ord-Date
The date the order was entered.
Order #
The sales order number.
#
The number of times the order went through the invoice cycle.
P
Indicates the pick ticket print status:
•
S - The order was suspended and no pick ticket printed.
•
P - The product is available and a pick ticket will print during the next print cycle or when the date to print is reached.
•
Blank - The pick ticket is not ready to print.
I
Indicates the order’s invoice status:
•
Y - The order is flagged for invoicing.
•
H - The order is on hold and cannot be invoiced until released. Refer to the Administrative Invoice Inquiry.
•
Blank - The order is not yet flagged for invoicing.
Product Number/ Description
Line 1: The product number.
Line 2: The description of the product.
O
Indicates if the product is obsolete:
•
Y - The product is obsolete.
•
Blank - The product is not obsolete.
C
Indicates if the line item is part of a kit:
•
K - Kit parent.
•
C - Kit component.
•
Blank - Not part of a kit.
S
Line 1: The line item’s supply code if the product is on backorder:
•
B - Purchase order.
•
E - Emergency purchase order.
•
D - Direct ship purchase order.
•
R - Emergency stock transfer.
•
T - Stock transfer.
•
W - Work order.
Line 2: The linked document number, if the product is linked to a purchase order, transfer, or work order.
Qty-Opn
The quantity ordered.
U/M
The unit of measure of the product as entered on the sales order.
Price/Ext.
Line 1: The unit price of the product.
Line 2: The extended price of the total quantity of the product.
Gross Profit
The gross profit dollar amount earned for this order.
GP Perc
The gross profit percentage earned for this product.
