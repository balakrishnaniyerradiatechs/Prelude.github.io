Creating a Price Matrix
You can use price matrices to give discounts and rebates for customers for a group of products for a period of time. You can set up a price matrix as:
•
A quantity break table.
•
A net price.
•
A discount off a column price.
•
A multiplier on a cost.
You can also use price matrices to cost a purchase order or assign vendor information. Refer to:
•
Defining Purchasing Cost.
•
Overriding Commission Cost.
•
Assigning Vendor Information to a Contract.
To use a price matrix to track customer contract information, refer to Contract Pricing.
To set up a price matrix:
1
Select Order Processing > Maintenance > Pricing Structures > Price Matrix Maintenance > Price Matrix Maintenance to open the Price Matrix Maintenance screen.
Modify an Existing Matrix
If you want to view or modify an existing matrix, press F5-Existing Matrix to open the Price Matrix Selection screen. Use the selection criteria to find a matrix or leave any of the fields blank to find multiple matrices. Once you select a matrix, the data automatically appears in the Price Matrix Input screen.
2
For Matrix Type, enter a valid matrix type set up in Price Matrix Types & Sequence Maintenance.
3
For Warehouse, enter the selling location if you want this price matrix to apply to only one warehouse. Leave blank if the price matrix is not warehouse specific.
4
For Drop Ship, indicate if the matrix applies to drop ship orders:
•
Y - The matrix applies to drop ship orders only. You must create a separate matrix for orders shipped from stock.
•
N - The matrix applies to both stock and direct orders.
This field is only accessible if the Drop Ship Price Matrix Order Processing Parameter is set to Y.
5
For Job#/Customer P/O#, enter a job or a customer P/O if this pricing is limited to just one job or purchase order. Leave blank if you want the price matrix to apply to all orders for the customer. The system looks for a customer P/O number first, then for a job number.
If you have Multiple Currency turned on, the Currency Indicator screen appears.
6
Enter the Currency type for this price matrix.
The next screen that appears depends on the Matrix Type you chose. For example, if you select Matrix Type 1 - Customer/Product, the Customer/Product Matrix screen appears.
7
In this case, enter the Customer and Product that the matrix applies to.
For all matrix types, after you enter the required information, the Price Matrix Input screen appears to allow you to define pricing, dates, and quantity information.
If you are creating a price matrix for matrix types 50, 51, 52, and 55, the Rental Price Matrix screen appears that allows you to enter a discount percent or a specific rate for a customer and rental product or product line.
For each Ln#, enter a set of pricing information for a date range. Since you can enter multiple lines for a matrix, enter each line in chronological order. Enter as many lines as necessary.
8
For Beg-Date, enter the date that the special pricing is to start. The beginning date for the first line defaults to the system date. All remaining lines default to the day after the previous line’s expiration date. For example, if the previous line has an expiration date of 06/30/06 then the next line’s beginning date defaults to 07/01/06.
9
For Exp-Date, enter the date that the special pricing is to end. Leave blank if there is no expiration date. If the previous line does not have an expiration date then the next line is not available.
10
For MinQty and MaxQty, if applicable, enter the minimum and/or maximum quantities that must be purchased to receive the special pricing.
11
If you enter a MaxQty, enter a quantity type in QT to determine how to price an order when a customer buys less than the MaxQty. Choose from the following quantity types to determine when to use the price matrix:
•
M - If the quantity ordered is less than the remaining quantity (MaxQty - quantity purchased) then the price matrix is used.
•
P - If the quantity ordered is less than the remaining quantity (MaxQty - quantity purchased) for a customer/product combination, then the price matrix is used.
•
T - If the quantity ordered is less than the remaining quantity (MaxQty - MinQty) for each sales order, then the price matrix is used.
If the remaining quantity is zero, the price matrix is skipped. If the quantity ordered is greater than the remaining quantity, a message notifies the salesperson that an available quantity remains and the line item should be split for pricing consideration. The price matrix is skipped.
12
For Ov, indicate if the price can be overridden on a transaction:
•
Y - The price can be overridden.
•
N - The price cannot be overridden.
•
H - The price can be increased but not decreased below gross price.
13
For Ct, indicate if this price is based on a contract:
•
Y - The price is based on a contract with a customer, group of customers, product, customer part number, product line, vendor, product price group, or major group. The system checks for a contract price first and then stops when it finds one, regardless of whether or not it is the best price. If you enter Y for Ct, you also have access to the following:
-
For U/M, enter the unit of measure to use with the contract price.
-
Enter the Contract Number for this customer contract price.
-
Press F7-Contract to open the Contract Price Information screen. Refer to Contract Pricing for more information on setting up and tracking contract prices for a customer.
•
N - The price is not from a contract.
14
For SR, indicate if Sum & Reprice applies to products affected by this price matrix:
•
Y - If multiple products on a sales order have the same matrix type, they are combined for a better quantity break discount. If the customer has Best Price turned off, then the first matrix it finds is used to calculate the customer's price. If the customer has Best Price turned on, then all available matrices are evaluated to determine the lowest price.
•
N - Sum & Reprice does not apply for this price matrix.
Refer to Sum & Reprice and Quantity Break Discounts.
Depending on whether you are setting up a matrix to price sales orders or to calculate a rebate, refer to the following:
•
Calculating Prices.
•
Calculating Rebates.
