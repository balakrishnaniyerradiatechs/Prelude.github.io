Customer Orders On Credit Hold Report
The Customer Orders on Credit Hold report lists sales orders on credit hold. A customer’s order will go on credit hold when the total of the customer’s open sales orders and accounts receivable balance is more than the Credit Limit amount set in the A/R Information Screen (Customer Maintenance > F6-A/R) for the customer.
You must be set up as a Credit Manager in User Identification Maintenance to be able to release an order from Credit Release Inquiry.
Menu Path: Order Processing > Reports > Daily Order Detail Reports > Customer Orders On Credit Hold
Selection Criteria
Use the following criteria to limit the information to print on the report:
•
Division. The highest possible grouping in your company (e.g., Eastern Division, Western Division).
•
Region. A subset of Division - if Division is used (e.g., Southeast Region, Northwest Region).
•
Department. A subset of warehouse. Typically used to separate expenses within a warehouse (e.g., accounting, sales, warehouse).
•
Cost Center. A subset of Department - if Department is used. Typically used to separate expenses within a warehouse or department (e.g., shipping, receiving, outside sales, inside sales).
•
Selling Warehouse. The warehouse that receives credit for the sale, regardless of which warehouse ships the merchandise.
•
Order Date. The date the order was entered.
•
Customer. The customer with orders on credit hold.
•
Quantity Ship Only?. Indicates if you want to include only orders with a shipped quantity (e.g., confirmation type orders or counter sales that already shipped). Choose from the following:
-
Y - Include only orders with a quantity shipped.
-
N - Include all orders.
Column Descriptions
Column Heading
Description
Cust #
The customer number.
Customer Name
The customer description.
Order #
The sales order on hold.
I
Indicates the order’s credit hold status:
•
Y - The order is flagged for invoicing.
•
H - The order is on hold and cannot be invoiced until released.
•
Blank - The order is not yet flagged for invoicing.
Sell
The selling warehouse.
Cust-P/O#
The customer purchase order number, if applicable.
Ord-Date
The date the order was entered.
Req-Date
The date the customer requires the merchandise.
#
The number of times the order has gone through the invoice cycle.
Order-Total
The total sales value of the order.
Order-Cost
The total cost of the sales order.
Gross-Prof
The total gross profit amount on the invoice.
GP%
The gross profit percentage on the invoice.
