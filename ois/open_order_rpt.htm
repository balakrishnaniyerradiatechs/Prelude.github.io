Open Sales Order Detail Report
The Open Sales Order Detail Report displays customer and product information for sales orders that are not yet invoiced.
Menu Path: Order Processing > Reports > Daily Order Detail Reports > Open Sales Order Detail
Selection Criteria
Use the following criteria to limit the information to print on the report:
•
Division. The highest possible grouping in your company (e.g., Eastern Division, Western Division).
•
Region. A subset of Division - if Division is used (e.g., Southeast Region, Northwest Region).
•
Department. A subset of warehouse. Typically used to separate expenses within a warehouse (e.g., accounting, sales, warehouse).
•
Cost Center. A subset of Department - if Department is used. Typically used to separate expenses within a warehouse or department (e.g., shipping, receiving, outside sales, inside sales).
•
Warehouse. The selling warehouse that receives credit for the sale, regardless of which warehouse ships the merchandise.
•
Customer. The customers for which you want to view open sales orders.
•
Salesrep. The numbers of the sales representatives to include.
•
Written By. The user ID of the person who entered the orders.
•
Order Type. The sales order types to include on the report. Choose one or more of the following:
-
01 - Regular.
-
02 - Ship complete.
-
03 - Tag & hold.
-
04 - Direct ship.
-
05 - Counter invoice.
-
06 - Confirmation.
-
07 - Regular consignment.
-
08 - Confirmation consignment.
-
09 - Return goods.
-
10 - Repair orders.
-
12 - Invoice when complete.
-
13 - Ship backorder complete.
-
14 - Ship line item complete.
-
15 - Show order.
-
16 - Order projection.
-
17 - Manufacturer’s representative.
-
18 - Crib replenishment.
•
Required Date Range. The beginning and ending Required Date range, at the order or line level, if you want to limit the number of orders to view.
•
Sort By Cust, Slsrep, WB, Date. The sort order for the report. Choose from the following to sort by:
-
C - Customer number.
-
S - Sales representative number.
-
W - Written-by user ID.
-
D - Order date.
•
Page Break On Salesrep/WB. Determines if the report should start a new page for each sales representative or written-by user ID:
-
Y - Start a new page for each sales representative number or written-by user ID, depending on which sort option you chose.
-
N - Do not start a new page for each sales representative or written by user ID.
Column Descriptions
Column Heading
Description
Cust#
Line 1: The number for the customer with open sales orders.
Line 2: The customer description.
OT
The sales order type:
•
01 - Regular.
•
02 - Ship complete.
•
03 - Tag & hold.
•
04 - Direct ship.
•
05 - Counter invoice.
•
06 - Confirmation.
•
07 - Regular consignment.
•
08 - Confirmation consignment.
•
09 - Return goods.
•
10 - Repair orders.
•
12 - Invoice when complete.
•
13 - Ship backorder complete.
•
14 - Ship line item complete.
•
15 - Show order.
•
16 - Order projection.
•
17 - Manufacturer’s representative.
•
18 - Crib replenishment.
Refer to Sales Order Types for a detailed explanation of each order type.
Slsr
The number for the sales representative assigned to the sales order.
Ord-Date
The date the customer ordered the material.
Order#
The sales order number.
I
Indicates the order’s invoice status:
•
Y - The order is flagged for invoicing.
•
H - The order is on hold and cannot be invoiced until released. Refer to the Administrative Invoice Inquiry.
•
Blank - The order is not yet flagged for invoicing.
Product
The product on the open sales order.
Written-by (unlabeled field)
The user ID of the person who entered the sales order.
Description
Line 1: The product description.
Line 2: The second line of description for the product.
Req-Date
The date the product is required by the customer. The asterisk (*) indicates that the line is past its delivery date.
C
Indicates if the line item is part of a kit:
•
K - Kit parent.
•
C - Kit component.
S
Line 1: The line item’s supply code (if one appears on the sales order):
•
B - Purchase order.
•
E - Emergency purchase order.
•
D - Direct ship purchase order.
•
R - Emergency stock transfer.
•
T - Stock transfer.
•
W - Work order.
Line 2: The linked document number, if the product is linked to a purchase order, transfer, or work order.
Qty-Opn
The quantity on the sales order not yet shipped to the customer.
U/M
The unit of measure of the product as entered on the sales order.
Price/Ext.
Line 1: The price of the product for the unit of measure.
Line 2: The extended price of the open quantity of the product (Qty-Opn x Price).
