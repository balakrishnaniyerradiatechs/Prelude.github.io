Using Single Sampling Tables
The American National Standards (ANSI) publishes AQL (acceptable quality level) tables that determine acceptable levels of products that must pass inspection. The number of products to inspect for each lot of products is determined by the Sample Size Code Letter (refer to Setting up Inspection Levels for Sample Sizes).
Generally, you would use the Normal Inspection Table to determine acceptance levels. If 2 out 5 consecutive lots do not pass inspection, you can then switch to the Tightened Inspection Table so that the products are more closely inspected. The Reduced Inspection Table is put into effect when the at least 10 lots have passed Normal Inspection without any failures.
