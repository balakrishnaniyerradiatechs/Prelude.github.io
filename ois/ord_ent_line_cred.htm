Line Item Credit Detail screen
The Line Item Credit Detail screen appears when you enter a product in RGA Entry or Credit Memo Entry. You can enter the quantity, price and return status information for a product being returned by a customer.
Menu Path: Order Processing > Optional Entry and Printing > RGA Entry or Credit Memo Entry
Field and Function Key Descriptions
Field/Function Key
Description
Ln#
The line number.
Quantity
The quantity to return. Enter a negative number to give credit.
U/M
The unit of measure for the product.
Whse
The warehouse the product is being returned to.
A
Indicates if all the remaining fields on the screen are correct:
•
Y - The remaining fields are correct. Closes the screen.
•
N - The remaining fields are not correct. Allows you to edit any of the remaining fields.
QtyStk
The quantity to return to stock.
QtyWty
The quantity to put into warranty inventory if the material is defective and you need to return it to the vendor for credit. Refer to Creating Warranty Tags for Returned Products.
QtyScr
The quantity to scrap or throw away.
QtyDmg
The quantity to move to damaged inventory. Only use this if the material is damaged and you cannot return it to the vendor but still want to track it.
Gross Price
The price of the product before any discounts.
Disc%
The discount amount this customer receives for this item.
Net Price
The end price for the product (Gross Price x Disc%). This information comes from the pricing assigned to the customer (refer to Product Pricing).
Src
The source code for the price. Refer to Price Source Codes for definitions of the source codes.
Tx?
Indicates if the product is taxable:
•
Y - The product is taxable.
•
N - The product is tax exempt. A customer’s tax exempt status is set up in the Customer Maintenance Customer Sales Tax Entry Screen or the Customer Ship-To Maintenance Ship-To Tax Information Screen.
Extension
The total amount of credit due to the customer (Quantity x Net Price).
Cost
The purchase cost of the product.
Gp%
The gross profit percentage lost due to the return of this product.
Profit
The dollar amount of profit lost due to the return of the product.
Available
The quantity of the product available for sale in this warehouse.
Onorder
The quantity of the product on order for this warehouse.
Stock Status
The stocking status of the product:
•
S - Stock.
•
N - Nonstock.
•
C - Consignment.
Cross Reference & Related Product Information
(unlabeled field at the bottom left corner of the screen)
Some products may display one or more of the following cross reference and related product information codes:
•
ALT - Alternate products exist for this product.
•
SUB - Substitute products exist for this product.
•
ACC - Accessory products can potentially be sold with this product.
•
COMP - Complement information exists for this product. Complements provide the sales person with helpful information when selling the product.
•
DESC - An additional extended product description exists for the product.
•
KIT - This product is a kit parent item. The number displayed is the number of kits you can make with the available components.
•
BOM - This product is a bill of material. The number displayed is the number of BOMs you can make with available components.
•
EQV - Equivalent products exists for product. Equivalent products are part numbers that do not exist in the system, but may in the future. Once the equivalent product becomes a valid product in the system, it becomes a substitute rather than an equivalent.
•
RSVD - The quantity in parentheses is reserved for a customer.
F2-Save
Saves the information and closes the screen.
F5-Add Info
Opens the Additional Information screen to allow you to enter additional customer and vendor information for the product.
F6-Substitutes
Opens the Product Substitutes Inquiry screen to display any substitutes available for the product.
F9-Inv Inq
Opens the Inventory Inquiry screen to display detailed stock status information for the product.
