Cost Override Audit Report
The Cost Override Audit Report lists all sales orders on which a cost was manually changed.
Menu Path: Order Processing > Reports > Daily Order Detail Reports > Cost Override Audit
Selection Criteria
Use the following criteria to limit the information to print on the report:
•
Division. The highest possible grouping in your company (e.g., Eastern Division, Western Division).
•
Region. A subset of Division - if Division is used (e.g., Southeast Region, Northwest Region).
•
Department. A subset of warehouse. Typically used to separate expenses within a warehouse (e.g., accounting, sales, warehouse).
•
Cost Center. A subset of Department - if Department is used. Typically used to separate expenses within a warehouse or department (e.g., shipping, receiving, outside sales, inside sales).
•
Warehouse. The selling warehouse. The selling warehouse is the warehouse that receives credit for the sale, regardless of which warehouse ships the merchandise.
•
Override Cost Date <. All cost overrides before this date will appear on the report.
•
Customer. The customers to include.
•
Product Line. The product lines to include.
Column Descriptions
Column Heading
Description
Customer
Line 1: The customer number.
Line 2: The customer name.
Order #
The sales order number containing the cost override.
Rep1
The Salesrep #1 assigned to the sales order.
Product Number
Line 1: The product number.
Line 2: The product description.
C
Indicates if the line item is part of a kit:
•
K - Kit parent.
•
C - Kit component.
Sc
Line 1: The line item’s supply code if the product is on backorder and varies depending on the product’s replenishment path and product settings. Commonly used options are: :
•
B - Purchase order.
•
E - Emergency purchase order.
•
D - Direct ship purchase order.
•
R - Emergency stock transfer.
•
T - Stock transfer.
•
W - Work order.
Line 2: The linked document number, if the product is linked to a purchase order, transfer, or work order.
Pline
The product line number.
Opn-Qty
The quantity ordered on the sales order.
U/M
The unit of measure of the product as entered on the sales order.
Orig-Cost
The original cost of the product on the sales order.
OV Date
The date the cost was overridden.
User
The user ID of the person that over wrote the cost.
Price/Ext.
Line 1: The unit price of the product on the order.
Line 2: The extended price of the total quantity of the product on the order (Price x Opn Qty).
Cost/Ext.
Line 1: The new cost after the cost override.
Line 2: The extended cost after the cost override (Cost x Opn-Qty).
Gp%
Line 1: The gross profit percentage earned after the override.
Line 2: The currency indicator, if you use multiple currency.
