Pricing Hierarchy
Once you set up special pricing for customers or products, the price that appears on each sales order depends on how you have your system set up to locate the correct price. Generally, the following hierarchy is used to price sales orders:
•
1st level: Negotiated contracts. Refer to Contract Pricing.
•
2nd level: Price matrices. Refer to Price Matrices.
•
3rd level: Sum and reprice and quantity breaks. Refer to Sum & Reprice and Quantity Break Discounts.
•
4th level: Column prices. Refer to “Pricing” in the Inventory documentation.
Marketing campaigns or pricing promotions may also be used depending on company procedures.
Use Best Price
If you want to always use the lowest price to price a customer’s orders, you can set best price flags at both the system level and the customer level. The setting at the customer level overrides the setting at the system level.
Order Processing Parameters > Utilize Best Price:
•
Y - Use the lowest price for the customer, including contracts, price matrices, quantity discounts, or column pricing. You can set Best Price in Customer Maintenance to N to override the system flag if a customer should not receive the best price.
•
N - Follow the pricing hierarchy. You can determine which sequence the system follows for the matrix hierarchy in the Matrix Types and Sequences Maintenance program (refer to Setting System Options for Price Matrices). The system defaults to a hierarchy of 1,2,3,4,5, etc., unless you change the sequence. You can set Best Price in Customer Maintenance to Y to override the system flag if a customer should receive the best price.
Order Processing Parameters > F5 > F5 > F5 > Utilize Best Contract Price.
•
Y - Use the best contract price.
•
N - Use the first contract price found.
Customer Maintenance > F7-Order > F5-Add Info > Best Price:
•
Y - All price column, matrix prices, and special prices are evaluated to calculate the lowest price possible for this customer.
•
N - The pricing hierarchy is used to determine a price.
