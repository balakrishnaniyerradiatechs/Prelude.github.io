Creating a Customer Price Contract
You can either create a customer contract within Price Matrix Maintenance (refer to Creating a Price Matrix) or use Contract Price Maintenance to automatically create a type 1 Customer/Product price matrix. Either program also allows you to compare the estimated ship quantity against to the actual ship quantity for a customer/ship-to/product combination.
To create a customer price contract:
1
Select Order Processing > Maintenance > Pricing Structures > Contract Price Maintenance > Contract Price Maintenance to open the Contract Price Maintenance screen.
To access an existing record, just enter the Customer, Contract # and Product. The remaining fields automatically fill in when the record is found.
2
Enter the Customer to assign the contract to.
3
Enter the Contract # to assign to the customer. Press F3 to see the customerâ€™s contracts, expiration dates, and products.
4
Enter the Start Date for when the prices take effect. This value becomes the Beg-Date in the Price Matrix Maintenance screen.
5
Enter the Expiration Date of the contract. This value becomes the Exp Date in the Price Matrix Maintenance screen.
6
Enter the Product covered by the contract. This value is assigned to Product in the Price Matrix Maintenance screen.
You can enter multiple products for a contract number but only one date range per product per contract.
7
If applicable, enter the Customer Part #. If you enter a customer part number that does not exist (refer to Setting Up Customer Product Numbers), you can create a new one.
8
For Est Qty Shipped, enter the estimated quantity that you think you might ship to the customer. This field is for information only and allows you to judge the effectiveness of the contract once the Act Qty Shipped is updated by orders confirmed as shipped to the customer.
9
If applicable, enter the customerâ€™s Purchase Order # associated with the contract. This field is for information only and does not update the price matrix.
10
If you are converting data from a previous system, you can enter a Line Number or any other value to use as a cross-reference for old contract information. If you enter a value, the Customer Part Number and Customer Part Number X-Ref files are updated.
11
For Minimum Qty, enter the minimum quantity the customer must buy on a sales order line item to receive the contract price. Leave blank if no minimum quantity is required. This value becomes the MinQty in the Price Matrix Maintenance screen.
12
For Maximum Qty, enter maximum quantity that the customer can buy to receive the contract price. Leave blank if no maximum quantity is required. This value becomes the MaxQty in the Price Matrix Maintenance screen.
13
Enter the contract Price for the product if the price matrix uses the $ method. Otherwise, leave it blank. If you enter a price, it becomes the Price in the Price Matrix Maintenance screen.
14
Enter the Unit of Measure for the product on this contract. This value becomes the U/M in the Price Matrix Maintenance screen. You cannot change the unit of measure once the price matrix record is created.
