Entering Stock Products
To enter stock products on the Sales Order Entry screen:
1
For Product, choose one of the following methods to enter products:
•
Enter the exact part number. The Line Item Detail screen appears. Go to step 2.
•
Enter a partial number, product description, customer part number, or other keyword to search for products and press ENTER or F3 to open the Product Tagging Entry screen. A list of all products with matching keywords appears:
-
Enter a Quantity for each Product to add to the order.
-
Press F2-Save to return to the Sales Order Entry screen. The products are added to the order.
Product Tagging
The Product Tagging flag in Option Parameters > Inventory Parameters determines how you can look up and enter part numbers when searching by keyword:
•
N - Product tagging is not activated. You must enter 1 item at a time on the order, then enter the quantity in the detail information screen.
•
Y - Activates the Product Tagging Entry screen for entry programs to allow you to enter quantities for multiple products to go directly on the order.
•
A - The same as Y, except that the Product Tagging Entry screen also displays for inquiries, but in the inquiry mode. The inquiry mode displays the available and on-order, but you can only select one product at time.
•
I - The Product Tagging Entry screen displays in the inquiry mode for both entry programs and inquiries. The inquiry mode displays the available and on-order, but you can only select one product at time.
If you entered an exact product number or if you move the cursor to one of the line items added by the Product Tagging Entry screen and press ENTER, the Line Item Detail screen appears to allow you to view or edit detail price and availability information for the product.
2
Enter or verify the Quantity, the U/M (selling unit of measure), and the Whse (shipping warehouse).
3
If you need to change any other data for the line item, enter N for Accept and edit the following fields as necessary:
•
Qty-Pck. The quantity available to pick.
•
Qty-B/O. The quantity backordered. Refer to the following for information on backorders:
-
To link a backorder for a stock product to an existing purchase order, refer to Sourcing a Backorder to an Existing Purchase Order.
-
To create a purchase order for a backordered product, refer to Linking a Purchase Order to a Backorder.
•
Sc. A supply code appears if the product is on backorder and varies depending on the product’s replenishment path and product settings. Valid supply codes vary by company. Commonly used options are:
-
B - Purchase order. Refer to Linking a Purchase Order to a Backorder.
-
E - Emergency purchase order. Refer to Linking a Purchase Order to a Backorder.
-
D - Direct ship purchase order. Refer to Entering a Direct Ship Order.
-
R - Emergency stock transfer. Refer to Linking a Transfer to a Backorder.
-
T - Stock transfer. Refer to Linking a Transfer to a Backorder
-
W - Work order.
You cannot change a B supply code to a D, since the B code is defined in a price matrix record. If the purchase order cost is removed, you can then change the supply code.
•
Gross Price, Disc%, or Net Price. This comes from the pricing assigned to the customer (refer to Product Pricing). If you change any one of these fields, the other two fields update accordingly. If you enter a price, discount, or gross profit that exceeds the limits set for the product line or company, you may receive a warning message and/or the order may go on Administrative Order Hold. Refer to the Administrative Order Inquiry.
Overriding Prices
Price Override Code in User ID Maintenance > F6-Add Info determines if you can override a price on a sales order:
•
A - You can change the price, even below cost.
•
C - You can change the price, but not below cost.
•
G - You can change the price, but if the gross profit falls below the minimum for the product line, the order goes on Administrative Order Hold.
•
N - You cannot access any price fields.
•
R - You can raise the price above the customer’s default price, but if you lower it below their default price, the order goes on Administrative Order Hold.
The Price Override Audit Report displays all overridden prices.
•
Src. The source code for the price. Refer to Price Source Codes for definitions of the source codes.
•
Tx. Indicates if the product is taxable:
-
Y - The product is taxable.
-
N - The product is tax exempt. A customer’s tax exempt status is set up in the Customer Maintenance Customer Sales Tax Entry Screen or the Customer Ship-To Maintenance Ship-To Tax Information Screen.
•
Cost. Based on the general ledger cost used by your company (e.g., replacement, average, standard, FIFO, LIFO).
•
Gp%, and Profit. Depending on your user ID, you may or may not be able to change the gross profit percent or gross profit amount that you want to earn. If you change one, the other updates accordingly. Changing these fields also changes the Disc% and the Net Price.
•
Req-Date and Pck-Date. You can override the required date and/or the pick ticket date from the Sales Order Header screen for this line only.
•
Promised. The date that you promised that the product would be delivered to the customer. This is for information only and can be used for custom reporting.
•
Under the inventory information (Available, Onorder, Stock Status), a list of options may appear for the product. Valid options are:
-
ALT - Alternate product numbers exist (e.g., UPC, UCC).
-
ACC - Accessory products are available. They appear after you press F2-Save on the Line Item Detail screen. Refer to Adding Accessory Products to an Order.
-
BOM<#> - The product is a bill of material parent. The number indicates how many finished goods can be made from existing stock.
-
COMP - Product complements exists. They appear after you press F2-Save on the Line Item Detail screen. Refer to Viewing Product Complements for more information.
-
DESC - An additional extended product description exists for the product.
-
EQV - Equivalent products exists for product. Equivalent products are part numbers that do not exist in the system but may in the future. Once the equivalent product becomes a valid product in the system, it becomes a substitute rather than an equivalent.
-
KIT<#> - The product is a kit parent. The number indicates how many kits can be assembled from existing stock. If the kit is flagged as an exploded kit, components can be broken into separate line items in the order. Refer to Entering Kit Parent Products for more information on kits.
-
PROMO - The product has promotional pricing.
-
RSVD - The quantity in parentheses is reserved for a customer.
-
SUB - Substitutes are available. To view the substitutes, press F6-Subs. The Product Substitutes Inquiry screen appears. Press F5 to substitute the highlighted product. Press F2-Save to close this window without using a substitute.
4
When the line item information is correct, press F2-Save to return to the Sales Order Entry screen.
Auto Order Split screen
If quantities of a backordered products exist in another warehouse, the Auto Order Split screen may appear depending on system settings. Refer to Shipping Split Quantities out of Other Warehouses for more information.
5
Repeat steps 1- 4 to add more stock items to the sales order.
6
Once all line items are entered, press F2-Save to complete the order.
Refer to Finishing the Sales Order for information on finishing the sales order and printing pick tickets.
