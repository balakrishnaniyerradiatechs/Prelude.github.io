Printing Summary Invoices
You can use Summary Invoicing to group invoices by P/O for a customer and then print them all on one invoice every day, month, or week. As sales orders go through Invoice Printing (refer to Printing Invoices), they are processed and held until the customer’s invoice print cycle comes up and then printed as one invoice for each P/O number. The summary invoice contains line item detail from each order including credits, tax, freight, and other charges.
The summary invoice number assigned is based on the first invoice number created for the customer in Invoice Printing. A cross-reference is kept for each summary invoice to reference the customer’s invoices.
Customer Maintenance F7-Order > F5-Add Info
Invoice Print Cycle. Determines how often invoices print for this customer:
•
D - Daily.
•
M - Monthly.
•
S - Semi-monthly.
•
W - Weekly.
Summary Invoices. Determines if a summary invoice prints for a customer:
•
Blank - Regular invoicing.
•
Y - Print summary invoices on a cycle basis.
•
R - Print summary invoices off-cycle for the customer. The summary invoice is printed on demand rather than a scheduled time such as daily, weekly, or monthly.
To print summary invoices:
1
Select Order Processing > Invoice Preview and Printing > Summary Invoice Printing to open the Summary Invoice Printing screen.
2
Enter the Customer Number or leave blank to print summary invoices for all customers set up for summary invoicing (Summary Invoices set to Y or R in Customer Maintenance). A separate summary invoice generates for each customer.
3
Enter the Warehouse number to limit the summary invoice printing to customers assigned to this selling warehouse in Customer Maintenance.
4
Enter the Printer Form Queue where the invoices should print.
5
For Print Source, indicate which types of invoices to include:
•
I - Print invoices only.
•
C - Print credit memos only.
•
B - Print both invoices and credit memos.
•
M - Mail duplicates of counter invoices to customers whose Mail Invoices flag is set to Y in the Customer Maintenance Additional Order Information Screen.
6
If necessary, enter an Invoice Message to print at the bottom of the summary invoices.
7
Once you press ENTER. The following message appears:
Load Invoice Forms In Printer & Align Visually
8
Choose one of the following:
•
Print Invoices - Print the invoices.
•
Mask Print Test - To test the alignment of the invoice forms in the printer. After the forms are correctly aligned, select Print Invoices.
•
Abort Printing - Cancel the invoice print process.
9
After you select Print Invoices, the following message appears:
Invoice Printing Complete....
10
Choose one of the following:
•
Process - The invoices printed successfully.
•
Reprint All - None of the invoices printed successfully. This option is useful if only a couple of invoices printed successfully from a large job. The invoices are re-printed and the message appears again.
•
Single Reprint - To re-print invoices individually. This option is useful if a couple of invoices jammed in the printer. Enter the sales order number for the invoice you want to re-print.
•
Abort - Cancel all invoice updating.
11
If you select Process, the Process? screen appears.
12
Choose from the following:
•
Enter Y and press ENTER if the invoices printed successfully. Updates the Order Log Inquiry and moves the order to the Sales Order History Inquiry.
•
Enter N and press ENTER if the invoices did not print successfully. Return to step 10.
