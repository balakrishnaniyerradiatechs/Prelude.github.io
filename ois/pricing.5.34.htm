Creating a Blanket Contract
You can set up a blanket contract to designate special pricing, shipping, and freight information for a group of products for a customer.
To set up a blanket contract:
1
Select Order Processing > Maintenance > Pricing Structures > Blanket Contract Maintenance > Blanket Contract Maintenance to open the Blanket Contract Maintenance screen.
2
Enter the Customer number.
3
Enter the Blanket Contract# that references the special pricing and terms.
The Header Information screen appears.
4
Enter a Description of the contract to display on reports and screens when the contract number is entered.
5
Enter the Start Date and the Expiration Date for the contract.
6
For Warning Days, enter the number of days before the contract expiration date that you want a warning to display when sales orders are entered using this contract. Leave blank if you do not want a warning message to appear.
7
Enter the negotiated Contract Amount. When this value is met for the customer, a warning message displays. Leave blank if you do not want a warning message to appear.
Administrative Order Hold for Blanket Contract Amount
If you want to put a sales order on Admin Order Hold if the customer exceeds their blanket P/O amount, enter an AOH Type of B (Blanket P/O Amount Exceeded) in the Administrative Order Parameters screen (Order Processing Parameters > F5-Add Info > Administrative Order Hold = Y). Refer to System Level Administrative Order Hold Options.
8
Enter the default A/R Terms Code, Ship Via Code, and Freight Terms to use for the customer when using this contract. These codes override the codes in Customer Maintenance.
Custom Purchasing
The following fields are available for custom purchasing:
•
Supplying Warehouse designates which warehouse will fill the order. This warehouse will use the Beginning On Hand (BOH) / Normal Order Quantity (NOQ) values for purchasing.
•
Auth. BOH/NOQ Mnt, determines if the Beginning On Hand / Normal Order Quantity values are maintained in a custom maintenance program used for reports. For Auth. Add. Products, determine if additional products can be added to a sales order if they do not exist on the blanket contract.
9
For Auth. Add. Products, determine if additional products can be added to a sales order if they do not exist on the blanket contract. Choose from the following:
•
Y - Allow additional products on a sales order that are not on the blanket contract.
•
N - Do not allow additional products on a sales order that are not on the blanket contract.
10
For Auth. Price Ov, determine if the sales price for the products can be overridden on a sales order:
•
Y - Allow the sales price for the product to be overridden on the sales order.
•
N - Do not allow the sales price for the product to be overridden on the sales order.
11
For Accept Backorder, determine if the customer accepts backorders when purchasing the products on the blanket contract. Choose from the following:
•
Y - The customer accepts backorders.
•
N - The customer does not accept backorders.
Adding Sub Accounts
If the customer is set up as a master account (Master Account Flag set to Y in the Customer Maintenance A/R Information Screen), You can add sub-account customers to the contract by using the Blanket Contract Customers screen (press F5-Add Cust from the Header Information screen). Any customer that you enter here must be set up as a sub-account of the customer you are creating the contract for (enter the Master Account in the Customer Maintenance A/R Information Screen).
12
For Product, enter the first part number to add to the blanket contract. The Blanket Contract Detail screen appears.
13
For Customer Product, if applicable, enter the customer’s part number that they use to identify your part number (refer to Setting Up Customer Product Numbers for information on setting up customer part numbers). If the customer part number does not already exist, it is automatically created and cross referenced. Leave blank if you do not use customer part numbers.
Cln# (contract line number) is only applicable when you are first going live on the system and you need to cross reference old contract information. You may enter any value that can be used as a cross-reference for the contract.
14
For the Price section, determine how you want to assign a price for the product. Choose from the following:
•
If you want to enter a set price for a product:
-
For Mtd, enter $.
-
Enter the Price.
•
If you want to calculate the price as a percent of a price or a multiplier over a cost:
-
For Mtd, enter *.
-
For Basis, enter the price or cost column to use to calculate the price.
-
For Pct, enter the percent to apply to the value in Basis to calculate the price for each line item. This value is a percent off a price if a price column was entered in Basis or a percent over a cost if a cost column was entered in Basis.
•
If you want to add a discount to the product (in addition to the discount set up for the customer in Add. Discount% in the Customer Maintenance Additional Order Information Screen):
-
For Mtd, enter A.
-
For Pct, enter the percent to add to the customer’s discount. The system calculates the discount as a chain discount.
Rebates
For the Rebate section, follow the same procedures as in step 14 to set up rebate prices for the customer.
Custom Reports
The following fields are commodity management fields for custom reporting:
•
BOH Min - The beginning on-hand minimum quantity required by the customer.
•
BOH Beg - The on-hand balance of the product for customer at the time the contract was created.
•
NOQ - The customer’s normal order quantity.
•
Target Price - The product’s target price for the item.
•
Use Code - Usage code for the product.
•
Type - The type code for the product.
15
Press F2-Save to complete the contract pricing for the product.
16
Repeat steps 12-15 for each product to add to the contract. The Blanket Contract Maintenance screen displays a summary of the pricing.
Products can have multiple lines for different pricing.
17
Once all the line items are complete, press F2-Save to save the blanket contract information.
Contract Pricing on Sales Orders
To use blanket contract prices on a customer’s order, you must enter the Blanket Contract# in Customer P/O # in the Sales Order Header. Any item that you enter on the order that is on the blanket contract, sources from the blanket contract for pricing. You can then keep track of the customer’s purchases against the blanket P/O (refer to Tracking Blanket P/O Information for a Customer).
