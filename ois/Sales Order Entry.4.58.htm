Entering a Consignment Order
To enter a consignment order
1
In the Sales Order Header screen (refer to Entering Sales Order Header Information) enter an Order Type of 07.
The Consignment Types screen appears.
2
For Consign Type, choose from the following (the first 4 types are for information only):
•
01 - Salesperson sample.
•
02 - Display.
•
03 - Consigned to customer.
•
04 - Reserved for customer.
•
05 - Invoice when complete. Prints a pick ticket and ships material when available. In this case, Accumulate is automatically set to Y.
3
For Accumulate, determine if all type 07 orders are accumulated into one type 08 order when the order is confirmed.
•
Y - Accumulate orders by customer. All consignment sales orders invoiced for a customer are accumulated on one order. Each time a product is added to the consignment order, it is added as a new line on the confirmed sales order.
•
C - Accumulate orders by product. All consignment sales orders invoiced for a product are accumulated on one line on a new consignment order if the selling warehouse and price for the item are the same.
•
A - Always accumulate by product number. Works the same as C except that all consignment sales orders invoiced for a product are accumulated on one line on a new consignment order using the first price found.
•
N - Do not accumulate orders. Each order is confirmed separately.
•
E - Expense (see your systems administrator to use this option).
4
Enter the Review Date to indicate when the order should be reviewed for consignment items on loan to the customer. The date is used for the Consignment Review Date Report.
5
Press F2-Save to continue with the order header information.
After you enter the order (refer to Entering a Sales Order) and print the pick ticket, the material is physically moved to the consigned location. Depending on what Type of consignment is created, the location could be the salesperson, display area, customer's location, or a reserved area for this customer.
After the order is confirmed and invoiced it turns into an 08 order type. However, the customer is not billed until the material is actually sold. Refer to Billing Consignment Orders.
