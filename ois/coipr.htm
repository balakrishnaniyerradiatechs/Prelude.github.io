Printing Consolidated Invoices
If you want to pay multiple invoices using one invoice number in Cash Receipts Entry, you can use Consolidated Invoicing. You can choose to print the invoices daily, monthly, or weekly, depending on their consolidated billing cycle. If a customer is set up to use consolidated invoicing, their sales orders are still processed through Invoice Printing (refer to Printing Invoices) and assigned an invoice number, but no invoice generates. You can then run Consolidated Invoice Printing to combine all the held orders and print one invoice. The consolidated invoice contains line item detail information from each of the orders including credit line items. Tax, freight, and other charges are summarized from the individual orders.
A separate consolidated invoice number is assigned when the consolidated invoice prints. You can use this number in Cash Receipts Entry to auto pay consolidated invoices. All consolidated invoices are paid, oldest first, until the auto pay process runs out of invoices to apply to or out of funds to apply. Refer to the Accounts Receivable documentation for more information.
Customer Maintenance F7-Order > F5-Add Info
Invoice Print Cycle. Determines how often invoices print for this customer:
•
D - Daily.
•
M - Monthly.
•
S - Semi-monthly.
•
W - Weekly.
Consolidate Invoices. Indicates the billing cycle for consolidated invoices:
•
D - Daily.
•
W - Weekly.
•
M - Monthly.
•
Blank - Consolidated invoicing is not used for this customer.
This value must match the value in Consolidated Invoice Printing to print invoices in the batch.
Consolidate Type. The types of invoices to print:
•
B - Both sales and service invoices.
•
N - Neither sales or service invoices.
•
S - Sales invoices only.
•
V - Service invoices only.
Invoice Print Cycle in Customer Maintenance determines when a customer’s sales orders are invoiced. Consolidate Invoices determines when a customer’s consolidated invoice prints. Therefore, a customer’s orders may be processed through Invoice Printing daily but may only print a consolidated invoice weekly.
To print consolidated invoices:
1
Select Order Processing > Invoice Preview and Printing > Consolidated Invoice Printing to open the Consolidated Invoice Print screen.
2
For Consolidated Billing Cycle, enter the billing cycle to include in this consolidated invoice print run. Choose from the following:
•
D - Daily.
•
W - Weekly
•
M - Monthly.
Only invoices for customers whose Consolidate Invoices flag matches this flag will print.
3
Enter the Warehouse so that only customers who are assigned this selling warehouse in Customer Maintenance are included.
4
Enter the Printer Form Queue where the documents should print.
5
Once you press ENTER. The following message appears:
Load Invoice Forms In Printer & Align Visually
6
Choose one of the following:
•
Print Invoices - Prints the invoices.
•
Mask Print Test - Tests the alignment of the invoice forms in the printer. After the forms are correctly aligned, select Print Invoices.
•
Abort Printing - Cancels the invoice print process.
After you select Print Invoices, the following message appears:
Invoice Printing Complete....
7
Choose one of the following:
•
Process - The invoices printed successfully.
•
Reprint All - None of the invoices printed successfully. This option is also useful if only a couple of invoices printed successfully from a large print job. The system re-prints the invoices and this message appears again.
•
Single Reprint - To re-print invoices individually. This option is useful if a couple of invoices jammed in the printer. Enter the sales order number for the invoice you want to re-print.
•
Abort - Cancel all invoice updating.
8
If you select Process, the Process? screen appears.
9
Choose from the following:
•
Enter Y and press ENTER if the invoices printed successfully.
•
Enter N and press ENTER if the invoices did not print successfully.
Re-print a Consolidated Invoice
To re-print a consolidated invoice, select Order Processing > Invoice Preview and Printing > Consolidated Invoice Reprinting.
