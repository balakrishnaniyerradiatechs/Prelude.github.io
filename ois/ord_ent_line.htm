Line Item Detail screen
The Line Item Detail screen allows you to view detail price and availability information for a product on a sales order. Depending on your user ID, you may be able to change the price and/or cost information.
Menu Path: Order Processing > Sales Order Entry > Line Item Detail
Field and Function Key Descriptions
Field/Function Key
Description
Ln#
The line number.
Quantity
The quantity ordered by the customer.
U/M
The unit of measure for the product.
Whse
The shipping warehouse the product.
Accept
Indicates if all the remaining fields on the screen are correct:
•
Y - The remaining fields are correct.
•
N - The remaining fields are not correct.
•
C - Allows you to override the salesrep that will receive commission for this line item. Refer to the Commission Subsystem documentation for more information.
•
S - Allows you to change cost for service items only.
Future Commit
(unlabeled field)
Indicates if the line item has a future pick ticket date, regardless of product availability.
•
P - The Pck Date is set to a number of days that is greater than the number of days set in Maximum LDT Commit Days in Order Processing Parameters > F5 > F5. This means that inventory will not be allocated to the line item until the number of days set in Maximum LDT Commit Days is reached.
•
F - The Pck Date is set to a number of days that is greater than the number of days set in Fut Commit Safety Days in Order Processing Parameters (the number of days to add to lead time from Product Vendor Maintenance to calculate when inventory on a future order should be committed).
If you set Commit Override to Y in the Sales Order Header > F5-Add info > Action Bar > Future Commit screen, inventory is committed to the order regardless of any values Order Processing Parameters.
Qty-Pck
The quantity available to pick for this sales order.
Qty-Shp
The quantity shipped. This remains blank until the line item is marked as shipped in Release Confirmation Entry.
Qty-B/O
The quantity of this product that is backordered for this sales order.
Sc
A supply code appears if the product is on backorder and varies depending on the product’s replenishment path and product settings. Valid supply codes vary by company. Commonly used options include:
•
B - Purchase order. Refer to Linking a Purchase Order to a Backorder.
•
E - Emergency purchase order. Refer to Linking a Purchase Order to a Backorder.
•
D - Direct ship purchase order. Refer to Entering a Direct Ship Order.
•
R - Emergency stock transfer. Refer to Linking a Transfer to a Backorder.
•
T - Stock transfer. Refer to Linking a Transfer to a Backorder.
•
W - Work order.
You cannot change a B supply code to a D, since the B code is defined in a price matrix record. If the purchase order cost is removed, you can then change the supply code.
Gross Price
The price of the product before any discount is applied.
Disc%
The discount to apply from the sales price.
Net Price
The customer’s price for the product. This information comes from the pricing assigned to the customer (refer to Product Pricing).
Src
The source code that indicates where the price comes from. Refer to Price Source Codes for definitions of the source codes.
Tx?
Indicates if the product is taxable:
•
Y - The product is taxable.
•
N - The product is tax exempt. A customer’s tax exempt status is set up in the Customer Maintenance Customer Sales Tax Entry Screen or the Customer Ship-To Maintenance Ship-To Tax Information Screen.
Extension
The total cost to the customer for the line item (Quantity x Net Price).
Cost
The purchase cost of the product.
Gp%
The gross profit percentage earned for the sale of this product.
Profit
The amount of profit earned due to the sale of the product.
Req-Date
The date the product is required by the customer. Defaults from the sales order header but can be changed if you want to change it to a future date. Refer to the Future Commitment Override screen
Pck-Date
The date the pick ticket is to print. Defaults from the header screen.
Promised
The date the customer was promised that they would receive the product.
Available
The quantity of the product available to sell for this warehouse.
Onorder
The quantity of the product on order for this warehouse.
Stock Status
The stocking status of the product:
•
S - Stock.
•
N - Nonstock.
•
C - Consignment.
Cross Reference & Related Product Information
(unlabeled field at the bottom left corner of the screen)
Some products may display one or more of the following cross reference and related product information codes:
•
ALT - Alternate products exist for this product.
•
SUB - Substitute products exist for this product.
•
ACC - Accessory products can potentially be sold with this product.
•
COMP - Complement information exists for this product. Complements provide the sales person with helpful information when selling the product.
•
DESC - An additional extended product description exists for the product.
•
KIT - This product is a kit parent item. The number displayed is the number of kits you can make with the available components.
•
BOM - This product is a bill of material. The number displayed is the number of BOMs you can make with available components.
•
EQV - Equivalent products exists for product. Equivalent products are part numbers that do not exist in the system, but may in the future. Once the equivalent product becomes a valid product in the system, it becomes a substitute rather than an equivalent.
•
PROMO - The product has promotional pricing established. Refer to Promotional Price Matrices.
•
RSVD - The quantity in parentheses is reserved for a customer. To find out who it is reserved for, from Inventory Inquiry, select the Action Bar > Inquiries > Reserved Inventory-Corporate View.
F2-Save
Saves the information and closes the screen.
F5-Add Info
Opens the Line Item Detail Additional Information screen to allow you to enter or verify additional product, customer, or vendor information.
F6-Subs
Opens the Product Substitutes Inquiry screen to allow you to view any substitutes available for the product.
F7-Notes
Opens the Notes display screen to allow you to enter, edit, or view notes regarding the line item.
F8-Source P/O
Opens the Allocate Backorders To Existing P/O’s screen to display open purchase orders that contain the backordered product. You can source the backorder from the purchase order so that when it arrives in your warehouse, it automatically allocates to your customer’s order. Refer to Sourcing a Backorder to an Existing Purchase Order.
F9-Inv Inq
Opens to Inventory Inquiry screen to display detailed stock status information for the product.
