Calculating Rebates
Once the header information is filled in on the price matrix, you can indicate how rebates are calculated for this matrix type.
To calculate rebates for the matrix:
1
From the Price Matrix Input screen (refer to Creating a Price Matrix), under “Rebates”, for Mtd, choose from the following methods to calculate the rebate:
•
$ - Use a specific rebate cost. You can use this method even with non-product matrices, but a warning displays. Enter the Rebate cost.
•
* - The price is a percentage of a column price or cost. Enter the following:
-
Basis: Indicate which price or cost column (from Product Maintenance or Product Warehouse Maintenance) to use. The discount calculated is a percentage over a price column if a price is referenced or a percentage over a cost if a cost is referenced.
-
Pct: If you entered a price column in Basis, enter the discount off of the price to appear in Disc% on the Line Item Detail screen. If you entered a cost column in Basis, enter a percent over the cost to calculate the sales price. For example if you enter 20 for Pct, the cost is divided by .80 to determine the sales price. This value appears in Net Price on the Line Item Detail screen.
•
C - Use the rebate base (current) to calculate the rebate. Enter the Rebate cost.
•
D - Use a chain discount. Opens the Chain Disc% screen to allow you to enter a discount percent to use to calculate the rebate and a Basis to indicate which price or cost column to use for the calculation.
•
M - Calculate the rebate as a percent of net margin. Enter the Basis and Pct.
•
P - Calculate the rebate as the difference between actual and rebate cost to use when price is higher than cost. Enter the Basis and Pct.
•
R - Use rebate base cost (replacement) to calculate the rebate. Enter a Rebate cost.
•
T - A Quantity Discount Table calculates the price. Choose an existing discount break Table. Refer to Discount Break Tables for information on setting up a discount break table.
2
If you entered an Exp Date in the first Ln#, enter another Ln# of pricing information for a subsequent date range.
3
Press F2-Save to save the price matrix information.
Price Matrix Inquiry allows you to view existing price matrices. The data is the same as Price Matrix Maintenance.
The Price Matrix Listings list the existing price matrices for a matrix type.
