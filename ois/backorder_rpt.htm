Open Backorder Detail Report
The Open Backorder Detail Report lists backordered products by customer.
Menu Path: Order Processing > Reports > Daily Order Detail Reports > Open Backorder Detail
Selection Criteria
Use the following criteria to limit the information to print on the report:
•
Division. The highest possible grouping in your company (e.g., Eastern Division, Western Division).
•
Region. A subset of Division - if Division is used (e.g., Southeast Region, Northwest Region).
•
Department. A subset of warehouse. Typically used to separate expenses within a warehouse (e.g., accounting, sales, warehouse).
•
Cost Center. A subset of Department - if Department is used. Typically used to separate expenses within a warehouse or department (e.g., shipping, receiving, outside sales, inside sales).
•
Warehouse. The warehouse responsible for shipping the orders.
•
Order Date. The date the order was entered if you want to limit the report to a date.
•
Customer. The customer with the open backorders, if you want to limit the report to one or more customers.
•
Order Type. The sales order types to include on the report. Choose one or more of the following:
-
01 - Regular.
-
02 - Ship complete.
-
03 - Tag & hold.
-
04 - Direct ship.
-
05 - Counter invoice.
-
06 - Confirmation.
-
07 - Regular consignment.
-
08 - Confirmation consignment.
-
09 - Return goods.
-
10 - Repair orders.
-
12 - Invoice when complete.
-
13 - Ship backorder complete.
-
14 - Ship line item complete.
-
15 - Show order.
-
16 - Order projection.
-
17 - Manufacturer’s representative.
-
18 - Crib replenishment.
•
With Invoiced B/O’s. Determines if you want to only include backorders that are still open after the orders are invoiced at least once.
-
Y - Only include backorders still open after invoicing.
-
N - Include all open backorders.
Column Descriptions
Column Heading
Description
Cust#/Name
Line 1: The customer number.
Line 2: The customer name.
Slsm
The number for the sales representative assigned in the sales order header screen.
Ord-Date
The date the sales order was entered.
Order#
The sales order number.
#
The number of times the order went through the invoice cycle.
P
Indicates the pick ticket print status:
•
S - The order was suspended and no pick ticket printed.
•
P - The product is available and a pick ticket will print during the next print cycle or when the pick ticket date is reached.
•
Blank - The pick ticket is not ready to print.
I
Indicates the order’s invoice status:
•
Y - The order is flagged for invoicing.
•
H - The order is on hold and cannot be invoiced until released. Refer to the Administrative Invoice Inquiry.
•
Blank - The order is not yet flagged for invoicing.
Product Number
The backordered product.
Description
The product description.
C
Indicates if the line item is part of a kit:
•
K - Kit parent.
•
C - Kit component.
S
Line 1: The line item’s supply code (depends on the product’s replenishment path). Commonly used options are:
•
B - Purchase order.
•
E - Emergency purchase order.
•
D - Direct ship purchase order.
•
R - Emergency stock transfer.
•
T - Stock transfer.
•
W - Work order.
Line 2: The linked document number, if the product is linked to a purchase order, transfer, or work order.
Qty-B/O
The quantity of the corresponding product that is backordered.
U/M
The unit of measure of the product as entered on the sales order.
Price/Ext.
Line 1: The price of the product for the unit of measure.
Line 2: The extended price of the backordered quantity of the product (Qty-B/O x Price).
