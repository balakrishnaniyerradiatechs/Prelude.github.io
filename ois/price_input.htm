Price Matrix Input screen
Depending on the Matrix Type you chose in the Price Matrix Maintenance screen, the Price Matrix Input screen allows you to enter the special pricing information for multiple date ranges. Refer to Creating a Price Matrix for more information.
Menu Path: Order Processing > Maintenance > Pricing Structures > Price Matrix Maintenance > Price Matrix Maintenance
Field and Function Key Descriptions
Field/Function Key
Description
Ln#
Each line number represents a set of pricing information for a date range. Enter each line in chronological order.
Beg-Date
The date that the special pricing is to start. The beginning date for the first line defaults to the system date. All remaining lines default to the day after the previous line’s expiration date. For example, if the previous line has an expiration date of 06/30/06 then the next line’s beginning date defaults to 07/01/06.
Exp Date
The date that the special pricing is to end. Leave blank if there is no expiration date. If a line does not have an expiration date then the next line is not available.
Min Qty
If applicable, the minimum quantity the customer must purchase to receive the special pricing.
MaxQty
If applicable, the maximum quantity the customer must purchase to receive the special pricing.
QT
If a value exists in MaxQty, the quantity type that determines how to price an order when a customer buys less than the MaxQty. Choose from the following quantity types to determine when to use the price matrix:
•
M - If the quantity ordered is less than the remaining quantity (MaxQty - quantity purchased within the date range) then the price matrix is used.
•
P - If the quantity ordered is less than the remaining quantity (MaxQty - quantity purchased within the date range) for a specific customer/product combination, then the price matrix is used.
•
T - If the quantity ordered is less than the remaining quantity (MaxQty - MinQty) for each sales order, then the price matrix is used.
If the remaining quantity is zero, the price matrix is skipped. If the quantity ordered is greater than the remaining quantity, a message notifies the salesperson that an available quantity remains and the line item should be split for pricing consideration. The price matrix is skipped.
Ov
Indicates if the price can be overridden on a sales order:
•
Y - The price can be overridden.
•
N - The price cannot be overridden.
•
H - The price can be overridden to a higher price only.
Ct
Indicates if this price is based on a contract:
•
Y - The price is based on a contract with a customer, group of customers, product, customer part number, product line, vendor, product price group, or major group. The system checks for the best price of all contract price matrices to price a sales order. If you enter Y for Ct, you also have access to U/M, Contract Number for this customer contract price. Press F7-Contract Info to open the Contract Price Information screen. Refer to Contract Pricing for more information on setting up and tracking contract prices for a customer.
•
N - The price is not from a contract.
U/M
The unit of measure for a contract price.
Contract Number
The contract number of CT is set to Y.
SR
Indicates if Sum & Reprice applies to products affected by this price matrix:
•
Y - If multiple products on a sales order have the same matrix type, they are combined for a better quantity break discount. If the customer has Best Price turned off, then the system uses the first matrix it finds to calculate the customer's price. If the customer has Best Price turned on, then all available matrices are stored so the system can determine the lowest price.
•
N - Sum & Reprice does not apply for this price matrix.
Refer to Sum & Reprice and Quantity Break Discounts.
*Pricing*
Mtd
Indicates how the price is calculated:
•
$ - The product has a set price. Enter the Price.
•
* - The price is a percentage of a column price or cost. Enter the Basis and the Pct.
•
T - A Quantity Discount Table calculates the price. The cursor moves to Table to allow you to enter a discount break table. Refer to Discount Break Tables for information on setting up a discount break table.
•
Q - Quantity Discount Breaks are used to calculate the price. The Price Matrix Quantity Break screen appears to allow you to enter the Qty Break Type and the Break Level Type. This information is set up in the same way as the Discount Break Tables.
Table
If you chose T for Mtd, enter the discount break table. Refer to Discount Break Tables for information on setting up a discount break table.
Price
If you chose $ for Mtd, enter the set price.
Basis
If you chose * for Mtd, this field indicates which price or cost column (from Product Maintenance > F7-Pricing) to use for the calculation. The discount calculated is a percentage over a price column if a price is referenced or a percentage over a cost if a cost is referenced.
Pct
If you entered a price column in Basis, enter the discount to give off of the price. This value appears in Disc% on the Line Item Detail screen on the sales order. If you entered a cost column in Basis, enter a percentage over the cost to calculate the sales price. For example if you enter 20 for Pct, the cost is divided by .80 to determine the sales price. This value appears in Net Price on the Line Item Detail screen on the sales order.
*Rebates*
Mtd
Indicates how a rebate price is calculated:
•
$ - Use a specific rebate cost. You can use this method even with non-product matrices, but a warning displays. Enter a cost in Rebate.
•
* - The price is a percent of a column price or cost. Enter the Basis and the Pct.
•
C - Use the rebate base (average) to calculate the rebate. The cursor moves to Rebate to allow you to enter a rebate cost.
•
D - Use a chain discount. Opens the Chain Disc% screen to allow you to enter a discount percent to use to calculate the rebate. You can then enter a Basis to indicate which price or cost column (from Product Maintenance > F7-Pricing) to use for the calculation.
•
M - Calculate the rebate as a percent of a net margin. You can then enter a Basis and a Pct.
•
P - Calculate the rebate as the difference between actual and rebated cost. This is used when the price field is higher than the cost. You can then enter a Basis and a Pct.
•
R - Use rebate base cost (replacement) to calculate the rebate. The cursor moves to Rebate to allow you to enter a rebate cost.
•
T - A Quantity Discount Table calculates the price. The cursor moves to Table to allow you to enter a discount break table. Refer to Discount Break Tables.
Table
The discount break table. Refer to Discount Break Tables for information on setting up a discount break table.
Rebate
If you chose $ for Mtd, enter the rebate cost.
Basis
If you chose * for Mtd, indicate which price or cost column (from Product Maintenance > F7-Pricing) to use for the calculation. The discount calculated is a percentage over a price column if a price is referenced or a percentage over a cost if a cost is referenced.
Pct
If you entered a price column in Basis, enter the discount to give off of the price. This value appears in Disc% on the Line Item Detail screen on the sales order. If you entered cost column in Basis, enter a percentage over the cost to calculate the sales price. For example if you enter 20 for Pct, the cost is divided by .80 to determine the sales price. This value appears in Net Price on the Line Item Detail screen on the sales order.
F2-Save
Saves the price matrix information.
F4-Del
Deletes the price matrix.
F5-Purchase Cost
Opens the Purchase Cost screen to allow you to define the purchase cost to use on a purchase order. If a line item on a sales order has a B supply code and a purchase cost matrix exists, then the system uses this cost when the line item is linked to a purchase order. Refer to Defining Purchasing Cost.
F6-Commission Cost
Opens the Commission Cost Entry screen to allow you to override a commission cost for a product on a sales order. This commission cost overrides any commission cost defined in the Purchase Cost screen. Refer to Overriding Commission Cost.
F7-Contract Info
Opens the Contract Price Information screen to allow you to create a customer contract within Price Matrix Maintenance (refer to Creating a Customer Price Contract).
Action Bar
Round Price
Allows you to define the number of decimals to round the calculated price to.
Copy
Opens the Customer Matrix Copy screen to allow you to copy a customer or customer price group matrix to a single customer, multiple customers, or a list of customers. Refer to Copying a Single Price Matrix.
Vend Info
Opens the Vendor Information screen so you can enter vendor contract information to carry over to a sales order that uses a price matrix. This vendor information appears in the Line Item Detail Additional Information screen on the sales order. It is also used in EDI transmissions for linked purchase orders. Refer to Assigning Vendor Information to a Contract.
Prev Price
Opens the Previous Prices screen to allow you to use previous prices from Product Maintenance as the Basis to price a line item until an expiration date is reached. Enter a Previous Price Expiration Date. Example: The price of a product increases from $10.99 to $11.99 but you agree to give the customer the old price for 3 months. For three months the discount is applied to the $10.99 price. After the three months are up, the discount is applied to the $11.99 price.
