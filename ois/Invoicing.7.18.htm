Voiding an Invoice
You can void a customer invoice by entering a reverse sales order. This option generates a credit memo (refer to Credit Memos & RGAs), but may be more useful if you want to reverse an entire sales order (e.g., if you billed the wrong customer, rather to just give credit for a single item).
To void a sales order after it is invoiced:
1
Select Order Processing > Sales Order Entry to open the Sales Order Entry screen.
2
Press ENTER over NEW.
3
From the Action Bar, select Auto Reverse to open the Auto Reverse Order screen.
4
Enter the Invoice # for the order you want to reverse. The Customer, Written By, Invoice Date, and Invoice Amount for the order appear.
5
Enter the Credit Reason Code to explain why the invoice is being reversed. Refer to Creating Credit Reason Codes. The restocking charge percent for the credit reason code is calculated for each product.
6
Choose from the following Default Return Type to indicate where the inventory on the sales order is going:
•
D - Damaged.
•
R - Return to stock.
•
S - Scrapped.
•
W - Warranty return.
7
If you entered W for Default Warranty Type, enter the Default Warranty Tag # or type in NEW to get a new warranty tag number.
8
For Shipping Whse Assign, choose from the following:
•
O - Original (default). The shipping warehouse is the same as the shipping warehouse on the original order.
•
V - Override. Allows you to change the shipping warehouse for the order. Enter the new warehouse number in the following field.
9
Enter the Order Type for the auto reverse. Typically an 06 confirmation. Refer to Sales Order Types.
10
Press F2-Save. The positive lines on the original sales order are reversed as negative lines on the void sale. If additional charges exist on the original sales order, the following message appears:
Retain Additional Charges?
11
Choose from the following:
•
Yes - Retain additional charges on the Sales Order Completion screen but with a negative amount. For example, if 5.00 Outbound Freight was included on the original invoice, the auto reverse entry is updated with - 5.00 Outbound Freight.
•
No - Do not retain additional charges on the Sales Order Completion screen.
12
When the Sales Order Header screen appears, edit the information as necessary and press F2-Save. The Sales Order Entry screen appears.
13
Edit line item information as necessary (refer to Entering Sales Order Line Items) and press F2-Save.
14
Select Complete to open the Sales Order Completion screen (refer to Using the Sales Order Completion Screen).
15
Edit the charges as necessary and press F2-Save. The following message appears:
Do You Wish To Designate An Open Invoice To Which This Credit Memo Should Be Applied?
16
Choose one of the following:
•
No. The credit memo is not applied to an invoice.
•
Yes. Allows you to enter an invoice number to apply the credit to. The invoice you entered in step 4 is the default.
17
Press F2-Save. The following message appears:
Are You Ready to Release This Order For Invoicing?
18
Choose from the following:
•
Yes. The credit is flagged to print. Refer to Printing Invoices.
•
No. The credit is not flagged to print. You must go back into the order and choose Yes at this prompt to generate the credit invoice.
