Using Manufacturer Rep Sales Orders
You can use the type 17 manufacturer’s rep order to track commission for a manufacturer’s rep (e.g., a vendor pays you commission for an order shipped directly to the customer). When the vendor notifies you that the material shipped, you can bill the order to update your information. This type of order is similar to a direct order except that you do not actually bill the customer. The customer pays the vendor and the vendor pays you a commission based on the order’s gross profit.
General Ledger Table Maintenance
To use Manufacturer Rep sales orders, you must have the following G/L accounts set up:
•
MFGR Rep Commission Due. The gross profit of the sale is posted to this account when a type 17 Manufacturer Rep sales order is invoiced. This account is overridden if an account is specified in Product Line Maintenance.
•
MFGR Rep Sales. The amount of the sale minus the commission is posted to this account when a type 17 Manufacturer Rep sales order is invoiced. This account is overridden if an account is specified in Product Line Maintenance.
•
MFGR Rep Cost of Sales. The total merchandise amount of the sale is posted to this account when a type 17 Manufacturer Rep sales order is invoiced. This account is overridden if an account is specified in Product Line Maintenance.
To enter a manufacturer rep sales order:
1
Enter a sales order using an Order Type of 17 (refer to Entering Sales Order Header Information).
2
Enter the products (refer to Entering Sales Order Line Items). The Line Item Detail screen will not show any quantity on-hand or on-order since no inventory is committed. SERVICE displays for Available. The Qty-Pck and the Qty-Ship are automatically set to the order Quantity. Verify or change the Gross Price and/or the Net Price to make sure it is the correct cost billed by the vendor to the customer.
3
After all line items are entered, press F2-Save to complete the order (refer to Using the Sales Order Completion Screen).
No taxes or other charges apply since the order is billed to the customer by the vendor. When the order is invoiced, the general ledger reflects the commission due as calculated from the gross profit of the sale.
