Entering Kit Parent Products
A kit parent is a list of components that make up one part number. Depending on the kit components, you can set up a kit parent in Product Maintenance with:
•
A list of components that cannot be changed on an order.
•
A list of components that can be changed on an order.
•
No components so that they can be added when the kit parent is entered on an order.
Once a kit parent is set up, any time you enter the kit on a sales order, KIT displays on the Line Item Detail screen to let you know that kit components exist for the product. The way a kit product appears on a sales order depends on the how the flags are set for the product in Kit Maintenance (Product Maintenance > F6-Xref/Notes > Kit Parent). Refer to “Kit Parent Products” in the Inventory documentation for information on setting up a kit product.
Regular or Assortment Type Kit Parent Products
When you enter a Type R (regular) or A (assorted) kit parent on a sales order, the following message appears after you exit the Line Item Detail screen:
Do you Wish to Continue with Kit Entry?
1
Choose from the following:
•
No - Use the kit parent as a regular product.
•
Yes - Opens the Kit Entry screen to display the components and quantities that make up the kit parent.
2
Change the components or quantities as necessary.
Display Type Kit Parent Products
When you choose a display kit parent type, the quantity defaults to 1 and automatically appears as a line item. Product quantities and components cannot be changed.
Exploded Type Kit Parent Products
When you enter a Type E (exploded) kit parent on a sales order, the following message appears after you exit the Line Item Detail screen:
Do You Wish To Explode The Kit Components On To The Sales Order?
1
Choose from the following:
•
No - The product is added as a regular kit parent product.
•
Yes - Opens the Multiplier screen.
2
Enter the quantity of kits ordered. The components are multiplied by this quantity and appear as individual line items on the sales order. The beginning and end of the component parts are indicated with comment lines.
