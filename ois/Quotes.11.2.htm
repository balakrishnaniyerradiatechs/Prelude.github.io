Entering a Quote
Quote Order Entry works similar to Sales Order Entry except that inventory is not allocated. You can give the customer price and availability information and if the customer approves, you can convert the quote into a sales order.
To enter a quote:
1
Select Order Processing > Additional Subsystems > Quotation Subsystem > Quotation Entry to open the Quote Order Entry screen.
2
Enter the customer name or number (refer to Selecting a Customer for information on selecting the correct customer and ship-to address). The Quotation Header screen appears.
3
For Quote Type, choose from the following:
•
01 - Regular Quote. A standard quotation to give the customer price and availability information.
•
02 - Planned Quote. When converting the quote into a sales order, use a multiplier to change the quantities. This type is not purged and can be used indefinitely for repeat quotes with different quantities.
•
03 - Repair Type Quote. Repair orders interface with the Work Order module to accumulate material, labor, and other costs associated with the repair. If you do not have the Work Order module, you can still choose one of the following repair options:
-
01 - Warranty: The vendor, distributor, and/or customer are charged all, part, or none of the charges depending on the product’s warranty policy.
-
02 - Time and Material: The customer is charged for material and labor expended on the repair order based on customer pricing.
-
03 - Fixed Fee: The customer is charged a fixed amount, based on an estimate of the repair, regardless of the materials and labor.
•
04 - Standing Quote. Opens the Standing Order Maintenance screen to allow you to enter information to convert the quote to a sales order on a regular basis for a given time period using the Standing Quote Conversion. Enter the following:
-
Required Date - The date to convert the quote to an order. This is updated each time in Standing Quote Conversion.
-
Expiration Date - The date the quote can no longer be converted. Standing Quote Conversion generates orders until this date.
-
Frequency - How often the quote is converted into an order.
Use one of the following methods to determine Frequency:
•
To convert the quote every set number of days, enter the number of days (e.g., enter 45 to convert the quote every 45 days).
•
To convert the quote once a month, enter a D in front of the day of the month (e.g., enter D15 to convert the quote on the 15th of every month).
•
To convert the quote bi-monthly (every other month) enter a B before the day (e.g., enter B15 to convert the quote on the 15th of every other month).
•
To convert the quote every quarter, enter a Q in front of the day of the month (e.g., enter Q15 to convert the quote on the 15th of every third month).
•
To convert the quote semi-annually, enter an S in front of the day of the month (e.g., enter S15 to convert the quote on the 15th every 6 months).
-
Convert to Order Type - The type of order created by the conversion process (excludes order types 09, 10, 11, 16, or 17). Refer to Sales Order Types.
-
Service Order - If you use the Prelude Service module, you can enter Y to generate the order as a service order.
Refer to Converting Standing Quotes for more information on standing quotes.
4
For Expiration Date, if applicable, enter the date the quote will expire if it is not deleted or converted to a quote.
5
For Follow Up Date, enter the date to be reminded to follow up on the quote if it is not yet converted or deleted. The quotes appear in the Quote Follow Up Inquiry and the Quote Follow Up Report. Leave blank if you do not want to be notified.
Default Dates
The default Expiration Date and Follow Up Date for quotes are set in Order Processing Controls > Order Type Defaults > Quotation Entry Types
6
Fill in the remaining information as necessary. Refer to Entering Sales Order Header Information for information on each field.
7
Press F2-Save on the header screen to return to the Quote Order Entry screen to enter line items for the quote.
8
Enter each Product Number, to open the Quote Entry Detail screen.
9
Enter the Quantity to quote and edit the prices as necessary. Refer to Entering Stock Products for an explanation of each field.
Quote Cost
The cost that is used for quotes depends on how Quote Cost is set in Order Processing Parameters:
•
A - Average cost.
•
S - Standard cost.
•
C - Cost for sales.
•
R - Replacement cost.
10
After you enter all the products to quote to the customer, press F2-Save to finish the quote. The following message appears:
Do You Wish To .......
11
Choose from the following:
•
Complete - Opens the Quotation Completion screen to allow you to add freight and extra charges. You can print, email, or fax the quote to the customer but no order generates. If the quote is not eventually turned into a sales order, it may be purged.
•
Order - Opens the Quote Conversion screen to allow you to convert the quote to a sales order. Refer to Converting a Quote to a Sales Order.
•
Suspend - Suspends the quote. Use this option if the customer does not need a copy or confirmation or if you are waiting for a response from a vendor on a vendor RFQ (refer to Creating a Vendor RFQ from a Quote).
•
Return - Returns to the quote to edit or verify products and prices.
