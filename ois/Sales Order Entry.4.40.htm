Setting Pick Ticket Rules
The Pick Ticket Date in the Sales Order Header screen indicates when a pick ticket will print. You can set maintenance options by customer, warehouse, or company to calculate pick ticket days, set minimum and maximum dollar levels, determine information to print on pick tickets, and establish shipping routes.
Pick ticket days are set at multiple levels in the system. The hierarchy to determine which level to use first is as follows:
•
1st - Pick Ticket Def Days in Route Maintenance.
•
2n - Pick Days in Customer Ship-to Maintenance.
•
3rd - Pick Days in Customer Maintenance.
•
4th - Pick Ticket Default Days Warehouse Maintenance.
•
5th - Pick Ticket Default Days in Order Processing Parameters.
Refer to the following:
Route Maintenance
To calculate pick ticket days for a truck delivery route:
1
Select Order Processing > Maintenance > Customer Control Files > Route Codes to open the Route Maintenance Screen.
2
For Pick Ticket Def Days, enter the number of days to subtract from the required date to calculate the pick ticket date for this route code. This value overrides the Pick Days in Customer Ship-To Maintenance, Customer Maintenance, Warehouse Maintenance, and Order Processing Parameters.
3
Press F5-Picking Days to open the Picking Rules screen.
4
For each day of the week, enter one of the following:
•
Y - The day is available for picking.
•
N - The day is not available for picking.
5
Press F2-Save twice to save the pick ticket days information.
Customer Ship-To Maintenance
To calculate pick ticket days for a customer ship-to account:
1
In Select Customer Ship-To Maintenance > F5-Add Info to open the Ship-To Additional Information screen.
2
For Pick Days, enter the number of days to subtract from the required date to calculate the pick ticket date. Enter T to always default to today. This value overrides the Pick Days in Customer Maintenance, Warehouse Maintenance, and Order Processing Parameters.
3
For Price Pick Tick, determine if sales prices print on pick tickets and packing slips for this customer ship-to.
•
Y - Print sales prices on the picking tickets and packing slips.
•
N - Do not print sales prices on the picking tickets and packing slips.
4
Press F2-Save twice to save the pick days information.
Customer Maintenance
To set pick ticket options for a customer account:
1
In Select Customer Maintenance > F7-Order > F5- Add Info to open the Additional Order Information Screen.
2
For Price Pick Tick, determine if sales prices print on pick tickets and packing slips for this customer.
•
Y - Print sales prices on the picking tickets and packing slips.
•
N - Do not print sales prices on the picking tickets and packing slips.
3
For Pick Days, enter the number of days to subtract from the required date to calculate the pick ticket date. Enter T to always default to today. This number overrides the Pick Ticket Default Days in Warehouse Maintenance and Order Processing Parameters.
4
Press F2-Save 3 times to save the pick ticket information.
Warehouse Maintenance
To set pick ticket information for a warehouse:
1
Select Warehouse Maintenance > F5-Add Info to open the Additional Information screen.
2
For Pick Ticket Default Days, enter the number of days to subtract from the required date to calculate the pick ticket date. Enter T to default to today. This value overrides Pick Ticket Default Days in Order Processing Parameters.
3
Press F6-Picking Days to open the Picking Rules screen.
4
For each day of the week, enter one of the following:
•
Y - The day is available for picking.
•
N - The day is not available for picking.
5
Press F2-Save twice to return to the Warehouse Maintenance screen.
6
Press F6-Printing to open the Continuous Printing Setup screen.
7
For S/O Pick Ticket, choose from the following:
•
Y - Pick tickets print upon completion of each sales order.
•
N - Pick tickets print by batch (Refer to Printing Pick Tickets by Batch).
•
D - Multiple printers continuously print pick tickets so you must designate the printer number or accept the default printer.
8
For Prt, enter the port number (created in Continuous Printer Port Maintenance) used to control continuous printing.
9
For Form Queue, enter the printer number where pick tickets print.
10
Press F2-Save twice to save the printer information.
Order Processing Parameters
To set default pick ticket information at the company level:
1
Select Option Parameters > Order Processing Parameters.
2
For Pick Ticket Default Days, define the number of days to subtract from the required date to calculate the pick ticket date. Enter T to default to today.
3
For Print CPO on Pick Tickets, determine if CPO comments should print on pick tickets (i.e., comments entered in Sales Order Entry to print on the purchase order). Choose from the following:
•
Y - CPO comments print on pick tickets.
•
N - CPO comments do not print on pick tickets.
4
Press F5 - Add Info to open the Additional Information screen.
5
For Print B/O On Pick Tickets, choose from the following:
•
Y - Include backordered items on the initial pick ticket.
•
N - Do not include backordered items on the pick ticket.
•
A - Always print backordered items on all pick tickets.
6
For Pick Ticket Suppress w/No CC Auth Code, choose from the following:
•
Y - Do not print credit card orders that do not have a valid credit card authorization. Depending on system settings (refer to Setting up Your System for Credit Cards) the order must be released from Administrative Order Hold before a pick ticket prints.
•
N - Print credit card orders even if they do not have a valid credit card authorization.
7
Press F5-Add Info to open the Additional Options screen.
8
For Pick Ticket Minimum Dollar, enter the minimum dollar amount required to print a pick ticket. If the total pickable value (pick qty x price for each line item) is greater than this amount, the sales order is released. If the total pickable value for each warehouse (pick qty x price for each line item) is less than this amount, the sales order is not released. This field works with the Pick Ticket Minimum Percent.
9
For Pick Ticket Minimum Percent, enter the percentage of merchandise that must be available before the pick ticket will print. If the percentage calculated from the total pickable amount (pick qty x price for each line item) divided by the total on the sales order is greater than this value, the sales order is released. If the percentage calculated from the total pickable amount (pick qty x price for each line item) divided by the grand total on the sales order is less than this value, the order is not released. This field works with the Pick Ticket Minimum Dollar. If both fields have a value and one of the conditions is met, the sales order is released.
Batch pick ticket printing does not check for minimum dollar values or percents. The releases will print even if they are below the minimum.
10
Press F2-Save twice to return to the Order Processing Parameters screen.
11
Press F6-Holidays to open the Holiday Mnt screen.
12
Enter the dates that your company is closed and does not pick inventory.
13
Press F2-Save twice to save the pick ticket information.
Company Maintenance
To set pick ticket information for a company:
1
Select Company Maintenance > F5-Add Info to open the Additional Information screen.
2
For Bin Loc Sort - S/O, determine how pick tickets are sorted:
•
Y - Sort releases in bin location sequence. Kit components on sales orders print in bin location sequence.
•
N - Sort pick tickets by product number.
3
Press F2-Save twice to save the pick ticket information.
Product Maintenance
To set pick ticket information for a product:
1
Select Product Maintenance > F5-Add Info > F5-Add Info to open the Additional Product Maintenance Information screen.
2
For S/O PTP Dependent, determine if the product depends on other products for a pick ticket to print:
•
Y - The pick ticket will not release unless the sales order contains another pickable item.
•
N - A release will generate when this product is available even if the other items on the sales order are not available.
Sales Order Entry
Once pick days are established throughout the system, the calculated Pick Ticket Date appears on the Sales Order Header screen. You can override this date for each sales order and line item.
