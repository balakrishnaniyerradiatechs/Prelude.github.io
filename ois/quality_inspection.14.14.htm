Tracking Quality Control Information
The Quality Inspection Subsystem allows you to control the quality of products received from manufacturers and to improve service to customers. You can keep a cumulative record of complaints and customer feedback regarding a defect on a product and to track solutions or corrective actions taken to fix the problem. You can also use formulas from American National Standards to determine sample inspection amounts and record results for future reference.
The quality control process consists of the following four steps:
•
Quality Feedback Entry. Refer to Recording a Product Complaint.
•
Quality Incident Entry - Refer to Collecting Quality Control Incident Information.
•
Quality Corrective Action Entry. Refer to Recording Corrective Actions.
•
Inspection Results Entry - Refer to Recording Inspection Results.
