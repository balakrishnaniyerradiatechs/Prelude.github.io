Entering a Credit Memo
You can enter a credit memo to issue credit for returned or damaged material. If you already issued an RGA to the customer to authorize the return of material, refer to Converting an RGA into a Credit . If no RGA exists, use the following steps to create a credit memo:
1
Select Order Processing > Optional Entry and Printing > Credit Memo Entry to open the Credit Memo Entry screen.
2
For information on selecting a customer and completing the order header, refer to Selecting a Customer and Entering Sales Order Header Information. The Order Type is automatically set to 06 for Confirmation.
Non Returnable Products
Nonreturnable Flag in Product Maintenance > F5-Add info determines if the product can be returned:
•
Y - The product should not be returned. The message “This Product is Non-Returnable! Do you Wish to Continue?” displays when the product is entered on a credit memo, RGA, or as a negative entry on a sales order, but you are still allowed to enter the item.
•
D - Disallow returns. The message “This product is set to Disallow Returns” displays when the product is entered on a credit memo, RGA, or as a negative entry on a sales order and the product is not allowed to be entered.
•
N - The product can be returned. No message displays when the product is entered on a credit memo or RGA.
3
Enter the first Product for which the customer is returning or requesting a credit. The Line Item Credit Detail screen appears.
4
Enter the Quantity to return as a negative number (-1 is the default). The Credit Information screen appears.
5
Enter the Credit Reason code. Credit reason codes are set up in Credit Reason Maintenance to define why a customer is returning material, to update inventory and usage, and to post to the appropriate general ledger account (refer to Creating Credit Reason Codes).
6
For Original Inv#, enter the original invoice number on which the customer purchased this item. Press F3 to search for valid invoices for the customer. If you do enter the original invoice number, you cannot enter a credit for more than what the customer was originally billed.
Invoice Number
Require Original Invoice# in User Identification Maintenance > F6-Add Info determines if a user must enter the original invoice number when entering an RGA or credit memo for returned material.
If a value is entered in Invoice Days Old in Order Processing Parameters > F5-Add Info > F5 Add info, a warning appears if you enter an invoice number that is more than this number of days old, but you are not prevented from completing the return.
7
Press ENTER to return to the Line Item Credit Detail Screen.
8
Verify that the Warehouse is where the material is being returned.
9
Enter the quantity as a negative number in one of the following fields to indicate where the material will go:
•
QtyStk - Quantity to return to available stock.
•
QtyWty - Quantity to put into warranty inventory if the material is defective and you will receive credit or a replacement from the vendor. Refer to Creating Warranty Tags for Returned Products.
•
QtyScr - Quantity to scrap or throw away. Scrapped inventory has no value.
•
QtyDmg - Quantity to move to damaged inventory. Damaged inventory has a value. Check with management before using this option.
The default is determined by Default Inventory Type assigned to the credit reason code. You can enter quantities in more than one field, but the total quantities must equal the original Quantity of the line item.
10
If necessary, change the pricing information.
Restocking Charge Flags
Restock Charge % (Company Maintenance) determines the default restocking charge to add to returned products.
Soe Restock Charge Method (Order Processing Parameters > F5 > F5). Determines if restocking charges are added to returned goods:
•
S (or blank) - Only add restocking charges to material returned to stock.
•
A - Add restocking charges to all types of returned goods.
Restocking Charge (Customer Maintenance > F7 > F5) determines if a restocking charge is calculated as each line item is entered on a credit memo:
•
Y - Calculate a restocking charge for each line item when credit is issued.
•
N - Do not calculate a restocking charges for each line when credit is issued.
11
Press F2-Save to exit the Line Item Credit Detail screen.
12
Once you enter all the line items, press F2-Save. The following message appears:
Do You Wish To...
13
When you are ready to complete the credit, select Complete to open the Sales Order Completion screen (refer to Using the Sales Order Completion Screen for a detailed explanation of each field).
14
Edit the charges as necessary and press F2-Save. The following message appears:
Do You Wish To Designate An Open Invoice To Which This Credit Memo Should Be Applied?
15
Choose one of the following:
•
No. The credit memo is not applied to an open invoice.
•
Yes. Allows you to enter an invoice number to apply the credit to.
16
Press F2-Save. The following message appears:
Are You Ready to Release This Order For Invoicing?
17
Choose from the following:
•
Yes. The credit memo is flagged to print. Refer to Printing Invoices.
•
No. The credit memo is not flagged to print. You must go back into the order and choose Yes at this prompt to generate the credit memo.
