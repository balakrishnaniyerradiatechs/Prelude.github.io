Creating Products in Sales Order Entry
Depending on company policy and system settings, you can create a product while in a sales order. You can enter basic information about the product and then complete the detail information in Product Maintenance at a more convenient time.
Creating Skeleton Products
Allow New Prod in S/O (Option Parameters > Order Processing Parameters) determines if products can be created within Sales Order Entry or Quote Order Entry:
•
N - You cannot create a new product on a sales order.
•
Y - If you enter a new part number, a dialog box appears to ask if you want to create a new product. If you select Yes, the Skeleton Product Maintenance appears and you can create a new product to add to the product file.
•
C - If you enter a new part number, a dialog box appears to ask if you want to create a comment line to reference the product. You can enter a description of the product that appears as a comment. No inventory is affected.
•
B - If you enter a new part number, a dialog box appears to ask if you want to create a new product or add a comment line. If you choose to create a product, the Skeleton Product Maintenance appears and you can create a new product to add to the product file. If you choose to add a comment, you can enter a description of the product that appears as a comment.
When you enter a product on the Sales Order Entry screen that does not currently exist in your system, the following message appears:
Product Not on File, Create New Product?
1
Select Yes to open the Skeleton Product Maintenance screen.
2
The product number that you entered on the Sales Order Entry screen displays in Product. You can edit the number if necessary.
3
The Warehouse Information screen appears. Enter the shipping Warehouse.
4
Enter a Description of the product.
5
For Description Two, enter an extended description of the product, if necessary.
6
Enter the Product Line that the product belongs to. Product lines group similar products together for selling purposes and are required for every part number.
7
Enter the Product Type. Product types are used to group products for reports. Choose from the following:
•
F - Finished good.
•
R - Raw material.
•
B - Both a finished good and a raw material.
•
E - Expense item.
•
N - Rental.
•
U - Used item.
•
P - Part. Same as raw material but is used for custom reporting.
8
For U/M Table, indicate if a unit of measure table is required for this product. If the product is purchased, stocked, and sold in different units of measure, you may require a unit of measure table:
•
* (Asterisk) - A unit of measure table already exists for the product.
•
Y - A unit of measure table is required for the product. The U/M Table Maintenance screen appears to allow you to enter a unit of measure table. The table uses Buy, Stock, and Price to affect how the items are bought, stocked and sold. It can also track inventory using multiple units of measure and inventory factors.
Refer to ”Establishing a Unit of Measure” in the Inventory documentation.
•
N - A unit of measure table is not required.
9
Enter the Weight of the product.
10
For Buy, enter the unit of measure to use when you purchase the product from a vendor.
11
For Stock, enter the unit of measure to use for stock in your warehouse. Enter all costs for the product and product-warehouse level in the terms of the Stock unit of measure.
12
For Price, enter the unit of measure to use to sell to your customers. Enter all prices at the product and the product/warehouse level in terms of the Price unit of measure.
13
Enter the List price and the column pricing for the product (e.g., Price 1, Price 2, Price 3). These are the default customer prices. Each customer is assigned a Price Code (Customer Maintenance > F7-Order) to use if special pricing does not exist for this product.
14
For Replacement Cost, enter the cost to replace the product. This cost appears on the purchase order.
15
For Product Prc Group, enter the product price group to group products for discounts. Refer to ”Establishing Product Price Groups” in the Inventory documentation for more information.
16
Enter the Primary Vendor for the product. The primary vendor is used by Nightly Processing when recommending purchase orders and vendor line targets.
17
Enter the Vendor Line for the product. Vendor lines group products for purchasing, discounting, and analyzing. Refer to “Vendor Line Maintenance” in the Purchasing module.
18
Use the function keys to complete any additional information for the product. Refer to “Product Maintenance” in the Inventory documentation for detailed explanations of each field in these screens.
19
Press F2-Save to complete the skeleton maintenance information. The Line Item Detail screen opens to allow you to enter a quantity and to verify or change other information for the line item (refer to Entering Sales Order Line Items).
You can go back into Product Maintenance to edit or add more information for the product.
