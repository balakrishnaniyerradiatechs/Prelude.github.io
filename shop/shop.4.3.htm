Starting or Editing a Shop Repair Order
To start a shop repair order:
1
Select Shop Repair > Shop Repair Order Entry to open the Shop Repair Order Entry screen.
If you have access to more than one warehouse, the Location Retrieval screen appears first. Enter the appropriate Selling Warehouse, Department, and/or Cost Center and press ENTER.
2
To enter an Order Number, choose one of the following:
•
To start a new shop repair order, press ENTER on the word NEW.
•
To retrieve the last order you entered in this session, press F8-Last Order #.
•
To access an existing order, enter the order number.
If the Identify CN By Model flag in Shop/Repair Parameters is set to Y, the Shop Repair Information screen appears. Go to step 4. Refer to Setting Shop Repair Parameters for more information on maintenance flags.
3
For Customer, choose one of the following:
•
Enter the customer’s number.
•
Enter a keyword.
•
Press F3 and enter search criteria.
Depending on system settings, the Customer Ship-to Verification screen may appear.
The address is taken from Customer Maintenance. If this is the correct customer and address, enter Y in Is This The Correct Customer (Y/N)?. If not, enter N and select another customer.
The Shop Repair Information screen appears.
4
Enter the Model (part number) or press F3 to search for models and serial numbers purchased by this customer.
5
If your company uses serial numbers for this model, enter the serial number in Serial.
If you have not sold this combination of model and serial number to this customer (if you’ve chosen one), the following error messages appear:
•
Warning! Invalid Unit/Serial Number Combination!
•
This Is Not A Valid Customer!
Press ENTER to go past these messages. You can still enter the order for this customer/model/serial number combination.
If you change the customer number after you enter the Model and Serial numbers, the selling warehouse information in the order header updates to the new customer’s information.
To review model/serial number information, press F6-Model Info to open the Engine/Transmission Information screen. Refer to Recording Model/Serial Number Information.
To review previous repairs on the model. serial number, press F7-Repair History. Refer to Model/Serial Repair Inquiry.
6
If you have not identified a customer for the order, for Customer, choose one of the following:
•
Enter the customer’s number.
•
Enter a keyword.
•
Press F3 and enter search criteria.
7
Edit any of the defaulted customer information if necessary (e.g., Ship-To Number, Contact, Phone, Fax).
8
Enter any Instructions for the order.
9
If you are recording hours for the model/serial number combination, enter the current hourly meter reading in Hours In. In this case, after the repair is complete, you must enter Hours Out for the product before completing the order.
10
If you are giving the customer a replacement product for the duration of the repair, enter Y in Loaner. The Loaner Information screen appears.
•
Enter the Model Number and Serial Number of the replacement item.
•
If the replacement product is on an order, enter the Order Number.
•
Press F2-Save to return to the Shop Repair Information screen.
11
For Setup, accept the default.
12
If you know the date on which the product was purchased, enter it in Purchased (defaults if the customer purchased the model/serial number combination from you).
13
If you know the date on which the product stopped working, enter it in Failed.
14
For Promised, enter the date on which you are promising that the item will be repaired (defaults to the next business day).
15
For Pick Up, enter the date the product will be picked up from the customer. The Notes Entry screen appears to allow you to enter pickup instructions.
16
If you are providing an estimate to the customer, enter the:
•
Amount of the estimate in Original Est. (or in Current Est. if you are changing the original estimate).
•
Name of the person who approved the estimate in Approved By.
•
Date on which you provided the estimate.
17
If you are using the scheduling calendar (refer to Scheduling Resources for Shop Repair Jobs) enter the date of the last contact with the customer. This is for information only and displays in the Scheduling Calendar.
18
When you finish entering information, press F2-Save. One of the following occurs:
•
If the order already has a job (e.g., from a setup order or a service bulletin), the Shop Repair Order Entry screen appears. Refer to Adding or Editing Line Items.
•
If the order does not have any existing jobs, the Shop Repair Job Entry screen appears. Refer to Creating the Job for more information.
•
If any service bulletins exist that you have not chosen to add as a job, a dialog box appears to let you know that service bulletins exist. Refer to Adding a Service Bulletin to an Order.
