Entering Service Bulletins
When a vendor issues a service bulletin for a product that should be repaired, you can enter the bulletin information into the system. When you enter the affected product/serial number combination on a shop repair order, a dialog box informs you of the existing bulletins and allows you to add the bulletin as a job on the order. Refer to Adding a Service Bulletin to an Order.
To enter a service bulletin:
1
Select Shop Repair > Maintenance > Service Bulletin Maintenance to open the Service Bulletin Maintenance screen.
2
In Bulletin Number, enter the code (up to 20 alphanumeric characters) for the bulletin from the vendor.
3
Enter a brief Description of the bulletin.
4
Enter the following dates:
•
Issue Date - The date the bulletin goes into effect.
•
Completion Date - The date by which the repairs to affected products should be complete.
•
Internal Date - The date by which your company wants to complete repairs.
5
Enter an Alert Level to indicate the urgency of the bulletin. This information appears when you enter the affected product on a shop repair order. Choose from the following:
•
1 - Safety rework.
•
2 - Repair.
•
3 - Repair bulletin.
•
4 - Information bulletin.
6
Enter a Pay Type to identify how the job should be billed. Refer to Creating Pay Types for more information.
7
If the job is associated with a repair type, enter a Repair Group. Refer to Identifying Repair Groups for more information.
8
For Repair Time, enter the published or expected time required to complete the repair. This time is compared to actual time clocked to calculate technician efficiency.
9
If you are storing the paperwork for the bulletin, enter the location of the paperwork in Location Filed.
10
To enter the affected products and serial numbers, press F5-Products. The Service Bulletin Product Coverage screen appears.
11
Enter the Product, Beginning Serial#, and Ending Serial# for all products associated with the bulletin.
If you need to enter multiple serial number ranges for the same product, enter separate lines for each range.
12
Press F2-Save to return to the Service Bulletin Maintenance screen.
13
If you want to enter a more complete description of the bulletin, press F6-Situation/Correction and enter the necessary text.
14
Press F2-Save.
15
When you are finished updating the service bulletin, press F2-Save.
