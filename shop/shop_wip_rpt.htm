Shop Work In Progress Report
The Shop Work In Progress Report shows information on open shop repair orders that are currently being worked on.
Menu Path: Shop Repair > Reports > Shop Work In Process Report
Selection Criteria
Use the following criteria to limit the information to print on the report:
•
Division. The highest possible grouping in your company (e.g., Eastern Division, Western Division).
•
Region. A subset of Division - if Division is used. (e.g., Southeast Region, Northwestern Region).
•
Selling Warehouse. The warehouse that will receive credit for the repair.
•
Department. A subset of Warehouse. Typically used to separate expenses within a warehouse (e.g., accounting, sales, warehouse).
•
Cost Center. A subset of Department - if Department is used. Typically used to separate expenses within a warehouse or department (e.g., shipping, receiving, outside sales, inside sales).
•
Customer. The customer(s) to include.
•
Pay Type. How the repair will be billed (e.g., customer bill, warranty, no charge). Pay types may affect pricing and shop charges. Refer to Creating Pay Types.
•
Order Date range. The beginning and ending order creation dates to include.
•
Status of Job. The job types to include:
-
Tech clocked in.
-
Open.
-
Quotation.
-
Pending warranty response.
-
Pending warranty submittal.
-
Tech clocked out.
-
Waiting on parts
-
Not completed/this invoice.
•
Model. The part number of the unit being repaired.
•
Serial. The serial number of the unit being repaired.
Column Descriptions
Column Heading
Description
Order Information
Order#
The shop repair order number.
Customer#/
Description
The number and description of the customer assigned to the order.
Ord-Date
The date the order was entered.
Model
The product number of the unit being repaired (if any).
Serial#
The serial number of the unit being repaired (if any).
Prm-Date
The promised date, which is when the repair should be completed.
Job Information (repeats for each job on an order)
Job
The job number (e.g., A, B, C) and description. Orders can have multiple jobs. Refer to Creating the Job.
Tech
The technician who is assigned to or is working on the repair. Refer to Setting up Technicians.
Rep Grp
The code for the type of repair (e.g., 01, 02). Refer to Identifying Repair Groups.
Pay Type
How the repair will be billed (e.g., customer bill, warranty, no charge). Refer to Creating Pay Types.
Status
The status of the job:
•
Completed.
•
Technician clocked in.
•
Open.
•
Quotation.
•
Pending warranty response.
•
Pending warranty submittal.
•
Technician clocked out.
•
Waiting on parts.
•
Not completed/this invoice.
Product Information (repeats for each product on a job)
Product#/
Description
The number and description of the product needed to repair the model.
SC
The system-generated supply code. The supply code only appears if the product is backordered and is being replenished using one of these replenishment methods:
•
B - Purchase.
•
T - Transfer.
•
D - Direct.
•
M - Manufacture.
•
O - Outplant.
•
E - Emergency purchase order.
•
R - Emergency transfer.
Qty-Opn
The quantity of the product needed to repair the model.
U/M
The product’s unit of measure.
Cost
The cost of the product.
Extension
The cost of the total quantity orders (Qty-Opn x Cost).
Price
The price for one unit of measure.
Extension
The price for the total quantity ordered (Qty-Opn x Price).
GP%
The percentage of gross profit for the product.
Job and Order Totals (for Parts, Labor, Misc, and Total)
Cost
The cost of the parts, labor, miscellaneous fee, or total.
Pct
The percent of the total for parts, labor, and miscellaneous fees.
Price
The price of the product, labor, miscellaneous fee, or total.
GP%
The percentage of gross profit for the product, labor, miscellaneous fee, or total.
