Creating Shop Charges
When you create a pay type, you can add shop charges to include each time a job is created with that pay type (refer to Creating Pay Types for more information).
Auto Shop Charges in Shop Option Parameters (refer to Setting Shop Repair Parameters) indicates if a separate shop charge is automatically created when you complete the shop order (refer to Completing the Order):
•
Y - Automatically create a shop charge for each job on the order, without prompting. A separate charge generates for each job and is determined by the pay type assigned to the job. Refer to Creating Pay Types.
•
N - Do not automatically create shop charges. The Shop Repair Job Entry screens appears when you press F6-Complete from the Shop Repair Recap Information screen, so that you can manually add each shop charge to each job.
To create a shop charge:
1
Select Shop Repair > Maintenance > Shop Charges Maintenance to open the Shop Charges Maintenance screen.
2
Enter a Charge Code (up to 8 alphanumeric characters).
3
Enter a Description of the charge code.
4
Choose a Charge Type:
•
$ - Dollar. The shop charge is a flat fee.
•
* - Percent. The shop charge is based on a percentage of parts, labor, or both.
5
For Amount:
•
If you chose $ (dollar) for Charge Type, enter the dollar amount of the flat fee.
•
If you chose * (percent) for Charge Type, enter the percent you want to charge.
6
If you chose * (percent) for Charge Type, choose a Basis for the charge:
•
B - Parts and Labor. The percent charge is based on all the parts and labor required to complete the job.
•
L - Labor. The percent charge is based only on the labor required to complete the job.
•
P - Parts. The percent charge is based only on the parts required to complete the job.
7
Enter the Maximum amount that may be billed to the customer for this charge type.
8
Enter the Product that should appear on the shop repair order to represent this shop charge. This must be a valid product number set up in Product Maintenance.
9
Press F2-Save.
