Setting Up Job Codes
If your company uses standard jobs (e.g,. oil change), you can set up the job as a standard job code that can be pulled into a shop repair order. When you use the job code on an order, you can edit the information as needed. For more information, refer to Creating the Job.
To set up a job code:
1
Select Shop Repair > Maintenance > Job Code Maintenance to open the Shop Job Code Maintenance screen.
2
For Job Code, enter a code (up to 10 alphanumeric characters) to represent a type of job performed by your shop.
3
If the job code applies to a specific part, enter the part number in Model Number.
4
For Job Description, enter a brief description of the job code.
5
Enter the Pay Type to identify how the job should be billed. Refer to Creating Pay Types for more information.
6
If the job is associated with a repair type, enter a Repair Group. Refer to Identifying Repair Groups for more information.
7
For Repair Amount, choose one of the following:
•
If you want to charge the customer a set fee for this job no matter what parts are needed or what labor is required, enter the total Repair Amount. The prices are recalculated to total this amount.
•
If you want the price of the job to depend on the products used and the labor required, leave Repair Amount blank. The price of the job is the price of the products and labor used to complete the job.
8
Enter the Repair Amount Type to indicate what costs are included in the Repair Amount, if applicable. Choose from the following:
•
L - Labor.
•
P - Parts.
•
B or null - Both labor and parts (default).
9
For Est. Repair Time, enter the total time that this job should take to complete. This information is used to calculate efficiency by job. The default is based on the time entered for a setup job or service bulletin.
10
For Tech, enter each technician that you want to assign to the job (refer to Setting up Technicians). As technicians clock in and out of the job, the Est. hrs and Act. hrs update automatically. If you assign a technician, he/she must be authorized to work on jobs with the correct repair group.
If Require Tech On Job in System Options for Shop/Repair is:
•
Y - You must assign a technician before the technician can clock into a job.
•
N - You do not have to assign a technician. Any technicians that clock into the job are automatically added to the list of assigned technicians.
11
To add default problem text, press F5-Problem, enter the text and press F2-Save.
12
To add default solution text, press F6-Solution, enter the text and press F2-Save.
13
To add products that are standard for this job code, press F7-Products to open the Shop Job Code Product screen.
14
Enter the first Product to add to the job code.
15
For Code, select one of the following:
•
R - The product is required for the job.
•
O - The product is optional for the job.
16
Enter a Price if you want to use a price other than regular pricing for the product.
17
Enter the Quantity of the product that is required for the job.
18
Repeat steps 14-17 to add additional products to the job code.
19
Press F2-Save to return to the Shop Job Code Maintenance screen.
20
To enter internal comments, press F8-Comments. Enter the text and press F2-Save.
21
When you are finished editing the job code, press F2-Save.
