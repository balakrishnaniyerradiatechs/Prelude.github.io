Entering Billable Time for Technicians
As a technician completes a job, you can use Technician Job Billing Entry to bill the customer for the time spent on the job and to enter the time for each job that a technician worked on for a single day. If more than one entry is made for a job at the same hourly billing rate, the hours are accumulated to show as one line item on the invoice.
To enter billable time:
1
Select Shop Repair > Shop Repair Time Clock > Technician Job Billing Entry to open the Technician Job Billing Entry screen.
2
For Technician#, enter the technician number. Refer to Setting up Technicians.
3
Enter the Date for which you are entering time worked. Defaults to the current date.
4
For Time (HH:MM), enter the total number of hours the technician worked on this date.
5
For Document/Job Code, enter the order number or the job code that the technician worked on. You can use F3 to search by:
•
Shop order number.
•
Shop code (refer to Setting up Time Clock Codes).
•
Regular orders.
•
Shop quotes.
•
Work orders.
6
For Job, if you entered a shop repair order number, enter the job number that the technician worked on. If the technician only worked on one job for that order, it automatically appears. This field does not apply for shop codes.
7
Product Number defaults based on settings in Shop Labor Type Maintenance (refer to Identifying Shop Labor Types). This field does not apply for shop codes.
8
For Type, enter the type of time that should be charged:
•
R - Regular time.
•
O - Overtime.
•
P - Premium time.
The amount charged for each type of time depends on the hourly pay rate that you entered for this technician in Technician Maintenance (refer to Setting up Technicians).
9
The method to enter Time worked depends on system options. Refer to the following:
•
If Enter Hours Only in System Options For Shop/Repair is set to Y, enter the total number of hours worked on the job in Hours. The Start and End time are automatically calculated.
•
If Enter Hours Only in System Options For Shop/Repair is set to N, enter the Start and End time that the technician worked on the job.
Time is calculated automatically based on the difference between the start and end time.
10
Repeat steps 6-9 for each job.
11
Press F2-Save to complete the entry.
If Time (HH:MM) and Total Time do not match, a warning message appears. Press ENTER and correct the discrepancy.
