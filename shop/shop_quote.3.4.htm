Entering a Job
After you open a shop quote (refer to Starting or Editing a Shop Repair Quote), you can enter one or multiple jobs. A job refers to a task to complete for the model on the quote and is assigned a job number (e.g., A, B, C, D). You can enter problem information for each job and indicate which parts are needed to complete the job.
You can access the Shop Repair Job Entry screen appears either:
•
The first time you press F2-Save from the Quote Repair Order Entry screen.
•
When you press F7-Job in the Quote Repair Order Entry screen.
To create a new job:
1
Press ENTER on the word NEW in Job Number.
2
For Description, choose one of the following:
•
Enter a job code (refer to Setting Up Job Codes for more information).
•
Enter a brief description of the work to be done for this job.
•
Create a new job code. Type the code and press ENTER. The following message appears:
Job code does not exist. Do you wish to create a new job code?
Choose Yes. The Shop Job Code Maintenance screen appears. Refer to Setting Up Job Codes for more information.
3
If the job code has any optional products assigned to it, the Parts Entry screen appears. Enter how many of each of the optional products you want to use in Quantity and press F2-Save.
4
For Pay Type, indicate how the job will be billed (e.g,. CB - customer bill, NC - no charge). Refer to Creating Pay Types).
5
For Repair Group, choose the type of repair for the job (e.g., 01 - maintenance, 02 - transmission). Refer to Identifying Repair Groups.
6
For Status of Job?, accept the default.
7
For Repair Amount, choose one of the following:
•
If you want to charge the customer a set fee for this job no matter what parts are needed or what labor is required, enter the amount in Repair Amount. The prices are recalculated to total this amount.
•
If you want the price of the job to depend on the products used and the labor required, leave Repair Amount blank. The price of the job is the price of the products and labor used to complete the job.
8
For Repair Amount Type, indicate what costs are included in the Repair Amount. Choose from the following:
•
L - Labor.
•
P - Parts.
•
B or null - Both labor and parts (default).
9
For Est. Repair Time, enter the total time that this job should take to complete. This information is used to calculate efficiency by job. The default is based on the time entered for a setup job or service bulletin.
10
For Up Sale?, indicate if this job is the result of an up-sale from the original job. An up-sale is when you offer a more expensive service than the one originally requested. Choose from the following:
•
Y - The job was initiated as the result of an up-sale.
•
N - The job is not an up-sale.
11
For Tech, enter each technician that you want to assign to the job. If you assign a technician, he/she must be authorized to work on jobs with the correct repair group. For more information on technicians, refer to Setting up Technicians.
If Require Tech On Job in System Options For Shop/Repair is:
•
Y - You must assign a technician before the technician can clock into a job.
•
N - You do not have to assign a technician. Any technicians that clock into the job are automatically added to the list of assigned technicians.
12
If you need to enter a more complete description of what needs to be repaired, press F5-Problem to open the Problem Notes screen. When you complete the job, you can press F6-Solution to enter the solution text. Both of these may already be defaulted if this job is based on a job code or service bulletin.
If Require Problem Notes in Shop Repair Parameters is set to Y, you must enter problem and solution text for a job. Refer to Setting Shop Repair Parameters.
13
If the job is for a warranty repair, press F7-Warranty to enter the warranty information. Refer to Entering Warranty Information for a Job.
14
When you are finished entering data for the job, press F2-Save.
