Establishing Warranty Information for Products
If you give a warranty to your customers for the products on a shop repair order, you can set up the warranty information for a product in Product Maintenance. Warranty periods can be set by a period of time or by the number of hours recorded on a piece of equipment.
To establish warranty information for a product:
1
Select Inventory > Maintenance > Product Maintenance > Product Maintenance > F5-Add Info > F5-Add Info to open the Additional Product Maintenance Information screen.
If the warranty is based on a period of time, go to step 2. If the warranty is based on the number of hours a product is in use, go to step 5.
2
For Warranty Period Unit, indicate how warranty time periods are measured. Choose from the following:
•
D - Days.
•
M - Months.
•
Y - Years.
3
For Wty-Per, if you entered a value for Warranty Period Unit, enter the number of days, months, or years that the product is under warranty.
4
If the product has a warranty for labor, enter the number of days, months, or years that labor for this product is under warranty in Wty-Labor.
5
If the warranty period is based on the number of hours a piece of equipment was used, enter the number hours in Wty-Hours. For example, the warranty period could be defined as one year or 1500 hours, whichever comes first. In Shop Repair Order Entry, a warning message alerts you that the unit being repaired is still under warranty.
6
Press F2-Save to save the warranty information
