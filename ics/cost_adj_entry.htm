Cost Adjustment Entry screen
The Cost Adjustment Entry screen allows you to adjust the cost of an item in inventory. The cost that is changed depends how your G/L Cost flag is set in Company Maintenance (e.g., standard cost, average cost, or FIFO cost). An increase in cost debits the Inventory account and credits the offsetting Cost Adjustment account set up for the reason code. A decrease in cost credits the Inventory account and debits the Cost Adjustment account.
Menu Path: Inventory > Inventory Adjustments > Cost Adjustment Entry
Field and Function Key Descriptions
Field/Function Key
Description
Adjustment Number
The cost adjustment number assigned by the system.
Entry Date
The date the cost is adjusted. The fiscal period displays to the right.
Product
Line 1: The product number.
Line 2: The product description.
Line 3: The second line of description for the product.
Warehouse
The warehouse where cost is being adjusted.
Quantity On Hand
The quantity of the product currently in stock.
Current Cost
The current cost of the quantity in stock.
Revised Cost
The new cost for the product. An increase in cost debits the Inventory account and credits the Cost Adjustment account. A decrease in cost credits the Inventory account and debits the Cost Adjustment account.
Cost Adj Reason
The cost adjustment reason code to explain why the cost is adjusted. Refer to Establishing Cost Adjustment Reason Codes.
F2-Save
Saves the adjusted cost.
