End of Year Procedure
You must run the End of Year Procedure at the end of the calendar year to roll your current year’s data into previous year buckets. The generated information is not related to your general ledger or sales analysis data.
The data that is moved includes the following:
•
Product Statistics (refer to Product Usage).
•
Customer Statistics (Refer to Customer Maintenance > F9-Statistics).
•
Vendor Statistics (refer to “Rebuilding Vendor Performance Statistics” in the Purchasing documentation).
•
Vendor Line Statistics (refer to “Vendor Line Maintenance” in the Purchasing documentation).
•
Contact Statistics (refer to “Customer Contact Management Maintenance” in the Marketing documentation).
Run the End of Month Calendar Close (refer to End of Month Calendar Close) before or after running the End of Year Procedure, but not at the same time.
Do not run the End of Year Procedure more than once per year. If you run it more than once, the date and history moves to 2 years previous, leaving you with no previous year data. It is best to assign only one person to run year end to prevent this from happening. If it does occur, contact Activant to correct the data.
Do not break out of the process once you begin. If you do cancel the process after it starts, contact Activant to correct the data.
Make sure that all distribution (invoicing, shipping, receiving) activity for the calendar year is completed and processed.
To run the End of Year Procedure:
1
Select Inventory > Processes > End of Year Procedure. The following message displays:
The Procedure will roll all data that is
stored on a calendar monthly basis from
the current year to previous year. It will
then clear all current year buckets to
prepare for the data processed in the new year. This procedure needs to be run at the end of the calendar year (12/31/##).
The last date Executed Was 12/31/05
Continue With Procedure?
2
Choose from the following:
•
No - Cancels the procedure.
•
Yes - Allows you to start the End of Year Procedure now or to enter a time to start the process later, preferably after all users are off of the system.
3
When you press ENTER, the process begins. You can press ESC to exit the program without beginning the procedure.
