Sales Order Commitments screen
The Sales Order Commitments screen displays a list of open sales orders for a product that is not invoiced.
Menu Path: Inventory > Inquiries > Inventory Inquiries > Sales Orders (under Commitments) > F5-Review
Field and Function Key Descriptions
Field/Function Key
Description
Order#
The sales order number.
N
An asterisk (*) indicates that notes exists on the sales order.
Customer
The number for the customer who purchased the product.
Description
The customer description.
Req-Date
The date (from the sales order header) that the customer requires the order.
QtyOrd
The original quantity ordered by the customer on this sales order.
U/M
The unit of measure as entered on the sales order.
Qty-Opn
The quantity not yet invoiced.
Sales Order Commit Days (unlabeled field)
Indicates if the pick date is in the future:
•
F - The line item on the sales order is set up as a future (e.g., the required date is too far in the future for the pick ticket to print).
•
P - The line item on the sales order is set up as a future and inventory is not allocated. The item has been considered for purchasing in advance to make sure it is in stock before the pick ticket date. This would occur if Maximum LDT Commit Days in Order Processing Parameters has a value entered to indicate the number of commitment days.
Qty-Pck
The quantity of the item available for picking.
Qty-Shp
The quantity of the item shipped (confirmed) on this sales order release.
Qty-BO
The backordered quantity of the item on this sales order.
S
The supply code that appears if the product is on backorder. The code varies depending on the product’s replenishment path and product settings. Possible supply codes include:
•
B - Purchase order.
•
E - Emergency purchase order.
•
T - Transfer.
•
R - Emergency transfer
•
W - Work order.
•
D - Direct ship purchase order.
Linkage
The document that the backordered item on the sales order is linked to. The prefix indicates the document type:
•
P - Purchase order.
•
T - Transfer.
•
W - Work order.
Order Type
The sales order type entered in the sales order header:
•
01 - Regular.
•
02 - Ship complete.
•
03 - Tag & hold.
•
04 - Direct ship.
•
05 - Counter invoice.
•
06 - Confirmation.
•
07 - Regular consignment.
•
08 - Confirmation consign.
•
09 - Return goods.
•
10 - Repair orders.
•
12 - Invoice when complete.
•
13 - Ship backorder complete.
•
14 - Ship line item complete
•
15 - Show order.
•
16 - Order projection.
•
17 - Manufacturer rep.
•
18 - Crib replenishment.
CH
Indicates if the sales order is on credit hold:
•
CH - The order is on credit hold.
•
Blank - The order is not on credit hold.
C
Indicates if the product is part of a kit:
•
K - The product is a kit parent.
•
C - The product is a kit component.
•
Blank - The product is not part of a kit.
F2-Finish
Closes the screen and returns to the Inventory Inquiry.
F5-Review Order
Opens the Sales Order Display screen to allow you to view detail information on the highlighted sales order.
F7-Log
Opens the Order Log Inquiry screen to allow you to view all transactions that have occurred for this sales order.
F9-Goto
Allows you to enter a line number, page number, or reference number to go directly to.
