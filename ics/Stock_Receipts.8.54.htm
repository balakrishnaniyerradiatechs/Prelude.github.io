Allocating Inventory Automatically
To automatically allocate received material to open sales orders, transfers, and work orders:
1
Select Option Parameters > Inventory Parameters to open the Inventory Parameters screen.
2
For Allocate By Age, indicate if you want to fill backorders by order date. Choose from the following:
•
Y - Allocate received material to the oldest orders first if all backorders cannot be filled.
•
N - Do not fill backorders if all orders cannot be filled. A report prints and you must allocate backorders manually. Refer to Allocating Inventory Manually.
3
For Auto Allocate Flag, determine if inventory is automatically allocated. Choose from the following:
•
Y - Inventory automatically allocates when the on-hand quantity is increased (e.g., stock receipts, stock transfer receipts, work orders, and inventory adjustments). Manual allocation is still required for increases in stock due to credit memos or consignment returns.
•
N - Inventory does not automatically allocate. You must allocate backorders manually. Refer to Allocating Inventory Manually.
4
For Auto Unallocate Flag, choose from the following:
•
Y - Inventory automatically un-allocates from committed documents when the available quantity falls below the committed quantity due to an inventory adjustment or a cycle count entry. Line items do not automatically unallocate if they meet the following criteria:
-
Sales orders with linked items, a supply code, a released pick ticket, or if the order type is 04, 05, 06, 08, 09, or 10.
-
Work orders with linked items, a supply code, or if the work order has an order status other than Entered.
-
Transfers with a linked item, a supply code, or a released pick ticket.
•
N - Inventory remains allocated when the on-hand quantity is decreased.
5
For Allocation Method, if you set Allocate By Age to Y, choose one of following methods to allocate received inventory:
•
R - Order date with priorities. Allocates from oldest to newest required date using the following priorities:
-
1st - Sales orders.
-
2nd - Work orders.
-
3rd - Transfers.
•
D - Order date only. Allocates from the oldest to the newest order date, regardless of document type.
•
O - Order date with priorities. Allocates from oldest to newest order date using the following priorities:
-
1st - Sales orders.
-
2nd - Work orders.
-
3rd - Transfers.
•
Q - Required date only. Allocates from oldest to newest required date, regardless of document type.
6
For Suppress Allocation Rpt, indicate if you want to suppress the printing of the Inventory Available for Backorders Report after the inventory is allocated:
•
Y - Do not print the report.
•
N - Print the report.
7
Press F5-Add Info to open the Inventory Add. Parameters screen.
8
For Alloc Stock to N/S, indicate if you want to allocate stock to line items on sales orders, transfers, or work orders that have a B supply code and no linkage:
•
Y - Allocate stock to items if the line item has a B supply code and no linkage.
•
N - Do not allocate stock to items with a B supply code and no linkage. You must manually allocate backorders. Refer to Allocating Inventory Manually.
9
Press F2-Save twice to save the allocation information.
Once you allocate inventory, you can release pick tickets for the filled backorders. Refer to Releasing Pick Tickets.
Credit Hold
If Credit Hold/Unallocate Inv is set to Y In Accounts Receivable Parameters, received inventory does not allocate to sales orders on credit hold. When the sales order is released from credit hold, inventory allocates based on the available quantity of each item at that time.
