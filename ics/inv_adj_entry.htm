Inventory Adjustment Entry screen
The Inventory Adjustment Entry screen allows you to manually adjust on-hand inventory balances.
Menu Path: Inventory > Inventory Adjustments > Inventory Adjustments Entry
Field and Function Key Descriptions
Field/Function Key
Description
I/A Number
The inventory adjustment transaction number.
Entry Date
The date of the adjustment. The fiscal period displays to the right.
Warehouse
The warehouse where the product quantity is being adjusted.
Adj.Type
The adjustment type to determine how inventory is adjusted:
•
INV - Regular onhand inventory. Enter a positive or a negative quantity to adjust inventory.
•
DMG - Damaged inventory. Enter a positive quantity to increase the damaged inventory amount for the product and decrease the on-hand inventory. Enter a negative quantity to decrease the damaged inventory amount and increase the on-hand amount.
•
SCR - Scrapped/lost inventory. Enter a positive quantity to increase the scrapped inventory for the product and decrease the on-hand inventory. Enter a negative quantity to decrease the scrapped inventory amount and increase the on-hand amount.
•
WTY - Warranty inventory. Enter a positive quantity to move the item from the on-hand quantity into warranty inventory. Refer to Creating Warranty Tags in Inventory Adjustment Entry for information on adjusting warranty inventory.
Product Number
The product number to adjust. The Inventory Adjustment Line Detail screen opens to allow you to enter the quantity, cost, and an inventory adjustment reason code.
Description
The description of the product displays.
N
An asterisk (*) indicates that notes were entered for the line item when inventory was adjusted.
Quantity
The positive or negative quantity to adjust.
U/M
The unit of measure for the item.
Cost
The cost of the item.
Quantity
The total quantity of the adjusted products.
Weight
The total weight of the adjusted products.
Cost
The total cost of the adjusted products.
F2-Save
Saves the adjusted inventory quantities.
F4-Del
Deletes the inventory adjustment.
F5-Header
Opens the Inventory Adjustments Header screen to allow you to enter a ship via code and a reference number if the product is shipping out of the warehouse.
F9-Goto Line
Allows you to enter a line number, page number, or reference number to go directly to.
