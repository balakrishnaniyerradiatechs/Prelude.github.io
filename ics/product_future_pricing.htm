Product Future Pricing Screen
If you have prices that will not take effect until a future date, you can use the Product Future Pricing screen in Product Maintenance. You can enter the prices directly or calculate each price/cost as a percentage of another price/cost column. Nightly Processing activates future price/cost changes on the effective date.
Menu Path: Inventory > Maintenance > Product Maintenance > Product Maintenance > F7-Pricing > Action Bar > Future
Field & Function Key Descriptions
Field/Function Key
Description
Future
The future price for this column. Leave blank if using Percent and Basis to calculate the values. If you do enter a Percent and Basis, the calculated value displays in this column when you press F5-Recompute.
Percent
The percentage or multiplier to use with a Basis column to calculate the price or cost for each of the columns. Leave blank if you entered a price under Current and want to maintain the price manually.
The calculated values depend on how the Prc/Cst Basis Calc Method flag is set in Inventory Parameters.
If Prc/Cst Basis Calc Method is set to G for gross profit, enter the gross profit percent that you want to make for this product for this price level. The Percent is used with the cost column indicated in Basis (refer to the Basis codes below) to calculate the price.
For example:
•
Replacement Cost is 10.00.
•
If you want Price 1 to provide 20% gross profit above Replacement Cost, enter 20 under Percent and R under Basis.
•
When you press F5-Recompute, 10.00 is divided by .80 to come up with a Price 1 of 12.50. Gross profit is 20%.
If Prc/Cst Basis Calc Method is set to M for markup, enter the markup percent that you want for this product at this price level. The Percent is used with the cost column indicated in Basis to calculate the price.
For example:
•
Replacement Cost is 10.00.
•
If you want Price 1 to be 20% above the value of Replacement, enter 20 under Percent and R under Basis.
•
When you press F5-Recompute, 10 is multiplied by 1.20 to come up with a Price 1 of 12.00. The gross profit is 16.6%.
Basis
The price/cost column used to calculate new price/cost values if using a percentage or multiplier for Percent:
•
0 - Price column 0 (Suggested retail price).
•
1 - Price column 1.
•
2 - Price column 2.
•
3 - Price column 3.
•
4 - Price column 4.
•
5 - Price column 5.
•
R - Replacement cost.
•
A - Average cost.
•
S - Standard cost.
•
O - Cost for sales.
List, Price 1 - Price 5
Default customer prices. Each customer is assigned a Price Code in Customer Maintenance to use if special pricing does not exist for this product. You can set these prices at the product level to use company wide or by warehouse in Product Warehouse Maintenance (refer to Entering Prices/Costs for a Product - by Warehouse).
Fut Repl Cst
The future replacement cost of the item for all warehouses and vendors. You can override this cost at the product/warehouse level.
Fut Std Cst
If you use standard cost as the accounting cost for your company (G/L Cost flag set to S in Company Maintenance), you can maintain the future cost at the product level or the product warehouse level. You must set up general ledger accounts to post the differences between the standard costs and the actual costs used in stock receipts, transfers, and vendor invoice reconciliation. Also, standard costs from all the components on a bill of material can be rolled up into one product.
Fut Cst/Sls
The future cost for sales orders and quotes. You may want to use this for calculating gross profit for commission.
Fut Dir Cst
The future direct ship purchase order for the item. If this field is blank, Fut Repl Cst is used.
Eff-Date
The date the future prices go into effect. Nightly Processing activates future price and/or cost changes based on this date. If Nightly Processing runs before midnight, move the effective date up one day.
Exp-Date
The date this set of future prices expires.
Last Eff-Date
The date the last time future prices became effective.
F2-Save
Saves the future pricing information.
F5-Recompute Prices
Recalculates the prices and costs if you used Percent and Basis to calculate future values.
