On The Water Maintenance screen
The On The Water Maintenance screen allows you to create an on-the-water purchase order for multiple items and purchase orders imported from an overseas vendor. The new purchase order holds the material in OTW status until it arrives in your warehouse. The original purchase orders stay open until all products are moved to the OTW purchase order.
Refer to Receiving On-The-Water Merchandise for more information.
Menu Path: Inventory > Stock Receipts > On the Water Maintenance
Field and Function Key Descriptions
Field/Function Key
Description
Reference #
The reference number that becomes the Vendor Order # for the purchase order you are creating. You can enter your own reference number or enter N for a system-generated number. You can use this number in Import Cost Inquiry if you want to inquire on import costs associated with a shipment.
Vendor Number
The number for the overseas vendor.
Warehouse
The number for the warehouse receiving the material.
Ship Date
The date the merchandise was loaded on the ship. This date displays in the header notes section of the on-the-water purchase order.
Expected Date
The date the merchandise is expected to arrive at the warehouse. This date becomes the delivery date for the purchase order.
Num Containers
The number of containers in the shipment.
Invoice Number
The vendor’s invoice number. Displays in the header notes section of the purchase order.
Bill of Lading #
The Bill of Lading number. Displays in the header notes section of the purchase order.
Direct Ship
Indicates if the merchandise is shipping directly to the customer:
•
Y - This is a direct ship (type 02) purchase order. You are prompted for a P/O number. Once you enter the order number, the line items from the direct ship PO automatically display. You can only change the cost of the line items.
•
N - This is not a direct ship order.
Ship Via Code
Indicates how the material shipped from the vendor.
Product Number
The products to include on this On The Water Purchase Order. Use one of the following methods to enter products:
•
Enter the product numbers to include on the new purchase order. The items must exist on an open purchase order for this vendor. If more than one open purchase order contains this item, the oldest order is used first. Any comments associated with each item from the original purchase order are included.
•
Press F6-Enter P/Os to enter open purchase orders from this vendor.
Description
The product description.
Link?
Determines if the product is linked to another transaction:
•
Y - The product is linked to a sales order, work order, or transfer. You can change this to Y if you want to link the product to an open order. You must enter the sales order, work order, or transfer number.
•
N - The product is not linked to another transaction.
Qty
The quantity to include on the OTW purchase order.
Cost
The cost of the item from the original purchase order. You can change the cost unless the Link? flag is set to Y.
Totals
The total quantity and total cost extensions.
F2-Save
Saves the OTW purchase order information and allows you to update OTW quantities or to suspend the order.
D4-Del
Deletes the OTW purchase order entry and purchase order quantities are not updated.
F5-Import Costs
Opens the Import Cost Entry screen to allow you to add or edit any import costs such as freight, duty, or brokerage fees.
F6-Enter P/O’s
Opens the OTW P/O# Entry screen to allow you to enter purchase orders from this vendor. All open items on the purchase orders are pulled into the OTW purchase order. You can changes costs and quantities as long as the item is not linked.
F7-Chg P/O Qty
Opens the OTW P/O Quantity Entry screen to allow you to adjust the new quantity. This function key only appears if you move the cursor to the Qty field. Refer to Changing Quantities in On The Water Maintenance.
