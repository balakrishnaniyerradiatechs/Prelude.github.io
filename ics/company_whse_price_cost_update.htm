Company & Whse Price/Cost Update screen
The Company & Warehouse Price/Cost Update screen allows you to enter future and current prices and costs for a vendor, vendor line, product line, or price group. The new prices are calculated based on a percentage markup of a designated cost or a percentage of a designated price.
Refer to Updating Price/Cost Using a Percent Markup for more information.
Menu Path: Inventory > Processes > Price/Cost Update Procedure > Company & Whse Price/Cost% Basis Update
Field and Function Key Descriptions
Field/Function Key
Description
Inventory Or Catalog
Indicates whether you are updating the inventory or catalog file:
•
I - Updates the inventory file (only products that exist in your system as stock, non-stock, or consignment).
•
C - Updates the catalog file (include products sold by the vendor that you do not stock).
Enter File, Product (P), Warehouse (W)
Indicates how prices are updated:
•
P - Recomputes prices by product.
•
W - Recomputes prices by warehouse.
Warehouse
The warehouse where the prices and costs are being updated.
Vendor
The vendor number if updating by vendor.
Vendor Line
The vendor line number if updating by vendor line.
Product Line
The product line number if updating by product line.
Price Group
The price group number if updating by price group.
%
The percentage of another price or a percentage above a cost to use to calculate the new value for each price or cost.
Basis
The price or cost column that the above percent is calculated from:
•
0 - Price column 0 (Suggested retail price).
•
1 - Price column 1.
•
2 - Price column 2.
•
3 - Price column 3.
•
4 - Price column 4.
•
5 - Price column 5.
•
R - Replacement cost.
•
A - Average cost.
•
S - Standard cost.
•
O - Cost for sales.
Suggested Retail
The percentage and basis to use to calculate the suggested retail price.
Price 1
The percentage and basis used to calculate price 1.
Price 2
The percentage and basis used to calculate price 2.
Price 3
The percentage and basis used to calculate price 3.
Price 4
The percentage and basis used to calculate price 4.
Price 5
The percentage and basis used to calculate price 5.
Replacement Cost
The percentage and basis used to calculate replacement cost.
Standard Cost
The percentage and basis used to calculate standard cost.
Cost for Sales
The percentage and basis used to calculate the cost for sales orders.
Direct Ship Cost
The percentage and basis used to calculate direct ship cost.
Recalculate Prices
The prices to recalculate:
•
C - Current.
•
F- Future.
•
B - Both.
•
N - None.
Effective Date
The date the prices go into effect if you chose F for Recalculate Price.
F2-Save
Recalculates the prices and cost based on the calculation method you chose.
F5-Add Prices
Allows you to update any additional price columns if the Maximum Price Columns field in Inventory Parameters is set to a number greater than 5.
