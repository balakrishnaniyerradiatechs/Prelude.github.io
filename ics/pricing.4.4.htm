Establishing a Unit of Measure
The unit of measure table defines how a product’s unit of measure impacts pricing, costing, and inventory. If you buy, stock, and sell a product in different units of measure, you can enter a factor to divide or multiply to convert the units of measure.
You cannot use the U/M table if you use the Stock/Buy Ratio field on the Product Maintenance Screen.
To set up units of measure for a product:
1
Select Inventory > Maintenance > Product Maintenance > Product Maintenance to open the Product Maintenance Screen.
2
Enter the Product.
3
Before you set up the unit of measure table, you must set up the default units of measure for Buy, Stock, and Price:
•
For Buy, enter the unit of measure that you use to purchase the item from your vendor. However, if the product-vendor level has a unit of measure (refer to Maintaining a Product by Warehouse/Vendor), that unit of measure is used.
•
For Stock, enter the unit of measure that you use to stock the item in inventory. Enter all costs at the product and product/warehouse level in the terms of the Stock unit of measure.
•
For Price, enter the unit of measure that you use to sell the product to your customers on sales orders and quotes. You can override this in the U/M Table Maintenance screen to allow multiple units of measure for sales and web-based orders. Enter all prices at the product and product/warehouse level in the terms of the Price unit of measure.
4
Enter Y for U/M Table. The U/M Table Maintenance screen opens.
5
For Allow U/M Override, indicate if you can change the unit of measure on all transactions:
•
Y - Allow overrides to the unit of measure.
•
N - Do not allow overrides to the unit of measure.
6
For Default S/O U/M, enter the default unit of measure for sales orders. If you leave this blank, sales orders use the Price unit of measure from the Product Maintenance screen.
7
For Default Web U/M, enter the default unit of measure for web orders. If you leave this blank, web orders use the Price unit of measure from the Product Maintenance screen.
8
For U/M Code, enter the unit of measure codes for the product. Press F3 to list available units of measure.
9
For Price/Cost, enter the factor to divide or multiply prices/costs by to convert from one unit of measure to another. Leave this field blank if a factor exists in the company unit of measure table and the Price/Cost factor is the same for this product.
10
For Inventory, enter the number to divide or multiply an inventory quantity by to convert from one unit of measure to another. Leave this field blank if it exists in the company unit of measure table and the Inventory factor is the same for this product.
11
For Valid in SOE, determine if the unit of measure is allowed in Sales Order Entry:
•
Y - The unit of measure is allowed in Sales Order Entry.
•
N - The unit of measure is not allowed in Sales Order Entry.
12
For Qty Per U/M, enter the quantity that applies for each unit of measure. This field is optional and does not affect calculations.
13
Press F2-Save to save the unit of measure information for the product.
14
Press F2-Save again from the Product Maintenance screen.

Example 1 – Price U/M is different from Stock U/M
A sales order is for 2 DZ and the Stock U/M is EA; the committed inventory must be converted.
Inventory Committed = Selling Quantity x Inventory Factor for Price U/M
Inventory Committed = 2 x 12 = 24 EA
Example 2 – Selling U/M is different from Price U/M
A sales order is for 24 EA and the Price U/M is DZ; the price must be converted from DZ to EA.
Price Quantity = Selling Quantity / Price/Cost Factor for Price U/M
Price Quantity = 24 EA / 12 = 2 DZ
Price per EA = (Price Quantity x Price)/Selling Quantity
Price per EA = (2 DZ x $48)/24 = $4 EA
Example 3 – Buy U/M is different from Stock U/M
A P/O is for 1 CS and the Stock U/M is EA; the cost and inventory must be converted from EA to CS.
Cost Quantity = Purchasing Quantity x Price/Cost Factor for the Buy U/M
Cost Quantity = 1 CS x 48 = 48 EA
Cost per CS = (Cost Quantity x Cost)/Purchasing Quantity
Cost per CS = (48 EA x $2)/1 = $96 CS
Inventory Received = Purchasing Quantity x Price/Cost Factor for the Buy U/M
Inventory Received = 1 CS x 48 = 48 EA

Example 4 – Selling U/M is different from Price U/M
A sales order is for 100 E and the Price U/M is C; the price must be converted from C to E.
Price Quantity = Selling Quantity / Price/Cost Factor for Price U/M
Price Quantity = 100 E / 100 = 1 C
Price per E = (Price Quantity x Price)/ Selling Quantity
Price per E = (1 C x 40) / 100 = $0.40 E
Example 5 – Purchasing U/M is different from Stock U/M
A purchase order is for 100 E and the Stock U/M is C; the cost must be converted from C to E.
Cost Quantity = Purchasing Quantity / Price/Cost Factor for the Stock U/M
Cost Quantity = 100 E / 100 = 1 C
Cost per E = (Cost Quantity x Cost)/Purchasing Quantity
Cost per E = (1 C x $20)/100 = $0.20 E
