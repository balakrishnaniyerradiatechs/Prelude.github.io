YTD Shrinkage Report
The YTD Shrinkage report displays products with a change in on-hand value that is a result of physical inventory counts. Refer to Physical Inventory.
Menu Path: Inventory > Reports > Inventory Valuation Reports > YTD Shrinkage Report
Selection Criteria
Use the following criteria to limit the information to print on the report:
•
Division. The highest possible grouping in your company (e.g., Eastern Division, Western Division).
•
Region. A subset of Division - if Division is used (e.g., Southeast Region, Northwestern Region).
•
Warehouse. The warehouse(s) to include on the report.
•
Department. A subset of warehouse. Typically used to separate expenses within a warehouse (e.g., accounting, sales, warehouse).
•
Cost Center. A subset of Department - if Department is used. Typically used to separate expenses within a warehouse or department (e.g., shipping, receiving, outside sales, inside sales).
•
Product. The product(s) to include on the report. Leave blank to include all products.
•
Product Type. The products type(s) to include on the report. Leave blank to include all product types or choose from the following:
-
N - Rental item.
-
F - Finished good.
-
R - Raw material.
-
B - Both finished and raw material.
-
E - Expense item.
-
U - Used item.
Column Descriptions
Column Heading
Description
Product Number
The product number.
Description
The description of the product.
Description Two
The second line of description for the product, if applicable.
Ave-Cost
The average cost of the product.
On-Hand
The current on-hand quantity of the product after the physical inventory counts are entered.
YTD-Shrk
The year-to-date shrinkage in quantity of the product as a result of an adjustment made during physical inventory. A positive number indicates that the inventory adjustment lowered the quantity in inventory. A negative number indicates that the inventory adjustment added to the quantity in inventory.
YTD-$Shrk
The year-to-date shrinkage in value of the product (Ave-Cost x YTD-Shrk) as a result of an adjustment made during the physical inventory. A positive number indicates a reduced inventory value. A negative number indicates an increased inventory value.
