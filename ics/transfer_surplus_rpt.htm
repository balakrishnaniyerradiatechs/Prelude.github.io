Transferring Surplus Inventory
If it is your responsibility to keep the correct inventory levels in multiple warehouses, you can use the Transfer Surplus Report & Update to calculate and transfer surplus inventory between warehouses. You can either generate the transfer directly from the report or use the calculated information to create a manual transfer.
If you use the EOQ purchase method to purchase stock, the surplus quantity is the EOQ plus the line point. If you use the min/max purchase method, surplus is the quantity over the maximum recommended quantity to keep in stock. Refer to the Product Purchasing Parameters Maintenance Screen for information on surplus quantities and purchase methods.
In order for a product to appear on the report, it must have a Replenishment Method of T (transfer) set in Product Warehouse Maintenance for the receiving warehouse.
To calculate surplus inventory and create a recommended transfer:
1
Select Inventory > Stock Movements > Transfer Surplus Report & Update to open the Transfer Surplus Report screen.
2
For Hub/Spoke Execution, indicate whether you are reviewing replenishment from a central warehouse or individual warehouse.
•
Y - Review all spoke locations that are configured as T replenishment from a central warehouse (Hub).
•
N - Define the from/to warehouse.
3
For Surplus Warehouse, enter the warehouse where the surplus inventory is located.
4
For Replenish Warehouse, enter the warehouse to receive the surplus inventory.
5
For Product Line, enter a product line to include in the surplus report. Leave this field blank to include all product lines.
6
For Sort by (P)roduct Line or (V)endor, indicate how to sort the report. Choose from the following:
•
P - By product line.
•
V - By primary vendor.
7
Press ENTER to view the surplus products from the Surplus Warehouse that have a Replenishment Method of T (refer to the Product Purchasing Parameters Maintenance Screen) in the Replenish Warehouse.
8
After the report generates, the following prompt displays:
Do You Wish to Convert This Report To A Stock Transfer?
Choose from the following:
•
Y - A stock transfer generates using the data from the report. The transfer number displays. Use Transfer Entry to edit and print the stock transfer.
•
N - A stock transfer is not generated. You can use the information on the report to manually create the transfer in Transfer Entry.
Column Descriptions
Column Heading
Description
Whse
The warehouse with the surplus inventory.
Product Number
The product with a surplus quantity in inventory. If using the EOQ purchase method, the surplus quantity is the order quantity plus the line point. If using the min/max purchase method, surplus is the quantity over the maximum recommended quantity to keep in stock.
Description
Line 1: The product description.
Line 2: The second line of description for the product, if applicable.
On-Hand
The quantity on hand in the surplus warehouse.
Commtd
The quantity committed to sales orders, work orders, vendor claims, and transfers in the surplus warehouse.
Op/Lp
The order point or line point of the product in the surplus warehouse.
Type
The type of purchase method used by the surplus warehouse:
•
MAX - Min/max.
•
EOQ - Economic order quantity.
•
AWU - Average weekly usage.
•
DRP - Distribution requirements planning.
•
DS - Days supply.
Max/Rbq
The maximum or the recommended buy quantity of the item.
Surplus
The surplus quantity available to ship to the replenish warehouse.
Cost
The cost of the item depending on how your G/L Cost flag is set:
•
Average cost if the G/L Cost flag is set to A, L, or F.
•
Replacement cost if the G/L Cost flag is set to R.
•
Standard cost if the G/L Cost flag is set to S.
Ext-Surplus
The extended surplus cost (Surplus x Cost).
Cls
The inventory class. Refer to Classifying Products.
