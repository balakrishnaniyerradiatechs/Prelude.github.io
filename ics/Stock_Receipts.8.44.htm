Creating an On-The-Water Purchase Order
When notified by the overseas vendor that products are in-transit, you can use On the Water Maintenance to create a new OTW purchase order to combine all the items and purchase orders that shipped. The original purchase order number stays open until all the items are moved to an OTW purchase order.
To create a new purchase order for shipped, on-the-water material:
1
Select Inventory > Stock Receipts > On the Water Maintenance to open the On The Water Maintenance screen.
2
For Reference #, either enter your own reference number or enter N for a system-generated number. This number appears in Vendor Order # on the OTW purchase order. You can use this number in Import Cost Inquiry to view import costs associated with a shipment.
3
Enter the Vendor Number.
4
Enter the number for the Warehouse receiving the material.
5
For Ship Date, enter the date the merchandise was loaded onto the ship. This date displays in the header notes section of the purchase order.
6
For Expected Date, enter the date the merchandise is expected to arrive at the warehouse. This date becomes the delivery date for the purchase order.
7
For Num Containers, enter the number of containers in the shipment.
8
Enter the vendor’s Invoice Number to display in the header notes section of the OTW purchase order.
9
Enter the Bill of Lading #, if applicable, which appears in the header notes section of the OTW purchase order.
10
For Direct Ship, indicate if the material is shipping directly to the customer:
•
Y - It is a direct ship (type 02) purchase order. The Direct Ship Order Entry screen appears so you can enter the Direct Ship P/O#, which automatically lists the products included on the purchase order. You can change the cost but not the quantity of the line items. Go to step 15.
•
N - It is not a direct ship order.
11
Enter the Ship Via Code to indicate how the material shipped.
12
For Product Number, choose one of the following methods to enter products:
•
By product. Enter the product numbers to include on the new purchase order. The items must exist on an open purchase order for this vendor. If more than one open purchase order contains this item, the oldest order is used first. Any comments associated with each item on the original purchase order are included. Go to step 14.
•
By purchase order. Press F6-Enter P/Os. The following prompt displays:
Choose from the following:
Select Products From
-
Multiple P/O’s. Opens the OTW P/O# Entry screen. Enter the purchase order numbers included in this shipment. Only purchase orders with open quantities are allowed. All open products on the purchase orders you enter will be pulled into the On The Water Maintenance screen.
 
-
Single Purchase Order. Opens the On The Water P/O Entry screen. Enter the P/O Number to list the open products included on the purchase order. For each product listed, enter the quantity included in the shipment under Wksht Qty. You cannot enter more than the Remain Qty.
13
Press F2-Save. The On The Water Maintenance screen lists the selected products.
Changing Quantities
If you need to change quantities on any of the purchase orders, refer to Changing Quantities in On The Water Maintenance.
14
Press F5-Import Costs to open the Import Cost Entry screen.
The import costs such as freight, duty, or brokerage fees display. To override the calculated cost, enter the new cost in Actual.
15
Once everything is correct, press F2-Save. The following message appears:
Do You Wish To Update Or Suspend For
Future Maintenance?
16
Choose from the following:
•
Return: Allows you to return to the screen to edit the order.
•
Suspend: Saves the OTW entry but does not update the existing purchase orders or create the new purchase order. You can access and update the order later.
•
Update: Creates the new OTW purchase order. Linked sales orders, transfers, or work orders are re-linked to the new order number.
-
Type 01 stock purchase orders - Creates a type 12 purchase order (OTW - Stock).
-
Type 02 direct ship purchase orders - Creates a type
13-purchase order (OTW - Direct).
If you entered the entire quantities of the products from the original purchase onto the new purchase order, the original purchase order is deleted. To change any quantities, you must update the new purchase order.
When the material arrives in your warehouse, use the new OTW purchase order number to receive the stock (refer to Receiving Stock).
The following inquiries allow you to check the status of OTW merchandise or to find the new purchase order number created in On The Water Maintenance:
•
Inventory Inquiry shows the updated quantity next to On The Water. Move the cursor to On The Water and press F5-Review to open the OTW Purchase Order Inquiry screen. You can view detail information on the item and the purchase order. Use the Examine option on any purchase order to see the original purchase order number and import cost information.
•
Purchase Order Inquiry displays all the open purchase orders for the vendor. If the entire quantity of the original purchase order was moved to the new OTW purchase order number, the original purchase order number does not display. You can view the detail information of each order.
•
Purchase Order Log Inquiry for the original purchase order displays a deletion code of OTWM. It also displays the new OTW purchase order.
•
Purchase Order Log Inquiry for the new OTW purchase order displays the original purchase order number(s).
•
Open On the Water Purchase Order Detail Report displays information on all open OTW (type 12) purchase orders. Type 13 (OTW-Direct) purchase orders are not considered open purchase orders and are excluded from the report.
•
The Import Cost Report lists expected and actual import costs for each receiver.
