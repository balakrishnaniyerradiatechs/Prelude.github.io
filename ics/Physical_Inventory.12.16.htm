Entering Physical Inventory Counts
You can begin entering inventory counts as the counters complete each count sheet.
To enter inventory counts:
1
Select Inventory > Physical Inventory > Inventory Sheet Procedure > Physical Count Sheet Entry to open the Physical Inventory Entry Selection screen.
2
Enter the Warehouse associated with the count sheet.
3
For Physical ListName, choose from the following:
•
Enter the ListName from the count sheet.
•
Enter M to enter products found that are not on any count sheet. In this case, once you start entering counts, you are prompted for a Manual Physical Inventory ListName as you enter each item.
4
Enter the Count Taker Initials. This information is not validated and can be up to three characters.
5
Enter a Starting Product and an Ending Product if you only want to enter counts for a range of part numbers or leave these fields blank to enter counts for all items on the list. Use these fields if you already partially entered counts for this list.
6
Press ENTER to open the Physical Count Sheet Entry screen.
The part numbers, the date each item was last updated by a physical count, and the saved on-hand quantity display.
7
Enter the Count from the count sheet for each item. This value becomes the new book on-hand quantity when you update inventory counts (refer to Updating Physical Inventory Counts).
If a part number was skipped on the count sheet, leave the field blank so that the part number is not updated. If you enter a zero, the new on-hand will be zero. If a count already exists in the field, possibly entered from a manual list, you must determine whether your count should replace the previous quantity.
8
Press F2-Finish after you enter all the counts.
Once you verify the counts, you can update the on-hand balance for each product in inventory (refer to Updating Physical Inventory Counts).

Fill With Products

The following reports are available to verify counts before updating inventory. If you adjust the count entries, you can run the reports as many times as necessary before you update inventory.
Reports
Description
Physical Inventory Deviation Exception Report
Compares the saved on-hand quantities to the new count entries and prints the products that fall within the defined deviation range.
Post Entry Count Sheets on page 11-28
Allow you to compare the entered count to the count written on the sheet.
 
Physical Inventory Shrinkage Report
The saved on-hand quantity, the counts entered for all inventory items, and the difference in value.
 
Physical Inventory Shrinkage Valuation Report
The dollar amount of inventory shrinkage for each item in inventory.
