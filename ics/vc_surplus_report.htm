Generating Vendor Claims From a Surplus Report
The Vendor Claim Surplus Report and Update generates a vendor claim for all surplus items for a vendor. You can run the report by warehouse, vendor, and inventory class to limit the items to review. The report also recommends other warehouses that may be candidates for the surplus inventory so that you can determine if you want to transfer the material rather than return it to the vendor.
If you choose to generate a vendor claim, the suggested items to return are put on a recommended vendor claim that appears in the work queue of the buyer assigned to the vendor. The buyer can edit the claim before transmitting it to the vendor. For more information, refer to Vendor Claims in the Buyer Queue Inquiry in the Purchasing documentation.
You can also view the generated vendor claims in the Open Vendor Claim Inquiry and the Vendor Claim Log Inquiry.
If a product uses the EOQ purchase method, the surplus quantity is the EOQ plus the line point. If the product uses the min/max purchase method, surplus is the quantity over the maximum recommended quantity to keep in stock. Refer to the Product Purchasing Parameters Maintenance Screen for information on purchase methods.
To generate a vendor claim from surplus inventory:
1
Select Inventory > Vendor Claims > Vendor Claim Surplus Report & Update to open the Vendor Claim Surplus Worksheet screen.
2
Enter the Warehouse to check for surplus items.
3
For Vendor, enter the number for the vendor whose surplus inventory you want to review.
4
For Class Greater Than, enter the class that is 1 number lower than the classes you want to include in the report. The report will only print products with a class greater than this number so that you only have to review less active products.
For example: Inventory classes range from 1 to 13, with class 1 being the most active and class 13 being dead stock. To only show items in classes 7-13, enter a 6.
Refer to Classifying Products for information on inventory class.
5
For Split Claims By Line, if the vendor is set up to create a new purchase order for each vendor line, indicate if you want to create a separate claim for each vendor line:
•
Y - A separate claim generates for each vendor line.
•
N - One claim generates for all vendor lines.
6
After you print, email, or fax the report, the following prompt displays:
Do You Wish To Convert This Report To Vendor Claims?
7
Choose from the following:
•
No - Does not convert the surplus inventory into a vendor claim.
•
Yes - Converts the surplus inventory into a recommended vendor claim that appears in the work queue of the buyer assigned to the vendor.
•
Return - Goes back to the Vendor Claim Surplus Worksheet to allow you to change the criteria.
Vendor Claim Surplus Worksheet Column Descriptions
Column
Description
Whse
The warehouse with the surplus inventory.
Product Number
The product with a surplus quantity in inventory. If using the EOQ purchase method, the surplus quantity is the order quantity plus the line point. If using the min/max purchase method, surplus is the quantity over the maximum recommended quantity to keep in stock.
Description
The product description.
On-Hand
The quantity of the product on-hand in the surplus warehouse.
Commtd
The quantity of the product committed to sales orders, work orders, and transfers.
Op/Lp
The order point or line point of the product in the surplus warehouse.
Type
The type of purchase method used by the surplus warehouse:
•
MAX - Min/max.
•
EOQ - Economic order quantity.
•
AWU - Average weekly usage.
•
DRP - Distribution requirements planning.
•
DS - Days supply.
Max/Rbq
The maximum or the recommended buy quantity of the item.
Surplus
The surplus quantity available to ship to the replenish warehouse.
Cost
The cost of the item depending on how your G/L Cost flag is set:
•
Average cost if the G/L Cost flag is set to A, L, or F.
•
Replacement cost if the G/L Cost flag is set to R.
•
Standard cost if the G/L Cost flag is set to S.
Ext-Surplus
The extended surplus cost (Surplus x Cost).
Cls
The inventory class of the item. Refer to Classifying Products.
Candidates to Transfer to
Whse
The warehouses that might be able to use the product.
Class
The inventory class of the item in the corresponding warehouse. Refer to Classifying Products.
On-Hand
The quantity of the product on-hand in the candidate warehouse.
Commtd
The quantity of the product committed to sales orders, work orders, and transfers in the candidate warehouse.
Op/Lp
The order point or line point of the product in the candidate warehouse.
Type
The type of purchase method used by the candidate warehouse:
•
MAX - Min/max.
•
EOQ - Economic order quantity.
•
AWU - Average weekly usage.
•
DRP - Distribution requirements planning.
•
DS - Days supply.
Max/Rbq
The maximum or the recommended buy quantity of the item.
Usage
Usage of the product in the candidate warehouse.
