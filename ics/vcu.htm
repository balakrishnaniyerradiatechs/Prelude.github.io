Updating Vendor Claims
After you verify and/or correct the vendor claim information and transmit it to the vendor, you must update the claim to:
•
Confirm that the claim shipped.
•
Adjust the quantity out of inventory.
•
Create an open receivable to reconcile with the vendor’s credit memo or replacement product.
You can view updated vendor claims in the Vendor Claim History Inquiry.
Vendor claims are posted to the Vendor Claim A/P Accrual account during Vendor Claim Update Processing. If this account is not set up, they are posted to the A/P Not Invoiced - Inventory account.
 
To update inventory for material returned on vendor claims:
1
Select Inventory > Vendor Claims > Vendor Claim Update Processing to open the Vendor Claim Update Processing screen.
2
Enter the Warehouse.
3
Enter the Beginning Claim Number to include if updating a range of claim numbers. Leave blank to update all available vendor claim numbers.
4
Enter the Ending Claim Number to include in a range of claim numbers or leave blank to update all available vendor claim numbers.
5
Enter the Inventory Type to include in the update. Choose from the following:
•
A - All inventory types.
•
R - Regular inventory (available).
•
W - Warranty inventory. Refer to Entering a Vendor Claim For Warranty Inventory.
•
M - Mixed stock (both regular and warranty inventory).
6
For Claim Type, choose from the following vendor claim types:
•
01 - Regular vendor claim. For regular or warranty inventory.
•
02 - Vendor show claim. Refer to Entering a Vendor Show Claim.
•
03 - Vendor consigned inventory. Products in stock that you do not pay for until sold.
•
04 - Non-Inventory Expense. An expense item that is not part of your on-hand inventory. You must enter a G/L account number instead of inventory.
7
Press F2-Save to update the claims and to adjust inventory. If you left the Beginning Claim Number and the Ending Claim Number blank, a message warns you that you may unintentionally update some claims. You can continue or go back and enter claim numbers.
