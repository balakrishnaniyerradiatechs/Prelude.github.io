Merge Products
You can merge product, pricing, and usage information from one product to another in Universal Product Merge Entry. If you merge multiple products into one product, the product line, price group, and usage information from the first product you enter is used for the merged product.
If you try to merge the following types of products, a warning message appears and will not allow the merge:
•
A superceded and its superceding product.
•
A lot product and a non-lot product.
•
A serialized product and a non-serialized product.
•
Products with different units of measure.
To merge product numbers:
1
Select Inventory > Maintenance > Universal Product Replacement & Merge > Universal Product Merge Entry to open the Universal Product Merge Entry screen.
2
For Old Product Number, enter the original product to merge into the New Product Number.
3
For New Product Number, enter the new or existing product to merge the old product information into. Depending on whether you enter an existing or a new product, one of the following messages appears:
•
If you enter a new product, the following message displays:
Choose from the following:
You Will Be Merging into A New Product
-
Return to Order: Allows you to enter a different part number.
-
Continue: Accepts the new part number and allows you to continue entering products.
•
If you enter an existing product, the following message displays:
Product Exists on File - Merge Old Product To This Product?
Choose from the following:
-
No: Allows you to enter a different part number.
-
Yes: Accepts the part number and allows you to enter more products.
4
Press F2-Save to save the information. The merge does not occur until you run the Universal Product Replacement/Merge Update (refer to Update Replaced/Merged Products).
When you merge one or multiple products into an existing product, not all of the information is updated or recalculated. You can manually update the merged product, if necessary. The update exceptions are:
•
Warehouse statistics for the merged product are not updated with physical inventory end-of-month numbers and ranking information until you run Inventory End of Month.
•
Seasonal, physical, EDQ, and PDQ calculations stored in the product/warehouse are not updated for the merged product. You must update these calculations manually in Product Warehouse Maintenance.
•
Product vendor statistics for the merged product are not updated with the original product’s lead-time. You must update the lead-time in Product Warehouse Maintenance.
•
When the merge update is running, you cannot update physical inventory for product numbers in the New Product Number field.
•
When kit parent items and bill of material items are merged, their components are not updated on any open sales orders or work orders. You must manually update the open sales orders and work orders with the merged product’s components.
•
If price matrices, retail price matrices, or commission matrices exist for the original products in Universal Product Replacement Entry, the matrices from the first product are merged into an existing product. All other products’ matrices are deleted because they are no longer valid.
