Implementing the Price/Cost Hierarchy
To automatically price sales orders and cost purchase orders based on pricing structures negotiated with customers and vendors, you must understand the hierarchy the system uses to find a price or cost. Prices and costs are set up at multiple levels in the system and can be based on quantities, weight, types of products, product lines, or vendor lines. Once you set up the pricing structures, you can then assign the values to any customer/product combination or vendor/product combination.
Sales Price Hierarchy
The system searches for price information in the following order to determine the price to use on a sales order or quote:
•
1st: Matrix or contract pricing set up for a customer.
•
2nd: Quantity break discounts based on the quantity of a product purchased or Sum and Reprice discounts based on the quantity of total products purchased.
•
3rd: Column pricing set up at the product level and the product/warehouse level. A Price Code is assigned to a customer to determine which price column to use.
Best Price
•
Utilize Best Price in Order Processing Parameters determines if the system searches all levels of pricing to determine the price on a sales order.
•
Best Price in Customer Maintenance determines if the system searches all levels of pricing to determine the price on a customer’s sales order. This setting overrides the Utilize Best Price flag in Order Processing Parameters.
Cost Hierarchy
The system searches for cost information in the following order to determine the cost to use on a purchase order:
•
1st: At the product/vendor/warehouse level (refer to Maintaining a Product by Warehouse/Vendor).
•
2nd: At the product/vendor level (refer to Using Company Vendor Cost).
•
3rd: At the product/ warehouse level (refer to Entering Prices/Costs for a Product - by Warehouse).
•
4th: At the product level (refer to Entering Prices/Costs for a Product).
