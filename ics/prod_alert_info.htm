Product Alert Information Screen
The Product Alert Information screen displays alert date and reason information for a product when it is sent to the buyer queue for the following reasons:
•
Min/Max Spike: When usage for a min/max product (with frozen purchasing parameters) exceeds the maximum usage quantity setting. This alert helps the buyer to adjust min/max settings for new products until a sufficient amount of history is available to accurately calculate product usage. The buyer must be set up as the purchasing buyer in Warehouse Manager Maintenance.
•
Safety Allowance: When the product is received, the available quantity falls above or below the safety allowance tolerances defined for the product’s class. Safety allowance tolerances are calculated by multiplying:
-
S/A High% x Safety Quantity.
-
S/A Low% x Safety Quantity.
•
Early Warning: When a product’s usage exceeds a pre-defined percentage of its Forecast quantity by the day of the month defined in Product Alert Parameters. For example, if by the 7th of the month, actual usage for the product exceeds 75% of the Forecast quantity, it will appear in the buyer queue.
•
Lost Sales: When a sale was lost. The lost sale reason code displays to identify why the sale was lost.
Refer to “Product Alerts” in the Purchasing documentation (Buyer Queue Inquiry) for more information on product alert calculations.
Menu Path: Inventory > Maintenance > Product Maintenance > Product Purchasing Parameters Maintenance > Action Bar > Safety Alerts
Field & Function Key Descriptions
Field/Function Key
Description
Product
The product number on alert.
Warehouse
The number of the warehouse containing the product on alert.
Alert-date
The date the product went on alert.
Alert-Time
The time of the day the product went on alert.
Reason
The reason why the product is on alert:
•
Min/Max Spike. Usage for a min/max product, whose purchasing parameters are frozen, exceeds the maximum usage quantity setting.
•
Safety Allowance. A product is received and the available quantity goes above or below the allowable safety allowance tolerances for the product’s class. Safety tolerances are calculated by multiplying the product’s Safety Quantity (Product Purchasing Parameters Maintenance) by the S/A High % / Low% for the product’s class (Purchasing Controls > Purchasing Parameters > F6-Class Settings).
•
Early Warning: When a product’s usage exceeds a pre-defined percentage of its Forecast quantity by the day of the month defined in Product Alert Parameters.
•
Lost Sales: When a sale was lost. The lost sale reason code displays to identify why the sale was lost.
Safety
The safety allowance for the product.
Avail
The quantity available of the product.
OK
Closes the screen.
F5-TAI
Opens the Transaction Audit Inquiry.
