Product Pricing Screen
The Product Pricing screen allows you to enter or calculate prices, costs, and discounts associated with a product.
Menu Path: Inventory > Maintenance > Product Maintenance > Product Maintenance > F7- Pricing
Field & Function Key Descriptions
Field/Function Key
Description
Current
The current price or cost for this product record. Leave blank if using Percent and Basis to calculate the values. If you enter a Percent and Basis, the calculated value displays when you press F5-Recompute.
Percent
The percentage or multiplier to use with a Basis column to calculate the price or cost for each of the columns. Leave blank if you entered a price under Current and want to maintain the price manually.
The calculated values depend on how the Prc/Cst Basis Calc Method flag is set in Inventory Parameters.
If Prc/Cst Basis Calc Method is set to G for gross profit, enter the gross profit percent that you want to make for this product for this price level. The Percent is used with the cost column indicated in Basis (refer to the Basis codes) to calculate the price.
For example:
•
Replacement Cost is 10.00.
•
If you want Price 1 to provide 20% gross profit above Replacement Cost, enter 20 under Percent and R under Basis.
•
When you press F5-Recompute, 10.00 is divided by .80 to come up with a Price 1 of 12.50. Gross profit is 20%.
If Prc/Cst Basis Calc Method is set to M for markup, enter the markup percent that you want for this product at this price level. The Percent is used with the cost column indicated in Basis to calculate the price.
For example:
•
Replacement Cost is 10.00.
•
If you want Price 1 to be 20% above the value of Replacement, enter 20 under Percent and R under Basis.
•
When you press F5-Recompute, 10 is multiplied by 1.20 to come up with a Price 1 of 12.00. The gross profit is 16.6%.
Basis
The price/cost column to calculate new price/cost values if using a percentage or multiplier for Percent:
•
0 - Price column 0 (Suggested retail price).
•
1 - Price column 1.
•
2 - Price column 2.
•
3 - Price column 3.
•
4 - Price column 4.
•
5 - Price column 5.
•
R - Replacement cost.
•
A - Average cost.
•
S - Standard cost.
•
O - Cost for sales.
Q/B
A Y indicates a column quantity break. Refer to the Price Column Quantity Breaks Screen.
List
Price 1 - Price 5
Default customer prices. Each customer is assigned a Price Code (Customer Maintenance > F7-Order) to use if special pricing does not exist for this product. You can set these prices at the product level to use company wide or by warehouse in Product Warehouse Maintenance (refer to Entering Prices/Costs for a Product - by Warehouse).
Replacement Cost
The cost to replace the product. You can override this cost at the product/warehouse level in the Warehouse Pricing Screen, the product vendor level (refer to Setting Up Company Vendor Cost), or the product/vendor/warehouse level (refer to Maintaining a Product by Warehouse/Vendor).
The Replacement Cost Update flag in Inventory Parameters determines if replacement cost is updated in Purchase Order Entry, Stock Receipts Entry, or in Product or Product/Warehouse Maintenance.
Standard Cost
If you use standard cost as the accounting cost for your company (G/L Cost flag set to S in Company Maintenance), you can maintain the cost at the product level or the product warehouse level. You must set up general ledger accounts to post the differences between the standard costs and the actual costs used in stock receipts, transfers, and vendor invoice reconciliation. Also, standard costs from all the components on a bill of material are rolled up into one product.
Cost For Sales
The cost for sales orders, which is different than the accounting cost. You can use this to calculate gross profit for commission.
Direct Ship Cost
The cost to direct ship the product if it is different than the Replacement Cost. This cost can be overridden at the product warehouse level, the company vendor cost level, the product vendor level, or on a purchase order.
Minimum Price
The minimum price for a sales order. If you go below this price you must enter a password or it will go on Administrative Order Hold. You can override this for a warehouse in Product Warehouse Maintenance > F7-Price/Cost > Minimum Price.
Product Prc Group
The product price group established in Product Price Group Maintenance to group products for pricing discounts. Refer to Establishing Product Price Groups for more information.
Pricing U/M
The unit of measure to use for this product on a sales order. Prices defined in this screen should be defined in this unit of measure. If pricing, buying, and stocking unit of measures are different (refer to Establishing a Unit of Measure).
Stocking U/M
The unit of measure used for this product in inventory. Costs defined in this screen should be defined in this unit of measure. If pricing and stocking unit of measures are different (refer to Establishing a Unit of Measure for more information on unit of measure).
Last Update Date
The date of the last price/cost change. This field is updated by the system but can be overridden when any price or cost is changed.
Product Q/B
Indicates if price quantity breaks are set up for this product. Refer to the Quantity Break Table Screen for information on setting up quantity breaks:
•
Y - Quantity break pricing is set up for this product. You can view and edit these by pressing F8-Prd QtyBrk
•
N - Quantity break pricing is not set up for this product.
Cost Q/B
Indicates if cost quantity breaks exist for this product. Refer to the Cost Quantity Break Table Screen for information on setting up quantity breaks:
•
Y - Quantity break costs are set up for this product. You can view these by pressing F9-Cst QtyBrk.
•
N - Quantity breaks costs are not set up for this product.
F2-Save
Saves the product price/cost information.
F5-Recompute
Recalculates the price and cost values if you entered prices using Percent and Basis.
F7-Col QtyBrk
Opens the Price Column Quantity Breaks Screen to allow you to determine whether the price columns are a set price, a percentage of a default price, or based on a quantity discount table.
F8-Prd QtyBrk
Opens the Quantity Break Table Screen to allow you to enter prices for a product based on a quantity breaks.
F9-Cst QtyBrk
Opens the Cost Quantity Break Table Screen to allow you to enter costs for a product based on quantities.
Action Bar
Future
Opens the Product Future Pricing Screen to allow you to enter future prices and the date for the prices to go into effect.
Previous
Opens the Previous Prices Screen to allow you to view the previous prices for a product. Previous prices are only available if you chose Y to the “Save Previous Prices?” prompt when you updated a price. Refer to Updating Prices and Costs.
Add Prices
Opens the Add. Price Columns Screen to allow you to enter additional price column descriptions and prices.
