Updating Price/Cost Using a Percent Markup
You can update prices using a percentage over a price or cost column. This method allows you to group products by vendor, vendor line, product line, or price group at the product level or warehouse level. The new percent and basis replace what was previously set up for the products.
To update the percentage and basis for a group of products:
1
Select Inventory > Processes > Price/Cost Update Procedures > Company & Whse Price/Cost% Basis Update to open the Company & Whse Price/Cost Update screen.
2
For Inventory or Catalog, indicate whether to update the inventory or catalog file:
•
I - Update the inventory file.
•
C - Update the catalog file.
3
Enter the Warehouse number for to update.
4
For Enter File, Product (P), Warehouse (W), indicate how prices are updated:
•
P - Recompute prices by product.
•
W - Recompute prices by warehouse.
5
If you selected W in step 4, enter the Warehouse for the price/cost update.
6
Enter a value in one of the following fields to determine how to group the products to update:
•
Vendor - Enter the vendor number to update.
•
Product Line - Enter the product number to update.
•
Vendor Line - Enter the vendor line to update.
•
Price Group - Enter the price group to update.
7
For %, enter a new percentage to calculate the new value for each price and cost field.
8
For Basis, enter the price or cost column that this price or cost is based on. Choose from the following:
•
0 - Price column 0 (Suggested retail price).
•
1 - Price column 1.
•
2 - Price column 2.
•
3 - Price column 3.
•
4 - Price column 4.
•
5 - Price column 5.
•
R - Replacement cost.
•
A - Average cost.
•
S - Standard cost.
•
O - Cost for sales.
9
For Recalculate Prices, select the prices to recalculate:
•
C - Current prices.
•
F - Future prices.
•
B - Both current and future prices.
•
N - No prices are re-calculated.
10
If you choose F for Recalculate Prices, enter the Effective Date for the future prices.
11
Press F2-Save to recalculate the prices and costs. If your company tracks previous prices, the following prompt displays:
Do You Wish To Save Previous Prices?
•
Yes - Saves the previous prices and brings you to the next item on the list to update. You can view previous prices in Product Maintenance or Product Warehouse Maintenance > F7-Pricing > Action Bar > Previous.
•
No - The previous prices are not saved.
A message displays to notify you that the update is complete.
Inventory Parameters
Disp Save Prev Pricing in Inventory Parameters determines if the “Save Previous Price?” prompt displays when you change a price. Choose from the following:
•
Y - You can choose to save the previous price when you change a price .
•
N - Previous prices are not saved.
•
U - Previous prices are automatically saved.
Updating Additional Price Columns:
If Maximum Price Columns in Inventory Parameters is set to a number greater than 5, F5-Add Prices in the Company & Whse Price/Cost Update screen allows you to update any additional price columns.
