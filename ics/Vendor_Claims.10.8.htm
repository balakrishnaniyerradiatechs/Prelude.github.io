Entering a Vendor Claim for Regular Inventory
To return on-hand inventory to a vendor due to a shipping error, overstocked inventory, or for any other reason, you can enter a vendor claim to identify and allocate the material to return. If you are returning a product out of warranty inventory, refer to the instructions for Entering a Vendor Claim For Warranty Inventory.
To enter a vendor claim:
1
Select Inventory > Vendor Claims > Vendor Claims Entry to open the Vendor Claim Entry screen.
If you have access to more than one warehouse, the Location Retrieval screen appears first. Enter the appropriate Selling Warehouse, Department, and/or Cost Center.
2
For Claim Number, choose one of the following:
•
To assign a new claim number, press ENTER.
•
To edit an existing claim, enter the claim number or press F3 to search for the order by vendor.
3
To select a Vendor, choose one of the following:
•
Enter the vendor’s number.
•
Enter a vendor keyword, part of the vendor’s name, or other search criteria defined by your company and press ENTER.
•
Press F3 and enter part of the vendor’s name or other search criteria defined by your company. You can press F5-Vendor Product Type Keyword to look up a vendor by product type.
4
Depending on your system settings, the Vendor Verification screen may appear. The address displayed is from Vendor Maintenance. If this is the correct address, enter Y for Is This The Correct Vendor?. If not, enter N and select another vendor.
Once you select the correct vendor, the Vendor Claim Header screen appears.
5
For Claim Type, choose from the following:
•
01 - Regular vendor claim. Regular or warranty inventory.
•
02 - Vendor show claim. Refer to Entering a Vendor Show Claim.
•
03 - Vendor consigned inventory. Products in stock that you do not pay for until they are sold. Refer to Adjusting Vendor Consigned Inventory Cost.
•
04 - Non-inventory expense. For non-inventory items (requires a G/L account number).
6
For Ship-To, choose from the following:
•
D - Uses the vendor’s default address.
•
M - Allows you to enter an address.
•
F3 - Searches for available ship-from addresses for the vendor.
7
The Claim Date defaults to today’s date. Change if necessary.
8
The Buyer, Contact, Email Address, Fax, Ship Via, Payment Terms, and Freight Terms default from the vendor record, but you can change the information if necessary.
9
Enter freight information if it is available or wait until after the material ships.
10
Enter any shipping Instructions if necessary.
11
Press F2-Save to save the header information and to open the Vendor Claim Entry screen.
12
For Vendor RMA#, enter the vendor’s Return Material Authorization (RMA) number if it is available.
If V/C RMA# Required? is set to Y in Vendor Maintenance, an RMA number is mandatory.
If the material you are returning is in warranty inventory, refer to Entering a Vendor Claim For Warranty Inventory.
If you enter a product that has open warranty tags, a warning message appears to allow you to return to the Product prompt and enter warranty tag information. If you want to return warranty inventory, follow the procedures for Entering a Vendor Claim For Warranty Inventory.
Non-stock or “*” part numbers are not allowed on vendor claims without a warranty tag. Refer to Moving Products into Warranty Inventory.
To return material that is in regular inventory, continue with the following steps.
13
For Product, enter the part number to return from regular inventory. Inventory is allocated immediately but does not decrease on-hand inventory until you update the vendor claim (refer to Updating Vendor Claims).
The Vendor Claim Detail Information screen appears.
14
For Qty-Opn, enter the quantity to return or receive credit for.
This quantity defaults into the Qty Ret field. If some or all of the product is being scrapped instead of returned to the vendor, enter N for Accept and enter the correct quantities for Qty Ret and Qty Scr. The total of these 2 fields must equal the Qty Opn.
15
If necessary, you can enter additional reference information in Ref #. This information can be used to search for a vendor claim number when reconciling the claim.
If Vend Claim Orig Invoice? in Inventory Parameters is set to Y, you must enter the Original Inv#. Press F3 to list any invoice numbers for this product/vendor combination.
16
Press F2-Save if everything on the screen is correct. The Vendor Claim Entry screen re-appears.
17
Repeat steps 13-16 for any additional items to add to the vendor claim.
18
Press F2-Save after you enter all the line items and comments. The following question appears:
Do you wish to ....
19
Choose from the following:
•
Print. Print the vendor claim.
•
Fax. Fax the vendor claim to the fax number set up in Vendor Maintenance.
•
Prt/Fax. Print and fax the claim.
•
Email. Email the claim to the vendor. Refer to Setting Flags for Vendor Claims & Warranty Inventory to determine the hierarchy the system uses to select an email address.
•
Suspend. Suspend the vendor claim so you can access and update it later.
•
Return. Return to the Vendor Claim Entry Screen.
The default transmission method depends on the vendor’s setting for Vendor Claims Doc Options in Vendor Maintenance. Refer to Setting Flags for Vendor Claims & Warranty Inventory.
If you batch print vendor claims, the vendor claim prints or transmits to the vendor when you run Vendor Claim Printing (refer to Printing Vendor Claims). The vendor claim remains open until you run Vendor Claim Update Processing to remove the quantity from inventory (refer to Updating Vendor Claims).
You can view open Vendor claims in the Open Vendor Claim Inquiry.
Changing a Vendor Claim
To modify a vendor claim to add items, change quantities, or enter freight/shipping information; either enter the claim number or press F3 to search for open claims. Open vendor claims for the selected vendor display. If you use the printed vendor claim as a packing list, you should reprint the claim to reflect the updated information. Refer to Printing Vendor Claims.
