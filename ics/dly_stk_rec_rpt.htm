Daily Stock Receipts Report
The Daily Stock Receipts Report lists all of the merchandise received in a day. The report also displays product cost, extended cost, import costs, bin locations, and serial number information.
Menu Path: Inventory > Reports > Stock Receipts Reports > Daily Stock Receipts Report
Selection Criteria
Use the following criteria to limit the information to print on the report:
•
Division. The highest possible grouping in your company (e.g., Eastern Division, Western Division).
•
Region. A subset of Division - if Division is used (e.g., Southeast Region, Northwestern Region).
•
Warehouse. The warehouse that received the material.
•
Department. A subset of warehouse. Typically used to separate expenses within a warehouse (e.g., accounting, sales, warehouse).
•
Cost Center. A subset of Department - if Department is used. Typically used to separate expenses within a warehouse or department (e.g., shipping, receiving, outside sales, inside sales).
•
Receipt Date. The date the material was received.
•
Receiver. The receiver number to limit the report to just one receiver. Leave blank to display all receivers for the day.
•
P/O Type. The purchase order type to limit the report to just one purchase order type. Leave blank to display receivers for all purchase order types for the day.
•
Vendor. The vendor number to limit the report to just one vendor. Leave blank to display receivers for all for all vendors for the day.
Column Descriptions
Column Heading
Description
P.O. #
The receiver number.
Vendor
The vendor number from the purchase order.
Received
The receipt date of the item.
Product Number /
Description
Line 1: The product number.
Line 2: The product description.
Additional lines: Serial and lot number information.
BIN: The bin location for the received item.
QTY: The quantity put into each bin location.
Qty-Rec
The total quantity of the item received. The sum of all the bin quantities.
Qty-Stk
The quantity to go into stock based on the stocking unit of measure. For example, if the quantity received was 10 and the stocking unit of measure is DZ (dozen), the quantity received is 10 and the quantity in stock is 120. Refer to Establishing a Unit of Measure for more information on unit of measure.
Net-Price
The net price of the item after discounts.
U/M
The unit of measure of the item as entered on the purchase order.
Extension
The extended price for the total quantity of the item (Qty-Rec x Net-Price).
Freight
The inbound freight charge for the item. Includes ocean freight, inland freight, and any import freight charges.
Import Cost
The import cost for the item, if you use import cost factors (e.g., duty, brokerage fees).
