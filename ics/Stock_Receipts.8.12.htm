Rejecting Inventory After it is Received
A vendor’s shipping performance is evaluated by tracking both the receipt date and the number of rejected shipments. If the purchase order arrives late, the on-time percentage is affected. If the receiver has a rejection, the quality percentage is affected.
Normally, you reject an item when you receive it so that it goes into warranty inventory (refer to Rejecting Inventory When it is Received). But, if you only want to record the vendor’s performance without affecting inventory balances, you can mark the vendor’s product as rejected after the purchase order is received.
To mark a product as rejected after it is received:
1
Select Inventory > Processes > Late Rejection Entry to open the Late Rejection Entry screen.
2
For Rejection#, press ENTER for a new number.
3
Enter the Product number to reject.
4
Enter the Vendor that shipped the material.
5
For P/O Number, enter the receiver number for the original receipt of this product. The Receive Location, Date Received, and Received By information displays.
6
For Reject Date, enter the date the product was rejected.
7
Enter a Rejection Reason code to indicate why the item was rejected. Refer to Setting Up Rejection Claim Reason Codes.
8
For Rejection Qty, enter the quantity to reject.
9
For Reject Notes, enter any additional information to explain why the product is rejected. These notes appear on the Vendor On-Time Receipts/Quality Report.
10
Press F2-Save to complete the rejection entry.
