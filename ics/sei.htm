Spike Exceptions Inquiry
The Spike Exception Inquiry lists all products with high and low spike exceptions. The products with the highest difference between actual and forecast usage display first.
Spike Exceptions
Spike exceptions are set at the company level (Purchasing Controls > Purchasing Parameters) and are calculated at End of Month Calendar Close.
•
The value in Spike Exc.- High is multiplied by the actual usage of each product. If the result is greater than the forecast for the month, the product will appear in the Spikes Exception Inquiry.
•
The value in Spike Exc.- Low is multiplied by the actual usage of each product. If the result is less than the forecast for the month, the product will appear in the Spikes Exception Inquiry.
Viewing Spike Exceptions
To view high and low spike exceptions:
1
Select Inventory > Processes > Usage Exception Menu > Spike Exception Inquiry
2
Enter the Class number to include in the search (for more information on Product class, refer to Classifying Products).
3
Enter the Warehouse to review.
4
For High or Low Spikes, choose from the following:
•
H - Limit the inquiry to products with high spikes only.
•
L - Limit the inquiry to products with low spikes only.
•
Blank - Include both high and low spikes.
The spike exceptions display.
Field & Function Key Descriptions
Field/Function Key
Description
Product
The product with the spike exception.
Description
The product description.
Class
The product classification (refer to Classifying Products).
Forecast
The calculated forecast for the product for this month.
Usage
The month-to-date usage of the product.
Hi/Lo
Indicates if the exception is a high or low spike:
•
H - High spike exception. The actual usage is more than the forecast by the multiple entered in Spikes Exception - High in Purchase Parameters Maintenance (Systems Menu).
•
L - Low spike exception. The actual usage is less than the forecast by the multiple entered in Spikes Exception - Low in Purchase Parameters Maintenance (Systems Menu).
Diff%
The difference between the forecasted usage and the actual usage (Forecast - Usage).
F2-Save
Closes the inquiry.
F5-Usage Inq
Opens the Product Usage Inquiry for the highlighted product.
F6-Perform Maintenance
Opens the Product Usage Maintenance Screen to allow you to manually override usage for a product.
F7-Auto Upd
Opens the Product Usage Maintenance Screen to allow you to view updated override usage for a product.
