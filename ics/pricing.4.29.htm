Adjusting Inventory Cost
When you adjust inventory cost, an increase in cost debits the Inventory account and credits the offsetting Cost Adjustment account set up for the reason code. A decrease in cost credits the Inventory account and debits the Cost Adjustment account.
General Ledger Cost
When you adjust the cost of products in inventory, the cost that is changed depends how your G/L Cost flag is set in Company Maintenance > F5-Add Info.
•
A - Average Cost.
•
F - Fifo Cost.
•
L - Lifo Cost.
•
R - Replacement Cost.
•
S - Standard Cost.
Before adjusting cost, you must set up cost adjustment reason codes (refer to Establishing Cost Adjustment Reason Codes).
To adjust cost:
1
Select Inventory > Inventory Adjustments > Cost Adjustment Entry to open the Cost Adjustment Entry screen.
2
For Adjustment Number, press ENTER to assign the next available cost adjustment transaction number.
3
For Entry Date, either accept the default of today’s date or enter a different date for the transaction. The fiscal period displays to the right.
4
Enter the Product for which you want to adjust cost. The product description displays. For serialized products, you must enter a serial number. For lot products, you must enter a lot number.
5
Enter the Warehouse for the cost adjustment. The warehouse description, Quantity On Hand, and the Current Cost for the product display.
6
For Revised Cost, enter the new cost. An increase in cost debits the Inventory account and credits the Cost Adjustment account. A decrease in cost credits the Inventory account and debits the Cost Adjustment account.
7
For Cost Adj Reason, enter the cost adjustment reason code to explain why you are adjusting cost. Refer to Establishing Cost Adjustment Reason Codes.
8
Press F2-Save to save the adjusted cost.
