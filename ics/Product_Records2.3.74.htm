Setting Parameters for Product Classifications
To determine the parameters for classifying products:
1
From the Purchasing Controls menu, select Purchasing Parameters to open the Purchasing Parameters Maintenance screen.
2
For Dead Stock Value, enter the annual cost of goods sold value under which an item is considered as dead stock. If a product does not have an annual cost of goods sold of at least this amount, it is considered dead inventory.
3
For Product Class Method, determine how products are classified:
•
W - By warehouse.
•
M - By warehouse/major group.
•
L - By product line.
4
For #Months Classification, enter the number of months a product must be a stocking product before it is included in the classification calculations.
5
Press F6-Class Settings to open the Product Class Settings screen.
6
.For Safety % for Dead Stock, enter the default safety allowance percentage for all products classified as dead stock. This percentage is applied during End of Month Calendar Close for products classified as dead stock (class13).
7
For Breakdown%, determine the percentage of the total number of products* (classes 1-12) to include in each class.
For example, if you want 7.5% of your products to be included in class 1, enter 7.5. The top 7.5% of products, based on usage value, are assigned to class 1.
8
Press F2-Save to save the product class settings.
To apply the new classification parameters to products, refer to Rebuilding Product Classifications.
* The total number of products does not include:
•
Stock products that have been in the system less than the number of months in #Months Classification.
•
Class 13 products.
