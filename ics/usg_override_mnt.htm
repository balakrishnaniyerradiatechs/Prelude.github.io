Adjusting Usage for Skipped Product Parameters
After you run the Product Parameters Calc Skipped Audit Report, you can adjust product usage for any of the products that were skipped due to unusual usage, stockout, or spike conditions. You can access the Product Usage Maintenance Screen either through the Buyer Queue Inquiry (Purchase Parameters Skipped) or through the following Usage Override Maintenance screen.
To adjust usage:
1
Select Inventory > Processes > Usage Exception Menu > Product Parameters Calc Skipped Audit Maintenance to open the Usage Override Maintenance screen.
2
Enter the Warehouse for which you want to adjust product usage.
3
Enter the Buyer code of the person responsible for the product or leave blank to display all products for all buyers.
4
For Auto Override Usage Only, choose which of the following types of products to include:
•
Y - Only include products with automatically recalculated usage based on the Classification Cutoff. Refer to Establishing the Weekly Usage Calculation Hierarchy (Capturing Weekly Usage at the Company Level).
•
N - Only include products that require a usage override.
•
Leave blank to include all products.
5
Choose Execute to open the Product Usage Maintenance Screen and to adjust usage for the first product listed on the Product Parameters Calc Skipped Audit Report.
6
For each month, enter an Override for sales order usage (S/O Usage), work order usage (W/O Usage), or transfer usage (T/R Usage).
7
Press F2-Save after you override usage or to go to the next product on the list. If you changed a usage value, the following prompt appears:
Changes were detected! Would you like to recalculate purchase parameters with new usage overrides?
8
Choose from the following:
•
Yes - Recalculate the new purchase parameters for the product.
•
No - Do not recalculate purchase parameters.
9
Continue to enter overrides for each product until all purchasing parameters are updated.
You can also override usage in the Buyer Queue Inquiry in the Purchase Parameters Skipped work queue. Refer to “Purchase Parameter Skipped” in the Buyer Queue Inquiry in the Purchasing documentation.
