Establishing Transfer Options
You must set flags in Inventory Parameters to determine how stock transfers are generated, entered, received, and how they update inventory in both the shipping and receiving warehouses.
To establish transfer options:
1
Select System Parameters > Maintenance > Option Parameters > Inventory Parameters > F5-Add Info to open the Inventory Add. Parameters screen.
2
For Two Step Transfer Process, determine if inventory at the receiving branch is updated when the transfer is shipped in Transfer Shipments Entry. Choose from the following:
•
Y - Use the 2-step transfer process to eliminate the need to receive the transfer. The 2-step transfer reduces the time required to complete a transfer. However, shipping errors may not be caught. If necessary, you can use Inventory Adjustment Entry to correct the inventory balance.
•
N - Use use the regular transfer process (enter, print, ship, and receive).
3
For Transfer Branch Hold, determine if a transfer goes on hold when the available quantity of an item goes below the safety stock at the shipping warehouse. Choose from the following:
•
Y - The transfer goes on Branch Manager Hold when it lowers the quantity of an item below the safety stock level, if the shipping warehouse is not in the replenishment path.
•
M - The transfer goes on Branch Manager Hold when it lowers the quantity of an item below the safety stock level regardless of the replenishment path.
•
N - The transfer does not go on Branch Manager Hold when it lowers the stock level of a product below safety stock.
4
For Auto Transfer Generation, determine if you can automatically generate a transfer in Sales Order Entry, Work Order Entry, or sales order conversions. This flag only applies to items with a Supply Code of T (transfer) or R (emergency transfer). Also, you must be authorized to automatically generate transfers. Choose from the following:
•
Y - Prompt to generate a transfer.
•
W - Automatically generate a transfer without prompting first.
•
N - Do not automatically generate transfers.
5
For Rec. Transfers By Buyer, determine if the recommended stock transfers generated by Nightly Processing are separated by buyer on the Recommended Stock Transfer Report. Choose from the following:
•
Y - Recommended stock transfers are separated by buyer. The buyers for transfers are set for each vendor in Vendor Maintenance, but can be overridden in Vendor Line Maintenance.
•
N - Recommended stock transfers are not separated by buyer.
6
For Transfer Freight Prompt, determine if the Freight Closeout screen should appear when you complete a transfer shipment. Choose from the following:
•
Y - The Freight Closeout screen appears when you complete a transfer shipment to allow the shipping warehouse to determine the freight amount. If you are using freight matrixes, you can override the calculated amount, if necessary.
•
N - The Freight Closeout screen does not appear when you complete a transfer shipment.
7
For Transfer Receipt Header Upd, determine if you can change and update the Stock Transfer Header screen in Transfer Receipts Entry and Batch Transfer Receipts Entry or if you can only view the header screen. Choose from the following:
•
Y - You can edit the Stock Transfer Header.
•
N - You can view, but not edit, the Stock Transfer Header.
8
For Allow Overage in TRE, determine if an item that is over-received in Transfer Receipts Entry or Batch Transfer Receipts Entry is allocated to a linked sales order. Choose from the following:
•
Y - The excess quantity is allocated to the linked sales order, and the quantity on the sales order is increased to the total received quantity.
•
N - The excess quantity is put into stock. The linked sales order only receives the quantity originally ordered.
9
For Allow Over Ships on Xfers, determine if an item that is over-received in Stock Receipts Entry or Batch Stock Receipts Entry is allocated to a linked stock transfer. Choose from the following:
•
Y - The excess quantity is allocated to the linked transfer and the quantity on the transfer is increased to the total received quantity.
•
N - The excess quantity is put into stock. The linked transfer only receives the quantity originally ordered.
10
For Transfers to Rec Whse BQI, determine where the Buyer Queue Inquiry is updated for transfers:
•
Y - Update the Buyer Queue Inquiry for the To warehouse when a stock transfer is generated.
•
N - Update the Buyer Queue Inquiry for the From warehouse when a stock transfer is generated.
11
For Auto Shp Whse Correction, determine if inventory is automatically adjusted at the shipping warehouse when an item is over-received in Transfer Receipts Entry or Batch Transfer Receipts Entry. Choose from the following:
•
Y - Automatically adjust inventory at the shipping warehouse when an item on a transfer is over-received at the receiving warehouse. Lot controlled items are adjusted also.
•
N - Do not automatically adjust inventory. You must manually adjust inventory at the shipping warehouse.
12
If Auto Shp Whse Correction is set to Y, enter a Default Reason Code to assign when inventory is automatically adjusted. Refer to Establishing Inventory Adjustment Reason Codes.
13
For Round Transfer to Pkg Qty, determine if Nightly Processing rounds to package quantities on recommended stock transfers. Choose from the following:
•
Y - Nightly Processing rounds to the closest package quantity (up or down) when recommending a stock transfer, regardless of turns and maximum quantity calculations.
•
N - Nightly Processing does not round to package quantities when recommending a stock transfer.
•
U - Nightly Processing always rounds up to the nearest whole package quantity.
14
For Transfer Consolidation, determine if batch printing consolidates stock transfers with the same ship-to/ship-from warehouse combination, the same ship via, and the same transfer type:
•
Y - Transfers are consolidated during batch printing. A separate line is created for each line item updated on the first transfer and linked sales orders are updated. This only applies for transfer types 01, 02, and 05 (refer to Defining Transfer Types).
•
N - Transfers are not consolidated during batch printing.
Consolidation Exceptions
This flag does not apply to:
•
Warehousing stock transfers.
•
Transfers that use continuous printing.
•
Transfers that have the Unusual Usage Flag set to Y.
•
Transfers with a future date that is different that the other transfers.
A separate line item is created on the transfer if:
•
Ship Complete for the line is set to Y.
•
The Emg? flag is set different for a line item.
•
Line items have different costs.
•
A linkage exists.
15
For Prorate Transfer Rate, determine if freight on a partial transfer receipt is pro-rated by the number of products received:
•
Y - Pro-rate the freight according to the transfer receipt quantity. The freight for each product is allocated according to the percentage set in Product Maintenance (refer to the Freight Information Screen).
•
N - Allocate all freight to the first receipt of the transfer.
