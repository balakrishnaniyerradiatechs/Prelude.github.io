Receiving On-The-Water Merchandise
 
When you import merchandise from an overseas vendor, the material usually becomes the property of your company before you physically receive it into your warehouse. Once the vendor notifies you that the material shipped, the on-the-water (OTW) receiving procedures allow you to receive the imported material for general ledger purposes, without updating inventory. You can set up import costs by vendor to estimate the total landed cost (product cost + import costs) before the shipment arrives.
Depending on how your warehouse is set up, you can use either the Purchase Order Method (recommended) or the Shipping Bin Method to receive on-the-water shipments. Refer to the following:
•
OTW Receiving Using a Purchase Order. Creates a new purchase order for the items in transit from the vendor. When the material arrives, receive the new purchase order in Stock Receipts Entry.
•
OTW Receiving Using Shipping Bins. Receives the items in transit from the vendor into a shipping bin. When the material arrives, use a bin move to put the items into inventory.
Import Cost Inquiry displays the shipping costs associated with an OTW shipment.
