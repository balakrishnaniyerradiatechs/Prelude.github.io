Transfer Entry Detail screen
The Transfer Entry Detail screen allows you to enter quantity information for each item on a stock transfer. Refer to Entering Stock Transfers for more information.
Menu Path: Inventory > Stock Movements > Transfer Entry > Detail Information
Field and Function Key Descriptions
Field/Function Key
Description
Ln#
The line number for the item on the transfer.
Qty Opn
The requested quantity to transfer.
U/M
The unit of measure of the product.
Accept
Indicates if everything on the Transfer Entry Detail screen is correct:
•
Y - Accepts the information on the screen and returns to the Transfer Entry screen.
•
N - Allows you to change any of the fields on the screen.
Pck Qty
The quantity of this item available to pick.
B/O Qty
The backordered quantity. You can change this quantity if you do not want any unavailable quantity to go on backorder for this transfer.
Sc
The supply code which appears if the product is on backorder. The code varies depending on the product’s replenishment path and product settings. Possible supply codes include:
•
B - Purchase order.
•
E - Emergency purchase order.
•
T - Transfer.
•
R - Emergency transfer
•
W - Work order.
•
D - Direct ship purchase order.
Ship Complete
Indicates if you want this line item to ship complete if backorders exist:
•
Y - Hold the transfer until the entire quantity ordered is available.
•
N - Ship the amount of the item currently available and ship backorders as they arrive.
Emg?
Indicates if the product is an emergency:
•
Y - The product transfer is an emergency. The receipt date is not used to calculate lead time.
•
N or Blank - The product transfer is not an emergency.
Cost
The unit cost of the item.
Available
The total quantity available in the shipping warehouse.
Onorder
The total quantity on order for the shipping warehouse.
Stock Status
The stock status of the item in the shipping warehouse:
•
S - Stock.
•
N - Non-stock.
•
C - Consigned.
Cross Reference & Related Product Information (unlabeled field at the bottom right of the screen)
 
Cross reference and related product information codes that may apply:
•
ALT - Alternate products exist for this product. Refer to Alternate Products for information on setting up alternate products.
•
SUB - Substitute products exist for this product. Refer to Substitute Products for information on setting up substitute products.
•
ACC - Accessory products can potentially be sold with this product. Refer to Accessory Products for information on setting up accessories.
•
KIT - This product is a kit parent item. The number displayed is the number of kits you can make with the available components. Refer to Kit Parent Products for information on setting up kits.
•
BOM - This product is a bill of material.
•
EQV - Equivalent products exists for product. Equivalent products are part numbers that currently do not exist in the system, but may in the future.
•
RSVD - The quantity in parentheses is reserved for a customer. Refer to Reserving Inventory for a Customer.
F2-Save
Saves the information in the detail screen and returns to the Transfer Entry screen.
F6-Subs
Opens the Product Substitutes Inquiry screen to allow you to view substitute information for the product.
F9-Invty Inq
Opens the Inventory Inquiry screen to allow you to view available, on order, and committed information for the product.
