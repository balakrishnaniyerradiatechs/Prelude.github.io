Inactive Product Report
The Inactive Product report displays products with no activity for a time period. You can use this report to identify incorrect part numbers and products to return, delete, or remove from inventory.
Menu Path: Inventory > Reports > Sales Status Reports > Inactive Product Report
Selection Criteria
Use the following criteria to limit the information to print on the report:
•
Division. The highest possible grouping in your company (e.g., Eastern Division, Western Division).
•
Region. A subset of Division - if Division is used (e.g., Southeast Region, Northwestern Region).
•
Warehouse. The warehouse(s) to include on the report.
•
Department. A subset of warehouse. Typically used to separate expenses within a warehouse (e.g., accounting, sales, warehouse).
•
Cost Center. A subset of Department - if Department is used. Typically used to separate expenses within a warehouse or department (e.g., shipping, receiving, outside sales, inside sales).
•
Product. The product(s) to include on the report. Leave blank to include all products.
•
Product Type. The products type(s) to include on the report. Leave blank to include all product types or choose from the following:
-
N - Rental item.
-
F - Finished good.
-
R - Raw material.
-
B - Both finished and raw material.
-
E - Expense item.
-
U - Used item.
•
Stocking Status Code: The stock status codes to include on the report. Leave blank to include all status codes or choose from the following:
-
S - Stock.
-
N - Nonstock.
-
C - Consigned.
•
Product Line. The product line(s) to include on the report. Leave blank to include all product lines.
•
Primary Vendor. The vendor(s) to include on the report. Leave blank to include all vendors.
•
Evaluation As Of Date. The date up to which you want to view activity (i.e., ending date).
•
Inactivity Date. The beginning date to review. For example, if you enter 01/01/05 for the Inactivity Date and 06/01/06 for the Evaluation As of Date, then the report will include all products with no activity from 01/01/05 until 06/01/05.
•
Onhand not equal to 0. Determines if you want to only show products with an on-hand quantity. Choose from the following:
-
Y - Only display products with an on-hand quantity or negative on-hand quantity.
-
N - Display all products, including products with an on-hand balance of zero.
•
TAI Type Exclusion List. The transaction audit inquiry codes to exclude from the analysis. For example, if you want to exclude returned products. Refer to Transaction Audit Inquiry Codes for code definitions.
Column Descriptions
Column Heading
Description
Product
The product number.
Description
Line 1: The product description.
Line 2: The second line of description for the product, if applicable.
Est-Date
The date the product was established in this warehouse.
Last-Act
The last date activity occurred for the product.
Onhand
The quantity of the product in on-hand inventory.
U/M
The unit of measure for the product as it exists in inventory.
Cost
The accounting cost of the product.
Extension
The total extended value of the quantity on-hand (Onhand x Cost).
