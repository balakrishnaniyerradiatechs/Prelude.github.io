Supercedure Products
A superceded product is a product that is replaced with another product, usually by the vendor (e.g., ABC is replaced by ABC-1). You can set up a new (superceding) part number to replace the superceded product. The usage from the old (superceded) product number is moved to the new product number.
You can sell the existing inventory of the superceded product but you cannot backorder it. The superceding product is recommended once the superceded product is out of stock. The superceded product no longer appears on recommended stock orders.
When you receive a superceding product that is linked to a sales order, you are prompted to substitute the old superceded product with the new product. If the cost is different, you are prompted to recost the line item on the purchase order. A comment line is added to the linked sales order to reference the original product. You must receive the purchase order by line item.
Kit Parents and non-stock items cannot be superceded.
To set up product supercedures:
1
Select Inventory > Maintenance > Supercedures to open the Supercedure Maintenance screen.
2
Enter the Superceded Product, which is the obsolete product number.
3
Enter the New Product, which is the superceding product number that replaces the Superceded Product. Depending on whether you entered an existing part number or are creating a new one, one of the following messages appears:
•
If the part number is new, the following message appears:
Select one of the following:
You Will Be Creating a New Product!
-
Return: Allows you to enter a different part number.
-
Continue: Creates a new part number to supercede the obsolete part number. The usage from the old part number moves to this part number.
•
If the part number you enter exists, the following message appears:
Select one of the following:
Caution! This Product Already Exists.
Do You Want to Proceed?
-
Yes: Updates the existing product number with the usage of the obsolete part number. The Description, Description Two, and Vendor Product from the existing product number are automatically filled in. Go to Step 7.
-
No: Brings you back to enter a different part number.
4
For Description, enter the description for the new product.
5
For Description Two, enter the second line of description for the new product, if applicable.
6
If applicable, enter the Vendor Product for the new product.
7
Enter the Date Superceded, which is the date you want to begin superceding the old product with the new product number.
8
Press F2-Save to update the superceding product with usage from the superceded part. The superceded product is flagged as obsolete.
Once the product supercedure is complete, you can view the information in the Supercedures Information screen in Product Maintenance > F6-Xref/Notes > Supercedures.
