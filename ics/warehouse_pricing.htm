Warehouse Pricing Screen
The Warehouse Pricing screen allows you to enter price and cost values for a product for each warehouse. You can also use this screen to calculate and recompute prices/costs. These values override the values entered at the product level in Product Maintenance (refer to the Product Pricing Screen).
Menu Path: Inventory > Maintenance > Product Maintenance > Product Warehouse Maintenance > F7-Price/Cost
Field & Function Key Descriptions
Field/Function Key
Description
Current
The current price or cost for this product warehouse record for each of the columns listed below. Leave blank if using Percent and Basis to calculate the values. If you do enter a Percent and Basis, the calculated value displays in this column when you press F5-Recompute.
Percent
The percentage or multiplier to use with a Basis column to calculate the price or cost for each of the columns. Leave blank if you entered a price under Current and want to maintain the price manually.
The calculated values depend on how the Prc/Cst Basis Calc Method flag is set in Inventory Parameters.
If Prc/Cst Basis Calc Method is set to G for gross profit, enter the gross profit percent that you want to make for this product for this price level. The Percent is used with the cost column indicated in Basis (refer to the Basis codes below) to calculate the price.
For example:
•
Replacement Cost is 10.00.
•
If you want Price 1 to provide 20% gross profit above Replacement Cost, enter 20 under Percent and R under Basis.
•
When you press F5-Recompute, 10.00 is divided by .80 to come up with a Price 1 of 12.50. Gross profit is 20%.
If Prc/Cst Basis Calc Method is set to M for markup, enter the markup percent that you want for this product at this price level. The Percent is used with the cost column indicated in Basis to calculate the price.
For example:
•
Replacement Cost is 10.00.
•
If you want Price 1 to be 20% above the value of Replacement, enter 20 under Percent and R under Basis.
•
When you press F5-Recompute, 10 is multiplied by 1.20 to come up with a Price 1 of 12.00. The gross profit is 16.6%.
Basis
The price/cost column to use to calculate new price/cost values if using a percentage or multiplier. Refer to the above instructions for Percent for information on using percentages or multipliers with a Basis:
•
0 - Price column 0 (Suggested retail price).
•
1 - Price column 1.
•
2 - Price column 2.
•
3 - Price column 3.
•
4 - Price column 4.
•
5 - Price column 5.
•
R - Replacement cost.
•
A - Average cost.
•
S - Standard cost.
•
O - Cost for sales.
•
H0 - Suggested retail price - from the product (header) level.
•
H1 - Price column 1 - from the product (header) level.
•
H2 - Price column 2 - from the product (header) level.
•
H3 - Price column 3 - from the product (header) level.
•
H4 - Price column 4 - from the product (header) level.
•
H5 - Price column 5 - from the product (header) level.
•
HR - Replacement cost - from the product (header) level.
•
HS - Standard cost - from the product (header) level.
•
HO - Cost for sales - from the product (header) level.
Q/B
A Y displays if the price level uses column quantity breaks.
List - Price 5
Default customer prices. Each customer is assigned a price code to use if special pricing does not exist for this product. You can set these prices at the product level to use company wide or by warehouse in Product Warehouse Maintenance if prices vary by location.
Replacement Cost
The cost to replace the product in this warehouse. This overrides the cost at the product level in the Product Pricing Screen, but can be overridden at the product vendor level (refer to Using Company Vendor Cost), or the product/vendor/warehouse level in Maintaining a Product by Warehouse/Vendor).
The Replacement Cost Update flag in Inventory Parameters determines if replacement cost is updated in Purchase Order Entry, Stock Receipts Entry, or in Product or Product Warehouse Maintenance.
Standard Cost
If you use standard cost as the accounting cost for your company (G/L Cost flag set to S in Company Maintenance), you can maintain the cost at the product level or in this field at the product warehouse level. You must set up general ledger accounts to post the differences between the standard costs and the actual costs from stock receipts, transfers, and vendor invoice reconciliation. Also, standard costs from all the components on a bill of material can be rolled up into one product.
Cost For Sales
The cost for sales orders, which is different than the accounting cost. You may want to use this to calculate gross profit for commission.
Direct Ship Cost
The cost to direct ship the product if it is different than the Replacement Cost. You can override this cost at the company vendor/cost level, the product vendor level, or on a purchase order.
Minimum Price
The minimum sales price to accept for this product without requiring a password or placing the order on administrative order hold (AOH Type = N). This price overrides the minimum price in Product Maintenance > F7-Pricing > Minimum Price.
Average Cost
The average cost calculated from receipts.
Pricing U/M
The unit of measure to use for this product on a sales order. Prices defined in this screen should be defined in this unit of measure. If pricing, buying, and stocking unit of measures are different, refer to Establishing a Unit of Measure.
Stocking U/M
The unit of measure used for this product in inventory. Costs defined in this screen should be defined in this unit of measure. If pricing and stocking unit of measures are different, refer to Establishing a Unit of Measure.
Add On Cost
The amount to add to the net cost per unit before the average cost is calculated in stock receipts. Stock receipts distributes the total add-on amount to the cost absorption account defined in the G/L table and in the product line G/L accounts.
Product Q/B
Indicates if price quantity breaks are set up for this product. Refer to the Warehouse Quantity Breaks Screen for information on setting these up:
•
Y - Quantity break pricing is set up for this product. You can view and edit these by pressing F8-Prc QtyBrks.
•
N - Price quantity breaks are not set up for this product.
F2-Save
Saves the warehouse pricing information.
F5-Recompute
Recalculates the prices and costs if you used Percent and Basis to calculate current values.
F7-Col Qty Brks
Opens the Column Quantity Breaks Screen to allow you to determine price levels for different quantity levels.
F8-Prc Qty Brks
Opens the Warehouse Quantity Breaks Screen to allow you to determine price levels for quantity breaks.
F9-Add Info
Opens the Additional Warehouse Pricing Screen to allow you to enter or change acquired and landed cost for a product.
Action Bar
Future
Opens the Product Warehouse Future Pricing Screen to allow you to enter future prices and the date these prices go into effect.
Previous
Opens the Previous Prices Screen to allow you to view previous prices set up for this product warehouse record.
Previous prices are only available if you chose Y to the “Save Previous Prices?” prompt when you updated a price. Refer to Updating Prices and Costs.
Add Prices
Opens the Add. Price Columns Screen to allow you to enter additional price column descriptions and prices for a product.
