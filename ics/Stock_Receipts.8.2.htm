Receiving Stock
You can receive stock and update on-hand quantities as products arrive in your warehouse. The first time you receive products on a purchase order, a receiver number is created which is the purchase order number with a –1 (e.g, 100020-1). If the purchase order is not received complete, a second receiver is created when you receive the backorder. The second receiver number is the purchase order with a - 2 (e.g., 100020-2). The receiver numbers continue to generate for each receipt until you receive or delete all items on the purchase order.
Related Reports
Description
Purchase Order Receiving Report
Allows you to receive products that arrive without a packing slip. Lists products on a purchase order or multiple purchase orders and provides a space for you to write in the received quantities.
Putaway Documents Report
Bin location and/or linked sales order information for received products to help determine where to put away stock.
To receive stock for a purchase order:
1
Select Inventory > Stock Receipts > Stock Receipts Entry to open the Stock Receipts Entry screen.
If you have access to more than one warehouse, the Location Retrieval screen appears first. Enter the appropriate Selling Warehouse, Department, and/or Cost Center.
2
Enter the P/O Number to receive. The Vendor and all un-received merchandise for this purchase order appear.
If the purchase order is on hold, you can not receive merchandise until it is released from Purchase Hold Release in the Buyer Queue Inquiry.
3
For Receipt Date, enter the day the material entered the warehouse.
4
For Received By, enter your user ID. The following prompt displays:
Do you Want to Receive Purchase Order By...
5
Choose from the following:
•
Line Item: Receives one line item at a time. You might use this option if only a few items or partial quantities arrive. Highlight the item to receive and press ENTER to open the Detail Information screen. Continue with step 6.
•
Exception: Automatically enters the ordered Quantity into the Received quantity on every item. You might use this option if most of the order arrived and you only need to change a few items or quantities. Highlight the item to change and press ENTER to open the Detail Information screen. Continue with step 6.
•
Complete: Receives the entire purchase order and closes the receiver. This option receives all items in full and does not allow changes. If the purchase order is in fact complete, you may want to use the Exception option which defaults all of the open quantities on the purchase order as received, but allows you to review and change the quantities.
•
Abort: Exits the receiver without updating any information.
Receiving Lot & License Plate Products
You must choose Line Item to receive lot items. Refer to Receiving Lot Number Products and Receiving License Plate Products for more information.
If you choose Line Item or Exception, the Detail Information screen opens to allow you to enter the received quantity for a line item.
6
For Qty-Rec, enter the quantity received into your warehouse. As you receive each item, the Recv Amount at the bottom-right corner of the screen displays the running total of the received amount.
7
If any information is incorrect, enter N for Accept and change the data as necessary. Whse is only accessible if the P/O type is multi-branch (04).
8
Once everything is correct, press F2-Save to receive the line item.
9
After you enter the correct received quantity for each line item, press F2-Save to update inventory.
You can add line items if products arrive that are not on the purchase order.
If you allocate received material automatically, you can release pick tickets after you enter one or more receivers to fill open backorders (refer to Releasing Pick Tickets). Refer to Allocating Inventory for information on allocating inventory.
Receiving Serialized Products
If the received item requires a serial number, you can either enter the number or press N to generate a new serial number. Refer to Entering and Tracking Serial Numbers for information how to set up a product to require a serial number.
Receiving Equipment Numbers
If the received item is set up with an option group, you are prompted to enter the applicable equipment option codes. Refer to Using Equipment Option Codes for information on equipment options and option groups.
Receiving Over-Shipments
Allow Overage in SRE in Inventory Parameters determines if a linked, over-received item is added to the sales order or put into stock. If you over-receive an item by more than the tolerance limits established in Warehouse Maintenance, the receiver appears in the Stock Receipts Discrepancy Inquiry.
Changing Cost in Stock Receipts
Stock Rec Cost Prompt in Inventory Parameters determines if you can access cost in the Stock Receipts Entry Line Item Detail screen.
Printing Labels
If a product requires labels, the Labels Information screen appears. Refer to Printing Barcode Labels when Receiving Stock for information on labels.
Rejected Inventory
If the item must be returned to the vendor because it is damaged or over-shipped, press F5-Reject Claim to open the Line Item Quality Control screen. Refer to Rejecting Inventory When it is Received.
Freight for Linked Sales Orders
If a sales order is linked to the purchase order, the Freight To Sales Order screen appears upon completion of the receipt to allow you to allocate all or part of the freight to the sales order. Refer to Allocating Freight to Linked Sales Orders.
Buyer Hold
To send the receiver to the buyer’s work queue for review, select Buyer Hold from the Action Bar. The buyer must release the receiver from the Stock Receipts Discrepancy Inquiry before Accounts Payable can reconcile it with the vendor invoice.
Non-Nettable Products
Refer to Receiving Carousel Non-Nettable Products for information on receiving non-nettable products and the carousel interface.
