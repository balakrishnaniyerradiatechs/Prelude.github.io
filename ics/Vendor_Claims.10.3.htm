Moving Products into Warranty Inventory
If you intend to return a product to the vendor and you do not want it to show as available for sale, you must put the product into warranty inventory. The Prelude definition of warranty inventory is any product that is damaged, defective, incorrectly shipped, or for any other reason should be returned to the vendor. The product is tagged and held in a separate inventory until it is returned to the vendor.
Warranty inventory is classified as one of the following:
•
Warranty return - refers to material sent back to the vendor for credit or replacement.
•
Warranty scrapped - refers to the material that is thrown away rather than returned. The vendor still issues a credit or sends a replacement.
A tag number tracks the material from the time it goes into warranty inventory until you enter it on the vendor claim and return it to the vendor for credit or replacement (refer to Creating Vendor Claims).
You can enter a warranty tag for inventory in one of three ways:
•
Enter a Credit Memo to issue credit to a customer for returned merchandise. Refer to Creating Warranty Tags in Credit Memo Entry.
•
Enter an Inventory Adjustment to move on-hand inventory into warranty inventory. Refer to Creating Warranty Tags in Inventory Adjustment Entry.
•
Enter a Rejection Claim when you receive the material in Stock Receipts Entry. Refer to Creating Warranty Tags in Stock Receipts Entry.
