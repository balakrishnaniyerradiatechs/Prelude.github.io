Vendor Open Claims Report
The Vendor Open Claims Report displays vendor claims entered in Vendor Claim Entry but not yet updated in Vendor Claims Update Processing. You should run this report regularly to investigate old vendor claims that should be shipped, updated, or deleted.
After you create a vendor claim (refer to Creating Vendor Claims), the quantity of the vendor claim is still included in the on-hand inventory for the product. You must run Vendor Claim Update Processing (refer to Updating Vendor Claims) to adjust inventory and create an open receivable to reconcile with the vendor’s credit memo or replacement.
Menu Path: Inventory > Reports > Vendor Claim Reports > Vendor Open Claims Report
Selection Criteria
Use the following criteria to limit the information to print on the report:
•
Division. The highest possible grouping in your company (e.g., Eastern Division, Western Division).
•
Region. A subset of Division - if Division is used (e.g., Southeast Region, Northwestern Region).
•
Warehouse. The warehouse where the vendor claims were entered.
•
Department. A subset of warehouse. Typically used to separate expenses within a warehouse (e.g., accounting, sales, warehouse).
•
Cost Center. A subset of Department - if Department is used. Typically used to separate expenses within a warehouse or department (e.g., shipping, receiving, outside sales, inside sales).
•
Transaction Type. The status of the transaction. Leave blank to include all types or choose from the following:
-
P - Printed or faxed.
-
S - Suspended.
•
Claim Type. The type of claim created in Vendor Claim Entry. Leave blank to include all claim types or choose from the following:
-
01 - Regular vendor claim. For on-hand or warranty inventory.
-
02 - Vendor show claim. Refer to Entering a Vendor Show Claim.
-
03 - Vendor consigned inventory. Consigned inventory refers to products in stock that you do not pay for until sold.
-
04 - Non-inventory expense.
•
Sort By (B)uyer or (V)endor. The report sort sequence. Choose from the following:
-
B - Sort by buyer.
-
V - Sort by vendor.
Column Descriptions
Column Heading
Description
Vendor # / Name / Buyer Name
Line 1: The vendor number.
Line 2: The vendor description.
Line 3: The buyer name.
Claim#
The vendor claim number.
Proc
Indicates if the claim was flagged to print, fax, or email in Vendor Claim Entry:
•
Y - The claim was flagged to print, fax, or email.
•
Blank - The claim was suspended.
Tag Number
The assigned warranty tag number. Refer to Moving Products into Warranty Inventory.
Product/Description
Line 1: The product number.
Line 2: The description of the product.
U/M
The unit of measure for the product.
Claimed
The quantity of the product entered on the vendor claim.
Returned
The quantity marked for return to the vendor.
Scrapped
The quantity to scrap, rather than return to the vendor (you will still receive a credit or a replacement product from the vendor).
Cost/
Extension
Line 1: The unit cost of the product:
•
Replacement cost if the product came from regular inventory.
•
Cost at the time the warranty tag was created if the product came from warranty inventory.
Line 2: The extended cost of the total quantity of the product on the claim (Claimed x Cost).
