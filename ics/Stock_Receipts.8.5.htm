Entering Batch Stock Receipts
Batch Stock Receipts assigns a batch number to a group of receipts. After you enter all the receipts in one batch, you can print the Batch Edit report to confirm product numbers and quantities before you update inventory.
To enter batch stock receipts:
1
Select Inventory > Stock Receipts > Batch Stock Receipts > Batch Stock Receipts to open the Batch Number Entry screen.
If you have access to more than one warehouse, the Location Retrieval screen appears first. Enter the appropriate Selling Warehouse, Department, and/or Cost Center.
2
Enter a Batch Number as designated by your company. This number is for reference only and is not validated. The Stock Receipts Batch Entry screen appears.
3
Enter the P/O Number to receive. The Vendor and all open merchandise for this purchase order appear.
4
For Receipt Date, enter the day the material entered the warehouse.
5
For Received By, enter your user ID. The following prompt displays:
Do you Want to Receive Purchase Order By...
6
Choose from the following:
•
Line Item: Receives one line item at a time. You might use this option if only a few items or partial quantities arrive. Highlight the item to receive and press ENTER to open the Detail Information screen. Continue with step 7.
•
Exception: Automatically enters the ordered Quantity into the Received quantity on every item. You might use this option if most of the order arrived and you only need to change a few items or quantities. Highlight the item to change and press ENTER to open the Detail Information screen. Continue with step 7.
•
Complete: Receives the entire purchase order and closes the receiver. This option receives all items in full and does not allow changes. If the purchase order is in fact complete, you may want to use the Exception option, which defaults all of the open quantities on the purchase order as received, but allows you to review and change the quantities.
•
Abort: Exits the receiver without updating any information.
Receiving Lot & License Plate Products
You must choose Line Item to receive the merchandise. Refer to Receiving Lot Number Products and Receiving License Plate Products for more information.
If you choose Line Item or Exception, the Detail Information screen displays to allow you to enter the received quantity for a line item.
7
For Qty-Rec, enter the quantity received into your warehouse.
8
If you need to change any of the information for the line item, enter N for Accept and change the information as necessary. Whse is only accessible if the P/O Type is multi-branch (04).
Receiving Serialized Products
If the received item requires a serial number, you can either enter the number or press N to generate a new serial number. Refer to Entering and Tracking Serial Numbers for information how to set up a product to require a serial number.
Receiving Over-Shipments
Allow Overage in SRE in Inventory Parameters determines if a linked, over-received item is added to the sales order or put into stock. If you over-receive an item by more than the tolerance limits established in Warehouse Maintenance, the receiver goes on hold. Refer to the Stock Receipts Discrepancy Inquiry.
Printing Labels
If a product requires labels, the Labels Information screen opens to allow you to enter label requirements. Refer to Printing Barcode Labels when Receiving Stock for more information on labels.
Rejected Inventory
If the item must be returned to the vendor because it is damaged or over-shipped, press F5-Reject Claim to open the Line Item Quality Control screen. Refer to Rejecting Inventory When it is Received
9
Once everything is correct, press F2-Save to receive the line item.
10
After you enter the correct received quantity for each line item, press F2-Save to update inventory.
You can enter more receivers for the same vendor or different vendors using the same batch number. Run the Batch Edit Report to verify part numbers and quantities before you update inventory (refer to Updating Batch Stock Receipts).
If you allocate received material automatically, you can release pick tickets after you enter one or more receivers to fill open backorders (refer to Releasing Pick Tickets). Refer to Allocating Inventory for information on allocating inventory either manually or automatically.
Freight for Linked Sales Orders
If a sales order is linked to the purchase order, the Freight To Sales Order screen appears upon completion of the receipt to allow you to allocate all or part of the freight to the sales order. Refer to Allocating Freight to Linked Sales Orders.
Buyer Hold
To send the receiver to the buyer’s work queue for review, select Buyer Hold from the Action Bar. The buyer must release the receiver from the Stock Receipts Discrepancy Inquiry before Accounts Payable can reconcile it with the vendor invoice.
Non-nettable Products
Refer to Receiving Carousel Non-Nettable Products for information on non-nettable products and the carousel interface.
