Allocating Optimal Reel Quantities
If you have a product that is sold in reels, you are often left with small remaining quantities of the product. To prevent wasting the product, you can let the system determine the reels to cut. Reel optimization allows you to enter the quantity and length of reel cuts on a sales order and then let the system select the optimal lot(s) to fill the order.
Example: If a customer orders 4 cuts of cable at 200 ft. lengths and an 800 ft. reel is available, the 800 ft. reel is used to fill the order.
The system avoids allocating a lot to a cut quantity to eliminate wasted quantities and to leave as little on the reel as possible.
Example: If a customer orders 1 cut at 200 ft.and you have a 150 ft. lot and a 50 ft. lot available, the 200 ft. cut is backordered even though you have 200 ft. in inventory.
You can also activate the Lot Reel Prompting screen to display in Sales Order Entry to allow you to evaluate other open orders for the product that have not yet generated a pick ticket. You can shuffle the lot allocations from other orders to use the optimal reel based on the cut and lengths. However, the system will not un-allocate a quantity for a customer unless another appropriate quantity is available to allocate to the order.
Example: If a customer orders a 200 ft. cable and all you have available is a 1000 ft. cable, the system will allocate the 200 ft. from the 1000 ft. cable. Then, later in the day, you receive a reel that is exactly 200 ft. and another customer wants a 1000 ft. cable.
If the 200 ft. order has not yet printed a pick ticket, you can un-allocate the 200 ft. order from the 1000 ft. cut and re-allocate it to the 200 ft. cut. Then you can allocate the 1000 ft. order to the 1000 ft. cut.
You can activate reel optimization by product or by warehouse. If all products in your inventory are reels, you may want to activate reel optimization by warehouse.
Optimizing Reels for a Warehouse
To optimize reel quantities for a warehouse:
1
Select Warehouse Maintenance from the system menu.
2
Enter the Warehouse.
3
Select Action Bar > Addl Opts > Reel Optimization to open the Reel Optimization Maintenance screen.
4
For Sales Order Reel Optimization, enter Y to use optimal allocation of a lot/reel quantity on sales orders. The system finds the lot number that contains the majority of the available quantity to fill the lot cut requirement.
If more than one sales order is entered for a lot reel quantity, the system evaluates the lot quantities available to find the best match of each reel quantity for each sales order.
Optimizing Reels for a Product
To optimize reel quantities for a product:
1
Select Inventory > Product Maintenance > Product Maintenance to open the Product Maintenance screen.
2
Enter the Product.
3
Press F5-Add Info to open the Additional Information Screen.
4
For Lot Number Flag, enter Y to open the Lot Information Screen.
5
For Reel Optimization, enter Y to use the optimal lot quantity available to allocate lots on sales orders. This flag is only checked if the Sales Order Reel Optimization flag in Warehouse Maintenance is set to N.
6
For Reel Optimization Minimum, enter the minimum quantity that should remain for each lot number for this product before the system allocates another lot number. For example, if the Reel Optimization Minimum is 20, the system skips any lot that has less than twenty feet when allocating to an order for more than 20 feet.
