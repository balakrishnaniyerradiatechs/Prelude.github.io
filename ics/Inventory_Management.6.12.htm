Adjusting Inventory
When inventory discrepancies occur in your warehouse, you can use Inventory Adjustment Entry to manually correct the on-hand quantity of a product. When you adjust inventory, you must indicate which type of inventory you are adjusting so that the general ledger and on-hand balances are correctly updated. Inventory may be classified as:
•
Regular on-hand inventory.
•
Damaged inventory.
•
Scrapped/lost inventory.
•
Warranty inventory. Refer to Creating Warranty Tags in Inventory Adjustment Entry for information on warranty inventory adjustments.
Before you can adjust inventory, you must set up reason codes to identify the reason for the adjustment. The Inventory Adjustment Inquiry lists all inventory adjustments for a warehouse. Refer to the following sections for information on adjusting inventory:
•
Establishing Inventory Adjustment Reason Codes.
•
Adjusting Inventory Quantities.
•
Inventory Adjustment Inquiry.
