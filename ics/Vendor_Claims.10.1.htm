Vendor Claims & Warranty Replacements
When you return inventory to a vendor, you must indicate whether the product is overstocked, damaged, defective, or incorrectly shipped. The reason for return determines how to adjust inventory and general ledger balances.
The following vendor claim and warranty replacement procedures allow you to identify products to return to your vendors and to track the status of replacements and reimbursements:
1
Move the product into warranty inventory: If you have a product to return to the vendor that is defective, damaged, or incorrectly shipped, you can designate the product as warranty inventory using a tag number. The product moves from regular inventory into warranty inventory so that it cannot be sold. You can tag warranty inventory by:
•
Entering a credit memo to the customer.
•
Adjusting the quantity in Inventory Adjustment Entry.
•
Rejecting the item in Stock Receipts Entry.
2
Enter a vendor claim: Vendor Claim Entry identifies the vendor and the material to return (whether it is in warranty inventory or regular inventory). You can also reference the vendor RMA number, the vendor invoice number, and shipping information, if available.
3
Print the vendor claim: Vendor Claims Printing allows you to print, email, or fax the claim. You can also use the printed copy as a packing list to ship the material to the vendor.
4
Assign serial shipping container codes: This step is optional if your company prints SSCCs on barcode labels to track containers and contents.
5
Update the vendor claim: Vendor Claim Update Processing confirms the shipment and relieves the quantities from inventory. When you receive the credit memo from the vendor, you can reconcile it with the vendor claim. Refer to “Vendor Claim Reconciliation Entry” in the Accounts Payable documentation.
6
Enter a warranty replacement: Warranty Replacement Entry receives the vendor’s replacement for the defective product and updates inventory and the general ledger.

Related Reports
Description
Vendor Open Claims Report
Vendor claims entered but not yet updated in Vendor Claim Update Processing.
Vendor Claims Aging Report
Updated vendor claims that are not yet reconciled or replaced.
Warranty Tags Not On Claim Report
Products entered into warranty inventory but not yet entered on a vendor claim.
Wty Inventory Register Report
The last transaction status code and related document information for every warranty tag that was created, but the vendor claim is not yet reconciled with the vendor invoice.
Daily Warranty Replacements Report
Warranty replacement entries; including the replaced and replacing products, for a day or date range.
