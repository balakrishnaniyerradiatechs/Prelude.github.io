Warranty Tags Not On Claim Report
The Warranty Tags Not On Claim report displays warranty tags assigned to products that are not on a vendor claim. You should run this report regularly to follow-up on products put into warranty inventory (refer to Moving Products into Warranty Inventory). You must either enter the products on a vendor claim (refer to Creating Vendor Claims) or adjust the quantities out of inventory if they are not valid warranty inventory products.
Menu Path: Inventory > Reports > Vendor Claim Reports > Wty Tags Not on Claim Report
Selection Criteria
Use the following criteria to limit the information to print on the report:
•
Division. The highest possible grouping in your company (e.g., Eastern Division, Western Division).
•
Region. A subset of Division - if Division is used (e.g., Southeast Region, Northwestern Region).
•
Warehouse. The warehouse(s) with the open warranty tags.
•
Department. A subset of warehouse. Typically used to separate expenses within a warehouse (e.g., accounting, sales, warehouse).
•
Cost Center. A subset of Department - if Department is used. Typically used to separate expenses within a warehouse or department (e.g., shipping, receiving, outside sales, inside sales).
•
Primary Vendor. The vendor assigned to the warranty tags. Leave blank to include all vendors.
Column Descriptions
Column Heading
Description
Tag Number
The warranty tag number assigned to the product. Refer to Moving Products into Warranty Inventory.
T
The tag type. Indicates if the product will be returned to the vendor or scrapped:
•
R - Returned.
•
S - Scrapped.
Product Number
Line 1: The product number.
Line 2: The description of the product.
Line 3: The second line of description of the product, if applicable.
Doc Number
The document number associated with the tag. The prefix indicates how the product tag was created:
•
R - The stock receiver number if the product was rejected in receiving. Refer to Creating Warranty Tags in Stock Receipts Entry.
•
A - The adjustment number if the product was adjusted into warranty inventory. Refer to Creating Warranty Tags in Inventory Adjustment Entry.
•
T - The transfer number if the warranty item was received on a type 03 Warranty transfer from another warehouse for return to the vendor.
•
S - The credit memo number if the tag was created when the customer was issued credit for the returned product. Refer to Creating Warranty Tags in Credit Memo Entry.
Doc Date
The date the warranty tag was created.
Status
 
The status code that represents the last transaction that occurred for the warranty tag:
•
A/P - The vendor claim was reconciled with the vendor’s credit memo.
•
AUPD - The credit memo to the customer was invoiced.
•
CLM - A vendor claim was entered for the product.
•
IAE - Inventory was adjusted to move the product from regular inventory to warranty inventory.
•
R - The product was rejected in receiving.
•
SOE - A credit memo was entered for the customer.
•
TE - The product was entered on a stock transfer.
•
TR - The product was received on a stock transfer.
•
TRR - Inventory was adjusted to move the product from warranty inventory to regular inventory.
•
TS - The product was shipped on a stock transfer.
•
VCP - The vendor claim was updated.
•
WTR - A warranty replacement was entered for the product.
Price
The net sales price of the product if the item was put into warranty inventory using Credit Memo Entry.
Cost
The cost of the product when the warranty tag was created.
Quantity
The quantity on the warranty tag.
U/M
The unit of measure for the product.
Ext. Price
The extended sales price of the product (Price x Quantity).
Ext. Cost
The extended cost of the product (Cost x Quantity).
