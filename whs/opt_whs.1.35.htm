Transfers
The Warehousing Module provides a seamless method for transferring material from one location (warehouse) to another location. The system accumulates the products on to a single shipping transfer until the shipment is processed. Transfers in the warehousing module can be entered manually or by using radio frequency guns; both methods are covered in this document. Once the proper maintenance flags are set to determine the transfer rules for your warehouse (refer to Transfer Picking Options (F5)), the following steps are used in the transfer process:

Entering a Transfer
Transfers are entered into the system to move material from one location (warehouse) to another. When using warehousing, transfers do not print continuously but are held until the material is ready to be picked and then combined for similar warehouses.
In Transfer Entry, you must enter the warehouse the material is shipping ‘FROM and the warehouse the material is shipping ‘TO, then enter the products to be transferred. Always select the Print option, so that when the transfer is complete, the material on this transfer will be held until the next time a picking report is printed for this FROM/TO warehouse combination.
Transfer Bin Allocation (/TBA)
Transfer Bin Allocation must be run after transfers are entered and before they can be picked. This process allocates the material at the bin level and determines which bins the products are picked from first.
•
From Warehouse: Enter the warehouse the material is being shipped FROM.
•
To Warehouse: Enter the warehouse the material is being shipped TO.
•
Emergency Transfers Only: Determines if emergency transfer lines only are allocated during the Transfer Bin Allocation process. The E - emergency flag can be set for each line item on the stock transfer.
-
Y - To allocate emergency transfer lines only at the time of Transfer Bin Allocation. The allocation process includes all transfer lines with the E flag set to N.
-
N - To not allocate emergency transfer lines only at the time of Transfer Bin Allocation. The allocation process includes all transfer lines with the E flag set to N, Y or Null.
After the allocation procedure is run, the Transfer Warehouse Picking Inquiry is updated to display the lines that are available for picking.
Transfer Committed Inventory Report
Path: Inventory Reports > Stock Movements Transfer Committed Inventory Report (/TRAN.COMM.INV.RPT)
This report displays a total line count, pick quantity, and weight for the stock transfer lines to be released in Transfer Bin Allocation. This report is updated when the stock transfer is processed in Transfer Entry. Once the Transfer Bin Allocation process is completed and the stock transfer is printed, the # Lines, Pck-Qty, and Weight columns on the report are reduced. This report is available in versions 17 and higher.
Picking Transfers
Transfer Picking Report (/TRN.PICK.PRINT)
A report can be run for manual transfer picking or as a backup for the radio frequency device. The report shows the to branch, product, quantity and pick from bin. The selection process prompts for a user and the number of lines the user will pick. This allows multiple people to pick material for transfers at the same time. Once this report is created, each line item is assigned to a user for picking entry.
•
Warehouse: Enter the number for the warehouse where material is being picked.
•
To Warehouse: Enter the number for the warehouse where the material is being shipped to.
•
User Id: Enter the user id of the person responsible for picking the material.
•
Emergency: Determines if emergency transfer lines are selected for the transfer picking.
-
N - To select regular non-emergency transfer lines.
-
Y - To select emergency transfer lines. The E supply code is entered at the line item level during Transfer Entry to indicate an emergency transfer.
•
Zone: Enter the zone to limit the material picked to a zone for a bin location. (Optional).
•
# of Lines: Enter the number of lines to be picked by the user entered above. If you want to select all lines that are ready for a warehouse, go to the Transfer Warehouse Picking Inquiry, /TWPI, to see the number of lines ready to be picked.
•
Product Number: Enter a product number to only process lines for this product.
Once this report prints, you can use it to pick material from the bins listed on the report. Make note of any changes in quantity available or picked, or any changes in bin location, so that corrections can be made during transfer picking entry.
Radio Frequency Transfer Picking
Path: Warehousing > Handheld RF Menu > Doc Picking > Transfer Picking (/RTPE)
You are prompted for from warehouse, to warehouse, zone, number of lines, and if you want emergency transfers only. This moves the selected products from the queue that is being maintained for radio frequency transaction to your own personal queue. It displays the product, quantity and bin to pick from. You are guided through the warehouse in the logical picking sequence for each product. As each product is picked, it is moved to a cart. When all products selected were picked they are moved to a staging bin to await shipping. As each line is picked it is deleted from the transfer it was entered on. It is then added to a new transfer which includes all products being picked for that from / to warehouse combination, emergency flag and staging bin.
If a partial quantity of a line item is picked, the balance of the pick quantity for that product appears after the first pick The balance of the product is required until the entire quantity was picked or a 0 pick quantity is entered.
Transfer Picking Entry (/TPE)
If using the Transfer Picking Report, this process is used to enter transfer picking amounts to verify the quantities picked and bin locations. A staging area is designated for the material to reside in until it is ready to ship.
Once you have entered all of the FROM and TO warehouse information and your user id, the system brings in all of the items assigned to your user id for confirmation. Enter through each line item until you reach the To Bin, and enter the staging bin where the material is going to be located until shipment time. Since all items may were combined from several original transfers for picking purposes, the system generates a new transfer number for shipping based on the staging bin. Enter the staging bin number to move the product from the pick bin to the staging bin.
Transfer Warehouse Picking Inquiry (/TWPI)
This inquiry can be used to determine how many emergency and non emergency items need to be picked. Each warehouse is displayed with the lines ready to be picked.
Shipping and Receiving Transfers
Transfer Bin Report (/TRN.BIN.RPT)
When the products are ready to be shipped, the Transfer Bin Report is printed for the destination warehouse. This verifies the products in the staging bin and serves as a packing slip with the new transfer number created for these products.
Transfer Shipments Entry (/TSE)
Once the material is shipped from the FROM warehouse, this process is used to tell the system that the products were shipped. The new transfer release number is used (for example; 3550030-1). It deducts the quantity shipped from the on hand inventory balance at the shipping warehouse. The material is considered In Transit until it is received at the TO warehouse.
Bill of Lading Printing (/TBLP)
This process can be used to print a bill of lading form for the stock transfer, if required by your company.
Receiving Transfers (/TRE)
This process receives the products at the TO warehouse using the new transfer number. The on hand inventory balance is increased at the receiving warehouse.
