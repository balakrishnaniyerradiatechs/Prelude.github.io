Segment/Truck Route Processing
Radio Frequency Truck Route Processing allows for sales orders to be organized by truck route. Multiple sales orders can be combined to allow picking for the same customer/ship-to, known as a stop. Each truck route consists of multiple stops. A stop consists of all the products for a customer and is divided into picking Segments. A single segment may contain all of the products to be picked for that stop, or there may be multiple segments generated to allow multiple pickers to gang pick the products for that stop. Each ship via can create gang-picking segments.
•
Example: There are 100 products to be picked for customer ABC Supply.
•
Gang Picking Method: The 100 products are divided into 4 segments of 25 products each. This allows four pickers to each pick a separate segment allowing this stop to be completed sooner than if just a single picker was assigned the entire order.
•
Non Gang Picking Method: There is a single segment created with all 100 products. A single picker picks all of the products.
There are two classifications of segments, floored and non-floored. Each product is assigned to one of these categories in product maintenance. Floored items are items that are picked and taken directly to the staging area to await truck loading. Non-floored items are picked and taken to a packing station. Multiple occurrences for the same product are combined so that one pick is attempted for the total quantity. Refer to Sales Order Segment Picking Options for more information on Warehouse setup).
