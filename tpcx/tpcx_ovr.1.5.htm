Rationalizing Items
Item Rationalization allows you to source and sell items through Trading Partner Connect. You and your trading partners most likely have different part numbers for the same item, so TPCx translates your part number into the corresponding part numbers of your partners. To rationalize items, TPCx searches through your items until it finds the UPC (Universal Product Code) or manufacturer part number, which depends on the manufacturer. TPCx then uses the UPC or manufacturer part number to translate your part number into your partners’ part number. If an item is not rationalized, it cannot be traded via TPCx because your partners’ systems cannot convert your part number. You must rationalize items to trade them via TPCx.
For example, you want to purchase part number APEX 185-000, but your trading partner’s code for that part is P320 APEX. TPCx looks for part number P320 APEX in your partner’s database, but reports to you as APEX 185-000.
The key to efficient rationalization with a high hit rate is having valid and available data in Product Maintenance, including your product number, description, and UPC and the manufacturer’s vendor number and UPC. For rationalization to have the best chances for success, use this data consistently and keep rationalization in mind when adding new products.
 
Part numbers with quotation marks will be rejected due to the coding language.
For more information on rationalizing items, refer to Using the TPCx Website.
Managing Duplicates
Quite often, more than one part number will exist for the same item in your system. The item is rationalized, but Trading Partner Connect does not allow more than one item from the same trading partner to be associated with the same manufacturer’s item. For example, you have part numbers MMM 42305 and MMM-42305. Both items rationalize to 3M item 511004423056. These items will be rejected.
Depending upon the number of duplicates, the DRS (data rationalization specialist) will manually review duplicates and update the item rationalization request before sending the file results to the web. These decisions are based upon whether a part number is a stock or catalog item, how well the description matches the manufacturer data, or consistency in the distributor’s part numbers. However, the part number chosen may not be the part number used consistently in your company. Also, it is possible that a determination cannot be made. If you have a large list of duplicates, you may review and edit your product data and resubmit it for rationalization.
Adding Manufacturer Data
When submitting an item rationalization request via www.tpcx.com, note the number of items available in the manufacturer database. We continually build this database and establishing relationships with manufacturers. At times, the number of items is limited or certain divisions have not been imported. To correct this, you can e-mail or post data you received from the manufacturer to your implementation consultant, the data rationalization specialist, or the Trading Partner Connect account manager. If this data is not available, send the manufacturer’s contact name and phone number so we can contact them and request data file we could add to our database.
