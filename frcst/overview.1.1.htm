Forecasting Overview
The Forecasting module combines forecast formulas, sales trends, and seasonal requirements to forecast product demand to help your company purchase and control inventory more efficiently. You can create your own forecast formulas or use the formulas provided by Prelude to capture actual usage and calculate a forecast quantity. You can apply the formulas to products at the following levels:
•
Product/warehouse level (Product Purchasing Parameters Maintenance)
•
Vendor level (Vendor Line Maintenance)
•
Product line level (Product Line Maintenance)
•
Company level (Purchasing Parameters)
Trend factors are used to account for conditions in your business that could affect forecasting demand (e.g., recessions, business requirements for the same month’s last year usage versus the same month’s this year usage, sales fluctuations due to marketing strategies or promotions). Two methods are available to establish trend factors:
•
Trend Months are used to calculate a quantity to add to the forecast. Trend Months in Product Purchasing Parameters Maintenance overrides the Trend Months in Forecast Formula Maintenance.
•
Trend Percent adjusts the demand for each month. This percent calculates the trend quantity to add to the forecast. The Trend % in Purchasing Forecast Parameters Maintenance overrides the Trend % in Product Line Maintenance.
Once you define the formulas and trends, you can use the Distribution Requirements Planning procedures and the forecasting reports to evaluate the accuracy of each formula. You can identify products where actual usage deviates from the forecast outside an acceptable range, modify the formulas as needed, and generate purchase orders according to the best formula.
