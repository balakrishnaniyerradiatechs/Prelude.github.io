Distribution Requirements Planning Report
The Distribution Requirements Planning report is similar in structure and information to the Distribution Requirements Evaluation Inquiry. The resulting spreadsheet provides a projected forecast for the upcoming 9 months based on selected records. You would typically use this report for long lead time or early purchase products. After you run the report, you can use the same selection criteria in the DRP Purchase Order Generation Process to generate a purchase order for the recommended products. Refer to Generating a DRP Purchase Order.
Menu Path: Purchasing > Forecasting Subsystem > Distribution Requirements Planning > Distribution Requirements Planning Report
Selection Criteria
Use the following criteria to limit the information to print on the report:
•
Warehouse. The warehouse for which you want to view purchasing forecasts.
•
Consolidate w/Warehouses. Any additional warehouses to accumulate totals with the primary Warehouse defined. Enter ALL to get a corporate view of all warehouses. The Corporate view allows you to evaluate purchasing requirements for all warehouses in the company that use the DRP purchase method for a product. Leave blank to only view the forecast for the warehouse that you entered for Warehouse.
The Corporate View flag in Warehouse Maintenance > Action Bar > Addl Opts > Forecasting, allows you to determine if the corporate view always applies. Distribution requirements include all warehouses, regardless of the warehouses entered in the selection criteria.
•
Purchase Method. The purchase method to review (e.g., EOQ, DRP, CLS, AWU). Established in the product warehouse record.
•
Primary Vendor. The primary vendors to include. Primary vendors are assigned in Product Vendor Maintenance.
•
Product Line. The product lines to include in the report.
•
Product Range. The beginning and ending product numbers to limit the report to a set of product numbers.
•
Class. The product classifications of the items to include (1-13). Products are classified based on the total value of a product that moves through inventory each month. Refer to “Classifying Products” in the Inventory documentation for more information.
•
Override Lead Time Horizon. The number of months to use to determine how far ahead to evaluate a purchase for today. Overrides all products selected for the report.
•
Sort By Product Number Only?. Determines the sort sequence:
-
N - Sort by product line/class/product.
-
Y - Sort by product only.
Column Descriptions
Column Heading
Description
Product
Line 1: The product number.
Line 2: The product description.
Line 3: The second line of the product description.
Vendor
The primary vendor number and description. The primary vendor is assigned to each product in Product Vendor Maintenance.
Weight
The weight of one unit of the product.
Cost
The unit cost of the item from the product warehouse record.
Onhand
The quantity currently on-hand.
Pkg/Buy
Pack quantity and minimum buy quantity.
# Months/Safety
The number of months of safety stock established for the product. For DRP items, the surplus threshold also includes forecasts during the leadtime of the product. For example, if a product has a 60 day lead time and a 1 month safety quantity of 30, Surplus Threshold is calculated using a quantity of 90.
Formula
Forecast formula assigned to the product. If you chose to exclude a formula for a warehouse in Forecast Formula Maintenance (refer to Creating Forecasting Formulas), it will not appear for the selected warehouse.
MTDUsg
Month to date usage for the product (invoiced sales orders).
Max. 2 Months Usage
The maximum quantity of this product sold in a consecutive two-month period.
Last 12 Months Usage
The quantity sold in the last 12 months.
Prev 12 Months Usage
The quantity sold in the previous 12 months.
Whse
The warehouse where the product is stocked.
Ldt
The lead time for the item.
Cls
The inventory classification. Refer to “Classifying Products” in the Inventory Module
Last 12 Months
Mth
The last 12 months including this month.
Actual
The quantity sold in each of the last 12 months.
Frcst
The quantity forecasted for each of the last 12 months.
Beg Avail
The beginning balance for each future month. Each month after that is increased/decreased by the data in the other rows (Fut+B/O’s, In Trans, On Order).
Fut+B/O’s
The total quantity of sales orders entered that are scheduled for release in future months. For example, if a sales order is entered with a September 2006 date, the sales order quantity in the Sep ’06 column is updated. The quantity in this column is subtracted from the Beg Avail quantity.
In Trans
The total quantity in transit for the item. All OTW (on the water) type 12 purchase orders created from the On The Water Maintenance process and in-transit transfers are included in the total. The quantity in this column is added to the Beg Avail quantity.
On Order
The total quantity of open purchase orders, transfers, and work orders for the item. The quantity is added to the Beg Avail quantity.
Frcst P/O
The expected delivery of suggested purchase order quantities. For example, if the purchase order is placed today with an expected 3- month leadtime, you can expect a purchase order placed in August to be received in November.
Forecast
The forecast quantity calculated from the assigned forecast formula. This represents the forecast projection for each of the 9 months. The column for the current month has a pro-rated value based on today’s date.
End Avail
The ending balance calculated as follows: End Avail = Beg Avail – (Fut+ B/Os) + In Trans + On Order - Forecast.
Safety
The safety amount to keep in stock calculated by multiplying the number of months of safety by the forecast for the next month.
Net Avail
The net available balance for each month (End Avail - Safety.)
Order
The recommended purchase order quantity. The order quantity is placed in the earliest possible month in which you can purchase an item to handle the negative available minus the current lead time period.
For example, if the product has a lead time of 90 days, the earliest the product could be received is 3 months from today. Therefore, the net available status 3 months from today is evaluated to determine if material should be ordered. Order quantities are only recommended to manage a negative net available.
Order$
Displays the dollar value of the recommended purchase order.
