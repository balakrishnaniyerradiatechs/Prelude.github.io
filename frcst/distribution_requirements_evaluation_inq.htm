Distribution Requirements Evaluation Inquiry
You can use the Distribution Requirements Evaluation Inquiry to explore different purchasing scenarios by changing the forecast data. Changing any of the fields affects the overall analysis of the product but does not update the product record.
Evaluating Distribution Requirements
To view Distribution Requirements:
1
Choose Purchasing > Forecasting Subsystem > Distribution Requirements Planning > Distribution Requirements Evaluation Inquiry to open the Distribution requirements Evaluation Inquiry screen.
2
Enter the Product to evaluate.
3
Enter the Warehouse number associated with the product number.
4
For Ldt, enter the default lead time. You can enter a new value to calculate the forecast using a different lead time.
5
Enter the number of months of Safety stock. You can enter a new value to calculate a different safety stock level.
6
Enter the default forecast Formula for the product. You can enter a new formula to calculate the forecast using a different formula.
Detailed forecasting information for the product/warehouse appears. You can change the Ldt, Safety, or Formula to explore different forecasting possibilities. You can also change values in the Beg Avail, Fut+B/Oâ€™s, In Trans, On Order, Frcst P/O, and Forecast columns to see how it affects forecasted quantities.

Safety
The safety amount to keep in stock calculated by multiplying the number of months of safety by the forecast for the next month. For DRP items, the surplus threshold includes forecasts during the leadtime of the product. For example, if a product has a 60 day lead time and a 1 month safety quantity of 30, Surplus Threshold is calculated using a quantity of 90.
Net Avail
The net available balance for each month (End Avail - Safety).
Order
The recommended purchase order quantity. The order quantity is placed in the earliest possible month in which you can purchase an item to handle the negative available minus the current lead time period.
For example, if the product has a lead time of 90 days, the earliest the product could be received is 3 months from today. Therefore, the net available status 3 months from today is evaluated to determine if material should be ordered. Order quantities are only recommended to manage a negative net available.
