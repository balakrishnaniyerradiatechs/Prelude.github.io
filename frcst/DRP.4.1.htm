Distribution Requirements Planning
Distribution Requirements Planning (DRP) can help you to optimize your forecasting ability and buying efficiency for the following situations:
•
Products that have extended lead times.
•
Products that require scheduled deliveries from the vendor.
•
A single purchase order issued to last for an extended period of time (e.g., a pre-season buy).
•
A foreseeable vendor shut-down or other disruption of supply.
Prelude’s DRP tools allow you to project your stock position of a product for up to nine months into the future and to calculate order quantities that will ensure a high level of customer service. Once you set up formulas and trends for your products, product lines, vendor lines, and warehouses, you can compare formulas, identify sales trends, and evaluate slow moving inventory or products with long lead times. You can then use the information to generate purchase orders.
The following DRP functions allow you to compare forecast formulas and generate purchase orders:
•
Distribution Requirements Evaluation Inquiry.
•
Distribution Requirements Planning Report.
•
Generating a DRP Purchase Order.
