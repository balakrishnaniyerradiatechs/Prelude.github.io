Order Processing (OE)
Sales order processing includes Sales Order Entry, Release Confirmation Entry, and Invoice Printing (refer to the Order Processing documentation). The types of sales orders include:
•
Stock sales: Stock material is shipped from your warehouse to the customer.
•
Linked sales: Linked non-stock material you shipped to your customer.
•
Direct (drop ship) sales: Material your vendor ships directly from the vendor to the customer.

Sales Order Postings
The following sales order postings are created when orders are invoiced:
•
Accounts Receivable is debited for the full amount of the invoice for on-account and COD customers.
•
Cash is debited for cash sales.
•
Freight Billed, Sales Tax Payable, Handling, Restocking Charge, and Minimum Invoice Adjustments are credited for the amounts from the invoice.
•
Sales is credited for the merchandise amount.
•
Cost of Sales is debited for the total accounting cost.
•
Inventory is credited for the total accounting cost.
Accounts receivable is debited for the full invoice amount and all the other accounts (credits) should equal the accounts receivable debit or total invoice amount.
The credit to your inventory account should be the merchandise amount only. Service items are non-inventory items. If you add a service item to the sales order, but have not assigned the service product to a product line with non-inventory G/L account overrides, the service item may post to an inventory account. When you invoice the order, the inventory account is credited for the full amount of the invoice; therefore, your inventory could be unbalanced due to the service item. You should set up a unique product line for the service item and enter a non-inventory G/L account override. and then assign it to the service product.
Credit Memo Postings
Credit memos can be for inventory returned to stock, warranty returns, scrapped inventory, or damaged inventory (refer to “Credit Memos and RGAs in the Order Processing documentation). The postings are reversed from when it was invoiced. The only difference is the inventory account debited (e.g, Warranty Return, Damaged, Scrapped).
Average cost is updated for material returned to stock via credit memo or consignment, except for in the following situations:
•
Direct ship material.
•
Asterisk (*) products (products not in Product Maintenance).
•
Service products (e.g., labor or a cutting charges).
•
Kit parents.
