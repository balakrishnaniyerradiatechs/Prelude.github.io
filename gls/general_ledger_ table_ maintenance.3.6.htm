Default Chart of Accounts
Prelude provides the following suggested account numbers for your default chart of accounts. You can use these account numbers or use General Ledger Table Maintenance to edit the accounts to suit your business.
Cash Account (1010)
Debited when a cash sale is completed in Sales Order Entry, regardless of whether the customer paid by cash, check, or credit card. The general ledger account numbers for company bank deposits and checks are maintained in Bank Code Maintenance.
Accounts Receivable (1100)
Debited when a sales order is invoiced or when a lease invoice is generated. Credited when customer payments are posted in Cash Receipts Entry.
Possible overrides: Customer Maintenance, Sales Representative Maintenance, or Currency Type Maintenance (when multiple currencies are in effect; refer to Using Multiple Currency).
Rebate Receivable (1150)
Debited when rebates are calculated from the price matrix in Sales Order Entry. This posting credits the receivable and reduces the net amount sent to cost of sales.
Core Receivable (1160)
Debited for the cost of the core when a sales order with an exchange item (but not the core item) is invoiced. This posting occurs when the Auto Core Charge option parameter is set to N and Bill Core Charge in Sales Order Entry is set to No.
Credited for the cost in the pending core record when a core item is billed against a pending core in Sales Order Entry, when a core item is returned in Inventory Adjustment Entry against a sales order (pending core), when a core bank item is applied against a pending core in Core Bank Issues Entry or in the Automatic Core Application Process, or when a pending core is written off in Core Bank Issues Entry.
Lease Contract Receivable (1170)
Debited when Invoice Processing completes for a lease invoice. Credited during Lease Invoice Printing.
Repair A/R Offset (1120)
This account is used as the accounts receivable override for no-charge shop/repair.
Possible override: Shop Labor Maintenance
Cash Receipts Write Off (4985)
Debited for amounts written off through Cash Receipts Entry or High Volume Cash Receipts Update.
Possible override: Customer Maintenance.
Inventory (1200)
Debited when vendor invoices are reconciled in Vendor Invoice Reconciliation Entry or when the Cost Adjustment Posting is completed after the FIFO Revaluation Process Update.
Credited in Sales Order Entry or when a lease order is invoiced.
Possible overrides: Product Line Maintenance or the credit reason code in Credit Memo Entry.
Inventory Received - Not Invoiced (1205)
Debited when products are received in Stock Receipts Entry before the vendor invoice is received. As invoices are paid in Vendor Invoice Reconciliation Entry, these anticipated amounts are reversed and actual amounts are debited to inventory. This account added to Inventory Indirect/Linked Received - Not Invoiced should equal Accounts Payable Not Invoiced - Inventory.
Possible override: Product Line Maintenance for a received product.
Inventory Direct Shipped (1210)
Debited when products are shipped direct from vendors to customers and the vendor is paid. Credited for the cost of the direct ship product when the customer is invoiced.
Possible override: Product Line Maintenance.
Inventory Direct/Link Rcvd-Not Invoiced (1208)
Debited when direct ship products are received in Direct Ship Receipt Entry (DSRE). The credit posted for the DSRE transaction is to the A/P Not Invoiced – Inventory account. The DSRE produces the invoice for the customer without waiting for the vendor invoice. When the vendor invoice is reconciled, the account is credited and A/P Not Invoiced – Inventory is debited (the posting will reverse the accrual postings made in DSRE). If the vendor invoice is paid first, this account is not affected.
Possible override: Product Line Maintenance.
Inventory Indirect/Linked (1215)
Debited when vendor invoices are paid in Vendor Invoice Reconciliation Entry, or when a link is created in a stock transfer or an inventory adjustment. Credited for the cost of the indirect product in Sales Order Entry. An indirect sale is when a sale is linked to a purchase order, transfer, or work order. Indirect Inventory is stock or non-stock products that are linked.
Possible override: Product Line Maintenance.
Inventory Indirect/Linked Rcvd - Not Invoiced (1206)
Debited when a product that is linked on a purchase order is received in Stock Receipts Entry. The corresponding A/P Not Invoiced – Inventory account is credited. As invoices are paid in Vendor Invoice Reconciliation Entry, these anticipated amounts are reversed and the actual amounts are debited to Inventory Indirect/Linked. This account added to Inventory Received - Not Invoiced should equal Accounts Payable Not Invoiced - Inventory.
Possible override: Product Line Maintenance for the received product.
Inventory Consigned from Vendors (1227)
Debited when products are received on a type 10 consigned purchase order in Stock Receipts Entry. Credited for the consigned cost when the product is sold in Sales Order Entry or when the product is adjusted out in Inventory Adjustment Entry. Inventory is relieved only if no other regular inventory is available in stock.
Inventory Consigned to Customers (1225)
Debited when products are shipped to customers on a consigned order. Credited when the sales orders are invoiced.
Possible override: Product Line Maintenance for the consigned inventory.
Inventory Damaged (1230)
Debited when a product is identified as damaged in Credit Memo Entry or Inventory Adjustment Entry.
Possible override: Product Line Maintenance or the credit reason code for damaged inventory in Credit Memo Entry.
Inventory Warranty Return (1240)
Debited when a product is identified as a warranty return in Credit Memo Entry, Inventory Adjustment Entry, or when rejected in Stock Receipts Entry. Credited when the vendor credit memo is received and reconciled in Vendor Claim Reconciliation Entry.
Possible override: Product Line Maintenance or the credit reason code for warranty return inventory in Credit Memo Entry.
Inventory Warranty Scrapped (1245)
Debited when a product is identified as warranty scrapped in Sales Order Entry, Inventory Adjustment Entry, or when rejected in Stock Receipts Entry. Credited when the vendor credit memo is reconciled in Vendor Claim Reconciliation Entry or Warranty Replacement Reconciliation Entry.
Possible override: Product Line Maintenance or the credit reason code for warranty scrapped inventory in Credit Memo Entry.
Inventory Warranty in Transit (1258)
Debited when a product is shipped to another location on a type 03-Warranty Inventory transfer. Credited when the product on a type 03-Warranty Inventory transfer is received at the destination.
Inventory in Transit to Location (1260)
Debited when a product is shipped to another location. Credited when the product is received at the destination.
Possible override: Product Line Maintenance.
Inventory Work in Process (1265)
Debited when products are issued to work orders. Credited when the work order is completed.
Possible override: Product Line Maintenance.
Inventory Warranty Return Accrual (1241)
Credited when a product is returned to the vendor on a warranty claim in Vendor Claim Update processing. Debited when the vendor credit memo is reconciled in Vendor Claim Reconciliation Entry or Warranty Replacement Reconciliation Entry.
Inventory Warranty Scrapped Accrual (1246)
Credited when a warranty product is scrapped rather than returned to the vendor and a warranty claim is entered and processed in Vendor Claim Update Processing. Debited when the vendor credit memo is reconciled in Vendor Claim Reconciliation Entry or Warranty Replacement Reconciliation Entry.
Inventory Vendor Claim Accrual (1251)
Credited when a non-warranty product is returned to the vendor on a claim and processed in Vendor Claim Update Processing. Debited when the vendor credit memo is reconciled in Vendor Claim Reconciliation Entry or Warranty Replacement Reconciliation Entry.
Possible override: Product Line Maintenance.
Inventory Lease (1270)
Debited when a lease order is invoiced.
Inventory Shop Work in Process (1266)
Debited when products are issued to shop repair orders. Credited when the order is completed.
Possible override: Product Line Maintenance.
FIFO Revaluation Clearing (1290)
Credited once the Cost Adjustment Posting process is complete after the FIFO Revaluation Process Update. The FIFO Revaluation Process determines if the replacement cost for items are audited or updated.
Vendor Claim A/P Accrual (2022)
Debited when the vendor claim is updated in Vendor Claim Update Processing. The anticipated amount of the vendor claim is reversed when the vendor credit memo is reconciled in Vendor Claim Reconciliation Entry.
Vendor Claim Freight Accrual (5431)
Credited when the freight amount in the vendor claim entry header is entered in Vendor Claim Update Processing.
Vendor Claim Misc Charge Accrual (5432)
Credited when a vendor claim header that has a miscellaneous item is updated in Vendor Claim Update Processing.
Vendor Claim Cost Difference Accrual (5434)
Debited or credited when the vendor claim is updated in Vendor Claim Update Processing. The amount is the difference between the accounting cost and the requested cost on the vendor claim. This entry will be reversed when the vendor credit memo is received and entered in Vendor Claim Reconciliation Entry or Warranty Replacement Reconciliation Entry.
Possible override: Product Line Maintenance.
Vendor Claim Cost Difference (5436)
Debited or credited when the vendor credit memo is updated in Vendor Claim Reconciliation Entry. The amount is the difference between the accounting cost used when the claim was updated and the cost allowed by the vendor to calculate the actual credit.
Possible override: Product Line Maintenance.
Vendor Claim Quantity Disallowed (5438)
Debited when the vendor credit memo is entered in Vendor Claim Reconciliation Entry or Warranty Replacement Reconciliation Entry. The amount is the merchandise extension of the quantity disallowed by the vendor to calculate the actual credit.
Possible override: Product Line Maintenance.
Physical Inventory Adjustment (5400)
Debited or credited in Inventory Adjustment Entry.
Possible overrides: Product Line Maintenance, Inventory Adjustment Reason Code Maintenance.
Depreciation Allowance (1600)
Credited for depreciation allowances from the Fixed Assets module. Used when an asset is depreciated and no account number exists on the table in Asset Maintenance or Class Maintenance. Credited when a lease order is invoiced.
Depreciation Expense (6460)
Debited for depreciation expenses from the Fixed Assets module. This account is used when depreciation is processed for an asset for which no account number exists on the table in asset maintenance or class maintenance. Debits are posted to this account after lease invoices are generated.
Gain/Loss on Sale of Asset (9500)
Posted to for the difference between the sale price and the book value when a rental item is sold and the Sell Rent Prod as Inventory in Rental Option Parameters is set to N.
Possible override: Assets Maintenance.
Accounts Payable (2000)
Credited when vendor invoices are paid in Vendor Invoice Reconciliation Entry. This account is the default but you can change it in Vendor Invoice Reconciliation Entry. Debited when payments are processed. During the Void Check Entry process vendor invoices are re-opened and this account is credited.
Possible override: Currency Type Maintenance.
Accounts Payable - Consigned (2001)
Credited when vendor invoices for type 10 - Consigned purchase orders are paid in Vendor Invoice Reconciliation Entry. Debited when payments are processed.
Accounts Payable Not Invoiced - Inventory (2010)
Credited when products are received in Stock Receipts Entry. These credits remain until invoices are reconciled in Vendor Invoice Reconciliation Entry, which reverse these anticipated amounts and the actual amounts are credited to the Accounts Payable account. This account and Inventory Received Not Invoiced plus Inventory Indirect/Linked Received Not Invoiced accounts should be equal and offset each other.
Possible overrides: Product Line Maintenance, Currency Type Maintenance.
Accounts Payable Not Invoiced - Supplies (2020)
Credited when fixed assets or supplies are received in Stock Receipts Entry (i.e., purchase order types 07 and 08). These credits remain until invoices are reconciled in Vendor Invoice Reconciliation Entry, which reverses these anticipated supply costs and the actual amounts are credited to the Accounts Payable account.
Accounts Payable Not Invoiced - Consigned (2021)
Credited when products are received on a type 10 - Consigned purchase order in Stock Receipts Entry. Debited for the consigned cost when the product is sold in Sales Order Entry or when the product quantity is adjusted out in Inventory Adjustment Entry.
A/P Import Accrual
Records stock receipt accrual of import costs. Credited in Stock Receipts Entry, debited in Vendor Invoice Reconciliation Entry to reverse the accrual.
Core Bank Payable (2050)
Credited for the cost of the core when a core item is placed into the core bank in Inventory Adjustment Entry. Debited for the cost in the core bank when a core bank item is applied in sales order entry or when a core bank item is applied against a pending core record in Core Bank Issues Entry or in the Automatic Core Application Process.
Generate A/R Cred Bal Refund (2030)
Credited when customer refunds are generated in Accounts Receivable > Generate Credit Balance Refund. The same account number should be used for Process A/R Credit Refund Check (following account).
Process A/R Credit Refund Check (2030)
Debited when customer refund payments are generated in Accounts Payable. Use the same account number for Generate A/R Credit Balance Refund (previous account).
Customer Deposits Received (2035)
Credited when deposits are received in Sales Order Entry. Debited when the deposit is used on a sales invoice.
Possible overrides: Customer Maintenance, Sales Representative Maintenance.
Sales Tax Payable (2045)
Credited in Sales Order Entry and when a lease order is invoiced.
Possible overrides: Sales Tax Jurisdiction Maintenance, the Vertex Sales Tax, Customer Maintenance, Sales Representative Maintenance, Currency Type Maintenance.
Customer Job Use Tax Payable (2055)
Credited when a line item is flagged as Use Tax instead of Sales Tax in job invoice processing.
Customer Job Use Tax Cost (2060)
Debited when a line item is flagged as Use Tax instead of Sales Tax in job invoice processing.
A/P Tax Amount
Debited in Vendor Invoice Reconciliation Entry or Accounts Payable Manual Invoice Entry for the tax amount entered. Usually used for the VAT tax.
Freight Clearing (5430)
Credited from Accounts Payable only if Allocate Freight is set to Yes in Company Maintenance. Postings occur when freight bills are processed separate from the vendor invoices. Include Freight in Vendor Invoice Reconciliation Entry should be set to No. This account is debited when the freight bill is processed separately through Manual Invoice Entry.
Duty Clearing (5429)
Debited in Vendor Invoice Reconciliation Entry for duty import costs.
Brokerage Clearing (5428)
Debited in Vendor Invoice Reconciliation Entry for brokerage costs.
Letter of Credit Draw (1090)
Debited when a financial institution letter of credit is posted to the general ledger. Credited when funds are committed and advanced to a purchase order in the Accounts Payable Letter of Credit Entry.
Letter of Credit Advance (1095)
Debited when funds are associated with a purchase order in the Accounts Payable Letter of Credit Entry. Credited when the product is received in Stock Receipts Entry.
Intercompany A/R Clearing (1140)
Used in Accounts Receivable when cash is applied and deposited by one location for another location. A credit is posted to the location that applies the cash and deposits it in the bank. A debit is posted to the other location. This entry only occurs if branch balance sheets are required. Otherwise, enter a prefix and account number since X in the prefix creates an entry.
Intercompany A/P Clearing (2040)
Used when payment is issued by one location for another location or when vendor invoices are processed. A credit is posted for the location that issued the invoice. A debit is posted for the location that incurred the expense. Only necessary if branch balance sheets are required. If they are not required, then enter a prefix and account number.
Transfer Freight In (5420)
Debited when freight is calculated on stock transfers for the receiving location.
Possible override: Division Maintenance.
Transfer Freight Out (5425)
Credited if freight is calculated on stock transfers for the shipping location.
Possible override: Division Maintenance.
Transfer Inventory In (2040)
Credited at the receiving location when the product is transferred and becomes a payable for the receiving location. Only necessary if branch balance sheets are required. Otherwise, use the same account number, including the prefix, for Transfer Inventory Out, Inter-company Sales A/R, and Inter-company Sales A/P.
Possible override: Division Maintenance.
Transfer Inventory Out (1140)
Debited to the shipping location when material is transferred. It then becomes a receivable for the shipping location. This entry is only required if branch balance sheets are required. If not, then use the same account number, including the prefix, for Transfer Inventory In, Inter-company Sales A/R, and Inter-company Sales A/P.
Possible override: Division Maintenance.
Transfer Cost of Goods (5350)
Debited to the shipping location when a Transfer Revenue Matrix exists for locations that transfer products. The posting occurs at Transfer Receipt Entry. The amount posted is the extended cost of the product in the shipping warehouse.
Transfer Revenue (4350)
Credited to the shipping location when a Transfer Revenue Matrix exists for locations that transfer products. The posting occurs at Transfer Receipt Entry. The amount posted is the cost of the product in the shipping warehouse multiplied by the matrix markup extended by the quantity shipped.
Transfer Std Cost Variance (5755)
Debited or credited to the shipping location if the standard cost accounting method is used. The difference between the shipping location standard cost and receiving location product standard cost is posted to this account.
Possible override: Product Line Maintenance.
Transfer Over Receive Variance (5610)
Credits are posted to the receiving location if items are received in Transfer Receipts Entry that were not on the original transfer. The cost of the over-received items are posted to this account.
Intercompany Sales A/R (1140)
Debited to the shipping location when products are shipped from one location and sold by another location. The amount then becomes a receivable for the shipping location. This entry is only required if branch balance sheets are required. If not, then use the same account number, including the prefix, for Transfer Inventory In, Transfer Inventory Out, and Inter-company Sales A/P.
Intercompany Sales A/P (2040)
Credited to the selling location when products are shipped from one location and sold by another location. The amount then becomes a payable for the selling location. This entry is only necessary if branch balance sheets are required. If not required, then use the same account number, including the prefix, for Transfer Inventory In, Transfer Inventory Out, and Inter-company Sales A/R.
Retained Earnings (3500)
Credited or debited for the profit or loss when End of Year Closing runs. You can set up a prefix or an X (wild card) prefix. If you use wild cards, a retained earnings account must exist in Chart of Accounts Maintenance for each possible combination. You are warned in the Year End process (refer toEnd-of-Year Closing Procedures) if an account does not exist.

Purchase Price Variance (5760)

Core Cost Variance (5765)

This account is also posted to when the applied core bank quantity does not match the pending core quantity in Sales Order Entry, Inventory Adjustment Entry, or Core Bank Issues Entry.

Do Not Use System Process (9988)
Used when an item on the table does not apply to your company’s activities. You can create a new account number but mark it Do Not Use somewhere in the description of the account (e.g., 9901 DNU – Rebate Receivable, 9902 DNU – Core Receivable). Always use a new account number for each item that you don’t expect to have general ledger activity in case it is accidentally used.
Suspense (9999)
Posted to when the system attempts to post to an account number that does not exist. Typically, these roll up to one account (headquarters or corporate). Run the Suspense Account Posting Report and then create Journal Entries to clear this account. By using the highest available account number for this purpose only, any suspense postings that may occur will stand out on the daily posting reports. Assign prefixes to this account.
