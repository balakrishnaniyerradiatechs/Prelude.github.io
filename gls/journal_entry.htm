Journal Entry Routine screen
Journal entries allow you to correct errors on general ledger postings or to enter year-end adjustments. Refer to Journal Entries for more information.
Menu Path: Financial > General Ledger > Journal Entries > Journal Entry
Field and Function Key Descriptions
Field/Function Key
Description
Journal Number
The journal entry number.
User
The user ID of the person that entered the adjustment.
Transaction Date
The date and fiscal period of the journal entry.
Status
The status of the journal entry:
•
O - Open. The status of the journal entry is open when you first enter it.
•
P - Posted. The status changes to P after you post the entry. If you go back into the journal entry, you will not be able to access any of the fields.
Reversal Date
The date and fiscal period of the reverse journal entry. The date must be later than the current transaction period. You can reverse a journal entry if you want to accrue an expense for one month and then back out the transaction the next month. When you reverse an entry, two entries are posted to the G/L. All debits are posted as credits and credits are posted as debits.
Description
The reason for the general ledger adjustment. The description displays on reports and inquiries and can be used as part of the search criteria.
Ln#
The line number to reference the general ledger adjustment.
Div
The division number if your company uses divisions. A division is the highest possible grouping in your company (e.g., Eastern Division, Western Division).
Reg
The region number, if your company uses divisions. Regions are a subset of Division - if Divisions are used (e.g., Southeast Region, Northwest Region).
Loc
The location number which could be a warehouse location (e.g., Denver, Dallas) where inventory is kept or it could be a corporate location where transactions may post to.
Dep
The department number if your company uses departments. Departments are a subset of Locations, typically used to separate expenses within a warehouse (e.g., accounting, sales, warehouse).
Cc
The cost center number if your company uses departments. Cost centers are a subset of Departments - if Departments are used. Typically used to separate expenses within a warehouse or department (e.g., shipping, receiving, outside sales, inside sales).
Account
The general ledger account to post to.
Description
The description of the general ledger account.
Debit Amount
The amount to debit to the general ledger.
Credit Amount
The amount to credit to the general ledger.
C
Indicates if you want to enter a comment regarding this journal entry:
•
Y - Opens the Journal Comments screen to allow you to enter a comment explaining this entry. After the comments are entered, you can use F5-Comment Display to review them.
•
N - No comments are necessary for this journal entry (default).
Comment
Any comments associated with the journal entry. These do not display until after the entry is posted.
Net Balance
Any difference between the debit and credit amounts.
Totals
The totals of the debit and credit amounts.
F2-Save
Posts the Journal Entry to the general ledger.
F4-Del
Deletes the journal entry.
F5-Comment Display
Opens the Journal comments screen to allow you to view any comments entered under C.
F9-Goto Line
Allows you to enter a line number, page number, or reference number to go directly to.
Action Bar
Add. Info
Opens the Journal Entry Additional Information screen to allow you to view the date and time the entry was entered and posted.
