Recurring Journal Entry Proof Report
The Recurring Journal Proof Entry report lists recurring journal entries set up for a date range.
To run the Recurring Journal Entry Proof report:
1
Select Financial > General Ledger > Journal Entries > Recurring Journal Entry Report to open the JERECC Selection Criteria screen.
2
For Journal Num Equal To, enter the journal number to list or leave blank to show all recurring journal entries.
The Recurring Journal Entry Proof Report lists recurring journal entries for a journal number.
Column Descriptions
Column Heading
Description
Journal #
The recurring journal entry number.
TransDay
The scheduled day of the month that the general ledger should be adjusted for this entry.
BPeriod
The fiscal period of the first occurrence of the recurring journal entry.
EPeriod
The fiscal period of the last scheduled occurrence for the recurring journal entry.
Batch #
The batch number if you entered a batch number in Recurring Journal Entry.
Status
Not applicable.
Description
The description of the recurring journal entry. The reason why the recurring entry was set up to post to the general ledger.
Div
The division number if your company uses divisions. A division is the highest possible grouping in your company (e.g., Eastern Division, Western Division).
Reg
The region number, if your company uses regions. Regions are a subset of Division - if Divisions are used (e.g., Southeast Region, Northwest Region).
Loc
The location number, which could be a warehouse location (e.g., Denver, Dallas) where inventory is kept or it could be a corporate location where transactions may post to.
Dep
The department number if your company uses departments. Departments are a subset of Locations, typically used to separate expenses within a warehouse (e.g., accounting, sales, warehouse).
Ccr
The cost center number if your company uses cost centers. Cost centers are a subset of Departments - if Departments are used. Typically used to separate expenses within a warehouse or department (e.g., shipping, receiving, outside sales, inside sales).
Account
The general ledger account that was adjusted.
Description
The description of the general ledger account.
Debit Amount
The adjustment amount debited to the corresponding account.
Credit Amount
The adjustment amount credited to the corresponding account.
Distribution Comment
Any comments entered for the recurring journal entry when the account was adjusted.
Journal Totals
The total of the debit and credit amounts.
