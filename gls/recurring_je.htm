Recurring Journal Entry screen
The Recurring Journal Entry screen allows you to set up the same adjustment to the same general ledger account for a range of fiscal periods. Refer to Creating Recurring Adjustments to General Ledger Accounts.
Menu Path: Financial > General Ledger > Journal Entries > Recurring Journal Entry
Field and Function Key Descriptions
Field/Function Key
Description
Journal Number
The recurring journal entry number.
Transaction Day
The day of the month for the adjustment to occur every month. For example, if you want the entry to post to the general ledger on the 28th of every month, enter 28.
Reversal Date
The date to reverse the journal entry every month. You can reverse a journal entry to accrue an expense for one month, and then back out the transaction the next month. The reverse date must be greater than the transaction date. When you post the recurring journal entry (refer to Posting Recurring Entries), the reversal date is cleared.
Description
Explanation of the recurring adjustment. This description displays on general ledger reports and inquiries.
Begin Period
The beginning fiscal period in which the recurring journal entry should post to the general ledger.
End Period
The ending fiscal period in which the recurring journal entry should post to the general ledger.
Batch Number
The batch number to assign to the recurring journal entry. You can use this number when posting recurring journal entries so that all entries with the same batch number are posted together (refer to Posting Recurring Entries).
Ln#
The line number to reference the general ledger adjustment.
Div
The division number if your company uses divisions. A division is the highest possible grouping in your company (e.g., Eastern Division, Western Division).
Reg
The region number, if your company uses divisions. Regions are a subset of Division - if Divisions are used (e.g., Southeast Region, Northwest Region).
Loc
The location number which could be a warehouse location (e.g., Denver, Dallas) where inventory is kept or it could be a corporate location where transactions may post to.
Dep
The department number if your company uses departments. Departments are a subset of Locations, typically used to separate expenses within a warehouse (e.g., accounting, sales, warehouse).
Cc
The cost center number if your company uses departments. Cost centers are a subset of Departments - if Departments are used. Typically used to separate expenses within a warehouse or department (e.g., shipping, receiving, outside sales, inside sales).
Account
The general ledger account to post to.
Description
The description of the general ledger account.
Debit Amount
The amount to debit to the general ledger on a recurring basis.
Credit Amount
The amount to credit to the general ledger on a recurring basis.
C
Indicates if you want to enter a comment regarding this journal entry:
•
Y - Opens the Journal Comments screen to allow you to enter a comment explaining this entry.
•
N - No comments are necessary for this journal entry (default).
F2-Save
Saves the recurring journal entry information. The debits and credit must balance before you can close the screen.
F4-Del
Deletes the recurring journal entry.
F5-Posting History
Opens the History of Recurring JE Postings screen to display any previous recurring journal entries for this G/L account number.
F9-Goto Line
Allows you to enter a line number, page number, or reference number to go directly to.
