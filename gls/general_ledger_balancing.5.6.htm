Accounts Payable (AP)
Accounts payables refers to money you owe others (e.g. vendors). When the vendor invoice is reconciled (refer to “Processing Vendor Invoices” in the Accounts Payable documentation), the following postings occur:
•
The accrual account postings from Stock Receipts are reversed.
•
Accounts Payable is credited.
•
The appropriate Inventory account is debited. If the product line for the product received has an override general ledger account, that account is used.
•
If the received cost was adjusted during reconciliation, average cost is updated. Inventory is debited for the correct, revised cost.
•
If the on-hand balance is less than the original quantity received, only the remaining on-hand quantity updates with the correct revised cost. The other portion of the cost adjustment posts to Cost of Sales Adjustment.
When material is returned to your vendor and a vendor claim is updated (refer to “Vendor Claims and Warranty Replacements” in the Inventory documentation), the following postings occur:
•
The posted updates from invoice reconciliation are reversed depending on the type of return (Inventory, Warranty Returned, or Warranty Scrapped).
•
Inventory is credited.
•
Accounts Payable is debited.
During Vendor Claim Reconciliation or Warranty Replacement Entry (refer to “Reconciling Vendor Claims” in the Accounts Payable documentation), if a quantity is disallowed, the following postings occur:
•
The accrual entries are reversed.
•
Vendor Claim Quantity Disallowed is debited.
•
If the replacement cost for the material is different than the accounting cost of the returned material, Cost Difference is debited or credited, depending on if the difference is negative or positive.
Average Cost Calculation for Unlinked Transactions Only
[(avg cost x previous stock on-hand) + (quantity x transaction cost)] / new stock on-hand.
