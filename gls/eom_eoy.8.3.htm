Constructing the Fiscal Period Table
You must construct the fiscal period table before you can post any transactions to the general ledger. The table contains all valid periods necessary to post accounting data and allows you to maintain accounting records by date without requiring a month-end cutoff. You can continue to enter transactions in a new month, even if the old month is still open.
For example, at end-of-month, you can open a new period but still post cash receipts and pay vendor invoices against previous periods. Then, on the 5th of the month, you can change to allow journal entries only, then on the 10th of the month, you can close it completely.
Prelude allows you to maintain between 3 and 7 years of history (the default is 4). This gives you plenty of time to settle this year’s financials before moving the data. After this year is completely closed and all entries are adjusted, you can delete a fiscal period to move last year’s financials and move each year to its new place.
Set the number of fiscal years (between 3 and 10) to keep on-hand in Company Maintenance > F5-Additional Information > # of Fiscal Years.
To maintain fiscal periods:
1
Select Financial > General Ledger > Processes > End of Month End Closing > Fiscal Period Table to open the Fiscal Period Maintenance screen.
2
Depending on what you want to maintain, select one of the following:
•
Change a fiscal period’s ending date. Move the cursor to End Date and enter the new ending date for the fiscal period. If you change the date, the next period’s beginning date is automatically adjusted. The change date and the user ID of the person that changed the date display. This normally only applies for leap years when you must manually change the February end date to 29.
•
Delete a fiscal year. Press F4-Delete Year to open the Delete a Fiscal Year screen. You can delete the entire fiscal year from the table. This process cannot be stopped once it begins. Once you are ready to delete a fiscal period, be sure to set Open? to N so that nobody can enter information in that period. Transactions that try to post to a deleted fiscal period will post to the next available open period.
•
Add a fiscal year. Press F5-Create New Year to open the Add a Fiscal Period screen. You can add the next year to the fiscal period table. The number of fiscal periods per year is determined in Company Maintenance > F5-Add Info. Fiscal Period Table Maintenance appends a YE (year end) period to use for Year-End Closing (refer to End-of-Year Closing Procedures).
•
Change the status of a fiscal year. Press F7-Maintain Open? to open the Maintain Open? screen. To maintain the status of a fiscal period, enter one of the following under Open?:
-
Y - Open for all module transactions to post within the date range.
-
N - Closed. If a transaction attempts to post to a closed period, the next available open period is used. If you use multiple currency, refer to Assigning Exchange Rates for Monthly G/L Reports for information how to update mutliple currency.
-
P - Open only for accounts payable transactions and journal entries. If a transaction attempts to post within the period range for any other module, the next available open period is used.
-
A - Open only for journal entries. If a transaction attempts to post within the period range for any other module, the next available open period is used.
3
Press F2-Save to save any changes to the fiscal periods.
The Viewing Fiscal Period Changes list the time, date and User ID for each change to a fiscal period.
