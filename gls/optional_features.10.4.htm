Setting up Fiscal Accounts
If you have multiple locations that share inventory but post to different fiscal periods, you can set up a parent account for the corporation and then set up fiscal accounts for each fiscal year-end month or company.
For example:
ABC Supply has stores in Dallas, Houston, Tulsa, and San Antonio that share inventory. The fiscal year-end is March for Dallas, June for Houston, and October for Tulsa and San Antonio.
1
Set up a parent account for ABC, which allows all the stores to share inventory.
2
Set fiscal accounts for MAR, JUN, and OCT for fiscal period postings. Tulsa and San Antonio will share the same fiscal account.
3
For each fiscal account, set up the fiscal period table in that account.
4
Each location is pointed to a fiscal account. For example, Dallas is pointed to the MAR account, Houston to the JUN account, and Tulsa and San Antonio are pointed to the OCT account. Tulsa and San Antonio will share a fiscal account since they have the same fiscal year. Refer to Location Maintenance for information on locations.
5
Define the fiscal period cross-references for each fiscal period/location in Multi-Fiscal Period Maintenance to indicate which fiscal period to post to in each fiscal account. For example, a transaction posted in 02-08 in the parent account may post to 02-12 in the fiscal account.
6
From the parent account, run the Multiple Fiscal Account Copy Procedure to set up related information in each fiscal account. This program will copy the appropriate CA, CADF, and CA.STAT records from the parent account to the fiscal account.
Important:
•
Maintain the G/L accounts in the parent account instead of in the fiscal accounts. The appropriate record in the fiscal account is automatically updated.
•
All postings should be done in the parent account. The G/L Balance Postings routine posts to the correct fiscal period on the location’s fiscal account.
