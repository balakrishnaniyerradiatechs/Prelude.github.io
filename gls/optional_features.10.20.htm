Posting Standard Cost
Stock Receipts Entry
When inventory is received:
•
The difference between the received cost and the standard cost is posted to Accrued Purch Price Variance.
•
Vendor Invoice Reconciliation Entry reverses the accrual posting and posts the actual variance to Purchase Price Variance.
•
Inventory Accrual is debited for the standard cost amount extended by the received quantity.
•
A/P Accrual is credited for the purchase order net price amount extended by the received quantity and debits/credits Purchase Price Variance Accrual for the difference.
Vendor Invoice Reconciliation Entry
When a vendor invoice is reconciled:
•
The accrual posting to Accrued Purch Price Variance from stock receipts is reversed and the difference between the invoice cost and the standard cost is posted to Purchase Price Variance.
•
A posting for a processed vendor invoice reverses the stock receipts posting and debits Inventory for the standard cost amount extended by the received quantity.
•
Accounts Payable is credited for the vendor invoice amount, and debits/credits Purchase Price Variance for the difference.
Cost Adjustment Entry
Cost adjustment entry allows you to change the standard cost for a product. Depending on how you set Standard Cost Level in Company Maintenance (refer to Using Standard Cost), the system updates the standard cost for the product or product warehouse. It locates each warehouse for the product that has an unlinked quantity on-hand and posts the difference between the old standard cost and the new standard cost extended by the quantity on-hand in that warehouse. The document ID for the posting is the next inventory adjustment number.
An increase in cost:
•
Debits Inventory.
•
Credits Cost Adjustment.
A decrease in cost:
•
Credits Inventory.
•
Debits Cost Adjustment.
If the cost adjustment reason code contains a general ledger account, the system will post to that account instead of the Cost Adjustment account.
Bill of Material Product Cost
You must use the Standard Cost Roll-Up Procedure to update standard cost for a bill of material product.
Standard Cost Roll-Up Procedure
The Standard Cost Roll-Up Procedure (BMW/Work Order > Processes) allows you to roll-up standard cost from all components on a bill of material into the bill of material product. Depending on how Standard Cost Level is set in Company Maintenance (refer to Using Standard Cost), standard cost is updated at the product level or the product warehouse level. You can enter a product line or a bill of material number to further define the products for which you want to calculate a roll-up cost.
Roll-up cost is calculated as follows:
1
Standard cost of each component in the bill of material is multiplied by the quantity required for assembly.
2
The cost for each burden item in the bill of material is multiplied by the quantity required for assembly.
3
The quantities in step 1 and 2 are added to determine the roll-up cost.
You can also roll-up a multiple-level bill of material (bill of material within a bill of material). The component bill of material is calculated, rolled up, and then used in the calculation for the bill of material parent.
An increase in cost:
•
Debits Inventory.
•
Credits Standard Cost Rollup Revaluation for the difference between the old standard cost and the new standard cost extended by the quantity on-hand for the warehouse. If the product line for the bill of material contains a different Standard Cost Rollup Revaluation account, then the system posts to that account instead of the Standard Cost Rollup Revaluation account in the general ledger table.
Transfer Entry
When inventory is transferred from one warehouse to another:
•
The difference between the shipping warehouse standard cost and the receiving warehouse standard cost is posted to Transfer Std Cost Variance at the shipping warehouse.
•
Inventory at the shipping warehouse is credited.
•
Inventory In Transit To Location is debited for the shipping warehouse.
When the material is received at the receiving warehouse:
•
Inventory In Transit To Location is credited for the shipping warehouse.
•
Intercompany A/R Clearing is debited for the product header standard cost amount extended by the received quantity.
•
Inventory at the receiving warehouse is debited.
•
Intercompany A/P clearing is credited for the product header standard cost amount extended by the received quantity.
Additional postings occur for the following transactions:
•
If a quantity is over-received in Transfer Receipts Entry:
-
Transfer Over Receive Variance at the shipping warehouse is credited.
-
Intercompany A/R Clearing is debited for the product header standard cost amount extended by the over-received quantity.
•
If a line is deleted in Transfer Receipt Entry:
-
Inventory at the shipping warehouse is debited.
-
Inventory In Transit To Location is credited for the product header standard cost amount extended by the quantity deleted.
•
When a transfer is received and standard cost is at the product warehouse level:
-
Inventory In Transit To Location at the shipping warehouse is credited for the shipping warehouse standard cost amount extended by the received quantity.
-
Intercompany A/R Clearing for the receiving warehouse is debited for the standard cost amount extended by the received quantity.
-
Transfer Std Cost Variance is debited/credited for the difference.
-
Inventory at the receiving warehouse is debited.
-
Intercompany A/P Clearing at the receiving warehouse is credited for the receiving warehouse standard cost amount extended by the received quantity.
