Detecting Inventory Discrepancies
If inventory and cost discrepancies exist when you attempt to balance the general ledger, you may need to review multiple transactions to see how they posted. The following are common causes of inventory discrepancies.
Asterisk (*) Products
If you use asterisk products to designate non-stock products, you may have balancing errors if any of the following occurs:
•
A product is returned to stock using a credit memo.
•
The quantity on a sales order is changed after the item is linked to a purchase order.
•
A linked line item is deleted.
•
The shipped quantity does not equal the sales order quantity.
•
A line item is partially linked.
Linked Products
When you link a line item (stock or non-stock) on a sales order to a purchase order, you may have balancing errors if any of the following occurs:
•
The quantity on a sales order is changed after the item is linked to a purchase order.
•
A linked line item is deleted.
•
The shipped quantity does not equal the sales order quantity.
•
A line-item is partially linked.
Deleted Receivers
If you delete a stock, linked, or vendor claim receiver in Vendor Invoice Reconciliation Entry or Vendor Claim Reconciliation Entry, the accrual postings are reversed from the receiver but the on-hand balance of the product does not change. If you delete a receiver, you must create a manual invoice or journal entry to account for the products that were on the receiver.
Negative Cost/On-Hand
If any product has a negative cost (linked or average), the general ledger will not balance.
Inventory Adjustments
An incorrect offsetting general ledger account in an inventory adjustment reason code can cause a balancing discrepancy.
Cost Adjustments
You can effectively adjust many purchase order costing errors when you reconcile a receiver with an invoice in Vendor Invoice Reconciliation Entry. To change a cost on any other transaction that already posted, you must reverse the transaction so that the G/L balances. An incorrect offsetting general ledger account in a cost adjustment reason code can also cause a balancing discrepancy.
Journal Entries
If you make a journal entry to adjust a general ledger balance, an incorrect general ledger account number can cause a balancing discrepancy. Do not use monthly journal entries against inventory accounts.
Units of Measures
Improper use of units of measure can create incorrect inventory balances at the perpetual and/or general ledger level.
