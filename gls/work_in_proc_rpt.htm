Work In Process Report
The Work In Process report lists work orders that have products issued to create a bill of material in Work Order Issues Entry.
Menu Path: Financial > General Ledger > Reports > Inventory Balancing > Work In Process > Work In Process Detail
Selection Criteria
Use the following criteria to limit the information to print on the report:
•
Work Order Number. The work order number to view.
•
Division. The highest possible grouping in your company (e.g., Eastern Division, Western Division).
•
Region. A subset of Division - if Division is used (e.g., Southeast Region, Northwestern Region).
•
Warehouse. The warehouse(s) to include on the report.
•
Department. A subset of warehouse. Typically used to separate expenses within a warehouse (e.g., accounting, sales, warehouse).
•
Cost Center. A subset of Department - if Department is used. Typically used to separate expenses within a warehouse or department (e.g., shipping, receiving, outside sales, inside sales).
•
Work Order Type. The work order type. Choose from the following:
-
Non-billable.
-
Warranty.
-
Billable.
-
Vendor Claim Expense.
Column Descriptions
Column Heading
Description
W/O#
The work order number.
Req-date
The date the work order is required for completion. The date is calculated from the assembly days entered in BOM Maintenance but can be changed in Work Order Entry.
Status
The status of the work order:
•
ENTERED - The work order is entered.
•
RELEASED - The work order is released.
Bill Number/Description
Line 1: The bill of material number.
Line 2: The bill of material description.
Product Number
Line 1: The products on the work order.
Line 2: The first line of description for the product.
Line 3: The second line of description for the product (if applicable).
U/M
The product’s unit of measure.
Iss Qty
The quantity of the product issued to this work order. The stock on-hand balance is decreased by this number and Work-in-Process is increased.
Cost
The cost of the product.
Ext-Cost
The total cost for the extended issued quantity (Iss Qty x Cost).
Total For Work Order
The work order totals.
Total Burden
The total burden costs for the work order.
Total Material
The total cost of materials and burden for the work order.
