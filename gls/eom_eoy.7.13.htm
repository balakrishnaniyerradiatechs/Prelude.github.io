G/L Year End Procedure
You must run the G/L Year End Procedure to close out all income and expense accounts to the retained earnings account defined in the general ledger (refer to Maintaining the General Ledger Chart of Accounts). The journal entry for the retained earnings posting uses a fiscal period of XX-YE where XX is the current year (e.g., 06-YE).
You can run the G/L Year End Close more than once per fiscal year since it creates additional closing journal entries for only those accounts which were posted to since the last time you ran it. You may need to close several times if you adjusted journal entries for previous periods.
 
Before you run the G/L Year End Procedure:
•
Create a new fiscal year (refer to Constructing the Fiscal Period Table).
•
Update the Fiscal Period Table with month-end dates for the new year (refer to Constructing the Fiscal Period Table).
•
Prepare recurring journal entries for the next year (refer to Creating Recurring Adjustments to General Ledger Accounts).
•
Finish closing the last regular monthly activities (refer to End-of-Month Closing Procedures).
•
Run a preliminary Trial Balance and review it for adjustments (refer to the Trial Balance Report).
•
Adjust journal entries as necessary (refer to Adjusting a General Ledger Account).
•
Delete inactive general ledger account numbers (the amount of time a general ledger account is inactive before it is deleted is determined by your company). To delete a general ledger account number, set Status to D in Chart of Accounts Maintenance.
•
Add any new chart of account numbers you plan to use in the coming year (refer to Chart of Accounts Maintenance). You may want to transfer balances to the re-numbered accounts before you close the year.
•
Run the Trial Balance Report and the Detail General Ledger Report. You may need copies for auditors and taxes.
•
Check with your system’s administrator to make sure the data files are backed up prior to closing.
To close your general ledger for a fiscal year:
1
Select Financial > General Ledger > Processes > End of Year Closing, the following message appears:
*** YEAR END CLOSE PROCEDURE***
This routine will close out all Income and Expense Accounts to the Retained Earnings Account. It should only run after all Financials have been verified. The routine will create and post the Closing JE.
Enter the Year to Close ___
2
Enter the last two digits of the year that you want to close.
3
Press F2-Begin Close. The GRIM screen for the Journal Entry Report appears.
The beginning and ending Journal Numbers are already filled in with the journal entry for year end (e.g., 006YE-2 for the second closing for 2006).
4
Once you choose Execute, the Journal Entry Proof reports prints. Refer to the Journal Entry Proof Report for an example and detailed explanation of the report.
 
Examples of Retained Earnings Posting:
If wildcards are used, each income and expense account posts to its own prefix. For example, if the G/L table accounts are:
•
Retained Earnings: 01 XX 01 9109
•
Income / Expense 1: 01 10 00 1234
•
Balance = 50.00
•
Income / Expense 2: 01 20 00 5678
•
Balance = 10.00
The year-end procedure posts to the following retained earnings accounts:
•
50.00 into account 01 10 01 9109
•
10.00 into account 01 20 01 9109
 
If wildcards are not used, all income and expense accounts post to the retained earnings account in the G/L table. For example, if the G/L table accounts are:
•
Retained Earnings: 01 10 01 9109
•
Income / Expense: 1 01 10 00 1234
•
Balance = 50.00
•
Income / Expense: 2 01 20 00 5678
•
Balance = 60.00
The year-end procedure posts to the following retained earnings account:
•
110.00 into account 01 10 01 9109
 
When you run the G/L End of Year Close and the Retained Earnings account is set up in the G/L Table with wildcards, the system verifies that all the appropriate Retained Earnings accounts are set up in Chart of Accounts Maintenance. A message appears to let you know which G/L prefixes are missing a Retained Earnings account.
Once you set up a Retained Earnings account for every G/L prefix in Chart of Accounts Maintenance, you can run the G/L End of Year Close to update the appropriate retained earnings accounts.
