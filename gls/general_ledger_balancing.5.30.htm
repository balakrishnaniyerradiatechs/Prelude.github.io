Using Reports/Inquiries to Balance the General Ledger
Depending on how you set up your general ledger and how you track inventory, you can use the Prelude reports and inquiries to help balance the general ledger.
General Ledger Balances
The following reports and inquiries allow you to view the general ledger balances.
Report/Inquiry
Description
Trial Balance Report
All the general ledger information needed to balance for each period.
Expanded Trial Balance Inquiry
The same information as the Trial Balance report.
 
Chart Detail Display
General ledger activity for one prefix and account number, including posting activity and the debit or credit amount.
Expanded G/L Summary Inquiry
General ledger activity for multiple prefixes and account numbers for a fiscal period range.
 
Perpetual Balances
Perpetual balances are necessary for general ledger balancing. The perpetual portion is the extended value representing the physical, tangible total. The following reports and inquiries allow you to view the perpetual balances.
Use summary reports for general ledger balancing. Use detail reports to research discrepancies.

Cost and Count Balances

Description

Products with negative linked on-hand balance.
