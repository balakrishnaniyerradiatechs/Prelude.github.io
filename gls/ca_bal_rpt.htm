Chart of Accounts Balance Report
The Chart of Accounts Balance report displays account balance information for a fiscal period for each G/L prefix combination associated with a general ledger account number.
Menu Path: Financial > General Ledger > Reports > Chart of Accounts Balance Report
Selection Criteria
Use the following criteria to limit the information to print on the report:
•
Account Number Range. The beginning and ending general ledger account numbers to include on the report. Leave blank to include all.
•
For Period Ending. The fiscal period to include.
•
Suppress Zero Balance Accts?. Choose from the following:
-
Y - Suppress the accounts that contain a zero balance.
-
N - Include the accounts that contain a zero balance.
•
Location. The locations to include. Leave blank to include all locations.
Column Descriptions
Column Heading
Description
General Ledger Account
The general ledger account number and description. Refer to the Default Chart of Accounts for a complete description of each account.
Div
The division number if your company uses divisions. A division is the highest possible grouping in your company (e.g., Eastern Division, Western Division).
Reg
The region number, if your company uses divisions. Regions are a subset of Division - if Divisions are used (e.g., Southeast Region, Northwest Region).
Loc
The location number, which could be a warehouse location (e.g., Denver, Dallas) where inventory is kept or it could be a corporate location where transactions may post to.
Dep
The department number if your company uses departments. Departments are a subset of Locations, typically used to separate expenses within a warehouse (e.g., accounting, sales, warehouse).
Ccr
The cost center number if your company uses departments. Cost centers are a subset of Departments - if Departments are used. Typically used to separate expenses within a warehouse or department (e.g., shipping, receiving, outside sales, inside sales).
Balance
The total balance for each general ledger prefix combination for this account and period.
