Closing the General Ledger for Year End
To close the G/L:
1
Update the Fiscal Period Table with month ending dates for the new year. Refer to Constructing the Fiscal Period Table.
2
Prepare Recurring Journal Entries for the next year.
3
Finish processing the last regular monthly activities.
4
Run a preliminary Trial Balance and review it for adjustments.
5
Make adjusting Journal Entries.
6
Delete account numbers that have been inactive for the required period.
7
Add new Chart of Account numbers you plan to use in the coming year. You may want to transfer balances to the re-numbered accounts before you close the year.
8
Prepare a Budget for the new fiscal year.
9
Run a Trial Balance and Detailed General Ledger Report. Make extra copies for an audit trail and archive one for tax purposes.
10
Backup the data files prior to closing.
11
Process General Ledger Year End Close (refer to G/L Year End Procedure). This procedure can be run more than once per fiscal year. It creates additional closing journal entries for only those accounts which have had postings made to them since the last time you ran it. Run this process now, and if you later need to make prior period adjusting journal entries, open any prior period and make the journal entries. Run it a second time or as many times as you need to close the fiscal year.
12
