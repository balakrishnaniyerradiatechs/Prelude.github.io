Recurring Journal Entry
To adjust a general ledger account on a recurring basis:
1
Select Financial > General Ledger > Journal Entries > Recurring Journal Entry to open the Recurring Journal Entry screen.
2
Enter the Journal Number or press ENTER over NEW to create a new journal number.
3
For Transaction Day, enter the day of the month for the adjustment to occur every month. For example, if you want the entry to post to the general ledger on the 5th of every month, enter 5.
If you enter 31, the system will just use the last day of the month if the month does not have 31 days.
4
For Reversal Date, enter the date to reverse the journal entry every month. You can reverse a journal entry to accrue an expense for one month, and then back out the transaction the next month. The reverse date must be greater than the transaction date. When you post the recurring journal entry (refer to Posting Recurring Entries), the reversal date is cleared.
Reversal Dates
Once you post the recurring entries, any existing reversal dates are cleared for the recurring journal entry. If the recurring journal entry is setup for multiple fiscal periods and requires a reversal entry for each month, you must bring up the journal entry in Recurring Journal Entry after each month's posting to update the reversal date before next month's posting.
5
Enter a Description to explain the purpose of the recurring adjustment. This description displays on general ledger reports and inquiries.
6
For Begin Period and End Period, enter the beginning and ending fiscal periods in which this journal entry should adjust the general ledger.
7
If you enter a Batch Number, you can use it when posting recurring journal entries so that all entries with the same batch number are posted together (refer to Posting Recurring Entries).
8
For each Ln#, enter the Division, Region, Location, Department, and Cost Center as required by your company (refer to Setting up the General Ledger Hierarchy).
9
Enter the general ledger Account number that the recurring journal entry should to post to.
10
Enter the Debit Amount of the recurring journal entry.
11
Enter the Credit Amount of the recurring journal entry.
12
For C, indicate if you want to enter a comment regarding this journal entry:
•
Y - Opens the Journal Comments screen to allow you to enter a comment explaining this entry. After the comments are entered, you can use F5-Comment Display to review them.
•
N - No comments are necessary for this journal entry (default).
13
Press F2-Save to complete the recurring journal entry. You must post the entry to update the general ledger (refer to Posting Recurring Entries).
Posting History
To list posted recurring journal entries, press F5-Posting History to open the History of Recurring JE Postings screen. The post date, debit/credit amount displays for each posted adjustment.
