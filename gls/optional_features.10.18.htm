Using Standard Cost
If your company uses standard cost for your accounting cost, you can set company parameters to use standard cost and to post to the correct general ledger accounts.

Posting Standard Cost
General Ledger Accounts for Standard Cost
Four general ledger accounts handle the posting of standard cost differences:
•
Std Cost Roll Inventory Revaluation. Posts the difference between the old standard cost and the new standard cost when the standard cost is rolled-up for a bill of material through the Bill of Materials Standard Cost Roll-Up Procedure. This account is overridden if an account exists in Product Line Maintenance.
•
Accrued Purch Price Variance. Posts the difference between the standard cost and the purchase order net cost when a product is received in Stock Receipt Entry. The posting occurs before the vendor invoice is reconciled. When the invoice is processed in Vendor Invoice Reconciliation Entry, this anticipated variance is reversed and the actual variance is posted to Purchase Price Variance.
•
Purchase Price Variance. Posts the difference between the standard cost and the net cost on the vendor invoice. This posting occurs when the vendor invoice is reconciled in Vendor Invoice Reconciliation Entry.
•
Transfer Std Cost Variance. Posts the difference between the shipping location standard cost and the receiving location standard cost when a product is transferred. The posting is made to the shipping location when the transfer is received at the receiving location.
