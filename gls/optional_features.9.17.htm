Entry Programs Affected by Multiple Currency
If you use multiple currencies (refer to Maintenance Requirements for Multiple Currency), the currency type, currency rate as of the transaction date, and the currency source are saved with each transaction.
For example, you receive inventory purchased from a Canadian vendor for 134.00 CDN dollars. At an exchange rate of 1.34 CDN to 1.00 US, the US (native) dollars is 100.00. The following entry programs convert foreign currencies based on the exchange rates to post to the general ledger.
Purchase Order Entry
Currency exchange information for the vendor is stored in the purchase order (e.g., $134 CDN).
Stock Receipts Entry
The currency type, source and exchange rate appear when you enter the receipt date. Amounts are in the purchase order’s currency. You can accept the exchange rate or enter a new one. When you save the data, the amounts convert to native currency using the exchange rate.

Accounts Payable Payment Selection/Print
The payment amounts are in the vendor’s currency. The general ledger postings reflect gains/losses if the exchange rate changed after the invoice was reconciled. If the exchange rate for 1 US dollar changes from 1.34 to 1.32, the conversion calculation is 134.00 /1.32 giving a US dollar amount of $101.52. Note: The original native currency amount was $100.00, which resulted in an exchange rate loss of $1.52.

Sales Order Entry
Currency exchange information is stored with each sales order. The costs and prices on the sales order are in the customer’s currency. Use Currency Conversion to add charges to an order for a non-native currency customer. Enter the amounts in native currency for each additional charge. When you save the charges, the amounts convert to the customer’s currency.

Service Charge Processing
Service charges generated both through Auto Service Charge Entry and Manual Service Charge Entry are created in the customer’s currency. If the customer does not have a currency rate in Customer Maintenance, General Ledger Parameters determine if the rate is from the daily exchange rate or the currency table (refer to Activating Multiple Currency).
