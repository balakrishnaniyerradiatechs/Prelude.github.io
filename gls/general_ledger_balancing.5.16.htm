Letters of Credit (LC)
A letter of credit is a contract between you and your bank to pay your vendor (typically an overseas vendor). The bank opens a letter of credit and promises to honor withdrawals on a pending transaction (e.g., purchase order). When that transaction occurs, the letter of credit is accepted and the transaction becomes a liability for your company (refer to “Using Letters of Credit” in the Accounts Payable documentation).
Letters of Credit post to two general ledger accounts:
•
Letter of Credit Draw:
-
Debited when a bank letter of credit is entered.
-
Credited when funds are committed and advanced to a purchase order in Letter of Credit Entry.
•
Letter of Credit Advance:
-
Debited when funds are associated with a purchase order in Letter of Credit Entry.
-
Credited when the product is received in Stock Receipts Entry.
