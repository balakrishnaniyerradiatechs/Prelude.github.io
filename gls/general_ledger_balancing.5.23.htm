Inventory Adjustments (SA)
When inventory discrepancies occur in your warehouse, you can use Inventory Adjustment Entry to manually correct the on-hand quantity of a product (Refer to “Adjusting Inventory” in the Inventory documentation). When you adjust inventory, you must indicate which type of inventory you are adjusting so that the general ledger and on-hand balances are correctly updated. The four types of inventory adjustments are:
•
INV - Regular on-hand inventory. Enter a positive or a negative quantity to adjust inventory. Average cost is updated when inventory is increased.
•
DMG - Damaged inventory. Enter a positive quantity to increase the damaged inventory amount for the product and decrease the on-hand inventory of the product. Enter a negative quantity to decrease the damaged inventory amount and increase the on-hand amount.
•
SCR - Scrapped/lost inventory. Enter a positive quantity to increase the scrapped inventory for the product and decrease the on-hand inventory of the product. Enter a negative quantity to decrease the scrapped inventory amount and increase the on-hand amount.
•
WTY - Warranty inventory. Enter a positive quantity to move the item from the on-hand inventory into warranty inventory. Refer to ”Creating Warranty Tags in Inventory Adjustment Entry” in the Inventory documentation for more information.
Before you can adjust inventory, you must set up reason codes in Inventory Adjustment Reason Maintenance to identify the reason for the adjustment and to assign the offsetting general ledger account (refer to “Establishing Inventory Adjustment Reason Codes” in the Inventory documentation). The Inventory Adjustment Inquiry lists all inventory adjustments for a warehouse.
