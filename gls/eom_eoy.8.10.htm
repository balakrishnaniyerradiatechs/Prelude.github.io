Closing Accounts Receivable for Year-End
To close out accounts receivable at year-end:
1
Run a preliminary Aging Report (Refer to the aging reports in the Account Receivable documentation) and review it with your principal financial officer.
2
Write-off un-collectible accounts to Bad Debt before the last month ends.
3
Finish processing the last regular monthly activities (refer to End-of-Month Closing Procedures).
4
Run a new final Accounts Receivable Aging Report, which includes only collectible accounts. You may need copies for auditors or tax records.
5
Delete inactive customers (the amount of time a customer account must be inactive before it is deleted is determined by your company).
