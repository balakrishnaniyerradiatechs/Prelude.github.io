Service Charge (SC)
A service charge is used to bill your customer based on past due invoices (refer to “Generating Service Charge Invoices” in the Accounts Receivable documentation).
The Accounts Receivable Option Parameter, Delay SC Post Until Collect?, determines if service charges appear in the General Ledger when the invoice generates or when the customer pays the service charge invoice:
•
N - The service charge posts to the general ledger when the service charge invoice prints. The following occurs during Service Charge Invoice Printing:
-
Accounts Receivable is debited.
-
Service Charge is credited.
•
Y - When the invoice generates, both a debit and a credit post to the Service Charge Account, so there is no net affect on the general ledger. The service charge posts to the general ledger when the customer pays the service charge invoice. The following occurs when the service charge invoice is paid in Cash Receipt Entry:
-
Accounts Receivable is not affected.
-
Cash is debited.
-
Service Charge is credited.
