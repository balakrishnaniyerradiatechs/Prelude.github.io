Journal Entry Proof Report
The Journal Entry Proof report lists journal entry debit, credit, and account information for a range of transaction dates or journal numbers.
Menu Path: Financial > General Ledger > Journal Entries > Journal Entry Report
Selection Criteria
Use the following criteria to limit the information to print on the report:
•
Description. The description of the journal entry if you want to limit the list to a type of entry. Leave blank to include all descriptions.
•
Transaction Date Range. The beginning and ending dates the journal entries were made.
•
Journal Number Range. The beginning and ending journal entry numbers to include. Leave blank to include all entries.
•
Status. The status of the journal entries to include:
-
O - Include journal entries that are still open.
-
P - Include journal entries that are already posted to the general ledger.
Column Descriptions
Column Heading
Description
Journal #
The journal entry number.
TransDte
The date of the journal entry.
Period
The fiscal period of the journal entry.
RevrsDte
The date the journal entry was reversed, if applicable.
RPeriod
The fiscal period of the reverse entry, if applicable.
Status
The status of the journal entry:
•
O - Open.
•
P - Posted.
Description
The description of the journal entry. The reason why the general ledger was adjusted.
Div
The division number if your company uses divisions. A division is the highest possible grouping in your company (e.g., Eastern Division, Western Division).
Reg
The region number, if your company uses regions. Regions are a subset of Division - if Divisions are used (e.g., Southeast Region, Northwest Region).
Loc
The location number, which could be a warehouse location (e.g., Denver, Dallas) where inventory is kept or it could be a corporate location where transactions may post to.
Dep
The department number if your company uses departments. Departments are a subset of Locations, typically used to separate expenses within a warehouse (e.g., accounting, sales, warehouse).
Ccr
The cost center number if your company uses cost centers. Cost centers are a subset of Departments - if Departments are used. Typically used to separate expenses within a warehouse or department (e.g., shipping, receiving, outside sales, inside sales).
Account
The general ledger account that was adjusted.
Description
The description of the general ledger account.
Debit Amount
The adjustment amount debited to the corresponding account.
Credit Amount
The adjustment amount credited to the corresponding account.
Distribution Comment
Any comments entered for the journal entry when the account was adjusted.
Journal Totals
The total of the debit and credit amounts.
